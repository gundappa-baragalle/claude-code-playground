**[Q4820]** [2000]
> In a certain code, MARCH is written as OCTEJ, how is RETURN written in that code ?

     A) TFUVSM
     B) QGSTQM
  ✅ C) TGVWTP
     D) TGRVSO

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may apply only +2 shift (Caesar cipher) without checking the pattern carefully. MARCH→OCTEJ: M+2=O, A+2=C, R+2=T, C+2=E, H+2=J. The shift is +2 for each letter.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Pattern: each letter shifted +2. RETURN: R→T, E→G, T→V, U→W, R→T, N→P → TGVWTP. Answer C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Coding pattern: +2 shift per letter (Caesar cipher variant). MARCH→OCTEJ; RETURN→TGVWTP

**[Q1753]** [1999]
> On another planet, the local terminology for earth, water, light, air and sky, are ‘sky’, ‘light’, ‘air’, ‘water’ and ‘earth’ respectively. If someone is thirsty there, what would he drink ?

     A) Sky
     B) Water
     C) Air
  ✅ D) Light

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may try to answer 'water' in the original language without decoding — reading 'water' directly from the question is the trap since terminology is flipped.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Build the translation table: our 'water' = their 'light'; thirsty person drinks water; water in their terminology is 'light' — answer is D (Light).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Classic coding-decoding: map original terms to new language systematically
     - Thirst → drink water; water in their language = 'light'

**[Q941]** [1998]
> If in a certain code SAND is VDQG and BIRD is ELUG, then what is the code for LOVE ?

     A) PRYG
     B) ORTG
     C) NPUH
  ✅ D) ORYH

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may apply a +3 shift correctly but must be careful with each letter: L→O, O→R, V→Y, E→H; confusing O with Q or Y with Z are common slip errors.
  💡 **Eliminate:** SAND→VDQG: S+3=V, A+3=D, N+3=Q, D+3=G. So LOVE: L+3=O, O+3=R, V+3=Y, E+3=H → ORYH (option D).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Caesar cipher: each letter shifted by +3 positions
     - LOVE → ORYH using +3 shift: L→O, O→R, V→Y, E→H

**[Q4783]** [1996]
> Six roads lead to a country. They may be indicated by letters X, Y, Z and digits 1, 2, 3. When there is storm, Y is blocked. When there are floods X, 1 and 2 will be affected. When road 1 is blocked, Z also is blocked. At a time when there are Hoods and a storm also blows, which road(s) can be used ?

     A) Z and 2
     B) Only Z
  ✅ C) Only 3
     D) Only Y

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse storm vs flood conditions; when BOTH occur simultaneously: storm blocks Y; floods block X, 1, 2; blocked 1 also blocks Z. Remaining: only 3.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Storm: Y blocked. Floods: X, 1, 2 blocked. Flood also blocks 1 → Z blocked. Available: only road 3. Answer: C (Only 3).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Storm + Flood: Y blocked (storm), X/1/2 blocked (flood), Z blocked (1 blocked → Z blocked)
     - Only road 3 remains unaffected by either storm or flood conditions
