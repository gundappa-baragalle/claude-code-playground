**[Q1379]** [2004]
> The resolution for removing the Vice-President of India can be moved in the:

     A) Lok Sabha alone
     B) Either House of Parliament
     C) Joint Sitting of Parliament
  ✅ D) Rajya Sabha alone

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often confuse removal procedures; the President's impeachment involves both Houses while Vice-President removal resolution must be initiated in Rajya Sabha (not Lok Sabha).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Article 67(b): Resolution to remove VP must be passed by Rajya Sabha by majority of its members AND agreed to by Lok Sabha — but the motion can only be MOVED in Rajya Sabha.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - VP removal: resolution moved in Rajya Sabha only, then agreed by Lok Sabha — Article 67(b)
     - VP is ex-officio Chairman of Rajya Sabha, hence removal initiated there

### 🟡 MEDIUM Priority — Full Options

**[Q1978]** [2008]
> Who among the following have held the office of the Vice-President of India? 1. Mohammad Hidayatullah 2. Fakhruddin Ali Ahmed 3. Neelam Sanjiva Reddy 4. Shankar Dayal Sharma Select the correct answer using the code given below: Code:

     A) 1, 2, 3 and 4
  ✅ B) 1 and 4 only
     C) 2 and 3 only
     D) 3 and 4 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Fakhruddin Ali Ahmed and Neelam Sanjiva Reddy are often confused; Fakhruddin Ali Ahmed was President (not VP), and Neelam Sanjiva Reddy was also President, not VP.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Mohammad Hidayatullah and Shankar Dayal Sharma were Vice Presidents; Fakhruddin Ali Ahmed was President; Neelam Sanjiva Reddy was President (and Speaker). Only 1 and 4 held VP office.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Fakhruddin Ali Ahmed = President, not Vice President
     - Neelam Sanjiva Reddy = President, not Vice President

### GS2

**[2022]** Q4. Discuss the role of the Vice-President of India as the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Vice President's constitutional role, Rajya Sabha functioning, Parliamentary procedure

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Vice President's role as Chairman of the Rajya Sabha is both procedurally vital and symbolically important — serving as the constitutional guardian of the upper house's proceedings and dignity.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Article 89: VP is ex-officio Chairman; Deputy Chairman elected from members handles absence
      • Powers as Chairman: presides over sessions, maintains order, decides points of order, certifies money bills
      • Cannot vote in debates; exercises casting vote only in case of tie
      • Chairman is not member of the House — cannot participate in debates
      • Procedural role: allots time for discussion, refers bills to select committees
      • Recent controversy: role in certifying bills, expulsion of members, managing disruptions
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The Vice President as Rajya Sabha Chairman serves as an impartial constitutional arbiter of upper house proceedings, whose effectiveness depends on maintaining non-partisan conduct consistent with constitutional propriety.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Polity — Rajya Sabha as revising house, Polity — Parliament procedures, Polity — Money Bill and Rajya Sabha's role
  🌐 Contemporary: Rajya Sabha disruptions and debates on Chairperson's powers of expulsion make this role periodically contentious.
