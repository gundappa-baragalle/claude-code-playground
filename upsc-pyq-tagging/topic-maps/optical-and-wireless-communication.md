**[Q3179]** [2016]
> With reference to ‘LiFi’, recently in the news, which of the following statements is/are correct?  1. It uses light as the medium for high-speed data transmission. 2. It is a wireless technology and is several times faster than ‘WiFi’.  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1 only.
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Both statements about LiFi are essentially correct — it uses light (visible/infrared) for data transmission and is faster than WiFi. No significant trap here.
  💡 **Eliminate:** LiFi (Light Fidelity) uses visible light for data transmission (S1 correct) and is indeed faster than WiFi theoretically (S2 correct). Answer: C (Both).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - LiFi uses LED light fluctuations for data transmission at speeds up to 224 Gbps
     - LiFi cannot pass through walls — high security but limited range compared to WiFi

**[Q2803]** [2014]
> With reference to technologies for solar power production, consider the following statements:  1. 'Photovoltaics' is a technology that generates electricity by direct conversion of light into electricity, while 'Solar Thermal' is a technology that utilizes the Sun's rays to generate heat which is further used in electricity generation process. 2. Photovoltaics generates Alternating Current (AC), while Solar Thermal generates Direct Current (DC). 3. India has manufacturing base for Solar Thermal technology, but not for Photovoltaics.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1, 2 and 3
     D) None

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is false: Photovoltaics produce DC, not AC (inverter converts DC to AC); Solar Thermal generates AC through turbines. Statement 3 is also false — India has PV manufacturing capacity.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Only Statement 1 is correct: PV converts light directly to electricity; Solar Thermal generates heat for steam turbines. Statement 2 reverses the AC/DC output. Statement 3 is outdated/wrong. Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Photovoltaics (PV): direct conversion of sunlight to DC electricity via semiconductor
     - Solar Thermal: heat → steam → turbine → AC electricity
     - India has PV manufacturing (though limited vs China)

**[Q4703]** [2024]
> Consider the following activities:  1. Identification of narcotics on passengers at airports or in aircraft 2. Monitoring of precipitation 3. Tracking the migration of animals  In how many of the above activities can the radars be used?

     A) Only one
  ✅ B) Only two
     C) All three
     D) None

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Radar CANNOT identify narcotics (statement 1 false) — it detects physical objects using radio waves, not chemical composition; X-ray/millimeter wave scanners do that instead. Radar CAN monitor precipitation (weather radar) and track animal migration via passive radar.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Radar cannot identify narcotics (chemical detection requires X-ray/spectroscopy); Radar CAN monitor precipitation (Doppler radar) and CAN track animal migration (ornithological radar studies). Answer: B (two — statements 2 and 3).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Doppler weather radar monitors precipitation patterns and intensity
     - Ornithological radar used to track bird and bat migration patterns
     - Narcotics detection: X-ray scanners, millimeter wave scanners, sniffer dogs — not radar

**[Q3960]** [2020]
> With reference to Visible Light Communication (VLC) technology, which of the following statements are correct?  1. VLC uses electromagnetic spectrum wavelengths 375 to 780 nm. 2. VLC is known as long-range optical wireless communication. 3. VLC can transmit large amounts of data faster than Bluetooth. 4. VLC has no electromagnetic interference.  Select the correct answer using the code given below:

     A) 1, 2 and 3 only
     B) 1, 2 and 4 only
  ✅ C) 1, 3 and 4 only
     D) 2, 3 and 4 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 says VLC is 'long-range' which sounds plausible for modern tech, but VLC is explicitly SHORT-range (limited by line-of-sight and light intensity). This is the key trap.
  💡 **Eliminate:** VLC uses visible light (375-780nm) ✓ and has no EM interference ✓. But VLC is short-range (line of sight, blocked by walls), not long-range. Eliminate options containing statement 2 (A,B,D). Answer: C (1,3,4).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - VLC uses 375-780nm (visible light spectrum)
     - VLC is short-range, line-of-sight technology
     - No electromagnetic interference — uses light not radio waves
     - Faster data transfer than Bluetooth possible

**[Q3434]** [2017]
> Organic Light Emitting Diodes (OLEDs) are used to create digital display in many devices. What are the advantages of OLED displays over Liquid Crystal displays?  1. OLED displays can be fabricated on flexible plastic substrates. 2. Roll-up displays embedded in clothing can be made using OLEDs. 3. Transparent displays are possible using OLEDs.  Select the correct answer using the code given below:

     A) 1 and 3 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) 1, 2 and 3
     D) None of the above statements is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may doubt all three advantages — flexible substrates and roll-up displays sound futuristic but are real OLED capabilities. All three are genuine advantages of OLED over LCD.
  💡 **Eliminate:** OLEDs are made of organic compounds on flexible substrates (advantage 1), can be made into roll-up/wearable displays (advantage 2), and can be made transparent (advantage 3). Answer is C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - OLED: Organic Light Emitting Diode — no backlight needed, self-illuminating
     - OLED advantage: flexible, foldable, rollable, transparent displays possible
     - OLEDs used in smartphones (AMOLED), TVs, and wearables

**[Q3083]** [2015]
> With reference to ‘fuel cells’ in which hydrogen-rich fuel and oxygen are used to generate electricity, consider the following statements:  1. If pure hydrogen is used as a fuel, the fuel cell emits heat and water as by-products. 2. Fuel cells can be used for powering buildings and not for small devices like laptop computers. 3. Fuel cells produce electricity in the form of Alternating Current (AC).  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Fuel cells do produce electricity but students may assume it's AC (like power plants); fuel cells produce DC which must be converted. Statement 2 (not for laptops) is also wrong.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Pure hydrogen fuel cells emit only heat and water (statement 1 correct). Fuel cells CAN power small devices (statement 2 wrong). Fuel cells produce DC, not AC (statement 3 wrong). Answer: 1 only.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Hydrogen fuel cell byproducts: heat and water (clean technology)
     - Fuel cells produce DC (Direct Current), not AC
     - Fuel cells power devices from laptops to buildings — not limited to buildings

**[Q2228]** [2009]
> WiMAX is related to which one of the following ?

     A) Biotechnology
     B) Space technology
     C) Missile technology
  ✅ D) Communication technology

  ⚠️ **Trap:** WiMAX sounds technical and could be confused with missile or biotech; it is Worldwide Interoperability for Microwave Access — a wireless broadband communication standard.
  💡 **Eliminate:** WiMAX (Worldwide Interoperability for Microwave Access) is a wireless communication technology for broadband internet access — answer is D (Communication technology).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - WiMAX: IEEE 802.16 standard for wireless broadband internet (like Wi-Fi but longer range)
     - WiMAX can provide broadband connectivity over large areas (up to 50 km)

**[Q1842]** [2007]
> What is the Galileo Project which has been in news recently?

     A) An intercountry programme of missile shield developed by the United States of America
     B) A project developed by India with assistance from Canada
     C) An environmental protection project being developed by Japan
  ✅ D) A multi-satellite navigation project being developed by the European Union

  ⚠️ **Trap:** European project sounds like it could be a missile shield; 'Galileo' as a name evokes the astronomer and science rather than navigation specifically.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Galileo is the EU's independent satellite navigation system (rival to US GPS) — a civilian multi-satellite navigation project.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Galileo: EU's civilian satellite navigation system (rival to US GPS)
     - Provides more accurate positioning than GPS for civilian use
