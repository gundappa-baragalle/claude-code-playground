**[Q4145]** [2021]
> Consider the following pairs : (Historical place) - (Well-known for)  1. Burzahom : Rock-cut shrines 2. Chandra-ketugarh : Terracotta art 3. Ganeshwar : Copper artefacts  Which of the pairs given above is/are correctly matched?

     A) 1 only
     B) 1 and 2
     C) 3 only
  ✅ D) 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Burzahom is associated with pit-dwellings and neolithic culture, not rock-cut shrines; Chandraketugarh is known for terracotta, making pair 2 correct; Ganeshwar for copper.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Pairs 2 (Chandraketugarh—terracotta) and 3 (Ganeshwar—copper) are correct; Burzahom is not associated with rock-cut shrines but with neolithic pit dwellings.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Chandraketugarh (West Bengal) is famous for its rich terracotta art tradition
     - Ganeshwar (Rajasthan) yielded one of India's largest collections of copper artefacts

**[Q3826]** [2019]
> The word 'Denisovan' is sometimes mentioned in media in reference to

     A) fossils of a kind of dinosaurs
  ✅ B) an early human species
     C) a cave system found in North-East India
     D) a geological period in the history of Indian subcontinent

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates unfamiliar with paleoanthropology may guess dinosaurs (option A) or a geological period. Denisovans are an early human species — a scientific discovery not widely taught in standard UPSC preparation.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Denisovans are an archaic human species discovered from DNA analysis of fossils found in Denisova Cave, Siberia. They are a sister group to Neanderthals. Answer is B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Denisovans: ancient human species identified from fossil remains in Denisova Cave, Siberia (2010)
     - Closely related to Neanderthals; some modern humans (especially in East Asia/Oceania) carry Denisovan DNA
     - DNA analysis revolutionized understanding of human evolution

### GS1

**[2015]** Q2. Mesolithic rock cut architecture of India not only reflects the cultural life of the times but also a fine aesthetic sense comparable to modern painting. Critically evaluate this comment.
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHAT | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Prehistoric art and cultural life, Aesthetic expression in ancient India, Bhimbetka as heritage site

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Mesolithic rock art of India, most magnificently preserved at Bhimbetka, Madhya Pradesh, offers a vivid window into the cultural life, belief systems, and remarkable aesthetic sensibility of prehistoric communities.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Cultural life reflected: Scenes of hunting, gathering, dancing, communal rituals, animal-human interactions — documenting the social organization of Mesolithic communities
      • Aesthetic sophistication: Dynamic compositions, overlapping figures, use of natural pigments (ochre, hematite, manganese oxide) showing technical skill
      • Modern comparisons: Geometric abstraction, movement depicted through multiple limbs (precursor to Cubism/Futurism in technique), symbolic representation of animals
      • Continuity of tradition: Some shelters at Bhimbetka show paintings from Paleolithic through historical periods — one of world's longest continuous artistic traditions
      • Limitations: Attributing 'fine aesthetic sense' risks imposing modern criteria; these were likely ritual/communicative rather than purely aesthetic acts
      • Bhimbetka as UNESCO site: Recognized for outstanding universal value in cultural heritage
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India's Mesolithic rock art demonstrates a sophisticated aesthetic sensibility and rich cultural life that stands comparison with modern art movements, while also serving as an irreplaceable record of prehistoric human experience.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 — Indian art and culture, GS1 — Prehistoric India
  🌐 Contemporary: Bhimbetka and other rock art sites face threats from tourism pressure and vandalism.
