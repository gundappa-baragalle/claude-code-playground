**[Q3771]** [2019]
> Among the following, which one is the largest exporter of rice in the world in the last five years?

     A) China
  ✅ B) India
     C) Myanmar
     D) Vietnam

  ⚠️ **Trap:** China is the world's largest rice producer but not the largest exporter — most of China's production is consumed domestically. Vietnam and Thailand are known rice exporters.
  💡 **Eliminate:** India has been the world's largest rice exporter for several consecutive years (2012 onwards), surpassing Thailand and Vietnam. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**

     - Non-basmati rice from Punjab, Andhra Pradesh; Basmati from Punjab, Haryana, UP

  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHY | 15m | ~250w*

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's transformation from a food-importing nation reliant on PL-480 to a net food exporter stands as one of the most dramatic turnarounds in agricultural history.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • 1960s context: severe shortage, PL-480 dependence, 'ship to mouth'

      • Export challenges: quality standards, trade barriers, climate risks
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India's agricultural success story must be sustained through diversification, technology, and climate-resilient farming to secure its position as a reliable food exporter.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 — Geography, Agriculture, GS3 — Economy, Agriculture

  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 Agriculture, GS3 Economy — food security, GS1 Geography — agricultural
  🌐 Contemporary: India's push for diversified agriculture under PM-KISAN and National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture reflects this challenge.

**[2020]** Q13. What are the major factors responsible for making rice-wheat system a success? In spite of this success how has this system become bane in India?
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHY | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Agricultural sustainability, Green Revolution legacy, Food security vs ecological balance

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The rice-wheat cropping system of the Indo-Gangetic Plains powered India's Green Revolution but has become an ecological and economic liability threatening long-term agricultural sustainability.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Success factors: HYV seeds, assured irrigation (canal + groundwater), MSP support, and procurement infrastructure made rice-wheat system highly productive
      • Contribution: India achieved food self-sufficiency, moved from food aid dependence to net exporter of rice and wheat
      • Ecological bane: Intensive groundwater use causes alarming depletion (Punjab water table falling at 1m/year); soil health deteriorating from mono-cropping and heavy fertiliser use
      • Stubble burning: Short turnaround between rice harvest and wheat sowing forces farmers to burn paddy straw, causing severe air pollution in north India
      • Economic stress: Diminishing returns — yield growth stagnating; high input costs eroding farm profitability
      • Solutions: Crop diversification (pulses, oilseeds), direct seeding of rice, zero-tillage wheat, Mera Pani Meri Virasat (incentives for crop change)
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The rice-wheat system must be transformed from an input-intensive monoculture into a diversified, resource-efficient system to balance food security with ecological sustainability.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3: Agriculture, environment, GS1: Geography — Indo-Gangetic plains, Environment: air/water pollution
  🌐 Contemporary: Punjab's groundwater crisis and Delhi's air pollution from stubble burning make this a pressing current issue.

  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Rural livelihoods, Non-farm employment, Agricultural diversification

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Livestock rearing, encompassing dairy, poultry, fisheries, and animal husbandry, offers a resilient pathway for non-farm income diversification in rural India.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Livestock provides employment to ~20 crore farmers, especially landless and marginal farmers
      • Potential in dairy, poultry, fisheries, and meat processing sectors

      • Measures: National Livestock Mission, PM Matsya Sampada Yojana, Animal Husbandry Infrastructure Development Fund
      • Promote cooperatives on Amul model; improve fodder availability and breed improvement
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Integrating livestock into mainstream agricultural policy with robust institutional support can transform rural livelihoods and reduce distress migration.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Rural development, Food security, Women empowerment
  🌐 Contemporary: Post-COVID rural distress has heightened focus on non-farm rural income through livestock.

### Common Trap Patterns on This Topic
- Students may exclude Statement II thinking primary product pricing is a market factor rather than a policy cause, or doubt whether all three are simultaneously valid.
- Without the actual figure, one must rely on knowledge of production trends; rice production in 1991-99 showed steady growth reaching ~90-95 MT, distinguishing it from wheat (fluctuating), oilseeds (volatile), and pulses (stagnant).
- Statement 3 claims sugar can ONLY be made from sugarcane — this is clearly false as sugar beet is a major sugar source globally. Students may not think this through if focused on India.
- Statement 4 (cheaper to import) sounds plausible in a globalized economy but is factually incorrect as India often imposes import duties on oilseeds; students may include it.
- Wheat vs Rice as the top crop is a perennial confusion — India produces more rice than wheat in most years, but candidates assume wheat leads due to Green Revolution association.
