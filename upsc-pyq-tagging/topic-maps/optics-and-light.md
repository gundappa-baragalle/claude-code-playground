**[Q4]** [1995]
> An air bubble in water will act like a

     A) convex mirror
     B) convex lens
     C) concave mirror
  ✅ D) concave lens

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Intuition says a bubble (curved like a lens) should behave like a convex lens. But it's filled with less-dense air inside more-dense water — a rarer medium inside denser medium acts as diverging (concave) lens.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Refractive index: water > air. A bubble is air (low n) surrounded by water (high n). Light entering a less-dense centre from denser medium diverges — same as concave lens. Contrast with glass (high n) in air = convex behaviour.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Air bubble in water: rarer medium inside denser → acts as concave lens (diverging)
     - Glass lens in air: denser medium inside rarer → acts as convex lens (converging)
     - Refractive index of water (~1.33) > air (~1.0)
