**[Q4675]** [2024]
> With reference to the Himalayan rivers joining the Ganga downstream of Prayagraj from West to East, which one of the following sequences is correct?

     A) Ghaghara - Gomati - Gandak - Kosi
  ✅ B) Gomati - Ghaghara - Gandak - Kosi
     C) Ghaghara - Gomati - Kosi - Gandak
     D) Gomati - Ghaghara - Kosi - Gandak

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Gomati vs Ghaghara order is the key confusion — Gomati joins Ganga at Varanasi (upstream/west) while Ghaghara joins further east near Saran district.
  💡 **Eliminate:** West to East order of Ganga tributaries downstream of Prayagraj: Gomati (joins near Varanasi) → Ghaghara (Saran, Bihar) → Gandak (Hajipur) → Kosi (Katihar). Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Gomati joins Ganga near Varanasi (Saidpur); Ghaghara joins near Chhapra (Saran); Gandak at Hajipur; Kosi at Kursela
     - These left-bank Himalayan tributaries join Ganga in sequence from west to east

**[2013]** Q22. (b) There is no formation of deltas by rivers of the Western Ghats. Why?
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHY | 5m | ~100w*
  🏷️ Themes: No delta formation by Western Ghats rivers, Drainage geography of Indian peninsula

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~100 words: intro 30w / body 80w / conclusion 25w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Rivers flowing from the Western Ghats do not form deltas due to their short course, steep gradient, limited sediment load, and direct discharge into the Arabian Sea.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Short course: Rivers like Periyar and Sharavathi have very short distances from source to sea — insufficient for sediment accumulation
      • Steep gradient: Fast-flowing streams erode rather than deposit sediment
      • Hard crystalline bedrock: Limited weathering means less sediment available for transport
      • Direct Arabian Sea discharge: Abrupt meeting with sea in narrow coastal plain — no broad tidal flat for delta growth
      • Contrast: Eastern rivers like Godavari, Krishna flow long distances across Deccan plains, carrying heavy sediment and forming large deltas
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The absence of deltas in Western Ghats rivers reflects fundamental contrasts in gradient, course length, and geological setting compared to east-flowing peninsular rivers.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 Physical Geography, Drainage systems of India, GEO.IND
