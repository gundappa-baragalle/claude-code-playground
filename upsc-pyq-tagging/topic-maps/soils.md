**[Q4172]** [2021]
> The black cotton soil of India has been formed due to the weathering of

     A) brown forest soil
  ✅ B) fissure volcanic rock
     C) granite and schist
     D) shale and limestone

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse granite/schist (which forms red soils) or shale/limestone with volcanic origin; Deccan black soil is specifically from basaltic lava weathering.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Black cotton soil (Regur) is formed by weathering of Deccan Trap volcanic rocks — fissure volcanic (basaltic) rock is the correct answer.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Black cotton soil (Regur) formed from weathering of Deccan Trap basalt (fissure volcanic rock)
     - Rich in calcium carbonate, magnesium, potash — good for cotton

**[Q3629]** [2018]
> With reference to agricultural soils, consider the following statements:  1. A high content of organic matter in soil drastically reduces its water holding capacity. 2. Soil does not play any role in the sulphur cycle. 3. Irrigation over a period of time can contribute to the salinization of some agricultural lands.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 and 2 only
  ✅ B) 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is a strong trap — high organic matter actually INCREASES water holding capacity (humus absorbs water); statement 2 ignores soil's role in sulphur cycle as a sulphur reservoir.
  💡 **Eliminate:** High organic matter increases (not reduces) water holding capacity (statement 1 wrong); soil plays a significant role in sulphur cycle as decomposer and reservoir (statement 2 wrong); irrigation causes salinization over time (statement 3 correct) — answer B (3 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Organic matter (humus) greatly increases soil's water retention and porosity — it holds water like a sponge
     - Waterlogging and evaporation from irrigation leave behind salts, causing soil salinization (secondary salinization)

**[Q2653]** [2013]
> Which of the following statements regarding laterite soils of India are correct?  1. They are generally red in colour. 2. They are rich in nitrogen and potash. 3. They are well-developed in Rajasthan and UP. 4. Tapioca and cashew nuts grow well on these soils.  Select the correct answer using the codes given below.

     A) 1, 2 and 3
     B) 2, 3 and 4
  ✅ C) 1 and 4
     D) 2 and 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 says laterite soils are rich in nitrogen and potash — they are actually poor in nitrogen and potash (leached away). Statement 3 is wrong — laterite soils are found in Kerala, Karnataka, Odisha, not Rajasthan/UP.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Laterite soils are red (iron-rich), poor in N and potash, found in humid tropics (Kerala, Odisha, parts of Maharashtra/Karnataka), and support tapioca/cashew. Only statements 1 and 4 are correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Laterite soils are red due to high iron/aluminium oxides
     - They are deficient in nitrogen, potash, lime — highly leached
     - Found in areas with heavy seasonal rainfall — Kerala, Odisha, Karnataka hills
     - Tapioca and cashew nuts grow well on laterite soils

**[Q2332]** [2010]
> When you travel in certain parts of India, you will notice red soil. What is the main reason for this colour ?

     A) Abundance of magnesium
     B) Accumulated humus
  ✅ C) Presence of ferric-oxides
     D) Abundance of phosphates

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may guess magnesium (red-tinged soils) or phosphates — ferric oxide (iron oxide) is what gives red soil its characteristic colour.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Red soil gets its colour from ferric oxides (iron(III) oxide / Fe₂O₃) — the same compound that makes iron rust red. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Red soils of Deccan plateau and Eastern Ghats are red due to ferric oxide (Fe₂O₃) content
     - Red soils are well-drained, low in nutrients but good for dry farming — found in AP, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka
