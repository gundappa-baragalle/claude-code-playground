**[Q3418]** [2017]
> Consider the following statements:  1. In India, the Himalayas are spread over five States only. 2. Western Ghats are spread over five States only. 3. Pulicat Lake is spread over two States only.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     C) 2 and 3 only
     D) 1 and 3 only

  💡 **Eliminate:** Eliminate by recalling Himalayas cover Jammu & Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh, and more — definitely more than 5 states, making statement 1 false.

     - Pulicat Lake is on AP-Tamil Nadu border
     - Himalayas span more than 5 states including J&K, HP, UK, Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh, and border states
     - Western Ghats span Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Goa, Maharashtra — 5 states, but statement 2 is tricky

     - Eight Degree Channel separates Minicoy (India) from Maldives

**[2013]** Q22. (a) Bring out the causes for more frequent occurrence of landslides in the Himalayas than in the Western Ghats.
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHY | 5m | ~100w*
  🏷️ Themes: Landslide frequency in Himalayas vs Western Ghats, Geological vulnerability and human activity

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~100 words: intro 30w / body 80w / conclusion 25w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Himalayas experience far more frequent and severe landslides than the Western Ghats due to their distinct geological age, tectonic setting, rock composition and anthropogenic pressures.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Young, fragile rocks: Himalayas are geologically young with soft, friable sedimentary rocks; Western Ghats have ancient, hard crystalline rocks
      • Tectonic activity: Himalayas sit on active plate boundaries (India-Eurasian collision); Western Ghats are tectonically stable
      • Steep gradients: Himalayan slopes are steeper, especially in the Greater Himalayas, amplifying mass movement risk
      • Rainfall pattern: Intense monsoonal rains combined with snowmelt saturate Himalayan slopes; Western Ghats receive heavy rain but on more stable terrain
      • Human interference: Road cutting, deforestation, construction in Himalayas destabilizes slopes; Western Ghats better forested
      • Seismic trigger: Earthquakes in Himalayan region (seismic zone IV-V) frequently trigger landslides
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Effective landslide mitigation in the Himalayas requires integrated geological mapping, slope stabilization, sustainable development planning, and early warning systems.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 Physical Geography, GS3 Disaster Management, ENV.DIS
  🌐 Contemporary: Joshimath subsidence (2023), Chamoli disaster (2021) and Kedarnath 2013 highlight escalating Himalayan landslide vulnerability.
