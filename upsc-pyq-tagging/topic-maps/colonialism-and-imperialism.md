**[Q2001]** [2008]
> What is Agent Orange?

     A) An ace spying group trained by the Police in London
  ✅ B) A weed-killing chemical used by the US Military in the Vietnam War
     C) A technique used in the advanced railway safety procedure
     D) A special chemical used in making alloy steel

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The orange color in the name may mislead students into thinking it's a surveillance program or chemical compound used in steel-making.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Agent Orange was a herbicide/defoliant used by the US military during the Vietnam War (1961–1971) to destroy jungle cover and crops. It contained dioxin, causing long-term health damage. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Agent Orange — herbicide defoliant used by US in Vietnam War; caused long-term health effects due to dioxin contamination
     - Named 'orange' from color of storage barrel stripe

### GS1

**[2013]** Q12. "Africa was chopped into States artificially created by accidents of European competition." Analyze.
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHY | 10m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Berlin Conference and artificial borders, European competition creating African states, Legacy of artificial borders in contemporary Africa

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The cartographic violence of European colonialism — drawing arbitrary lines across Africa's diverse civilizational landscape at the Berlin Conference of 1884-85 — created the 'artificial states' whose instabilities continue to define African politics.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Berlin Conference (1884-85): 13 European powers divided Africa without consulting Africans; only Ethiopia and Liberia remained independent
      • Nature of artificiality: Borders drawn by rivers/meridians split ethnic communities (Somali across 5 countries), amalgamated hostile groups (Hutu-Tutsi in Rwanda/Burundi)
      • European competition: Britain (Cape-to-Cairo), France (west-east), Belgium, Germany, Portugal — African borders reflect power bargains, not geography
      • Consequences: Post-independence secessionism (Biafra, South Sudan), ethnic conflicts (Rwanda genocide), irredentism (Greater Somalia concept)
      • Post-colonial challenge: OAU's 1963 Cairo Resolution preserving colonial borders as pragmatic choice — yet the borders' artificiality persists as source of instability
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Africa's post-colonial instability is partly a legacy of borders created by European competition rather than organic political development — a reminder that state-building requires legitimacy, not just cartographic precision.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 World History, GS2 IR Africa, GS1 Society
  🌐 Contemporary: Contemporary conflicts in Sudan, DRC and Sahel echo the border-drawing legacies of colonial-era European competition.

**[2013]** Q13. "American Revolution was an economic revolt against mercantilism." Substantiate.
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHY | 10m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: American Revolution, Anti-mercantilism, Colonial economics

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The American Revolution, while draped in the language of liberty and natural rights, was fundamentally triggered by colonial resentment against Britain's mercantilist exploitation — making it as much an economic revolt as a political one.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Mercantilism: colonies as raw material suppliers and captive markets; profits flowed to Britain; Navigation Acts (1651+) restricted colonial trade to British ships
      • Economic grievances: Stamp Act, Townshend Acts, Tea Act — taxation without representation was fundamentally about economic control
      • Colonial counter: boycott of British goods, non-importation agreements, continental association — economic resistance preceding armed resistance
      • Boston Tea Party (1773): direct resistance to monopolistic East India Company tea trade — economic catalyst for political rupture
      • Adam Smith's critique: published Wealth of Nations (1776) same year as Revolution — mercantilism was inefficient and exploitative
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The American Revolution demonstrated that sustained economic exploitation, even within a shared cultural framework, breeds political revolt — mercantilism's contradictions ultimately dissolved the empire that created it.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 World History, GS3 Economy, Political Philosophy
  🌐 Contemporary: Parallels drawn with contemporary neo-mercantilist trade wars (US-China) and resource nationalism debates.
