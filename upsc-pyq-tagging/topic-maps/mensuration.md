**[Q4951]** [1997]
> The length, breadth and height of a room are 1, b and h respectively. The perimeter of the ceiling expressed as a percentage of the total area of the four walls is

     A) 100 h
  ✅ B) 100/h
     C) h
     D) h/100

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse perimeter of ceiling with area of ceiling, or incorrectly compute total wall area; dimensional analysis is key.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Ceiling perimeter = 2(l+b); total wall area = 2(l+b)h; ratio as percentage = [2(l+b)/2(l+b)h] × 100 = 100/h — answer is B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Perimeter of ceiling = 2(l+b)
     - Total area of four walls = 2(l+b)h

**[Q708]** [2002] A solid cube just gets completely immersed in water when a 0.2 kg mass is placed on it. If the mass is removed, the cube is 2 cm about the water level. What is the length of each side of the cube?

     A) 12 cm
  ✅ B) 10 cm
     C) 8 cm
     D) 6 cm

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates must set up two equations: one for full immersion with 0.2kg mass, another for floating 2cm above water without mass. Confusing which condition applies to which equation is the main trap.

     A) 0.75 : 1
  ✅ B) 1 : 1
     C) 1 : 0.75

**[Q2824]** [1997] A rectangular plot of lawn shown in the figure has dimensions x and y and is surrounded by a gravel pathway of width 2 m. What is the total area of the pathway ?

     A) 2x + 2y + 4
     B) 2x + 2y + 8
     C) 4x+ 4y + 8
  ✅ D) 4x + 4y + 16

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often calculate only the border area as 2×(length + width)×width, forgetting the four corner squares; the correct formula accounts for the full outer rectangle minus the inner lawn.

**[Q208]** [1995] In track meets both 100 yards and 100 metres are used as distances. By how many metres is 100 metres longer than 100 yards ?

     A) 0.856 m
  ✅ B) 8.56 m
     C) 0.0856 m
     D) 1.0 m

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse yards and metres; 1 yard = 0.9144 metres, so 100 yards = 91.44 m; difference = 100 - 91.44 = 8.56 m.
