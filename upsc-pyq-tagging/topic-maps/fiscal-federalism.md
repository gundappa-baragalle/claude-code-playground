**[Q2524]** [2012]
> Which of the following is/are among the noticeable features of the recommendations of the Thirteenth Finance Commission?  1. A design for the Goods and Services Tax, and 台 compensation package linked to adherence to the proposed design. 2. A design for the creation of lakhs of jobs in the next ten years in consonance with India's demographic dividend 3. Devolution of a specified share. of central taxes to local bodies. as grants  Select the correct answer using the codes given below:

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
  ✅ C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 about 'creating lakhs of jobs' sounds plausible as Finance Commissions often address employment; students may include it. Statement 3 about devolution to local bodies is correct and often missed.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The 13th Finance Commission is known for GST design and local body grants — statements 1 and 3 are correct. Job creation (statement 2) is not a Finance Commission mandate.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 13th Finance Commission recommended GST framework with compensation package
     - Devolution to local bodies as grants is part of 13th FC recommendations

**[Q73]** [1995]
> Corporation tax

     A) is levied and appropriated by the States
     B) is levied by the Union and collected and appropriated by the States
  ✅ C) is levied by the Union and shared by the Union and the States
     D) is levied by the Union and belongs to it exclusively

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option D (Union exclusively) sounds plausible since corporation tax appears in the Union List. But the Constitution mandates sharing corporation tax proceeds with states through the Finance Commission.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Corporation tax is in Union List (levied by Union) but proceeds are shared between Union and States as per Article 270/271 on divisible pool. Correct: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Corporation tax — Union List item, levied by Centre but shared with states
     - Articles 270-271 — divisible pool taxes shared between Centre and States

> Which of the following statements with regard to recommendations of the 15th Finance Commission of India are correct?  I. It has recommended grants of ₹ 4,800 crores from the year 2022-23 to the year 2025-26 for incentivizing States to enhance educational outcomes. II. 45% of the net proceeds of Union taxes are to be shared with States. III. ₹ 45,000 crores are to be kept as performance-based incentive for all States for carrying out agricultural reforms. IV. It reintroduced tax effort criteria to reward fiscal performance.  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) I, II and III
     B) I, II and IV
  ✅ C) I, III and IV
     D) II, III and IV

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The 15th FC devolution share is 41% (not 45%) — Statement II is a common trap. Statement IV about tax effort is also incorrect for the 15th FC.
  💡 **Eliminate:** 15th FC devolved 41% (not 45%) to states — II wrong. Statement IV (tax effort criteria reintroduced) — 15th FC did NOT reintroduce this — wrong. Statements I (education grants) and III (agriculture reforms) are correct. Answer: C (I, III and IV? — Let's verify: answer given is C = I, III and IV). Since the correct answer is C (I, III, IV), IV must be correct and II is the wrong one.
  📌 **Key facts:**

**[Q4511]** [2023]
> Consider the following :  1. Demographic performance 2. Forest and ecology 3. Governance reforms 4. Stable government 5. Tax and fiscal efforts  For the horizontal tax devolution, the Fifteenth Finance Commission used how many of the above as criteria other than population area and income distance?

     A) Only two
  ✅ B) Only three
     C) Only four
     D) All five

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Stable government and governance reforms sound like criteria but were not used by 15th FC; the 15th FC criteria beyond population, area and income distance are: demographic performance, forest and ecology, and tax/fiscal efforts — three criteria.
  💡 **Eliminate:** 15th FC used three additional criteria beyond population, area and income distance: Demographic Performance, Forest and Ecology, and Tax & Fiscal Efforts — answer is 'Only three'.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 15th Finance Commission additional criteria: Demographic Performance (25%), Forest & Ecology (10%), Tax/Fiscal Efforts (2.5%), plus Area (15%) and Income Distance (45%)
     - Demographic performance rewarded states that controlled population growth (inverse incentive to UP/Bihar)

**[Q3556]** [2018]
> Consider the following statements :  1. The Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management (FRBM) Review Committee Report has recommended a debt to GDP ratio of 60% for the general (combined) government by 2023, comprising 40% for the Central Government and 20% for the State Governments. 2. The Central Government has domestic liabilities of 21% of GDP as compared to that of 49% of GDP of the State Governments. 3. As per the Constitution of India, it is mandatory for a State to take the Central Government's consent for raising any loan if the former owes any outstanding liabilities to the latter.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
  ✅ C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 has inverted figures—Centre has ~49% domestic liabilities, States ~21%—which traps candidates who remember numbers but confuse which government has which.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Statement 2 is wrong (figures are reversed); Statements 1 and 3 are correct—FRBM recommended 60% (40%+20%) and Article 293 requires state consent from Centre for borrowing.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - FRBM Review Committee: 60% debt-to-GDP (40% Centre + 20% States)
     - Article 293(3): States need Centre consent to borrow if outstanding dues exist

**[Q3454]** [2017]
> Consider the following statements :  1. Tax revenue as a percent of GDP of India has steadily increased in the last decade. 2. Fiscal deficit as a percent of GDP of India has steadily increased in the last decade.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
  ✅ D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Both statements sound like reasonable macro trends; tax-to-GDP has actually fluctuated (not steadily risen) and fiscal deficit has also not steadily increased — both statements are wrong.
  💡 **Eliminate:** India's tax-to-GDP ratio has not steadily increased (fluctuated around 10-12%). Fiscal deficit has varied widely — it fell post-FRBM, then rose during COVID. Neither statement is correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - India's tax-to-GDP ratio fluctuates around 10-12%; not steadily increasing
     - Fiscal deficit: 6.4% (2012), reduced to 3.4% (2019), spiked during COVID — not steady increase
     - FRBM Act targets fiscal consolidation — deficit reduction is the policy goal

**[Q2267]** [2010]
> Which one of the following was not stipulated in the Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management Act, 2003 ?

     A) Elimination of revenue deficit by the end of the fiscal year 2007-08
     B) Non-borrowing by the central government from Reserve Bank of India except under certain circumstances
  ✅ C) Elimination of primary deficit by the end of the fiscal year 2008-09
     D) Fixing government guarantees in any financial year as a percentage of GDP

  ⚠️ **Trap:** FRBM stipulated elimination of revenue deficit by 2007-08 and reduction of fiscal deficit; elimination of primary deficit by 2008-09 was NOT stipulated — this makes option C the answer.
  💡 **Eliminate:** FRBM 2003 targets: eliminate revenue deficit by 2007-08, reduce fiscal deficit to 3% of GDP; primary deficit elimination was not a specific mandated target — so C is not stipulated.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - FRBM Act 2003 mandated elimination of revenue deficit and 3% fiscal deficit target
     - Primary deficit = fiscal deficit minus interest payments — was not a standalone FRBM target

**[Q840]** [2000]
> The Standing Committee of State Finance Ministers recommended in January 2000 uniform rates across the States in respect of

     A) value-added tax
  ✅ B) sales tax
     C) stamp duty and registration fees
     D) agricultural income-tax

  ⚠️ **Trap:** VAT (Value Added Tax) is often associated with uniformity across states as it was the major tax harmonization effort of the 2000s, but in January 2000 the recommendation was for sales tax uniformity.
  💡 **Eliminate:** VAT implementation came after 2003 in India; in 2000, the Standing Committee recommended uniform sales tax rates — a precursor to VAT reform; choose B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Sales tax uniformity recommended in 2000; VAT implemented from 2005 across states
     - GST eventually replaced both sales tax and VAT in 2017

**[2025]** Q11. Explain how the Fiscal Health Index (FHI) can be used as a tool for assessing the fiscal performance of states in India. In what way would it encourage the states to adopt prudent and sustainable fiscal policies?
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: State fiscal management, Cooperative fiscal federalism, Incentive-based governance

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Fiscal Health Index serves as a multi-dimensional diagnostic tool enabling evidence-based assessment and comparison of state fiscal performance.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • FHI parameters: revenue mobilization, expenditure quality, fiscal prudence, debt sustainability, debt service efficiency
      • Creates competitive benchmarking among states — encouraging fiscal discipline through transparency
      • Can inform Finance Commission's fiscal transfers and grants — linking performance to incentives
      • Encourages states to reduce revenue deficits, avoid off-budget borrowings, improve capital expenditure quality
      • Limitations: index may not capture welfare expenditure adequacy or state-specific development contexts
      • Complementary to FRBM Act — adds qualitative assessment to rule-based compliance framework
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The Fiscal Health Index, by making state fiscal performance transparent and comparable, has potential to shift state incentives toward sustainable fiscal management while respecting federal autonomy.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: Federalism, GS2: Finance Commission, Economy: government budget
  🌐 Contemporary: Highly relevant given rising state debt concerns and post-COVID fiscal consolidation challenges.

**[2020]** Q12. Explain the rationale behind the Goods and Services Tax (Compensation to States) Act of 2017. How has COVID-19 impacted the GST compensation fund and created new federal tensions?
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: IMPACT | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Fiscal federalism, GST, Centre-state relations

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The GST Compensation Act 2017 was a crucial political compact to assure state governments revenue protection during the GST transition, a compact severely tested by the COVID-19 pandemic.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Rationale: states surrendered revenue autonomy (VAT, excise) under GST; compensation guaranteed 14% CAGR for 5 years
      • COVID impact: GST collections collapsed in 2020-21; compensation cess shortfall exceeded ₹2 lakh crore
      • Federal tensions: states demanded funds, Centre proposed back-to-back loans as proxy for compensation
      • Resolution: special borrowing window created; compensation period extended
      • Structural issue: cess-funded compensation was time-limited, creating long-term uncertainty
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The GST compensation crisis exposed structural vulnerabilities in cooperative fiscal federalism, necessitating a more robust revenue-sharing mechanism for GST's second decade.
  🔗 Interlinkages: COVID economic impact, Federalism, State finances
  🌐 Contemporary: GST compensation period ended June 2022, creating renewed debate about revenue adequacy for states.
