**[Q4165]** [2021]
> Among the following, which one is the least water-efficient crop?

  ✅ A) Sugarcane
     B) Sunflower
     C) Pearl millet
     D) Red gram

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse water efficiency with drought tolerance; sugarcane is water-intensive (requires most water per unit of yield), making it the least water-efficient.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Sugarcane requires 1,500-2,000 mm of water annually — highest among field crops; millets and pulses are far more water-efficient.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Sugarcane is the most water-intensive crop in India
     - Pearl millet and red gram are highly water-efficient/drought-tolerant crops

**[Q4008]** [2020]
> "The crop is subtropical in nature. A hard frost is injurious to it. It requires at least 210 frost-free days and 50 to 100 centimeters of rainfall for its growth. A light well-drained soil capable of retaining moisture is ideally suited for the cultivation of the crop." Which one of the following is that crop?

  ✅ A) Cotton
     B) Jute
     C) Sugarcane
     D) Tea

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Jute and sugarcane are also subtropical but require higher rainfall and different soil conditions; tea needs well-drained acidic soil but more rainfall. Cotton specifically needs 50-100cm rainfall and well-drained soil.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Key identifiers: 210 frost-free days, 50-100cm rainfall, light well-drained moisture-retaining soil — these match cotton cultivation requirements precisely.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Cotton requires: 6-8 frost-free months, 50-100cm rainfall, black cotton soil (regur) or well-drained loam
     - Black cotton soil (vertisol) in Deccan is ideal for cotton — self-ploughing, moisture retaining

**[Q1367]** [2004]
> Consider the following crops : 1. Cotton 2. Groundnut 3. Maize 4. Mustard Which of the above are Kharif crops?

     A) 1 and 2
  ✅ B) 1, 2 and 3
     C) 3 and 4
     D) 1, 2, 3 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Mustard is often assumed to be a Kharif crop because it's associated with summer; it is actually a Rabi crop (winter). Students must know crop seasons precisely.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Cotton (1), Groundnut (2), and Maize (3) are Kharif crops (sown in June-July, harvested Oct-Nov). Mustard is a Rabi crop (sown in Nov-Dec) — choose B (1, 2 and 3).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Kharif crops: Cotton, Groundnut, Maize, Rice, Jowar, Bajra, Sugarcane
     - Rabi crops: Wheat, Mustard, Barley, Gram, Peas
     - Zaid crops: Watermelon, Muskmelon (summer short season)

**[Q1213]** [2003]
> Assertion (A) : The eastern coast of India produces more rice than the western coast. Reason (R) : The eastern coast receives more rainfall than the western coast.

     A) Both A and R are individually true and R is the correct explanation of A
     B) Both A and R are individually true but R is NOT the correct explanation of A
  ✅ C) A is true but R is false
     D) A is false but R is true

  ⚠️ **Trap:** R (eastern coast gets more rainfall) seems correct as the east coast gets NE monsoon; but the western coast gets more total rainfall (SW monsoon windward side).
  💡 **Eliminate:** A is true (eastern coast produces more rice); R is false (western coast gets MORE rainfall — Western Ghats create orographic rainfall). Delta plains of east coast, not rainfall, explain more rice production. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Eastern coast: fertile river deltas (Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri) ideal for rice
     - Western coast gets more rainfall but is hilly/rocky — less flat arable land for rice

**[Q208]** [1996]
> Which one of the following sets of conditions is necessary for a good cultivation of wheat ?

  ✅ A) Moderate temperature and moderate rainfall
     B) High temperature and heavy rainfall
     C) High temperature and moderate rainfall
     D) Low temperature and low rainfall

  ⚠️ **Trap:** High temperature with moderate rain (C) sounds plausible. The key is wheat needs a cool growing season and moderate rain — not high temperatures which damage grain formation.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Wheat is a rabi crop needing cool winters (moderate temperature) and moderate rainfall (~50–75 cm). Eliminate B (high temp + heavy rain = rice) and D (too cold/dry = impossible).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Wheat: rabi crop, moderate temp (10–25°C), moderate rain 50–75 cm
     - Major wheat states: UP, Punjab, Haryana, MP

**[Q4159]** [2021]
> How is permaculture farming different from conventional chemical farming?  1. Permaculture farming discourages monocultural practices but in conventional chemical farming, monoculture practices are pre- dominant. 2. Conventional chemical farming can cause increase in soil salinity but the occurrence of such phenomenon is not observed in permaculture farming. 3. Conventional chemical farming is easily possible in semi-arid regions but permaculture farming is not so easily possible in such regions. 4.      A) 1 and 3
  ✅ B) 1, 2 and 4
     C) 4 only
     D) 2 and 3

**[Q2533]** [2012]

     A) Cultivation of both cash crops and food crops
     B) Cultivation of two or more crops in the same field
  ✅ C) Rearing of animals and cultivation of crops together
     D) None of the above

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Options A (cash + food crops) and B (two or more crops) are common misconceptions — they describe diversified and multiple cropping respectively, not mixed farming.

  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Mixed farming combines crop cultivation + livestock rearing
     - Common in Europe and parts of Northwest India

  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Understanding the physiographic determinants of non-farm primary activities is essential for designing regionally sensitive livelihood and resource management policies.
