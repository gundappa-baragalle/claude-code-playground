  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: WHY | 12.5m | ~200w*

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's persistently middling ranking on Transparency International's CPI reflects a systemic corruption culture with deep roots across legal, political, economic, social, and cultural domains.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Legal: weak enforcement, slow courts, under-resourced prosecution — Lokpal operationally weak
      • Political: money-power nexus in elections, opacity of political funding, criminalization of politics

  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Reversing public morality decline requires structural reforms (electoral funding transparency, anti-corruption laws), institutional strengthening, and cultural shift modeled by political leadership.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS4 Ethics — probity, GS2 Governance — anti-corruption, GS2 — electoral reforms

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*

  - 📋 **Body:**
