**[Q4700]** [2024]
> With reference to radioisotope thermoelectric generators (RTGs), consider the following statements:  1. RTGs are miniature fission reactors. 2. RTGs are used for powering the onboard systems of spacecrafts. 3. RTGs can use Plutonium-238, which is a by-product of weapons development.  Which of the statements given above are correct?

     A) 1 and 2 only
  ✅ B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is wrong: RTGs are NOT fission reactors. They generate electricity from the heat of radioactive decay (not nuclear fission chain reaction). This is the key distinction.
  💡 **Eliminate:** RTGs use radioactive decay heat (thermoelectric effect) — NOT fission. Statement 1 wrong; Statement 2 correct (powering spacecraft like Voyager, Cassini, Curiosity rover); Statement 3 correct (Pu-238 from weapons programs). Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - RTG: converts heat from radioactive decay of Pu-238 into electricity via Seebeck effect — NOT a nuclear reactor/fission device
     - Used in Voyager 1 & 2, Cassini, New Horizons, Mars Curiosity and Perseverance rovers
     - Pu-238 is produced as a by-product of Pu-239 weapons production in reactors

**[Q4539]** [2023]
> Which one of the following countries has its own Satellite Navigation System?

     A) Australia
     B) Canada
     C) Israel
  ✅ D) Japan

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Israel and Canada have advanced space programmes; students may associate satellite navigation with Israel (due to Arrow missile systems) or overlook Japan's QZSS.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Japan has its own regional satellite navigation system — QZSS (Quasi-Zenith Satellite System), operational since 2018; Australia, Canada and Israel do not have their own independent navigation satellite systems.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Japan's QZSS (Michibiki) is a regional navigation system complementing GPS for Japan and Asia-Pacific
     - Countries with independent satellite navigation: USA (GPS), Russia (GLONASS), EU (Galileo), China (BeiDou), India (NavIC), Japan (QZSS)

**[Q4335]** [2022]
> If a major solar storm (solar flare) reaches the Earth, which of the following are the possible effects on the Earth?  1. GPS and navigation systems could fail. 2. Tsunamis could occur at equatorial regions. 3. Power grids could be damaged. 4. Intense auroras could occur over much of the Earth. 5. Forest fires could take place over much of the planet. 6. Orbits of the satellites could be disturbed. 7. Shortwave radio communication of the aircraft flying over polar regions could be interrupted.  Select the correct answer using the code given below:

     A) 1, 2, 4 and 5 only
     B) 2, 3, 5, 6 and 7 only
  ✅ C) 1, 3, 4, 6 and 7 only
     D) 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 and 7

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statements 2 (tsunamis) and 5 (forest fires) are wrong — solar flares don't cause tsunamis or forest fires. These distractor statements sound plausible as 'extreme natural events'. Candidates must distinguish electromagnetic effects from physical effects.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Solar flares cause electromagnetic effects: GPS failure (1), power grid damage (3), auroras (4), satellite orbit disturbance (6), radio communication disruption (7). Tsunamis and forest fires are physical events, not EM effects. Answer C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Solar flares cause geomagnetic storms that disrupt GPS, power grids, satellite communications, and radio signals
     - Aurora borealis/australis are visible effects of solar particle bombardment on Earth's atmosphere

**[Q3766]** [2019]
> For the measurement/estimation of which of the following are satellite images/remote sensing data used?  1. Chlorophyll content in the vegetation of a specific location 2. Greenhouse gas emissions from rice paddies of a specific location 3. Land surface temperatures of a specific location  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 (greenhouse gas measurement from paddies) may be doubted since GHG from agricultural fields seems ground-level — but remote sensing of methane flux is now technically possible.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Remote sensing/satellite data can measure all three: vegetation chlorophyll content via NDVI (S1), GHG from rice paddies via spectral analysis (S2), and land surface temperature via thermal infrared bands (S3). Answer: D (all three).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) from satellites measures vegetation chlorophyll/health
     - Thermal infrared sensors on satellites measure land surface temperatures; TROPOMI measures methane from agriculture

  ✅ A) 1, 2 and 3 only
     B) 1, 3 and 4 only
     C) 2 and 4 only
     D) 1, 2, 3 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 4 about 22,236 km altitude is often taken as correct; but the standard altitude is approximately 35,786 km — 22,236 miles is sometimes confused with km.

  📌 **Key facts:**

     - 22,236 miles ≈ 35,786 km — the question uses incorrect km figure

**[2017]** Q5. How does the Juno Mission of NASA help to understand the origin and evolution of the Earth?
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Space exploration and Earth science, Planetary origins

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** NASA's Juno Mission to Jupiter acts as a time machine, revealing the primordial conditions from which Earth and all solar system bodies were born.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Juno's objectives: Jupiter's composition, magnetic field, interior structure
      • Jupiter as solar system archive: water abundance, heavy element ratios

  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Understanding Jupiter through Juno ultimately deepens our understanding of Earth's birth and the conditions that made life possible.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 — Space Technology, GS1 — Physical Geography, Earth Science
