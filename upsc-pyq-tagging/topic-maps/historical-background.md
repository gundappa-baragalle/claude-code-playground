**[Q4730]** [2024]
> Who was the Provisional President of the Constituent Assembly before Dr. Rajendra Prasad took over?

     A) C. Rajagopalachari
     B) Dr. B.R. Ambedkar
     C) T.T. Krishnamachari
  ✅ D) Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often confuse Rajendra Prasad (permanent President) with the provisional president; C. Rajagopalachari is a well-known name that may distract.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha was the oldest member of the Constituent Assembly and therefore became its Provisional President on 9 December 1946. Dr. Rajendra Prasad was elected permanent President on 11 December 1946.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha served as Provisional President of Constituent Assembly (Dec 9, 1946)
     - Convention: oldest member presides as provisional chair — Sinha was the eldest member

**[Q4515]** [2023]
> Which one of the following statements best reflects the Chief purpose of the 'Constitution' of a country?

     A) It determines the objective for the making of necessary laws.
     B) It enables the creation of political offices and a government.
  ✅ C) It defines and limits the powers of government.
     D) It secures social justice, social equality and social security.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option D (social justice) is desirable but an outcome, not the chief purpose. Option B (creating government) is a function but secondary to limiting power.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The primary purpose of a constitution is to define and limit the powers of government — protecting citizens from arbitrary state action. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Constitution = fundamental law limiting government power and defining its scope
     - Constitutionalism = principle that government is bound by law

**[Q4567]** [2023]
> Consider the following statements in respect of the Constitution Day :  1. Statement-I : The Constitution Day is celebrated on 26th November every year to promote constitutional values among citizens. 2. Statement-II: On 26th November, 1949, the Constituent Assembly of India set up a Drafting Committee under the Chairmanship of Dr. B. R. Ambedkar to prepare a Draft Constitution of India.  Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

     A) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct and Statement-II is the correct explanation for Statement-I
     B) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct and Statement-II is not the correct explanation for Statement-I
  ✅ C) Statement-I is correct but Statement-II is incorrect
     D) Statement-I is incorrect but Statement-II is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement II is wrong: the Drafting Committee was set up on 29 August 1947 — not on 26 November 1949. On 26 November 1949, the Constitution was adopted/enacted by the Constituent Assembly.
  💡 **Eliminate:** 26 November 1949 = Constitution adopted (not Drafting Committee formation); Drafting Committee was formed on 29 August 1947. Statement I is correct; Statement II is wrong. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Constitution Day (Samvidhan Divas): 26 November — date Constitution was adopted in 1949
     - Drafting Committee under Dr. B.R. Ambedkar was set up on 29 August 1947, not 26 November 1949

**[Q3932]** [2020]
> A Parliamentary System of Government is one in which

     A) all political parties in the Parliament are represented in the Government
  ✅ B) the Government is responsible to the Parliament and can be removed by it
     C) the Government is elected by the people and can be removed by them
     D) the Government is chosen by the Parliament but cannot be removed by it before completion of a fixed term

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option D sounds plausible (Parliament chooses but fixed term) — this actually describes a Presidential system with parliamentary selection; option A (all parties in government) describes a national unity government.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Parliamentary system's defining feature is executive accountability to legislature — government can be removed by parliament through vote of no-confidence (Option B).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Parliamentary system = government accountable to and removable by legislature through no-confidence vote
     - Westminster model: executive drawn from legislature and responsible to it

**[Q3079]** [2015]
> There is a Parliamentary System of Government in India because the

     A) Lok Sabha is elected directly by the people
     B) Parliament can amend the Constitution
     C) Rajya Sabha cannot be dissolved
  ✅ D) Council of Ministers is responsible to the Lok Sabha

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Lok Sabha being directly elected seems like the defining feature; Parliament amending the Constitution also seems fundamental — but parliamentary system is specifically defined by executive accountability.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Parliamentary system's core feature is that the executive (Council of Ministers) is collectively responsible to/accountable to the Lok Sabha — this is what distinguishes it from presidential system.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Parliamentary system: Council of Ministers collectively responsible to Lok Sabha (Article 75)
     - Direct election of Lok Sabha and Parliament amending power exist in both parliamentary and presidential systems
     - Council of Ministers loses power if it loses Lok Sabha confidence

**[Q1641]** [2005]
> Who among the following was the Chairman of the Union Constitution Committee of the Constituent Assembly?

     A) B. R. Ambedkar
     B) J. B. Kripalani
  ✅ C) Jawaharlal Nehru
     D) Alladi Krishanaswami Ayyar

  ⚠️ **Trap:** B.R. Ambedkar was Chairman of the Drafting Committee (not Union Constitution Committee), making him the most common wrong answer. Candidates confuse the two committees.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Jawaharlal Nehru chaired the Union Constitution Committee while Ambedkar chaired the Drafting Committee — the distinction between these two bodies is the key.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Nehru chaired Union Constitution Committee of Constituent Assembly
     - Ambedkar chaired the Drafting Committee — different body

**[Q1369]** [2004]
> Which one of the following statements correctly describes the Fourth Schedule of the Constitution of India?

     A) It contains the scheme of the distribution of powers between the Union and the States.
     B) It contains the languages listed in the Constitution.
     C) It contains the provisions regarding the administration of tribal areas.
  ✅ D) It allocates seats in the Council of States.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students confuse the 4th Schedule with the 7th Schedule (distribution of powers) or 8th Schedule (languages). All schedules are frequently tested.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Fourth Schedule allocates seats in the Council of States (Rajya Sabha) to states and union territories — a fundamental constitutional provision tested repeatedly.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 4th Schedule: Allocation of Rajya Sabha seats to States/UTs
     - 7th Schedule: Distribution of legislative powers (Union/State/Concurrent Lists)
     - 8th Schedule: Recognized languages of India

**[Q1051]** [2002]
> The members of the Constituent Assembly which drafted the Constitution of India were

     A) nominated by the British Parliament
     B) nominated by the Governor General
  ✅ C) elected by the Legislative Assemblies of various provinces
     D) elected by the Indian National Congress and Muslim League

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse the method of election (indirect by provincial assemblies) with nomination by British Parliament or Governor-General. The Constituent Assembly was elected by provincial Legislative Assemblies under Cabinet Mission Plan.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Under the Cabinet Mission Plan (1946), members of Constituent Assembly were elected by provincial Legislative Assemblies using proportional representation. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Constituent Assembly members elected by Provincial Legislative Assemblies
     - Elections held July-August 1946 under Cabinet Mission Plan
     - Total strength: 389 members (296 from British India + 93 princely states)

**[Q573]** [1998]
> The Indian parliamentary system is different from the British parliamentary system in that India has

     A) both a real and a nominal executive
     B) a system of collective responsibility
     C) bicameral legislature
  ✅ D) the system of judicial review

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Options A, B and C are features shared by both Indian and British parliamentary systems. Option D (judicial review) is uniquely Indian — British Parliament has parliamentary supremacy with no judicial review of legislation.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Collective responsibility, bicameralism and real/nominal executive exist in both systems; only judicial review distinguishes India (with written constitution) from UK (parliamentary supremacy). Answer D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - UK has parliamentary supremacy — no court can strike down an Act of Parliament
     - India has judicial review under Articles 13, 32, 226 allowing courts to invalidate unconstitutional laws

### GS2

**[2018]** Q13. India and USA are two large democracies. Examine the basic tenets on which the two political systems are based.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: VS | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Comparative constitutional design, Democracy and federalism, Rights and governance

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India and the USA, despite both being federal democracies with constitutionally guaranteed rights, are built on distinct political philosophies reflecting their unique historical, social and cultural contexts.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Both: written constitution, federalism, independent judiciary, fundamental rights
      • India: parliamentary sovereignty, single citizenship, quasi-federal; US: presidential system, dual citizenship, more rigid federalism
      • India: DPSPs as positive obligations; US: no socioeconomic rights in constitution
      • Both: independent judiciary with power of judicial review (Marbury v Madison vs Kesavananda Bharati)
      • India: emergency provisions allow temporary centralization; US: no equivalent
      • Both value separation of powers — US strictly; India with checks and balances
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** While both constitutions uphold democratic values and rights, their structural differences reflect India's need for a strong adaptive state capable of addressing social inequality versus America's emphasis on limiting governmental power.
  🔗 Interlinkages: International Relations — India-US democratic partnership, Polity — constitutional amendments and judicial review
  🌐 Contemporary: India-US Comprehensive Global Strategic Partnership draws on shared democratic values as a foundational element.
