**[Q3982]** [2020]
> In India, which of the following can be considered as public investment in agriculture?  1. Fixing Minimum Support Price for agricultural produce of all crops 2. Computerization of Primary Agricultural Credit Societies 3. Social Capital development 4. Free electricity supply to farmers 5. Waiver of agricultural loans by the banking system 6. Setting up of cold storage facilities by the governments  Select the correct answer using the code given below:

     A) 1, 2 and 5 only
     B) 1, 3, 4 and 5 only
  ✅ C) 2, 3 and 6 only
     D) 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6

  ⚠️ **Trap:** MSP setting (1), free electricity (4), and loan waivers (5) are subsidies/price support — not capital investment. Social capital (3) and computerization of credit societies (2) and cold storage (6) represent actual public investment creating productive assets.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Public investment = actual capital expenditure creating assets/capacity. Computerization of PACS (2), social capital development (3), and cold storage by govt (6) are genuine public investment. MSP, free electricity, loan waivers are price/subsidy interventions, not investment. Answer is C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Public investment = expenditure creating capital assets/capacity in agriculture
     - Cold storage, rural roads, irrigation infrastructure = genuine public investment
     - MSP and loan waivers are income support, not capital formation

**[2016]** Q8. What is allelopathy? Discuss its role in major cropping systems of irrigated agriculture.
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Allelopathy in agriculture, Natural weed management, Cropping systems

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Allelopathy, the chemical interference between plants, offers a sustainable and cost-effective complement to chemical herbicides in irrigated cropping systems.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Definition: allelopathy is the direct or indirect effect of one plant on another through release of chemicals (allelochemicals) into environment
      • Mechanism: allelochemicals inhibit seed germination, root growth and nutrient uptake of target plants
      • Role in cropping: sorghum-wheat, rice-wheat and maize intercropping systems use allelopathic varieties to suppress weeds
      • Irrigated systems: weed pressure is high in irrigated agriculture due to favorable moisture; allelopathy reduces herbicide dependency
      • Benefits: reduces input costs, minimizes herbicide resistance, promotes ecological balance
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Harnessing allelopathy through crop breeding and judicious intercropping can significantly reduce the herbicide burden in irrigated agriculture while promoting sustainability.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Agriculture, Environment, Biotechnology
  🌐 Contemporary: Natural farming movement and zero-budget farming promote allelopathic principles.

### Common Trap Patterns on This Topic
- Option IV (maximum price) is a trap — CACP recommends Minimum Support Price (MSP), not maximum price. Students often add IV thinking it protects farmers.
- NABARD is the apex rural credit institution, making option B tempting; RRBs (Regional Rural Banks) are important rural creditors often overlooked in favor of commercial banks.
- Map-based matching of agricultural research institutes to cities is highly specific — students need to know that CMFRI is in Kochi, NDRI is in Karnal, and NAARM is in Hyderabad.
- Minimum Support Price (MSP) is the most familiar term and candidates often confuse it with procurement price; issue price is what government sells grain to consumers, making option B easily overlooked.
- Jaya and Padma are HYV rice varieties; Arjun and Sonalika are wheat — candidates often mix up rice and wheat HYVs from the Green Revolution era.
