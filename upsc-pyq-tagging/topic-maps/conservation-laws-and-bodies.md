**[Q4384]** [2022]
> With reference to Indian laws about wildlife protection, consider the following statements :  1. Wild animals are the sole property of the government. 2. When a wild animal is declared protected, such animal is entitled for equal protection whether it is found in protected areas or outside. 3. Apprehension of a protected wild animal becoming a danger to human life is sufficient ground for its capture or killing.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 and 2
  ✅ B) 2 only
     C) 1 and 3
     D) 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 (wild animals are sole property of government) sounds correct as government manages wildlife, but WPA 1972 does not vest ownership — it creates a regulatory/protection framework. Statement 3 seems reasonable but apprehension alone is insufficient under law.
  💡 **Eliminate:** WPA 1972: wild animals are not government 'property' (1 wrong); protected animals have equal protection inside and outside protected areas (2 correct); killing requires formal declaration as vermin or specific permission, not mere apprehension (3 wrong). Answer is B (2 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Wildlife Protection Act 1972: protected animals have legal protection both inside and outside Wildlife Sanctuaries/National Parks
     - WPA does not make wild animals 'government property'; it regulates hunting and trade

**[2020]** Q7. How does the draft Environment Impact Assessment (EIA) Notification, 2020 differ from the existing EIA Notification, 2006?
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Environmental governance, Regulatory reform vs environmental protection, Public participation in governance

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Draft EIA Notification 2020 proposed significant changes to the existing EIA framework of 2006, sparking debate about the balance between ease of doing business and environmental protection.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Post-facto clearance: Draft 2020 allowed regularisation of projects that began without prior clearance — EIA 2006 strictly prohibits this
      • Reduced public consultation: Draft 2020 reduced public notice period from 30 to 20 days and exempted more project categories from public hearings
      • Strategic projects: Draft 2020 allowed government to classify certain projects as strategic, exempting them from public disclosure — not in 2006 notification
      • Compliance reporting: EIA 2006 required half-yearly reports; Draft 2020 proposed annual reporting, reducing monitoring frequency
      • Criticism: Civil society argued Draft 2020 weakened environmental safeguards; proponents said it streamlined business processes
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The EIA 2020 draft highlighted the tension between development imperatives and environmental governance, underscoring the need for robust, participatory, and precautionary environmental regulation.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3: Environment, conservation, GS2: Governance, civil society participation, Polity: NGT, judicial review of environmental decisions
  🌐 Contemporary: Draft EIA 2020 was challenged in courts; final revised EIA notification remains pending as of 2024.

**[2018]** Q17. How does biodiversity vary in India? How is the Biological Diversity Act, 2002 helpful in conservation of flora and fauna?
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Biodiversity conservation legislation, Access and benefit sharing, Flora-fauna protection frameworks

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's extraordinary biological diversity necessitates both legal and institutional frameworks for conservation and sustainable use.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • India's biodiversity: 4 hotspots, 7-8% of world's flora/fauna; variation across Western Ghats, Himalayas, northeast
      • BDA 2002 creates three-tier structure: National Biodiversity Authority, State Biodiversity Boards, Biodiversity Management Committees
      • Access and Benefit Sharing (ABS) mechanism regulates use of biological resources and traditional knowledge
      • People's Biodiversity Registers document local biodiversity; BMCs at gram panchayat level ensure grassroots participation
      • Limitations: weak enforcement, overlap with Forest Rights Act, inadequate funding for BMCs
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Strengthening BDA implementation through integration with PESA, FRA, and digital biodiversity registries is essential for holistic conservation.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 Environment: Biodiversity hotspots, GS2 Governance: Environmental legislation, GS3: CBD and Nagoya Protocol
  🌐 Contemporary: Post-2020 Global Biodiversity Framework (Kunming-Montreal) makes BDA implementation more critical for India's international commitments.
