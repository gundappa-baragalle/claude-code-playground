**[Q902]** [2001]
> A worker reaches his factory 3 minutes late if his speed from his house to the factory is 5 km/hr. If he walks at a speed of 6 km/hr, then he reaches the factory 7 minutes early. The distance of the factory from his house is

     A) 4 km
  ✅ B) 5 km
     C) 6 km
     D) 7 km

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Combining 3 minutes late and 7 minutes early as a difference of 10 minutes is correct, but many students set up the equation as d/5 − d/6 = 3/60 (only using 3 min) giving wrong answer.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Total time difference = 3+7 = 10 min = 1/6 hr. Equation: d/5 − d/6 = 1/6 → d(1/30) = 1/6 → d = 5 km. Verify: at 5 km/h takes 60 min; at 6 km/h takes 50 min — difference = 10 min ✓
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - d/s = time formula
     - When late+early, add both times for total difference
