**[Q1222]** [2003]
> Assertion (A) : Marathas emerged as the strongest native power in India after the decline of Mughal empire. Reason (R) : Marathas were the first to have clear concept of a united Indian nation.

     A) Both A and R are individually true and R is the correct explanation of A
     B) Both A and R are individually true but R is NOT the correct explanation of A
  ✅ C) A is true but R is false
     D) A is false but R is true

  ⚠️ **Trap:** R (Marathas had concept of united Indian nation) seems historically debatable; Marathas were the strongest post-Mughal power (A true) but their motivation was Hindu Pad Padshahi, not united India.
  💡 **Eliminate:** A is true (Marathas were strongest native power after Mughals); R is false (they had no clear concept of united Indian nation — that's a modern nationalist retrospection). Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Marathas emerged as dominant power after Mughal decline in 18th century
     - Maratha confederacy: Peshwas, Sindhia, Holkar, Bhonsle, Gaekwad — but no unified national vision

**[Q451]** [1998]
> The member of Shivaji’s Astha Pradhana who looked after foreign affairs was

     A) Peshwa
     B) Sachiv
     C) Pandit Rao
  ✅ D) Sumant

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Peshwa (PM) is the most remembered Ashta Pradhan minister; students guess Peshwa for foreign affairs. Sachiv (secretary) sounds administrative. Sumant/Dabir is the least-known minister name.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Peshwa = Prime Minister. Sachiv = correspondence/letters. Pandit Rao = religious affairs. Sumant = foreign affairs/protocol (also called Dabir). Senapati = military. Lock Peshwa=PM first, then Sumant=Foreign by elimination.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Sumant (Dabir): Shivaji's foreign affairs minister in Ashta Pradhan
     - Peshwa: PM of Maratha council
     - Pandit Rao: head of religious/charity affairs
     - Senapati: commander-in-chief

**[Q822]** [2000]
> Who among the following streamlined the Maratha administration after Sambhaji ?

     A) Raja Ram
  ✅ B) Balaji Viswanath
     C) Ganga Bai
     D) Nanaji Deshmukh

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Raja Ram was Sambhaji's successor but was involved in resistance; Balaji Viswanath reorganized the Maratha administration after the troubled interregnum — students confuse the two.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Balaji Viswanath (Peshwa from 1713) is credited with reorganizing and strengthening Maratha administration post-Sambhaji; he was the first effective Peshwa who transformed the empire.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Balaji Viswanath (1st Peshwa) reorganized Maratha administration and made Peshwa the de facto ruler
     - After Sambhaji's death (1689), Raja Ram and then Tarabai continued resistance before Shahu's succession

**[Q16]** [1995]
> Ashtapradhan was a Council of Ministers

     A) in the Gupta Administration
     B) in the Chola Administration
     C) in the Vijayanagar Administration
  ✅ D) in the Maratha Administration

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Ashtapradhan is associated with Shivaji's Maratha state; candidates may confuse it with Gupta or Vijayanagar administrative systems.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Ashtapradhan (council of eight ministers) was Shivaji's Maratha administrative innovation; select D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Ashtapradhan: Shivaji Maharaj's eight-minister council for Maratha administration
     - Peshwa was the most important among the eight ministers
     - Each minister had specific portfolios (Nyayadhish, Senapati, etc.)

**[Q139]** [1995]
> The ‘Modi script’ was employed in the documents of the

     A) Wodeyars
     B) Zamorins
     C) Hoysalas
  ✅ D) Marathas

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Modi script sounds like it could be associated with any western Indian kingdom; Zamorins (Kerala) and Hoysalas (Karnataka) are distractors — Modi script was specifically the Maratha administrative script.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Modi script was the cursive script used for official Maratha Empire documents (Peshwa records, land grants); derived from Devanagari. Answer: D (Marathas).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Modi script was the official administrative script of the Maratha Empire and later Peshwa records
     - Modi script is derived from Devanagari and was used for business and administrative records in Maharashtra

**[Q109]** [1995]
> Examine the map given below : The places marked 1, 2, 3 and 4 were respectively the seats of powers of the

  ✅ A) Scindias, Holkars, Gaekwads and Bhonsles
     B) Holkars, Scindias, Gaekwads and Bhonsles
     C) Gaekwads, Bhonsles, Scindias and Holkars
     D) Scindias, Holkars, Bhonsles and Gaekwads

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Map-based questions require knowing specific geographic seats — Gwalior (Scindias), Indore (Holkars), Baroda (Gaekwads), Nagpur (Bhonsles) — easy to mix up.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Remember: Scindias=Gwalior (north), Holkars=Indore (central), Gaekwads=Baroda (west), Bhonsles=Nagpur (east). Match accordingly — Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Scindias ruled from Gwalior, Holkars from Indore, Gaekwads from Baroda, Bhonsles from Nagpur
     - These were the four major successor states of the Maratha Confederacy
