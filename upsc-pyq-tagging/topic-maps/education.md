**[Q3568]** [2018]
> Consider the following statements :  1. As per the Right to Education (RTE) Act, to be eligible for appointment as a teacher in a State, a person would be required to possess the minimum qualification laid down by the concerned State Council of Teacher Education. 2. As per the RTE Act, for teaching primary classes, a candidate is required to pass a Teacher Eligibility Test conducted in accordance with the National Council of Teacher Education guidelines. 3. In India, more than 90% of teacher education institutions are directly under the State Governments.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 and 2
  ✅ B) 2 only
     C) 1 and 3
     D) 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 says SCTE sets minimum qualifications — actually NCTE (National Council for Teacher Education) sets the national minimum qualification, not state SCTEs. Statement 3 about 90% state-run TEIs is also incorrect — most are private.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Under RTE Act, teacher qualification minimum is set by NCTE (not state SCTE, making statement 1 wrong). TET is conducted per NCTE guidelines (statement 2 correct). Over 90% of TEIs are private sector, not state-run (statement 3 wrong). Answer: 2 only.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - RTE Act: NCTE sets national minimum qualification for teachers, not state SCTEs
     - TET (Teacher Eligibility Test) mandated for primary teachers under NCTE guidelines
     - Teacher Education Institutions in India: majority are private sector (~90%), not state-run

### Common Trap Patterns on This Topic
- Dharamshala is associated with Tibetan studies but the Central Institute of Higher Tibetan Studies is actually in Varanasi (Sarnath). NIMHANS is in Bangalore. IGIDР is in Mumbai.
- All options seem plausible as 'national importance' institutions; the Lakshmibai NI of Physical Education (Gwalior) has Deemed University status but its 'Institute of National Importance' classification is different. Dakshina Bharat Hindi Prachar Sabha does not have this status.

- Rajiv Gandhi's connection to states like Karnataka or Uttaranchal (his politics and assassination) can mislead students away from Tamil Nadu.
