**[Q3597]** [2018]
> Despite being a high saving economy, capital formation may not result in significant increase in output due to

     A) weak administrative machinery
     B) illiteracy
     C) high population density
  ✅ D) high capital-output ratio

  ⚠️ **Trap:** High population density and illiteracy are commonly blamed for low output; but the specific economic concept here is capital-output ratio — how much capital is needed per unit of output.
  💡 **Eliminate:** High capital-output ratio means more capital is required to produce each unit of output, so even high savings do not translate to significant output increase — answer D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Capital-output ratio (COR) = Capital invested / Output produced; high COR implies inefficient capital use
     - India's ICOR (Incremental COR) is a key planning metric for investment requirements

**[Q2183]** [2009]
> Consider the following statements regarding Indian Planning : 1. The Second Five-Year Plan emphasized on the establishment of heavy industries. 2. The Third Five-Year Plan introduced the concept of import substitution as a strategy for industrialization. Which of the statements given above is/are correct ?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often mix up which Five-Year Plan introduced which concept; import substitution is associated with Second Plan era but formally conceptualized earlier; Third Plan continued this strategy.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Second Plan (Nehru-Mahalanobis model) emphasized heavy industry — Statement 1 is correct; Third Plan also stressed import substitution as industrialization strategy — Statement 2 is correct. Both are correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Second Five-Year Plan = Mahalanobis model emphasizing heavy industries
     - Import substitution industrialization was a key strategy in early Five-Year Plans

**[Q843]** [2000]
> Economic liberalisation in India started with

  ✅ A) substantial changes in industrial licensing policy
     B) the convertibility of Indian rupee
     C) doing away with procedural formalities for foreign direct investment
     D) significant reduction in tax rates

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Rupee convertibility and FDI procedural changes were also part of 1991 reforms — candidates may pick these as the starting point rather than industrial licensing changes.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Economic liberalization in India formally began with the Industrial Policy of July 1991, which abolished industrial licensing for most sectors — this was the foundational reform.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Industrial Policy 1991 abolished licensing for most industries except 18 strategic sectors
     - 1991 LPG reforms: Liberalization, Privatization, Globalization — licensing change was the first pillar

**[Q3804]** [2019]
> With reference to India's Five-Year Plans, which of the following statements is/are correct?  1. From the Second Five-Year Plan, there was a determined thrust towards substitution of basic and capital good industries. 2. The Fourth Five-Year Plan adopted the objective of correcting the earlier trend of increased concentration of wealth and economic power. 3. In the Fifth Five-Year Plan, for the first time, the financial sector was included as an integral part of the Plan.  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

  ✅ A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 2 only
     C) 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 3 is wrong — financial sector was not included in the Fifth Plan; it was the Eighth Plan (1992-97) that prominently integrated financial sector reforms post-liberalization. Candidates may mix up plan periods.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Second Plan (Mahalanobis model) pushed heavy/basic industries substitution (statement 1 correct); Fourth Plan corrected wealth concentration trend (statement 2 correct); financial sector was NOT in Fifth Plan (statement 3 wrong). Answer is A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Second Five Year Plan: Mahalanobis model — thrust on heavy and capital goods industries
     - Fourth Plan (1969-74): aimed to reduce concentration of economic power (Indira Gandhi era)
     - Financial sector planning came much later, prominently in Eighth Plan after 1991 reforms

**[Q2853]** [2014]
> Which of the following are associated with 'Planning' in India?  1. The Finance Commission 2. The National Development Council 3. The Union Ministry of Rural Development 4. The Union Ministry of Urban Development 5. The Parliament  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1, 2 and 5 only
     B) 1, 3 and 4 only
  ✅ C) 2 and 5 only
     D) 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Finance Commission and Parliament both have roles in fiscal matters; students may include them in planning-related bodies. Finance Commission's role is different (devolution formula); Parliament approves plans.
  💡 **Eliminate:** NDC and Parliament are directly associated with planning (NDC approves; Parliament passes budget). Finance Commission, rural/urban ministries are separate from plan formulation. Answer is C (2 and 5 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - National Development Council approved Five-Year Plans; Parliament voted appropriations
     - Finance Commission decides tax devolution but does not 'plan' — it's a separate constitutional body

**[Q1394]** [2004]
> Consider the following statements: 1. The highest deciding body for planning in India is the Planning Commission of India. 2. The Secretary of the Planning Commission of India is also the Secretary of National Development Council. 3. The Constitution includes economic and social planning in the Concurrent List in the Seventh Schedule of the Constitution of India. Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 and 2
  ✅ B) 2 and 3
     C) 2 only
     D) 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 says Planning Commission is highest planning body — but NDC (National Development Council) is actually the highest body; Planning Commission prepares plans but NDC approves them.
  💡 **Eliminate:** NDC (not Planning Commission) was the highest deciding body for planning; Statement 2 (PC Secretary = NDC Secretary) is TRUE; Statement 3 (planning in Concurrent List) is TRUE — so B (2 and 3) is correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - NDC (National Development Council) was the highest planning body — Planning Commission prepared, NDC approved
     - Planning is in the Concurrent List (Schedule 7) — both Centre and States can legislate

     B) With markets assuming a central place, the role of central planning in many sectors has been rendered redundant
     C) The focus of planning has shifted to sectors like human resource development, infrastructure, population control and welfare

**[Q27]** [1995]
> The main reason for low growth rate in India, in spite of high rate of savings and capital formation is

     A) high birth rate
     B) low level of foreign aid
     C) low capital / output ratio
  ✅ D) high capital / output ratio

  ⚠️ **Trap:** High birth rate and low foreign aid seem intuitive causes; the real issue is structural—high capital-output ratio means investments yield less output and growth.
  💡 **Eliminate:** High capital/output ratio means each unit of capital produces less output, resulting in low growth despite high savings; select D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - High capital-output ratio = inefficient capital utilization = low growth per unit of investment
     - India's capital-intensive industrialization led to low employment and output growth
     - Harrod-Domar model: growth rate = savings rate / capital-output ratio

### GS3

**[2018]** Q11. How are the principles followed by the NITI Aayog different from those followed by the erstwhile Planning Commission in India?
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: VS | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Planning reforms, Cooperative federalism, Development policy

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The transition from Planning Commission to NITI Aayog represents a fundamental shift from centralized command planning to cooperative, competitive federalism.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Planning Commission: Five Year Plans, financial allocation powers, centralized planning, one-size-fits-all approach
      • NITI Aayog: advisory body, no fund allocation; emphasis on states as partners, not recipients
      • Cooperative federalism: NITI promotes state-led development; State Best Practices, Aspirational Districts Programme
      • Bottom-up approach: policy formation involves states' inputs; sub-groups of Chief Ministers
      • Think tank role: research and advocacy rather than command; NITI's 3-year action agenda, 7-year strategy, 15-year vision
      • Criticism: loss of financial leverage reduces NITI's ability to enforce development priorities
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** NITI Aayog's evolution reflects India's development philosophy shift toward market-led growth with states as primary implementers, though its advisory-only role limits transformative impact.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Economy, GS2 – Governance, GS2 – Polity
  🌐 Contemporary: NITI Aayog's Viksit Bharat@2047 framework makes this directly relevant.
