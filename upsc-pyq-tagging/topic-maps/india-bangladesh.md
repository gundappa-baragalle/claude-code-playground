**[Q4948]** [2025]
> Consider the following statements in respect of BIMSTEC :  I. It is a regional organization consisting of seven member States till January 2025. II. It came into existence with the signing of the Dhaka Declaration, 1999. III. Bangladesh, India, Sri Lanka, Thailand and Nepal are founding member States of BIMSTEC. IV. In BIMSTEC, the subsector of 'tourism' is being led by India.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) I and II
     B) II and III
  ✅ C) I and IV
     D) I only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** BIMSTEC has 7 members (Statement I — correct); it came into existence with Bangkok Declaration 1997 (not Dhaka Declaration 1999 — Statement II is wrong); founding members were Bangladesh, India, Sri Lanka, Thailand (not Nepal); tourism subsector led by India (Statement IV — correct).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Statement I correct (7 members); Statement II wrong (Bangkok Declaration 1997, not Dhaka 1999); Statement III wrong (Nepal not a founding member); Statement IV correct (India leads tourism subsector). Answer: C (I and IV).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - BIMSTEC founded via Bangkok Declaration 1997 (not Dhaka 1999); 7 members: Bangladesh, India, Myanmar, Sri Lanka, Thailand, Nepal, Bhutan
     - Nepal and Bhutan joined later; original 4 founders were Bangladesh, India, Sri Lanka, Thailand
     - India leads several BIMSTEC subsectors including tourism, communication, transport

**[Q3985]** [2020]
> Consider the following statements:  1. The value of Indo-Sri Lanka trade has consistently increased in the last decade. 2. "Textile and textile articles" constitute an important item of trade between India and Bangladesh. 3. In the last five years, Nepal has been the largest trading partner of India in South Asia.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct ?

     A) 1 and 2 only
  ✅ B) 2 only
     C) 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 (consistent increase in India-Sri Lanka trade) is wrong — trade has not consistently increased (COVID, Sri Lanka crisis caused dips). Statement 3 (Nepal as largest South Asia trade partner) is wrong — Bangladesh is generally larger. Only statement 2 is likely correct.
  💡 **Eliminate:** India-Bangladesh trade involves significant textile/garment related trade; Bangladesh exports readymade garments to India and India exports fabric/yarn to Bangladesh. Statement 2 is correct. Statements 1 and 3 are factually problematic. Answer is B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - India-Bangladesh trade: textiles are major component — India exports fabric; Bangladesh exports garments
     - Bangladesh has consistently been India's largest South Asian trading partner (not Nepal)
     - India-Sri Lanka trade fluctuates; not consistently increasing

### GS1

**[2013]** Q10. Critically examine the compulsions which prompted India to play a decisive role in the emergence of Bangladesh.
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHY | 10m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Bangladesh liberation, India's strategic role, 1971 war

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's decisive military intervention in the 1971 war was driven by a complex interplay of humanitarian compulsion, strategic calculation, and the imperatives of regional security — producing an independent Bangladesh as both consequence and objective.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Humanitarian compulsion: Operation Searchlight (March 1971) by Pakistan Army created 10 million refugees flooding into India — untenable economic and social burden
      • Strategic calculation: creation of Bangladesh would permanently weaken Pakistan, eliminating the two-front threat; Indira Gandhi sought to leverage the situation
      • Diplomatic preparation: Indo-Soviet Treaty (August 1971) provided security umbrella against US-China pressure; Indira Gandhi's international tour built global sympathy
      • Military precision: 13-day war (Dec 3-16, 1971); combined ops with Mukti Bahini; Pakistani surrender (93,000 POWs) — India's most decisive military victory
      • Legality debate: India justified intervention on humanitarian grounds; Pakistan called it aggression; Simla Agreement (1972) settled terms bilaterally
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India's 1971 intervention was both humanitarian and strategically rational — but its enduring significance lies in demonstrating that a decisive, well-prepared military-diplomatic strategy can reshape the regional order.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 IR, GS1 Modern India, Internal Security
  🌐 Contemporary: Bangladesh political upheaval (2024) and India-Bangladesh relations under Sheikh Hasina's successor are reshaping the 1971 legacy.

### GS2

**[2013]** Q21. The protests in Shahbag Square in Dhaka in Bangladesh reveal a fundamental split in society between the nationalists and Islamic forces. What is its significance for India?
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: IMPACT | 10m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Shahbag protests and Bangladesh polity, Implications for India's neighbourhood policy, Religious extremism in South Asia

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Shahbag protests revealed a deep civilizational contest in Bangladesh between secular nationalist identity rooted in 1971 and the Islamist revisionism of war crime apologists.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Background: War Crimes Tribunal convictions triggered demands for capital punishment — Shahbag Square movement
      • Societal split: pro-Shahbag secular/nationalist vs Hefazat-e-Islam/Jamaat Islamic response
      • India's concerns: rise of Islamic extremism bordering India, Hindu minority safety, border infiltration
      • Opportunity: secular Bangladesh favours India strategically; Islamist Bangladesh would be hostile
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India must calibrate its Bangladesh engagement to strengthen secular democratic forces while addressing legitimate concerns about minority rights and extremism.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 IR — India's neighbourhood policy, GS3 Internal Security — border and extremism
  🌐 Contemporary: The 2024 fall of Hasina government and rise of Muhammad Yunus-led interim government rekindles these concerns.
