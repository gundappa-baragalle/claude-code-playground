**[Q2824]** [2014]
> What does venture capital mean?

     A) A short-term capital provided to industries
  ✅ B) A long-term start-up capital provided to new entrepreneurs
     C) Funds provided to industries at times of incurring losses
     D) Funds provided for replacement and renovation of industries

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Venture capital is not emergency rescue funding (option C) or replacement capital (option D); it is specifically long-term equity funding for new/early stage businesses.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Venture capital = long-term start-up capital for new entrepreneurs with high-risk high-return profile; it is NOT short-term or rescue funding — answer is B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Venture capital provides equity funding to startups with high growth potential
     - VCs take equity stake in exchange for capital; exit through IPO or acquisition

> With reference to Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) rules in India, consider the following statements:  1. CSR rules specify that expenditures that benefit the company directly or its employees will not be considered as CSR activities. 2. CSR rules do not specify minimum spending on CSR activities.      B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

     - Direct employee benefits, business-benefiting activities NOT counted as CSR under Schedule VII

  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Manufacturing policy, Skill development, Labour reforms, Employment generation

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Make in India initiative's potential to transform India into a global manufacturing hub is intrinsically linked to addressing twin supply-side constraints: a skilled workforce and flexible labour regulations.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Make in India requires competitive manufacturing: skill gaps (India's low vocational training enrolment vs China/Germany) directly raise production costs and reduce quality
      • Labour reforms needed: complex labour laws (44 Central + 100+ State laws) increase compliance burden; four Labour Codes aim to address this
      • Skill India must align with industry demand through PPP models, industry-linked curricula, and recognition of prior learning
      • Without both, FDI may go to Vietnam, Bangladesh due to better skill-cost competitiveness
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Make in India can become a transformational programme only when Skill India provides the human capital foundation and labour reforms create the enabling regulatory environment for competitive manufacturing.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 — Labour welfare and social security, GS3 — Industrial policy, employment, GS2 — Governance and ease of doing business
  🌐 Contemporary: India's PLI schemes and China+1 strategy make the Make in India-Skill India linkage more urgent than ever.

**[2015]** Q10. There is a clear acknowledgement that Special Economic Zones (SEZs) are a tool of industrial development, manufacturing and exports. Recognizing this potential, the whole instrumentality of SEZs requires augmentation. Discuss the issues plaguing the success of SEZs with respect to taxation, governing laws and administration.
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Export promotion, Industrial development, Governance of SEZs

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Special Economic Zones are vital export promotion tools, but governance, taxation, and legal challenges have constrained their full potential.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Tax issues: sunset of income tax exemptions post-2020; MAT applicability reduced competitiveness
      • Governing law issues: land acquisition under SEZ Act conflicted with LA Act 2013; delays
      • Administration: cumbersome bureaucratic procedures; multiple approvals reduce ease of doing business
      • Labour laws: states' labour law flexibility in SEZs inconsistently applied
      • Success factors: Noida SEZ, SEEPZ (Mumbai) achieved export excellence; IT SEZs dominant
      • Way forward: digital SEZs concept, reform of sunset provisions, single-window clearance
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Revitalizing SEZs requires simplifying taxation, streamlining governance, and aligning them with sunrise sector opportunities like electronics and semiconductors.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Economy, GS2 – Governance, GS3 – Industry
  🌐 Contemporary: Development of Economic Zones Policy 2022 proposed to replace SEZ framework.
