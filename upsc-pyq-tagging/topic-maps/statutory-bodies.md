**[Q1413]** [2004]
> According to the National Human Rights Commission Act, 1993, who amongst the following can be its Chairman?

     A) Any serving Judge of the Supreme Court
     B) Any serving Judge of the High Court
  ✅ C) Only a retired Chief Justice of India
     D) Only a retired Chief Justice of High Court

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse NHRC chairperson qualifications with those of the Supreme Court. The Act specifies a retired Chief Justice of India — not a serving judge or High Court Chief Justice.
  💡 **Eliminate:** NHRC Act 1993 requires its Chairman to be a retired Chief Justice of India (Supreme Court) — not serving, not High Court. Eliminate options A, B, D → Answer C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - NHRC Chairman must be a retired Chief Justice of India
     - NHRC was established in 1993 under Protection of Human Rights Act

**[Q655]** [1999]
> Consider the following statements regarding the National Human Rights Commission of India: I. Its Chairman must be a retired Chief Justice of India. II. It has formations in each state as State Human Rights Commission. III. Its powers are only recommendatory in nature. IV. It is mandatory to appoint a woman as a member of the Commission. Which of the above statements are correct ?

     A) I, II, III and IV
     B) II and IV
     C) II and III
  ✅ D) I and III

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement II (NHRC formations in each state) confuses NHRC with SHRC — states have separate SHRCs, not formations of the NHRC; Statement IV about mandatory woman member sounds progressive and plausible.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Only statements I and III are correct: NHRC Chairman must be retired CJI (true at time of question, now amended to retired SC judge), and its powers are recommendatory. SHRCs are separate bodies, not NHRC formations → Option D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - NHRC: Chairman = retired Chief Justice of India (originally); powers are recommendatory
     - State Human Rights Commissions (SHRCs) are separate bodies, not sub-units of NHRC

**[Q4954]** [2025]
> Consider the following statements about Lokpal :  I. The power of Lokpal applies to public servants of India, but not to the Indian public servants posted outside India. II. The Chairperson or a Member shall not be a Member of the Parliament or a Member of the Legislature of any State or Union Territory, and only the Chief Justice of India, whether incumbent or retired, has to be its Chairperson. III. The Chairperson or a Member shall not be a person of less than forty-five years of age on the date of assuming office as the Chairperson or Member, as the case may be. IV. Lokpal cannot inquire into the allegations of corruption against a sitting Prime Minister of India.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) III only
     B) II and III
     C) I and IV
     D) None of the above statements is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement IV (Lokpal cannot inquire against sitting PM) is incorrect — Lokpal CAN inquire against PM but with stringent safeguards (two-thirds bench required). Statement II (only CJI can be chairperson) is also wrong — retired CJI or eminent person qualifies.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Only Statement III is correct: Chairperson/Member minimum age = 45 years. Statement I (excludes posted abroad) is wrong; II (only CJI can chair) is wrong; IV (cannot inquire against PM) is wrong. Answer: A (III only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Lokpal minimum age: Chairperson and Members must be 45 years old
     - Lokpal can inquire against PM with full bench approval and subject to safeguards

**[Q4374]** [2022]
> With reference to the "Tea Board" in India, consider the following statements :  1. The Tea Board is a statutory body. 2. It is a regulatory body attached to the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare. 3. The Tea Board's Head Office is situated in Bengaluru. 4. The Board has overseas offices at Dubai and Moscow.  Which of the statements given above are correct?

     A) 1 and 3
     B) 2 and 4
     C) 3 and 4
  ✅ D) 1 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Tea Board is attached to Ministry of Commerce (not Agriculture); headquarters is in Kolkata (not Bengaluru); overseas offices include Dubai but not Moscow.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Tea Board: statutory body (correct, statement 1); head office in Kolkata not Bengaluru (statement 3 wrong); overseas offices include Dubai (statement 4 partially correct but Moscow may be wrong). Statements 1 and 4 — answer D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Tea Board of India is a statutory body under Ministry of Commerce (not Agriculture)
     - Tea Board headquarters: Kolkata; overseas offices in Dubai and others (not Moscow as of recent information)

**[Q2253]** [2010]
> With reference to Lok Adalats, which of the following statements is correct ?

     A) Lok Adalats have the jurisdiction to settle the matters at pre-litigative stage and not those matters pending before any court
     B) Lok Adalats can deal with matters which are civil and not criminal in nature
     C) Every Lok Adalat consists of either serving or retired judicial officers only and not any other person
  ✅ D) None of the statements given above is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** All three statements about Lok Adalats contain plausible-sounding but incorrect restrictions — candidates may pick B or C thinking one statement is correct.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Lok Adalats can handle pre-litigation AND pending matters; can deal with criminal compoundable offences; can include lay persons alongside judicial officers — all three statements are wrong. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Lok Adalats have jurisdiction over both pre-litigation and court-pending matters
     - They can handle criminal compoundable offences (not just civil)

**[Q2343]** [2010]
> With reference to the Consumer Disputes Redressal at district level in India, which one of the following statements is not correct ?

     A) A State Government can establish more than one District Forum in a district if it deems fit
     B) One of the members of the District Forum shall be a woman
  ✅ C) The District Forum entertains the complaints where the value of goods or services does not exceed rupees fifty lakhs
     D) A complaint in relation to any goods sold or any service provided may be filed with a District Forum by the State Government as a representative of the interests of the consumers in general

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often confuse the monetary jurisdiction of District Forums; the correct limit was Rs 20 lakh (now Rs 1 crore under CPA 2019), not Rs 50 lakh as stated in C.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Under the Consumer Protection Act 1986, District Forum handled complaints up to Rs 20 lakh (not Rs 50 lakh). All other statements are correct. Answer is C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Under CPA 1986, District Forum jurisdictional limit was Rs 20 lakh; raised to Rs 1 crore under CPA 2019
     - State govt can establish multiple District Forums in one district

### GS2

**[2025]** Q2. Comment on the need of administrative tribunals as compared to the court system. Assess the impact of the recent tribunal reforms through rationalization of tribunals made in 2021.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: WHY | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Judicial reform, Specialized adjudication, Tribunal rationalization

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Administrative tribunals were conceived to address the limitations of the ordinary court system — but their proliferation over decades necessitated rationalization to preserve their original efficiency mandate.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Need for tribunals: courts suffer from massive pendency (4.5 crore cases); tribunals offer speed, technical expertise, cheaper adjudication in specialized domains (tax, service matters, environment)
      • Comparison with courts: tribunals lack procedural rigidity of courts; members have domain expertise; decisions can be appealed to HC/SC
      • Tribunals Reforms Act 2021: merged several specialized tribunals (Film Certification Appellate Tribunal, IPAB abolished); rationalized service conditions; Supreme Court struck down some provisions for compromising judicial independence
      • Impact: reduced multiplicity of forums but raised concerns about adequate benches, delays in appointments, and independence of tribunal members from executive influence
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Tribunal reform must balance efficiency with independence — mergers reduce fragmentation but cannot come at the cost of specialized expertise and judicial autonomy.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 Polity, Judiciary, GS3 Economy
  🌐 Contemporary: Supreme Court rulings (2021) on Tribunals Reforms Act raised critical questions about separation of powers.
