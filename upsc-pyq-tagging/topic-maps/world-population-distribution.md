**[Q301]** [1997]
> About 50% of the world population is concentrated between the latitudes of

     A) 5°N and 20°N
  ✅ B) 20°N and 40°N
     C) 40°N and 60°N
     D) 20°S and 40°S

  ⚠️ **Trap:** 5°N–20°N seems correct as it includes tropical India/SE Asia/Africa; 40°N–60°N seems correct as it includes all of Europe. The actual answer 20°N–40°N covers China, India's most populated belt, USA, and Europe.
  💡 **Eliminate:** 20°N–40°N covers: North China, Indo-Gangetic plain, Bangladesh, Pakistan, North Africa coast, USA, Southern Europe — together these hold >50% of world population. Eliminate equatorial belt (too sparse in Amazon/Congo) and high northern latitudes.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 20°N–40°N: densest human habitation belt
     - Includes: China's heartland, India's north, USA, Mediterranean Europe
     - World population concentration follows monsoon + temperate zones

**[Q946]** [2001]
> The high density of population in Nile Valley and Island of Java is primarily due to

  ✅ A) intensive agriculture
     B) industrialisation
     C) urbanisation
     D) topographic constraints

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Industrialisation and urbanisation are common reasons for population concentration in Europe/Americas; Nile Valley and Java are specifically agricultural zones where intensive farming explains density.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Nile Valley and Java: fertile alluvial soils support intensive wet rice and irrigated agriculture — option A (intensive agriculture) is the primary factor; industrialisation is not the historic reason for their density.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Nile Valley: world's most fertile land due to annual Nile floods depositing silt, enabling intensive agriculture
     - Java (Indonesia): volcanic soils and wet rice cultivation support one of world's highest rural population densities

**[Q605]** [1999]
> The language spoken by the largest number of people in the world is

     A) Hindi
     B) English
  ✅ C) Mandarin
     D) Spanish

  ⚠️ **Trap:** English is frequently cited as the world's most spoken language due to its global spread, but Mandarin (Putonghua) has more native speakers. The question specifies 'largest number of people' — native speakers.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Mandarin has over 1 billion native speakers, far exceeding English (~400 million native). Hindi (~600 million) is second. Answer: C (Mandarin).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Mandarin: ~1+ billion native speakers (largest)
     - English: widest geographic spread but fewer native speakers
     - Hindi: growing rapidly in total speakers

**[Q1449]** [2004]
> Consider the following international languages : 1. Arabic 2. French 3. Spanish The correct sequence of the languages given above in the decreasing order of the number of their speakers is :

  ✅ A) 3-1-2
     B) 1-3-2
     C) 3-2-1
     D) 1-2-3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Arabic is often overestimated; Spanish has far more native speakers (~500M) than Arabic (~300M) and French (~80M) — students confuse total speakers with native speakers.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Spanish (most speakers ~550M) > Arabic (~300M) > French (~280M) as total speakers; answer is Spanish-Arabic-French = 3-1-2.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Spanish has more speakers than Arabic globally
     - French has fewest speakers among the three

**[Q1210]** [2003]
> Which among the following countries has the largest population?

  ✅ A) Indonesia
     B) Japan
     C) Pakistan
     D) Sudan

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Pakistan's rapid population growth may make it seem comparable; Japan has a large but aging population.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Indonesia (population ~270 million) is the largest among the given options; it is the world's 4th most populous country. Note: question is dated (2003 era context).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Indonesia: ~270 million — world's 4th most populous nation
     - Sudan has ~45 million; Pakistan ~220 million; Japan ~125 million

**[Q1291]** [2003]
> Which one among the following languages has largest number of speakers in the world?

     A) Bengali
     B) French
     C) Japanese
  ✅ D) Portuguese

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Many students assume Mandarin/Chinese is largest by native speakers and confuse it with total speakers. Bengali is often underestimated. Portuguese is the surprise answer here.
  💡 **Eliminate:** By total speakers: Mandarin > Spanish > English > Hindi > Portuguese > Bengali. Among these options (Bengali, French, Japanese, Portuguese), Portuguese has the most speakers globally — Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Portuguese spoken in Brazil, Portugal, Mozambique, Angola — large total speaker base
     - Bengali has ~230 million native speakers but fewer total speakers than Portuguese

     B) 2, 4, 3, 1
     C) 4, 2, 3, 1

Without complete question text, answer A (4,2,1,3 = Sri Lanka, India, Bangladesh, Pakistan) suggests ranking by HDI/literacy in descending order.
