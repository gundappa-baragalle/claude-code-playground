**[Q4910]** [2025]
> Consider the following statements :  I. The Constitution of India explicitly mentions that in certain spheres the Governor of a State acts in his/her own discretion. II. The President of India can, of his/her own, reserve a bill passed by a State Legislature for his/her consideration without it being forwarded by the Governor of the State concerned.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) I only
     B) II only
     C) Both I and II
     D) Neither I nor II

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement I is correct — the Constitution does explicitly mention Governor's discretionary powers in certain articles (e.g., Article 163). Statement II is wrong: the President cannot suo motu reserve a State bill — a bill must first be reserved by the Governor.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Only Statement I is correct. The President acts on State bills only when the Governor forwards them under Article 200/201; he cannot act independently. Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 163: Governor acts in discretion in certain specified matters
     - President's reservation of State bills requires Governor's prior forwarding (Article 201)

**[Q4915]** [2025]
> With reference to the Indian polity, consider the following statements :  I. The Governor of a State is not answerable to any court for the exercise and performance of the powers and duties of his/her office. II. No criminal proceedings shall be instituted or continued against the Governor during his/her term of office. III. Members of a State Legislature are not liable to any proceedings in any court in respect of anything said within the House.  Which of the statements given above are correct?

     A) I and II only
     B) II and III only
     C) I and III only
  ✅ D) I, II and III

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may doubt Statement III — but legislative privilege (freedom of speech in the House) is a well-established constitutional provision under Article 194.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three are correct: Governor's immunity (Article 361), no criminal proceedings against Governor (Article 361(2)), legislative privilege for MLAs (Article 194). Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 361: Governor not answerable to any court for official acts
     - Article 194: MLA cannot be sued for anything said in the legislature

**[Q3800]** [2019]
> Which one of the following suggested that the Governor should be an eminent person from outside the State and should be a detached figure without intense political links or should not have taken part in politics in the recent past?

     A) First Administrative Reforms Commission (1966)
     B) Rajamannar Committee (1969)
  ✅ C) Sarkaria Commission (1983)
     D) National Commission to Review the Working of the Constitution (2000)

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse the Sarkaria Commission with the Punchhi Commission (2010) or Rajamannar Committee (Tamil Nadu autonomy). The Sarkaria Commission specifically recommended Governor qualification norms.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Sarkaria Commission (1983) on Centre-State relations recommended that Governor should be an eminent person, not from the state, and politically detached. Answer is C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Sarkaria Commission (1983): comprehensive review of Centre-State relations
     - Recommended Governor be apolitical, from outside state, eminent person
     - Punchhi Commission (2007-10) also endorsed these recommendations

**[Q3588]** [2018]
> Consider the following statements:  1. No criminal proceedings shall be instituted against the Governor of a State in any court during his term of office. 2. The emoluments and allowances of the Governor of a State shall not be diminished during his term of office.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Both statements about Governor's immunity seem plausible — students may doubt statement 1 (criminal proceedings) thinking it applies only to President.
  💡 **Eliminate:** No criminal proceedings against Governor during term of office (statement 1 correct — Article 361). Governor's emoluments cannot be diminished during term (statement 2 correct — Article 158). Both correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 361: Governor has immunity from criminal proceedings in any court during term
     - Article 158: Governor's emoluments and allowances cannot be diminished during term
     - Governor also immune from civil proceedings for official acts (unlike President who has total immunity)

**[Q3199]** [2016]
> Consider the following statements :  1. The Chief Secretary in a State is appointed by the Governor of that State. 2. The Chief Secretary in a State has a fixed tenure.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
  ✅ D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Since Governor appoints the Chief Minister, students may incorrectly assume the Governor also appoints the Chief Secretary; in reality, the Chief Secretary is a senior IAS officer appointed by state government.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Chief Secretary is not appointed by Governor (statement 1 wrong) — appointed by state Council of Ministers/CM; Chief Secretary has no fixed tenure (statement 2 wrong) — answer D (neither correct).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Chief Secretary is the senior-most IAS officer in a state, appointed by the state government, not the Governor
     - Chief Secretary has no constitutionally fixed tenure — serves at the pleasure of the state government

**[Q2856]** [2014]
> Which of the following are the discretionary powers given to the Governor of a State?  1. Sending a report to the President of India for imposing the President's rule 2. Appointing the Ministers 3. Reserving certain bills passed by the State Legislature for consideration of the President of India 4. Making the rules to conduct the business of the State Government  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1 and 2 only
  ✅ B) 1 and 3 only
     C) 2, 3 and 4 only
     D) 1, 2, 3 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Appointing ministers (statement 2) is done by Governor but on CM's advice — not a discretionary power; making rules for government business (statement 4) is done by Chief Minister under Article 166, not Governor.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Governor's discretionary powers: Sending President's rule report (1) and reserving bills for President (3) — statements 2 and 4 are conventional powers/CM's domain; answer is B (1 and 3 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Governor's discretionary powers: recommending President's Rule and reserving bills for President
     - Appointing ministers is on CM's advice — not discretionary

**[Q2593]** [2013]
> Which one of the following statements is correct?

     A) In India, the same person cannot be appointed as Governor for two or more States at the same time
     B) The Judges of the High Court of the States in India are appointed by the Governor of the State just as the Judges of the Supreme Court are appointed by the President
  ✅ C) No procedure has been laid down in the Constitution of India for the removal of a Governor from his/her post
     D) In the case of a Union Territory having a legislative setup, the Chief Minister is appointed by the Lt. Governor on the basis of majority support

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option A is incorrect — Constitution allows one person to be Governor of multiple states. Option B is incorrect — HC judges are appointed by President (not Governor). Option D is also incorrect — UT CM is appointed by Lt Governor but not purely on majority support.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Option C is correct: Constitution (Art 156) has no explicit provision for removal of Governor; President can remove at pleasure, but no formal impeachment procedure exists. Answer is C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Constitution has no laid-down procedure for removal of Governor — President removes at pleasure under Art 156(1)
     - One person can be Governor of two or more states simultaneously (Art 153 proviso)

**[Q68]** [1995]
> Article 156 of the Constitution of India provides that a Governor shall hold office for a term of five years from the date on which he enters upon his office. Which of the following can be deduced from this ? I. No Governor can be removed from office till completion of his term. II. No Governor can continue in office beyond a period of five years. Select the correct answer from the codes given below :

     A) I only
     B) II only
     C) Both I and II
  ✅ D) Neither I nor II

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The constitutional text says 'five years from the date of entry' but this is qualified — Governor holds office at President's pleasure (Art. 156(1)), so neither deduction I nor II is correct.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Art. 156 provides 5-year term BUT Art. 156(1) says Governor holds office 'during the pleasure of the President.' So Governor CAN be removed before 5 years and CAN continue beyond if asked. Neither I nor II can be deduced. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Art. 156(1) — Governor holds office at pleasure of President (removable anytime)
     - Art. 156(3) — normal term 5 years, but can be extended or cut short

**[Q2169]** [2009]
> Consider the following statements : 1. The Governor of Punjab is concurrently the Administrator of Chandigarh. 2. The Governor of Kerala is concurrently the Administrator of Lakshadweep. Which of the above statements is/are correct ?

  ✅ A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 assigns Lakshadweep to Kerala's Governor — but the Administrator of Lakshadweep is actually the Lt. Governor appointed separately (not the Kerala Governor concurrently).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Punjab's Governor concurrently serves as Administrator of Chandigarh (UT) — established convention (statement 1 correct). Lakshadweep has its own separate Lt. Governor/Administrator, not Kerala's Governor (statement 2 wrong).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Punjab Governor is concurrently the Administrator of Chandigarh UT by convention/notification
     - Lakshadweep has a separate Administrator (Lt. Governor rank) — Kerala Governor has no role there

### GS2

**[2022]** Q12. Discuss the essential conditions for exercise of the legislative powers by the Governor. Discuss the legality of re-promulgation of ordinances by the Governor without placing them before the Legislature.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Executive legislation, Constitutional governance, Separation of powers

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Governor's power to promulgate ordinances under Article 213 is a legitimate executive function subject to strict constitutional conditions, with re-promulgation being an unconstitutional subversion.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Conditions for ordinance: legislature not in session, immediate necessity
      • SC in D.C. Wadhwa: repeated re-promulgation without legislature approval is unconstitutional
      • Krishna Kumar Singh (2017): 7-judge bench — re-promulgation is fraud on Constitution
      • Governor bound by CM's advice in ordinance matters (7th Schedule)
      • Legality: re-promulgation illegal as it bypasses Art 213(2) requirement of laying before legislature
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Re-promulgation of ordinances without legislative approval is a constitutional fraud that undermines parliamentary democracy and must be categorically rejected.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: State Government, Constitutional Framework, Governance: accountability
  🌐 Contemporary: Several state governors have delayed or refused to assent to legislation, renewing questions about gubernatorial discretion.
