**[Q3459]** [2023] With reference to India, consider the following pairs : Action - The Act under which it is covered  1. Unauthorized wearing of police or military uniforms - The Official Secrets Act, 1923 2. Knowingly misleading or otherwise interfering with a police officer or military officer when engaged in their duties - The Indian Evidence Act, 1872 3. Celebratory gunfire which can endanger the personal safety of others - The Arms (Amendment) Act, 2019  How many of the above pairs are correctly matched?

     A) Only one
  ✅ B) Only two
     C) All three
     D) None

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Pairs 1 and 2 may seem uncertain; unauthorized wearing of police/military uniform is covered under Indian Penal Code (Section 140/171), not Official Secrets Act. Arms Act 2019 covers celebratory gunfire. Pair 2 should be covered under police regulations or IPC, not Indian Evidence Act.

**[2025]** Q9. Terrorism is a global scourge. How has it manifested in India? Elaborate with contemporary examples. What are the counter measures adopted by the State? Explain. (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Internal security threats, Counter-terrorism policy, Role of state apparatus

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Terrorism, a global menace, manifests in India through cross-border infiltration, insurgency-linked violence, and radicalization-driven domestic attacks.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Manifestations: Pakistan-sponsored cross-border terrorism in J&K, Northeast insurgency with external linkages, ISIS-inspired lone-wolf attacks
      • Contemporary examples: Pulwama attack 2019, targeted killings in J&K post-Article 370 abrogation
      • Counter measures: NIA strengthened post-2008, UAPA amendments 2019 (individual designation as terrorist), Multi-Agency Centre (MAC)
      • Technological measures: NATGRID, CCTNS, surveillance drones along borders
      • Soft measures: de-radicalization programs, community policing, Sadbhavana Mission
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** A holistic counter-terrorism strategy combining hard security measures with community engagement, intelligence fusion, and addressing root causes of radicalization is imperative.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: Governance and policing, GS3: Border management, IR: India-Pakistan relations
  🌐 Contemporary: Ongoing terrorism in J&K and global concerns about ISIS-linked radicalization make this a perpetually high-priority security challenge.

**[2023]** Q20. Give out the major sources of terror funding in India and the efforts being made to curtail these sources. In the light of this, also discuss the aim and objective of the 'No Money for Terror (NMFT)' Conference recently held at New Delhi in November 2022.  (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Terror financing in India, International cooperation against terror funding

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Cutting off financial lifelines to terrorist organizations is as critical as kinetic counter-terrorism operations, requiring robust domestic and international coordination.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Major sources: hawala transfers, drug trafficking (narco-terrorism), counterfeit Indian currency notes (FICN), misuse of NGOs/charities, cryptocurrency
      • State-sponsored terror financing from across borders (Pakistan ISI)
      • Legislative measures: PMLA, UAPA, NDPS Act, Benami Transactions Act
      • Institutional measures: Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU-IND), FATF compliance, NIA
      • NMFT Conference 2022: 72 countries participated; focus on disrupting financing channels, improving international information sharing, countering online radicalization funding
      • Objectives of NMFT: no safe haven for terror funds, stronger AML/CFT regimes
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Effective counter-terror financing requires a whole-of-government approach combining strong legislation, inter-agency coordination and robust international partnerships.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 Internal Security, GS2 IR — International Organizations, Economy — External Sector
  🌐 Contemporary: With FATF grey-listing concerns and rising cryptocurrency use by terror groups, counter-terror financing remains a top security priority.

**[2019]** Q19. Indian Government has recently strengthened the anti-terrorism laws by amending the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act (UAPA), 1967 and the NIA Act. Analyze the changes in the context of prevailing security environment while discussing the scope and reasons for opposing the UAPA by human rights organizations.  (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: IMPACT | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Anti-terrorism law, Security vs civil liberties, Legislative framework

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The 2019 amendments to UAPA and NIA Act significantly strengthened India's counter-terrorism legal architecture while raising concerns about civil liberties.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • UAPA changes: individual designation as terrorist without trial; stringent bail conditions; enhanced investigation timelines
      • NIA changes: extended jurisdiction to extraterritorial terror offences; state-level NIA branches
      • Security context: cross-border terrorism, lone-wolf attacks, online radicalization justify enhanced powers
      • Human rights opposition: arbitrary individual designation, denial of bail, chilling effect on activists/journalists
      • Organizations opposing: PUCL, Amnesty International, civil rights lawyers
      • Balance needed: due process protections, judicial oversight, sunset clauses, periodic review
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Strengthening anti-terror laws is necessary but must be balanced with constitutional safeguards to prevent misuse against legitimate dissent.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Internal Security, GS2 – Governance, GS2 – Polity
  🌐 Contemporary: SC judgments on UAPA bail (Zubair, Bhima Koregaon cases) and NIA's expanding operations make this live.

**[2017]** Q19. Mob violence is emerging as a serious law and order problem in India. By giving suitable examples, analyze the causes and consequences of such violence. (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: IMPACT | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Internal security, Social cohesion, Governance of social media

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Mob violence, fueled by social media misinformation and communal polarization, has emerged as a serious challenge to law and order in India.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Causes: social media rumors (WhatsApp), communal polarization, cow vigilantism, child-lifting fears, political provocation
      • Examples: Alwar, Jharkhand lynching cases; 2020 Delhi riots; Manipur ethnic violence
      • Consequences: communal divide deepening, erosion of rule of law, vigilantism normalized, economic impact on minorities
      • Legal gaps: no specific anti-lynching law nationally (Manipur has one); delayed accountability
      • SC interventions: Tehseen Poonawalla case (2018) — asked Parliament to legislate against lynching
      • Solutions: media literacy, swift prosecution, community peace committees, FIR accountability for false rumors
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Curbing mob violence requires a combination of legal reform, social media regulation, community engagement, and zero-tolerance enforcement.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Internal Security, GS1 – Society, GS2 – Governance
  🌐 Contemporary: Manipur ethnic violence (2023) and continued incidents of mob violence make this highly relevant.
