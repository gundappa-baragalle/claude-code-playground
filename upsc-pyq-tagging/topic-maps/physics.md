**[Q3609]** [2018]
> Consider the following phenomena :  1. Light is affected by gravity. 2. The Universe is constantly expanding. 3. Matter warps its surrounding space-time.  Which of the above is/are the prediction/predictions of Albert Einstein's General Theory of Relativity, often discussed in media ?

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may think the expanding universe is Hubble's observation (not Einstein's prediction), and that only 1 and 3 are Einstein's predictions, missing Statement 2.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three — light affected by gravity (gravitational lensing), expanding universe (predicted by GR equations), and matter warping spacetime — are predictions of Einstein's General Theory of Relativity. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - General Relativity predicts: gravity bends light, spacetime curvature, expanding universe
     - Hubble observed expanding universe empirically, but Einstein's equations predicted it

**[Q2680]** [2013]
> Consider the following phenomena :  1. Size of the sun at dusk 2. Colour of the sun at dawn 3. Moon being visible at dawn 4. Twinkle of stars in the sky 5. Polestar being visible in the sky  Which of the above are optical illusions?

     A) 1, 2 and 3
     B) 3, 4 and 5
  ✅ C) 1, 2 and 4
     D) 2, 3 and 5

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Moon visible at dawn (3) is NOT an optical illusion — the moon can genuinely be visible during daytime. Twinkling of stars (4) IS an optical illusion (atmospheric refraction). Polestar visibility (5) is a real phenomenon.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Optical illusions: sun appearing larger at dusk (1, atmospheric refraction), sun's colour at dawn (2, scattering), twinkling of stars (4, atmospheric refraction). Moon visibility at dawn (3) and Polestar visibility (5) are real. Answer: C (1, 2, 4).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Sun appears larger at horizon: optical illusion (Ponzo illusion + refraction)
     - Star twinkling: atmospheric refraction (turbulence), an optical illusion
     - Moon visible at dawn: real phenomenon, not an illusion
