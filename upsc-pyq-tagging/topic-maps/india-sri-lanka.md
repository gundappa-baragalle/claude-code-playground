## Prelims Questions

### 🟡 MEDIUM Priority — Full Options

**[Q2122]** [2009]
> Elephant Pass, which is frequently in the news, is mentioned in the context of the affairs of which one of the following ?

     A) Bangladesh
     B) India
     C) Nepal
  ✅ D) Sri Lanka

  ⚠️ **Trap:** India is a plausible answer since Elephant Pass sounds like it could be in India; Elephant Pass (Aanaithivu) is actually a strategic land passage in northern Sri Lanka (Jaffna Peninsula).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Elephant Pass is a narrow isthmus connecting Jaffna Peninsula to mainland Sri Lanka — it was a key strategic battleground in the Sri Lankan civil war; answer is D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Elephant Pass (Kilali) is a strategic causeway in northern Sri Lanka connecting Jaffna
     - It was a major LTTE stronghold and site of multiple battles in the Sri Lankan civil war

**[Q720]** [1999]
> Which one of the following statements is correct ?

     A) Kacchativu and Tin Bigha were territories acquired by the Indian Republic from the French
  ✅ B) Kacchativu and Tin Bigha are territories handed over to Sri Lankan and Bangladeshi sovereignty respectively by the Government of India
     C) Kacchativu and Tin Bigha are areas that were annexed by the Chinese in the 1962 Sino-Indian War
     D) Kacchativu and Tin Bigha are enclaves which were transferred to India by lease arrangements with Sri Lanka and Pakistan respectively

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Kachhativu is associated with Sri Lanka and Tin Bigha with Bangladesh; students may confuse which territory was given to which country or the mechanism (lease vs sovereignty).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Kachhativu: ceded to Sri Lanka (sovereignty transfer, 1974 Indira Gandhi-Sirimavo Bandaranaike agreement); Tin Bigha: leased to Bangladesh (not sovereignty). Statement B says 'handed over to Sri Lankan and Bangladeshi sovereignty' — partially correct; Tin Bigha was leased, not given sovereignty.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Kachhativu island ceded to Sri Lanka in 1974 by Indira Gandhi
     - Tin Bigha corridor: India gave Bangladesh a lease/access corridor in 1992, not sovereign territory

**[Q506]** [1998]
> Which one of the following was the venue for the preliminary talks between the Sri Lankan Government and representatives of Tamil United Liberation Front and other militant groups ?

     A) New Delhi
     B) Colombo
  ✅ C) Zirconiun
     D) Chennai

  ⚠️ **Trap:** New Delhi and Colombo are obvious diplomatic capitals that appear logical for such talks; Chennai is plausible given Tamil Nadu's proximity to the Sri Lankan Tamil issue.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The correct answer 'Zirconiun' (likely Thimpu/another city — possibly a misprint in the original) is obscure; eliminate obvious capitals and recall that preliminary talks often happen in neutral/third locations.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Sri Lanka peace talks in 1990s involved multiple rounds at different venues
     - Tamil United Liberation Front was a key negotiating party

### GS2

**[2022]** Q9. 'India is an age-old friend of Sri Lanka.' Discuss India's role in the recent crisis in Sri Lanka in the light of the preceding statement.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: IMPACT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Neighbourhood First policy, India's humanitarian diplomacy, Geopolitics of Indian Ocean

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's response to the 2022 Sri Lankan economic crisis exemplified its Neighbourhood First policy through timely financial assistance, humanitarian aid, and diplomatic support.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • India extended $4 billion in credit lines, currency swaps, and deferred payments for fuel, food, and medicines
      • Humanitarian dimension: supply of essential commodities to prevent civilian suffering
      • Geopolitical dimension: countering China's footprint in Sri Lanka (Hambantota port)
      • India's role at IMF: supported Sri Lanka's debt restructuring process as a bilateral creditor
      • Historical baggage—Tamil question—balanced with strategic and humanitarian imperatives
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India's support to Sri Lanka during the 2022 crisis reinforced its role as the region's first responder while advancing strategic interests in the Indian Ocean.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 – International Relations, GS3 – Indian Economy (forex), GS2 – Governance
  🌐 Contemporary: Sri Lanka's ongoing IMF programme and India-China competition in the Indian Ocean make this highly relevant.

**[2013]** Q23. In respect of India – Sri Lanka relations, discuss how domestic factors influence foreign policy.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Domestic factors in foreign policy, India-Sri Lanka ties, Ethnic politics

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India-Sri Lanka relations uniquely demonstrate how domestic political pressures, particularly from Tamil Nadu, can override purely strategic calculations in foreign policy.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Tamil issue: India's sympathy with Tamils driven by Tamil Nadu electoral politics (DMK/AIADMK pressure)
      • 1987: India-Sri Lanka Accord and IPKF — partly domestically driven by Tamil sentiment
      • Fishermen's issue: Tamil Nadu fishermen in Palk Strait — persistent bilateral irritant
      • 13th Amendment: India's sustained pressure to implement reflects domestic compulsion
      • Lessons: domestic politics as a structural factor, not aberration, in India's neighbourhood policy
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India-Sri Lanka relations exemplify how sub-national politics can shape national foreign policy, requiring diplomatic finesse to balance domestic pressures with strategic interests.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: IR, Polity: Federalism, Society: Diaspora
  🌐 Contemporary: Sri Lanka's economic crisis (2022) and China's growing influence renewed India-Sri Lanka strategic engagement.
