**[Q4943]** [2025]
> Consider the following statements :  I. On the dissolution of the House of the People, the Speaker shall not vacate his/her office until immediately before the first meeting of the House of the People after the dissolution. II. According to the provisions of the Constitution of India, a Member of the House of the People on being elected as Speaker shall resign from his/her political party immediately. III. The Speaker of the House of the People may be removed from his/her office by a resolution of the House of the People passed by a majority of all the then Members of the House, provided that no resolution shall be moved unless at least fourteen days' notice has been given of the intention to move the resolution.  Which of the statements given above are correct?

     A) I and II only
     B) II and III only
  ✅ C) I and III only
     D) I, II and III

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement II is the trap — the Constitution does NOT require the Speaker to resign from the political party upon election; the Speaker is expected to be impartial but not formally resign party membership.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Statement I (Speaker continues till first sitting of new House after dissolution) is correct per Article 94. Statement II (resignation from party) is wrong — no constitutional provision. Statement III (removal by majority with 14 days notice) is correct per Article 94. Answer: C (I and III).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 94: Speaker continues in office until the new House's first sitting after dissolution
     - Speaker can be removed by effective majority (majority of all members) with 14 days' notice

**[Q4762]** [2024]
> With reference to the Speaker of the Lok Sabha, consider the following statements: While any resolution for the removal of Speaker of the Lok Sabha is under consideration:  1. He/She shall not preside. 2. He/She shall not have the right to speak. 3. He/She shall not be entitled to vote on the resolution in the first instance.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 only
     B) 1 and 2 only
     C) 2 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often think Speaker loses all rights including voting when removal resolution is pending — the Speaker retains right to speak and a casting vote, but must NOT preside.
  💡 **Eliminate:** When removal resolution is under consideration: Speaker shall not preside (1 correct), but retains right to speak (2 wrong) and can vote in the first instance — not just casting vote (3 wrong). Only Statement 1 is correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 181: Speaker shall not preside while removal resolution is being considered; Deputy Speaker presides
     - Speaker retains right to speak and vote (including first instance vote) during removal proceedings — only presiding is prohibited

**[Q4310]** [2022]
> Which of the following is/are the exclusive power(s) of Lok Sabha ?  1. To ratify the declaration of Emergency 2. To pass a motion of no-confidence against the Council of Ministers 3. To impeach the President of India  Select the correct answer using the code given below:

     A) 1 and 2
  ✅ B) 2 only
     C) 1 and 3
     D) 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Ratifying Emergency (Statement 1) requires both Houses; impeaching the President (Statement 3) also involves both Houses — only no-confidence motion is Lok Sabha exclusive.
  💡 **Eliminate:** No-confidence motion (Statement 2) is the ONLY exclusive power of Lok Sabha. Emergency ratification needs both houses; President's impeachment involves both houses. Answer is B (2 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - No-confidence motion against government can only be moved and passed in Lok Sabha
     - President's impeachment requires 2/3rd majority in EACH house separately — both houses involved

**[Q3436]** [2017]
> Consider the following statements:  1. In the election for Lok Sabha or State Assembly, the winning candidate must get at least 50 percent of the votes polled, to be declared elected. 2. According to the provisions laid down in the Constitution of India, in Lok Sabha, the Speaker's post goes to the majority. party and the Deputy Speaker's to the Opposition.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
  ✅ D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is a common misconception — India uses First Past the Post (FPTP), not the 50%+ majority system. Statement 2 about Deputy Speaker going to Opposition is a convention, not a constitutional provision.
  💡 **Eliminate:** FPTP means the candidate with most votes (not necessarily 50%+) wins; eliminating statement 1 as false leads to 'Neither' as the answer since statement 2 is also not a constitutional provision.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - India uses First Past the Post electoral system — winner needs only plurality, not 50% majority
     - The convention of Deputy Speaker going to Opposition is not constitutionally mandated

**[Q2500]** [2012]
> Regarding the office of the Lok Sabha Speaker, consider the following statements!  1. He/She holds the office during the pleasure of the President. 2. He/She need not be a member of the House at the time of his/her election but has to become a member of the House within six months from the date of his/her election. 3. If he/she intends to resign, the letter of his/her resignation has to be addressed to the Deputy Speaker.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 and 2 only
  ✅ B) 3 only
     C) 1, 2 and 3
     D) None

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 (Speaker holds office at President's pleasure) is false — Speaker is elected by Lok Sabha and holds office till dissolution; candidates may wrongly select it as true.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Only statement 3 is correct — Speaker's resignation letter goes to Deputy Speaker; Statement 1 is false (Speaker is not at President's pleasure); Statement 2 is false (Speaker must already be a member of Lok Sabha). Answer: B (3 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Lok Sabha Speaker resigns by writing to Deputy Speaker (Article 94)
     - Speaker is elected by Lok Sabha members from among themselves
     - Speaker holds office as long as she/he is a member of Lok Sabha

**[Q1364]** [2004]
> Consider the following statements: 1. The Speaker of Lok Sabha has the power to adjourn the House sine die but, on prorogation, it is only the President who can summon the House. 2. Unless sooner dissolved or there is an extension of the term, there is an automatic dissolution of the Lok Sabha by efflux of time, at the end of the period of five years, even if no formal order of dissolution is issued by the President. 3. The Speaker of Lok Sabha continues in office even after the dissolution of the House and until ‘immediately before the first meeting of the House’. Which of the statements given above are correct?

     A) 1 and 2
     B) 2 and 3
     C) 1 and 3
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 about automatic dissolution after 5 years is nuanced — the Constitution doesn't explicitly say 'automatic'; it requires Presidential proclamation. However, dissolution by efflux of time is effectively automatic.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three statements are constitutionally sound: Speaker adjourns sine die (1); Lok Sabha dissolves by efflux (effectively, 2); Speaker continues until first meeting of new House (3). Answer: D (all correct).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Speaker adjourns House sine die; President prorogues (summons/prorogues)
     - Lok Sabha dissolves at 5-year term end; President's order is formal not substantive
     - Speaker continues in office after dissolution until first meeting of new House

**[Q1081]** [2002]
> The term of the Lok Sabha

     A) cannot be extended under any circumstances
     B) can be extended by six months at a time
  ✅ C) can be extended by one year at a time during the proclamation of emergency
     D) can be extended-for two years at a time during the proclamation of emergency

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may remember that Lok Sabha term can be extended during emergency but forget the specific unit — it is one year at a time (not two years or six months at a time).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Article 83(2): Lok Sabha can be extended by Parliament by law for one year at a time during National Emergency, for as long as the emergency is in force. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Lok Sabha term can be extended by one year at a time during National Emergency (Article 83)
     - Extension cannot exceed beyond the emergency period

> Which of the following statements about the Ethics Committee in the Lok Sabha are correct ?  1. Initially it was an ad-hoc Committee. 2. Only a Member of the Lok Sabha can make a complaint relating to unethical conduct of a member of the Lok Sabha. 3. This Committee cannot take up any matter which is sub-judice.  Select the answer using the code given below :

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
  ✅ C) 1 and 3 only

**[Q4315]** [2022]
> With reference to Deputy Speaker of Lok Sabha, consider the following statements :  1. As per the Rules of Procedure and Conduct of Business in Lok Sabha, the election of Deputy Speaker shall be held on such date as the Speaker may fix. 2. There is a mandatory provision that the election of a candidate as Deputy Speaker of Lok Sabha shall be from either the principal opposition party or the ruling party. 3. The Deputy Speaker has the same power as of the Speaker when presiding over the sitting of the House and no appeal lies against his rulings. 4. The well established parliamentary practice regarding the appointment of Deputy Speaker is that the motion is moved by the Speaker and duly seconded by the Prime Minister.  Which of the statements given above are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 and 3 only
     B) 1, 2 and 3
     C) 3 and 4 only
     D) 2 and 4 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is false — there is no constitutional or statutory mandatory provision requiring Deputy Speaker to be from either opposition or ruling party. Statement 4 is also false — Deputy Speaker is not appointed via Speaker's motion; it is elected by the House.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Statements 2 and 4 are clearly wrong conventions vs actual rules; statements 1 and 3 are correct — answer is A (1 and 3 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Deputy Speaker is elected by Lok Sabha members under Article 93
     - Deputy Speaker has same powers as Speaker when presiding; rulings are final

**[Q1368]** [2004]
> Which one of the following statements is not correct?

  ✅ A) In Lok Sabha, a no-confidence motion has to set out the grounds on which it is based.
     B) In the case of a no-confidence motion in the Lok Sabha, no conditions of admissibility have been laid down in the Rules.
     C) A motion of no-confidence, once admitted, has to be taken up within ten days of the leave being granted.
     D) Rajya Sabha is not empowered to entertain a motion of no-confidence.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement A says no-confidence motion must set out grounds — actually Rules of Lok Sabha do NOT require grounds to be stated; any member can move it without specifying reasons.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Under Lok Sabha Rules, a no-confidence motion need NOT set out the grounds — Statement A is incorrect. B (no conditions of admissibility), C (10-day time limit), D (Rajya Sabha can't entertain) are all correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - No-confidence motion in Lok Sabha: no grounds need to be stated
     - Must be admitted and taken up within 10 days
     - Rajya Sabha cannot entertain no-confidence motion — only Lok Sabha

**[Q189]** [1996]
> If the number of seats allocated to a state in the Lok Sabha is 42, then the number of seats reserved for the Scheduled Castes in that state will be

     A) 21
     B) 14
     C) 7
  ✅ D) 6

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may calculate 1/7 of 42 = 6, or remember the national SC population proportion (~15%) to arrive at different numbers. The actual reservation ratio needs to match population proportion.
  💡 **Eliminate:** SC reservation in Lok Sabha is approximately proportional to SC population (~15%). For a 42-seat state, 15% ≈ 6. Correct: D (6). Note: the exact formula is SC seats proportional to SC population of that state.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - SC reservation in Lok Sabha: seats reserved ∝ SC population of state
     - Nationally SCs ≈ 15% of population; for 42 seats × 15% ≈ 6 seats

### GS2

**[2020]** Q5. 'Once a Speaker, Always a Speaker' ! Do you think this practice should be adopted to impart objectivity to the office of the Speaker of Lok Sabha? What could be its implications for the robust functioning of parliamentary business in India?
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: IMPACT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Parliamentary democracy, Neutrality of constitutional offices, Anti-defection

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The question of adopting 'Once a Speaker, Always a Speaker' convention raises fundamental issues about institutional neutrality versus political realities in India's parliamentary democracy.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • UK convention: Speaker contests without opposition from major parties; creates institutional neutrality and independence
      • Case for adoption: would reduce partisan decisions on anti-defection, disqualifications; enhance prestige of office
      • Case against: India's coalition dynamics make such convention impractical; Speaker is elected by majority party
      • Current partisan problem: Speakers consistently rule in favor of ruling party in defection cases; SC had to intervene (Nabam Rebia case)
      • Implications: if adopted — stronger parliamentary scrutiny of executive, less partisan adjudication, more robust opposition
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Adopting 'Once a Speaker, Always a Speaker' would strengthen parliamentary neutrality, but requires broader political consensus and reforms like independent defection adjudication tribunals to be truly meaningful.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: Polity — Parliament, GS2: Governance — accountability, GS4: Ethics — neutrality in constitutional offices
  🌐 Contemporary: Speaker Om Birla's tenure controversies and Supreme Court's intervention in Maharashtra defection case keep this debate live.
