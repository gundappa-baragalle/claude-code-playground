**[Q4572]** [2023]
> Consider the following statements:  1. Recently, all the countries of the United Nations have adopted the first-ever compact for international migration, the 'Global Compact for Safe, Orderly and Regular Migration (GCM)'. 2. The objectives and commitments. stated in the GCM are binding on the UN member countries. 3. The GCM addresses internal migration or internally displaced people also in its objectives and commitments.  How many of the above statements are correct?

     A) Only one
     B) Only two
     C) All three
  ✅ D) None

  ⚠️ **Trap:** All three statements contain subtle errors: 'all UN countries' (not all adopted), 'binding' (it is non-binding), and 'internal migration' (GCM covers only international migration). All three are wrong — answer D (none).
  💡 **Eliminate:** GCM adopted in 2018: not all UN members (US, Hungary etc. opted out); it is non-binding; it covers only international migration, not internal displacement (that is GCR — Global Compact on Refugees).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Global Compact for Migration (2018): non-binding, voluntary framework
     - Not all UN countries adopted — US, Hungary, Australia among those that did not
     - GCM covers international migration only; internal displacement is under UNHCR

### 🟡 MEDIUM Priority — Full Options

**[Q1638]** [2005]
> In which one of the following countries, is Tamil a major language?

     A) Myanmar
     B) Indonesia
     C) Mauritius
  ✅ D) Singapore

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Mauritius also has a significant Tamil-origin population, making it a tempting distractor. Myanmar has Tamil communities but Tamil is not a major official/recognized language there.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Singapore is the only country among the options where Tamil is an official language (one of four). This is a constitutional fact, making elimination straightforward.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Tamil is one of four official languages of Singapore
     - Singapore's official languages: English, Mandarin, Malay, Tamil

### GS2

**[2023]** Q10. Indian diaspora has scaled new heights in the West.  Describe its economic and political benefits for India.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: IMPACT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Diaspora and Indian foreign policy, Soft power, Remittances and development

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's diaspora in the West — particularly in the USA, UK, and Canada — has emerged as a significant force for both economic development and diplomatic influence.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Economic: $125 billion remittances (2023), highest globally; NRI investments in real estate, startups; tech leaders driving FDI
      • Political: Indian-Americans in US Congress and Cabinet positions lobby for India-friendly policies; Indian community in UK influences India-UK FTA negotiations
      • Soft power: cultural diplomacy through yoga, Bollywood, Indian food; promoting Brand India globally
      • Challenges: diaspora divided on India's political issues; Khalistan elements in Canada complicating bilateral ties
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India must strategically engage its diaspora — celebrating economic contributions while carefully navigating the political complexities of diaspora activism in bilateral relations.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 IR — foreign policy, GS3 Economy — remittances and external sector
  🌐 Contemporary: India-Canada diplomatic tensions over Khalistan and India-US relations with Indian-American political ascendancy make this immediately relevant.

**[2020]** Q10. 'Indian diaspora has a decisive role to play in the politics and economy of America and European Countries'. Comment with examples.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: IMPACT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Indian diaspora influence, Political and economic role, Soft power

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Indian diaspora, particularly in the United States and Europe, has emerged as a decisive force in both the politics and economies of their host countries, while simultaneously strengthening India's global stature.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Economic role: Indian-Americans are among the highest-earning groups in the US (median income >$130,000); CEOs of Google, Microsoft, IBM are of Indian origin
      • Political role in America: growing representation in Congress (Kamala Harris, Ro Khanna); Indian-American PACs influencing elections; lobbying for India-US relations
      • European context: British diaspora (Rishi Sunak as PM); diaspora influencing Brexit discourse and trade policy discussions
      • Remittances: India receives ~$100+ billion annually — supports consumption, real estate, and foreign exchange reserves
      • Soft power: Indian diaspora promotes yoga, Bollywood, Indian food — enhancing India's cultural footprint
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The Indian diaspora's political and economic influence in Western nations represents a strategic asset for India's foreign policy and a source of pride for its democratic credentials.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: IR and diaspora, GS3: Remittances and economy, GS1: Indian society and migration
  🌐 Contemporary: Rishi Sunak as UK PM, Kamala Harris as US VP, and Indian-origin tech leaders make this highly relevant.

**[2017]** Q20. Indian diaspora has an important role to play in South-East Asian countries' economy and society. Appraise the role of Indian diaspora in South-East Asia in this context.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: IMPACT | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Indian diaspora, South-East Asia relations, Cultural and economic linkages

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's diaspora in Southeast Asia, rooted in centuries of trade, indentured labor, and migration, serves as a unique bridge connecting India's Act East Policy with regional economies and societies.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Economic role: Indian-origin businesses dominate sectors in Malaysia (rubber, palm oil historically), Singapore (IT, finance, services), Myanmar (trade)
      • Cultural role: Tamil-origin communities preserve Indian culture; Hindu temples, festivals serve as soft power
      • Remittances: significant contributor to bilateral economic ties; though SE Asian diaspora is less remittance-focused than Gulf diaspora
      • Political influence: Singapore's founding leadership (S. Rajaratnam — Indian-origin) exemplifies political integration
      • India's Act East Policy: diaspora as 'human bridge' for strengthening bilateral diplomatic and economic ties
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The Indian diaspora in Southeast Asia constitutes a strategic soft power asset for India's Act East Policy, though leveraging this asset requires active engagement through the Pravasi Bharatiya network and cultural diplomacy.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: India's foreign policy — Act East, GS1: Society — diaspora communities, GS3: Economy — remittances and FDI
  🌐 Contemporary: ASEAN-India relations and India's Indo-Pacific strategy make SE Asian diaspora engagement a foreign policy priority.
