**[Q4863]** [2025]
> Consider the following statements :  Statement I : As regards returns from an investment in a company, generally, bondholders are considered to be relatively at lower risk than stockholders. Statement II : Bondholders are lenders to a company whereas stockholders are its owners. Statement III : For repayment purpose, bond- holders are prioritized over stockholders by a company.  Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

  ✅ A) Both Statement II and Statement III are correct and both of them explain Statement I
     B) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct and Statement I explains Statement II
     C) Only one of the Statements II and III is correct and that explains Statement I
     D) Neither Statement II nor Statement III is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may overthink whether both Statements II and III are needed or just one; the key is understanding WHY bondholders are at lower risk.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Bondholders are lenders (II) AND have repayment priority (III) — both together explain why they are at lower risk than stockholders (I). Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Bondholders are creditors; stockholders are equity owners
     - In liquidation, bondholders are paid before stockholders

**[Q4360]** [2022]
> With reference to Convertible Bonds, consider the following statements:  1. As there is an option to exchange the bond for equity, Convertible Bonds pay a lower rate of interest. 2. The option to convert to equity affords the bondholder a degree of indexation to rising consumer prices.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is subtle — equity indexation link is counterintuitive; students may reject it thinking bonds don't hedge inflation.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Statement 1 is straightforwardly true (conversion option = lower coupon). Statement 2 refers to equity's inflation-hedging property transferred to the bondholder via conversion option.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Convertible bonds pay lower interest because conversion option has intrinsic value
     - Equity acts as inflation hedge; conversion option gives bondholder indirect inflation protection

> Consider the following statements :  I. India accounts for a very large portion of all equity option contracts traded globally thus exhibiting a great boom. II. India's stock market has grown rapidly in the recent past even overtaking Hong Kong's at some point of time. III. There is no regulatory body either to warn the small investors about the risks of options trading or to act on unregistered financial advisors in this regard.  Which of the statements given above are correct?

  ✅ A) I and II only
     B) II and III only
     C) I and III only

**[Q3389]** [2017]
> Who among the following can join the National Pension System (NPS)?

     A) Resident Indian citizens only
     B) Persons of age from 21 to 55 only
  ✅ C) All State Government employees joining the services after the date of notification by the respective State Governments
     D) All Central Government employees including those of Armed Forces joining the services on or after 1st April, 2004

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option D sounds partially right (all central government employees) but Armed Forces are excluded from NPS for a specific reason. Option A (resident Indians only) is wrong as NRIs can join NPS.
  💡 **Eliminate:** NPS is open to all State Government employees joining after the notification date by respective State Governments — answer is C. Central government employees join from April 1, 2004 but Armed Forces are excluded.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - NPS applicable to Central Govt employees from April 1, 2004 (except Armed Forces)
     - State Govt employees join based on state notification dates
     - NPS is also open to private sector and self-employed individuals

**[Q1327]** [2003]
> Debenture holder of a company are its

     A) Shareholders
  ✅ B) Creditors
     C) Debtors
     D) Directors

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Shareholders own equity; debenture holders are debt holders — candidates sometimes confuse creditors with shareholders.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Debenture = debt instrument; holders are creditors of the company — they lend money at fixed interest and have priority over shareholders in liquidation.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Debenture holders = creditors (lend money); shareholders = owners (equity)
     - Debentures: fixed interest, no voting rights, priority in liquidation over equity

**[Q193]** [1996]
> Which one of the following is correct regarding stabilization and structural adjustment as two components of the new economic policy adopted in India ?

     A) Stabilization is a gradual, multi-step process while structural adjustment is a quick adaptation process
  ✅ B) Structural adjustment is a gradual, multi-step process, while stabilization is a quick adaptation process
     C) Stabilization and structural adjustment are very similar and complementary policies. It is difficult to separate one from the other
     D) Stabilization mainly deals with a set of policies which are to be implemented by the Central Government while structural adjustment is to be set in motion by the State Governments

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option A (stabilization gradual, structural adjustment quick) reverses the correct relationship. Option C (they are inseparable) is partially true but not the best description.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Stabilization = short-term macroeconomic adjustment (reducing fiscal/current account deficit quickly). Structural adjustment = long-term reforms of the economy (gradual, multi-step process). Correct: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Stabilization — quick measures: cut deficit, control inflation, devalue currency (IMF prescription)
     - Structural adjustment — gradual reforms: trade liberalization, privatization, labour reforms

**[Q303]** [1997]
> The average rate of domestic savings (gross) for the Indian economy is currently estimated to be in the range of

     A) 15 to 20 per cent
  ✅ B) 20 to 25 per cent
     C) 25 to 30 per cent
     D) 30 to 35 per cent

  ⚠️ **Trap:** This is a time-specific factual question (1997 data). The 'currently estimated' in 1997 = 20-25%; by 2007 it had risen to ~32-34%. Modern students applying current data pick option D.
  💡 **Eliminate:** This question is dated (1997); the answer B (20-25%) reflected India's savings rate in the late 1990s. Modern savings rate ~30-35% (option D). Flag as historically-contextual — note year before answering.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - India's gross domestic savings rate 1997: ~20-24% of GDP
     - India's savings rate peaked ~36% in 2008, now ~30%
     - High savings rate supports investment and growth

### GS3

**[2017]** Q1. Among several factors for India's potential growth, savings rate is the most effective one. Do you agree? What are the other factors available for growth potential?
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHY | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Sources of economic growth, Role of savings, Inclusive growth drivers

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** While savings rate is a critical enabler of investment-led growth, India's growth potential rests on a constellation of factors beyond savings alone.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Savings rate importance: high domestic savings reduce dependence on foreign capital and finance investment
      • But limitations: savings alone cannot drive growth without efficient financial intermediation and investment climate
      • Other factors: FDI, TFP improvements, infrastructure quality, human capital, institutional quality
      • Demographic dividend: India's young workforce can drive consumption and labor supply simultaneously
      • GST, IBC reforms, ease of doing business improve investment efficiency independent of savings
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Savings is necessary but not sufficient — sustainable growth requires synergy between savings, investment, human capital and institutional reforms.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Economy, Banking sector, Infrastructure
  🌐 Contemporary: India's savings rate decline post-COVID raises concerns about long-term investment sustainability.
