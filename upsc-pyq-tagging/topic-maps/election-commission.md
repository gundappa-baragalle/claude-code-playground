**[Q3450]** [2017]
> Consider the following statements:  1. The Election Commission of India is a five-member body. 2. Union Ministry of Home Affairs decides the election schedule for the conduct of both general elections and bye-elections. 3. Election Commission resolves the disputes relating to splits/mergers of recognised political parties.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 2 only
     C) 2 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** All three statements are traps: ECI is not five-member (it is 1+2 = 3 currently), Home Ministry does not decide election schedule (ECI does), and only statement 3 about party splits/mergers is correct.
  💡 **Eliminate:** ECI is a multi-member body (CEC + 2 ECs), not fixed at five. ECI — not Home Ministry — decides election schedules. ECI does resolve disputes on party splits/mergers. Answer: statement 3 only.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - ECI structure: Chief Election Commissioner + up to 2 Election Commissioners (currently 3 total)
     - ECI independently decides election schedule — not the Home Ministry
     - ECI adjudicates intra-party disputes, splits, and mergers of recognised parties

**[Q1746]** [2006]
> Which one among the following Commissions was set up in pursuance of a definite provision under an Article of the Constitution of India?

     A) University Grants Commission
     B) National Human Rights Commission
  ✅ C) Election Commission
     D) Central Vigilance Commission

  ⚠️ **Trap:** All options — UGC, NHRC, Election Commission, CVC — seem like constitutional bodies since they are statutory bodies. Only EC has explicit constitutional mandate (Article 324).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Election Commission is established under Article 324 of the Constitution. UGC, NHRC, CVC are statutory bodies created by Parliament, not directly by the Constitution. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Election Commission — Article 324 of Constitution
     - UGC, NHRC, CVC — statutory bodies (no direct constitutional provision)

**[Q1381]** [2004]
> Consider the following tasks: 1. Superintendence, direction and conduct of free and fair elections. 2. Preparation of electoral rolls for all elections to the Parliament, State Legislatures and the Office of the President and the Vice-President. 3. Giving recognition to political parties and allotting election symbols to political parties and individuals contesting the election. 4. Proclamation of final verdict in the case of election disputes. Which of the above are the functions of the Election Commission of India?

  ✅ A) 1, 2 and 3
     B) 2, 3 and 4
     C) 1 and 3
     D) 1, 2 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Task 4 (election dispute verdict) sounds like it should be ECI's role but final verdict on election disputes rests with High Courts/Supreme Court, NOT the Election Commission.
  💡 **Eliminate:** ECI's functions include superintendence (1), electoral rolls (2), and party recognition/symbols (3); election DISPUTES are adjudicated by judiciary under Article 329 — not ECI.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Election disputes (election petitions) are decided by High Courts, not the Election Commission
     - ECI functions: Article 324 — superintendence, direction, and control of elections

**[Q1062]** [2002]
> Consider the following statements with reference to India: 1. The Chief Election Commissioner and other Election Commissioners enjoy equal powers but receive unequal salaries 2. The Chief Election Commissioner is entitled to the same salary as in provided to a judge of the Supreme Court 3. The Chief Election Commissioner shall not be removed from his office except in like manner and on like grounds as a judge of the Supreme Court 4. The term of office of the Election Commissioner is five years from the date he assumes his office or till the day he attains the age of 62 years, whichever is earlier Which of these statements are correct?

     A) 1 and 2
  ✅ B) 2 and 3
     C) 1 and 4
     D) 2 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is false (all Election Commissioners enjoy equal powers AND equal salary); Statement 4 is false (term is 6 years or 65 years of age, not 5 years/62 years); students may incorrectly validate these.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Only statements 2 (CEC salary = Supreme Court judge) and 3 (removal same as SC judge) are correct — answer B (2 and 3); statement 1 is false (equal salary, not unequal); statement 4 is false (6 years or 65, not 5 years or 62).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - CEC and ECs: same powers AND same salary (= SC judge salary) — not unequal
     - CEC tenure: 6 years or until age 65, whichever earlier; removal: like SC judge (Parliamentary process)

**[Q57]** [1995]
> Which one of the following is correct In respect of the commencement of the election process in India ?

     A) The recommendation for election is made by the government and the notification for election is issued by the Election Commission
     B) The recommendation for election is made by the Election Commission and the notification for election is issued by the Home Ministry at the Centre and Home Departments in the States
  ✅ C) The recommendation for election is made by the Election Commission and the notification for election is issued by the President and Governors of the States concerned
     D) Both the exercises of making a recommendation for election and that of issuing a notification in respect of it are done by the Election Commission

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option D (both done by ECI) seems modern and empowering but is incorrect. Students confuse the role of EC in recommending vs the constitutional role of President/Governors in issuing notifications.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Under Articles 324/85/174, the President (for Lok Sabha) and Governors (for State assemblies) issue election notifications on the advice of the Election Commission. Correct: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Election Commission recommends dates; President/Governors issue formal notification
     - Article 324 — ECI superintendence; Articles 85 & 174 — dissolution and election summons

**[Q1246]** [2003]
> Under which Article of the Indian Constitution did the President make a reference to the Supreme Court to seek the Court’s opinion on the Constitutional validity of the Election Commission’s decision on deferring the Gujarat Assembly elections (in the year 2002)?

     A) Article 142
  ✅ B) Article 143
     C) Article 144
     D) Article 145

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Article 142 (complete justice) sounds like what President might use; Article 143 (advisory opinion) is less known, making students guess incorrectly.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Article 143 specifically allows the President to refer questions of law or fact of public importance to the SC for its opinion — this is the Presidential Reference provision.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 143 = Presidential Reference to Supreme Court for advisory opinion
     - The 2002 Gujarat election deferral controversy led to a Presidential Reference
     - SC's advice under Article 143 is not binding on the President

### GS2

**[2022]** Q15. Discuss the role of the Election Commission of India in the light of the evolution of the Model Code of Conduct.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Electoral integrity, Institutional autonomy, Democratic accountability

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Election Commission's evolution through the Model Code of Conduct exemplifies how a non-statutory instrument became a cornerstone of India's electoral integrity.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • MCC evolution: from 1960 Kerala model to nationwide application 1979 onwards
      • ECI's expanding interpretation of Article 324 to enforce MCC
      • MCC provisions: government largesse ban, campaign conduct, rallies
      • ECI's proactive role: de-recognition warnings, model code strictness in 2014/2019/2024 elections
      • Criticism: MCC not legally enforceable, complaint disposal delays
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The ECI's evolution through MCC reflects the maturation of electoral democracy, though statutory backing would strengthen its enforcement capacity.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: Polity, Constitutional Bodies, Elections
  🌐 Contemporary: Debates about ECI independence post new appointment process (2023 amendment) and MCC enforcement.

**[2018]** Q1. In the light of recent controversy regarding the use of Electronic Voting Machines (EVM), what are the challenges before the Election Commission of India to ensure the trustworthiness of elections in India?
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Challenges to Election Commission ensuring EVM trustworthiness, Institutional mechanisms for electoral integrity, Technology and democratic trust

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Electronic Voting Machine controversy has placed the Election Commission of India at the intersection of technology, democracy, and public trust, demanding robust responses to ensure credible elections.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • EVM tampering allegations: opposition parties and some technologists questioned security — ECI's challenge of managing perception
      • VVPAT solution: Voter Verified Paper Audit Trail introduced for paper-based verification — partial audit conducted
      • Technical challenges: ECI must demonstrate EVMs are standalone (not internet-connected) and tamper-proof
      • Administrative challenge: ensuring uniform, transparent EVM preparation, commissioning, storage across lakhs of units
      • Institutional credibility: independence of ECI from executive pressure is key to public confidence
      • International scrutiny: making EVM audit process transparent to domestic and international observers
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Trustworthy elections demand not just technically secure EVMs but transparent processes, robust audit mechanisms like 100% VVPAT verification, and a fiercely independent Election Commission that commands cross-party confidence.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 Polity, Democratic institutions, Electoral reforms
  🌐 Contemporary: EVM debate continues to be raised before every major election cycle in India.
