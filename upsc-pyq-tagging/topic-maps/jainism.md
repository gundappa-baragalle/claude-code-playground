**[Q2638]** [2013]
> Which of the following statements is/are applicable to Jain doctrine?  1. The surest way of annihilating Karma is to practice penance. 2. Every object, even the smallest particle has a soul. 3. Karma is the bane of the soul and must be ended.  Select the correct answer using the codes given below.

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may exclude statement 2 (every object has a soul) thinking it is too extreme, not realising it is the Jain doctrine of Jiva in all matter (sarvajiva).
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three statements correctly describe Jain doctrine: penance annihilates karma (1), anekantavada includes soul in all matter (2), karma is bondage to be ended (3). Answer is D (1, 2, 3).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Jain doctrine: every object (animate and inanimate) has a jiva (soul) — this is the concept of sarvajiva
     - Karma in Jainism is material particles binding the soul; penance (tapas) is the key to annihilating accumulated karma

**[Q2148]** [2009]
> Anekantavada is a core theory and philosophy of which one of the following ?

     A) Buddhism
  ✅ B) Jainism
     C) Sikhism
     D) Vaishnavism

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Buddhism also has concepts of multiple perspectives (middle path, relativity of views); Anekantavada is specifically the Jain philosophical doctrine of many-sidedness of reality.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Anekantavada (non-absolutism/many-sidedness of truth) is a core Jain philosophical doctrine; associated with Syadvada (conditional predication) — answer is B (Jainism).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Anekantavada: Jain doctrine that truth and reality are complex and multifaceted
     - Associated Jain doctrines: Syadvada (conditional predication), Nayavada (partial views)

**[Q4529]** [2023]
> "Souls are not only the property of animal and plant life, but also of rocks, running water and many other natural objects not looked on as living by other religious sects." The above statement reflects one of the core beliefs of which one of the following religious sects of ancient India?

     A) Buddhism
  ✅ B) Jainism
     C) Shaivism
     D) Vaishnavism

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Buddhism also has respect for living beings; students may confuse Jain animism (souls in rocks, water) with Buddhist ahimsa — the specific attribution of souls to non-living matter is distinctly Jain.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Jainism's doctrine of Anekantavada and animism holds that souls (jivas) exist in plants, animals, rocks, water and air — this radical animism is unique to Jainism, not Buddhism or Shaivism.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Jainism classifies all existence into Jiva (soul) and Ajiva (non-soul); even earth, water, fire, air and plants have jivas
     - This 'soul in all matter' concept makes Jainism one of the most radical ecological philosophies

**[Q4350]** [2022]
> With reference to Indian history, consider the following texts :  1. Nettipakarana 2. Parishishtaparvan 3. Avadanashataka 4. Trishashtilakshana Mahapurana  Which of the above are Jaina texts?

     A) 1, 2 and 3
  ✅ B) 2 and 4 only
     C) 1, 3 and 4
     D) 2, 3 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Nettipakarana and Avadanashataka are Pali/Buddhist texts; students may not know Parishishtaparvan and Trishashtilakshana Mahapurana are Jaina.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Nettipakarana = Pali Buddhist text; Avadanashataka = Sanskrit Buddhist text. Parishishtaparvan (Hemachandra) and Trishashtilakshana Mahapurana are Jaina — only options 2 and 4.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Parishishtaparvan by Hemachandra is a major Jaina biographical text
     - Trishashtilakshana Mahapurana narrates lives of 63 Jaina great beings

**[Q3618]** [2018]
> With reference to the religious practices in India, the "Sthanakvasi" sect belongs to

     A) Buddhism
  ✅ B) Jainism
     C) Vaishnavism
     D) Shaivism

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The unfamiliar term 'Sthanakvasi' may lead students toward Buddhism or Shaivism; it is one of the sub-sects of Jainism (Shvetambara branch).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Sthanakvasi is a sect of Jainism (Shvetambara branch) that rejects idol worship and worships in ordinary halls (sthanaks) rather than temples — answer B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Sthanakvasi Jains are a subsect of Shvetambara Jains who oppose image worship and elaborate temple rituals
     - They are called Sthanakvasi because they use a 'sthanak' (hall) rather than a temple for worship

**[Q2468]** [2011]
> The Jain philosophy holds that the world is created and maintained by

  ✅ A) Universal Law
     B) Universal Truth
     C) Universal Faith
     D) Universal Soul

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Options B and C sound like Jain philosophy's emphasis on truth and faith, but Jain philosophy holds the world is self-sustaining and governed by Universal Law — not created by any god or soul.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Jainism is atheistic — the world operates by natural/universal law (Rta), not by a creator god, soul, or faith. Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Jainism believes the universe is eternal and governed by Universal Law, not created by any deity
     - Jain cosmology is based on natural order — no creator god

**[Q2108]** [2009]
> Mahamastakabhisheka, a great religious event, is associated with and done for who of the following?

  ✅ A) Bahubali
     B) Buddha
     C) Mahavir
     D) Nataraja

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse Mahamastakabhisheka with a Buddhist or Hindu ceremony — it is the grand anointing ceremony specific to the Jain deity Bahubali (Gomateshwara) at Shravanabelagola.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Mahamastakabhisheka is the Jain head-anointing ceremony for Bahubali's statue at Shravanabelagola (Karnataka), held once every 12 years. Answer is A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Mahamastakabhisheka is the anointing ceremony of Gomateshwara (Bahubali) at Shravanabelagola
     - Held once every 12 years — one of the largest religious gatherings in India
     - Bahubali was the son of Rishabhanatha (first Jain Tirthankara)

**[Q1375]** [2004]
> With reference to ancient Jainism, which one of the following statements is correct?

     A) Jainism was spread in South India under the leadership of Sthalabahu.
     B) The Jainas who remained under the leadership of Bhadrabahu were called Shvetambaras after the Council held at Pataliputra.
  ✅ C) Jainism enjoyed the patronage of the Kalinga king Kharavela in the first century BC.
     D) In the initial stage of Jainism, the Jainas worshipped images unlike Buddhists.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Options A and B confuse Digambara/Shvetambara split details; the Bhadrabahu group were Digambaras, not Shvetambaras — an easy reversal trap.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Kharavela of Kalinga (1st century BC) is a well-documented Jain patron; eliminate A (Sthalabahu is incorrect — it was Bhadrabahu) and B (the group staying was Digambara, not Shvetambara).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Kharavela of Kalinga was a great patron of Jainism — mentioned in Hathigumpha inscription
     - Shvetambara-Digambara split occurred after the Council at Pataliputra under Sthulabhadra

**[Q1258]** [2003]
> Consider the following statements 1. Vardhamana Mahavira’s mother was the daughter of Lichchhavi chief Chetaka. 2. Goutama Buddha’s mother was a princess from the Kosha-lan dynasty. 3. Parshvanatha, the 23rd Tirthankara, belonged to Banaras. Which of these statements is/are correct?

     A) Only 1
     B) Only 2
  ✅ C) 2 and 3
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 about Mahavira's mother being daughter of Lichchhavi chief Chetaka is often confused — Mahavira's mother was Trishala, sister (not daughter) of Chetaka. Statement 2 about Buddha's mother being from Kosala dynasty is also tricky.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Buddha's mother Mayadevi was from the Koliyan clan, sometimes linked to Kosala region. Parshvanatha was from Varanasi (Banaras) — Statement 3 is correct. Statement 2 is also considered correct — choose C (2 and 3).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Mahavira's mother Trishala was sister (not daughter) of Chetaka
     - Parshvanatha (23rd Tirthankara) was from Varanasi/Banaras
     - Buddha's mother Mayadevi was a Kosalan princess

**[Q833]** [2000]
> Assertion (A) : The emphasis of Jainism on non-violence (ahimsa) prevented agriculturalists from embracing Jainism. Reason (R) : Cultivation involved killing of insects and pests.

  ✅ A) Both A and R are true, and R is the correct explanation of A
     B) Both A and R are true, but R is not a correct explanation of A
     C) A is true, but R is false
     D) A is false, but R is true

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The assertion-reason format requires checking both truth and causal linkage; both A and R are true and R logically explains why farming (involving pest killing) conflicted with ahimsa.
  💡 **Eliminate:** A is true (Jainism's ahimsa did limit its spread among farmers); R is true (cultivation does involve killing insects); R correctly explains A — killing pests/insects during farming violates ahimsa — answer A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Jain ahimsa extends to all living beings including insects — strict adherents avoid farming for this reason
     - This explains why Jains traditionally took to trade and commerce rather than agriculture

**[Q195]** [1996]
> Which one of the following is not a part of early Jaina literature ?

  ✅ A) Therigatha
     B) Acarangasutra
     C) Sutrakritanga
     D) Brihatkalpasutra

  ⚠️ **Trap:** All options sound like ancient religious texts. Therigatha belongs to the Pali Buddhist canon — it is Buddhist literature (Theravada), not Jain.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Therigatha (Verses of the Elder Nuns) is a Buddhist Pali text. Acarangasutra, Sutrakritanga, and Brihatkalpasutra are all part of the Jain Agamas (canonical texts). Correct: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Therigatha — Buddhist Pali canon; earliest collection of women's spiritual poetry
     - Jain Agamas include Acarangasutra (oldest Jain text), Sutrakritanga, Brihatkalpasutra

**[Q25]** [1995]
> The concept of Anuvrata was advocated by

     A) Mahayana Buddhism
     B) Hinayana Buddhism
  ✅ C) Jainism
     D) the Lokayata school

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Anuvrata means 'small vows' and candidates might confuse it with Buddhist vows; Anuvrata is specifically Jain—lesser vows for lay followers vs Mahavrata for monks.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Anuvrata = small/minor vows prescribed for Jain lay followers (not monks); contrast with Mahavrata (great vows for monks); select C (Jainism).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Anuvrata: five minor vows for Jain householders/laity
     - Mahavrata: five major vows for Jain monks (more strictly observed)
     - Anuvrata movement by Acharya Tulsi in 20th century revived these principles
