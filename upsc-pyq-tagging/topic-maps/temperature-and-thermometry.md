**[Q3]** [1995]
> The normal temperature of human body on the Kelvin scale is

     A) 280
     B) 290
     C) 300
  ✅ D) 310

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students confuse the conversion: some add 273 to Fahrenheit (98.6°F), others mistakenly use 37+260=297 or 37+270=307, arriving at 300K.
  💡 **Eliminate:** K = °C + 273. Normal body temperature = 37°C. 37 + 273 = 310K. Eliminate 300K (uses 27°C=room temp), 280K and 290K (too cold).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - K = °C + 273.15
     - Normal body temperature = 37°C = 310K = 98.6°F
     - Room temperature ≈ 27°C = 300K
