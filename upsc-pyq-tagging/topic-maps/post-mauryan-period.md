**[Q3429]** [2017]
> The painting of Bodhisattva Padmapani is one of the most famous and oft-illustrated paintings at

  ✅ A) Ajanta
     B) Badami
     C) Bagh
     D) Ellora

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Ellora has famous cave paintings too, but Bodhisattva Padmapani is specifically and most famously associated with Ajanta Cave 1. Candidates may confuse Ajanta with Ellora.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Bodhisattva Padmapani painting is in Cave 1 at Ajanta — one of the most celebrated examples of ancient Indian painting. Answer is A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Ajanta Caves: Buddhist cave paintings, 2nd century BCE to 6th century CE
     - Bodhisattva Padmapani in Cave 1 — UNESCO World Heritage Site
     - Padmapani = lotus-bearer Bodhisattva

**[Q3239]** [2016]
> What is/are common to the two historical places known as Ajanta and Mahabalipuram?  1. Both were built in the same period. 2. Both belong to the same religious denomination. 3. Both have rock-cut monuments.  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1 and 2 only
  ✅ B) 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
     D) None of the statements given above is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is a strong trap — candidates may think both are ancient rock-cut sites from a similar era, but Ajanta is Buddhist (2nd century BCE–5th CE) while Mahabalipuram is Hindu/Pallava (7th–8th century CE).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Both are rock-cut monuments (statement 3 is correct). Statement 1 is false — different periods. Statement 2 is false — Ajanta is Buddhist, Mahabalipuram is Hindu. Answer is B (3 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Ajanta is Buddhist rock-cut (2nd BCE–5th CE); Mahabalipuram is Hindu Pallava (7th–8th CE)
     - Both are UNESCO World Heritage Sites with rock-cut architecture

**[Q1762]** [2006]
> With reference to the invaders in ancient India, which one of the following is the correct chronological order?

  ✅ A) Greeks-Sakas-Kushans
     B) Greeks-Kusans-Sakas
     C) Sakas-Greeks-Kushans
     D) Sakas-Kushans-Greeks

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may mix up Kushans and Sakas who were contemporaneous in parts; Sakas arrived before Kushans but after Greeks in India.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Chronological order: Greeks (post-Alexander, 4th–2nd BCE) → Sakas (2nd–1st BCE) → Kushans (1st–3rd CE); so Greeks—Sakas—Kushans ascending chronologically.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Greeks (Indo-Greeks) entered India after Alexander; Menander most notable
     - Sakas (Scythians) arrived in 2nd–1st BCE; Kushans followed from 1st CE onward

**[Q1266]** [2003]
> Consider the following statements: 1. The last Mauryan ruler, Brihadratha was assassinated by his commander-in-chief, Pushyamitra Sunga. 2. The last Sunga king, Devabhuti was assassinated by his Brahmana minister Vasudeva Kanva who usurped the throne 3. The last ruler of the Kanva dynasty was deposed by the Andhras. Which of these statements is/are correct?

     A) 1 and 2
     B) Only 2
     C) Only 3
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may think the Andhras replaced the Sunga dynasty directly, not the Kanva dynasty. The sequence Maurya → Sunga → Kanva → Satavahana is frequently confused.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three statements are correct: Brihadratha (Maurya) killed by Pushyamitra; Devabhuti (Sunga) killed by Vasudeva Kanva; Kanva dynasty ended by Andhra/Satavahanas. Answer: all 1, 2 and 3.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Pushyamitra Sunga killed Brihadratha (last Maurya) and founded Sunga dynasty
     - Vasudeva Kanva killed Devabhuti (last Sunga) and founded Kanva dynasty

**[Q910]** [2001]
> Which one of the following pairs is correctly matched ?

     A) Harappan Civilisation ... Painted Grey Ware
  ✅ B) The Kushans ... Gandhara School of Art
     C) The Mughals ... Ajanta Paintings
     D) The Marathas ... Pahari School of Painting

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Ajanta Paintings are Gupta-era (not Mughal); Painted Grey Ware is Iron Age (not Harappan); Pahari School belongs to Rajput hill kingdoms (not Marathas). Kushans and Gandhara art is the correct match.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Gandhara School of Art (Greco-Buddhist) flourished under Kushans in Gandhara (NW India/Afghanistan) — the most distinctive art-patron relationship in ancient India. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Gandhara art: Kushan period, Greco-Buddhist style, depicted Buddha in human form
     - Ajanta Paintings: Gupta/Vakataka era, not Mughal
     - Painted Grey Ware: associated with later Vedic/Iron Age, not Harappa

**[Q240]** [1996]

     A) Maurya sculptures
     B) Mathura sculptures

  ✅ D) Gandhara sculptures.

**[Q2595]** [2013]
> With reference to the history of Indian rock-cut architecture, consider the following statements:  1. The caves at Badami are the oldest surviving rock-cut caves in India. 2. The Barabar rock-cut caves were originally made for Ajivikas by Emperor Chandragupta Maurya. 3. At Ellora, caves were made for different faiths.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
  ✅ C) 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 (Badami caves oldest) is wrong — Barabar caves (3rd century BCE) predate Badami (6th century CE). Statement 2 (Barabar made for Ajivikas by Chandragupta Maurya) is wrong — it was Ashoka who donated them.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Only statement 3 is correct: Ellora has Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain caves. Badami caves are not oldest; Barabar was donated by Ashoka (not Chandragupta). Answer is C (3 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Barabar rock-cut caves (Bihar): donated to Ajivikas by Emperor Ashoka, not Chandragupta Maurya
     - Ellora caves: 34 caves of Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain faiths (6th–11th century CE)

**[Q2573]** [2012]
> With reference to the guilds (Shreni) of ancient India that played a very important role in the country's economy, which of the following statements is/are correct?  1. Every guild was registered with the central authority of the State and the king was the chief administrative authority on them. 2. The wages, rules of work, standards and prices were fixed by the guild, 3. The guild had judicial powers over its own members.  Select the correct answer using the codes given below:

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 3 only
  ✅ C) 2 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is false — guilds were NOT directly registered with the central state; they were largely autonomous bodies. The king did not directly control them. Statement 3 is correct — guilds had internal judicial powers.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Statement 2 (guilds fixed wages, standards, prices) and Statement 3 (judicial powers over members) are correct. Statement 1 is wrong — guilds were autonomous, not state-registered. Answer: C (2 and 3 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Ancient Indian guilds (Shreni) were autonomous economic organizations
     - Guilds had own rules (guild laws), could adjudicate member disputes
     - Guilds fixed wages, working standards, and commodity prices

  ✅ A) Greeks
     B) Shakas

     D) Mughals

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Shakas and Parthians also established foreign rule in India; the distinction is that Greeks first introduced the specific institution of military governorship (satrapy system).

### GS1

**[2019]** Q1. Highlight the Central Asian and Greco-Bactrian elements in the Gandhara art.
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Hellenistic influence on Indian art, Cultural synthesis in Gandhara

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Gandhara art represents one of the most remarkable cultural syntheses in world history, where Greco-Bactrian artistic traditions merged with Buddhist iconographic needs to create a distinct school.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Greek/Hellenistic elements: wavy hair, toga-like drapery, realistic facial features, contrapposto posture in Buddha sculptures
      • Central Asian elements: use of stucco, local stone (schist), narrative relief panels influenced by Parthian and Scythian styles
      • Buddha depicted in human form for the first time (vs earlier aniconic tradition)
      • Geographical context: Kushana Empire's crossroads position between Rome, China, India facilitated this synthesis
      • Bodhisattva figures modelled on Greco-Roman deities
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Gandhara art exemplifies India's historical openness to external influences, converting foreign aesthetics into distinctly Indian spiritual expression.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 Indian Culture — art and architecture, Ancient India — Kushana Empire
  🌐 Contemporary: Gandhara heritage sites in Afghanistan/Pakistan face destruction by extremist groups, a UNESCO concern.

**[2014]** Q2. Gandhara sculpture owed as much to the Romans as to the Greeks. Explain.
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHY | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Cultural synthesis in ancient India, Hellenistic influence on Indian art

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Gandhara art represents a unique cultural synthesis where Buddhist spirituality was expressed through the artistic vocabulary of the Greco-Roman world.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Greek influence: idealized human proportions, wavy hair (Apollo-like Buddha), facial features from Hellenistic tradition
      • Roman influence: toga-like folds in drapery (contrapposto posture), acanthus leaf decorative motifs
      • Context: Indo-Greek kings (Menander), Kushana patronage, Silk Route cultural exchange facilitated this fusion
      • Distinction from Mathura school: Gandhara uses grey schist stone, foreign techniques; Mathura uses red sandstone, indigenous
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Gandhara sculpture's Greco-Roman Buddhist synthesis stands as enduring testimony to ancient India's cosmopolitan artistic culture at the crossroads of civilizations.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 Art & Culture, GS1 Ancient History, IR — India's civilizational links
