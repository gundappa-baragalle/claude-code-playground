### 🟡 MEDIUM Priority — Full Options

**[Q3051]** [2015]
> The Genetic Engineering Appraisal Committee is constituted under the

     A) Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006
     B) Geographical Indications of Goods (Registration and Protection) Act, 1999
  ✅ C) Environment (Protection) Act, 1986
     D) Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Food Safety and Standards Act seems plausible as GEAC deals with GM food crops; Biological Diversity Act is also associated with biodiversity and genetic resources.
  💡 **Eliminate:** GEAC is constituted under the Environment (Protection) Act 1986 — it is an environment regulation body, not a food safety or biodiversity body.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - GEAC (Genetic Engineering Appraisal Committee) constituted under Environment Protection Act 1986
     - GEAC approves large-scale trials and environmental release of genetically engineered organisms
     - Under Ministry of Environment, Forest & Climate Change

**[Q2555]** [2012]
> What are the reasons for the people's resistance to the introduction of Bt brinjal in India?  1. Bt brinjal has been created by inserting a gene from a soil fungus into its genome. 2. The seeds of Bt brinjal are terminator seeds and therefore, the farmers have to buy the seeds before every season from the seed companies. 3. There is an apprehension that the consumption of Bt brinjal may have adverse impact on health. 4. There is some concern that the introduction of Bt brinjal may have adverse effect on the biodiversity.  Select the correct answer using the codes given below:

     A) 1, 2 and 3 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
  ✅ C) 3 and 4 only
     D) 1, 2, 3 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is false — Bt brinjal gene comes from the bacterium Bacillus thuringiensis (not a soil fungus). Statement 2 about terminator seeds is also incorrect for Bt brinjal specifically.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Bt brinjal concerns: health impact (statement 3) and biodiversity threat (statement 4) are valid concerns. Statements 1 (soil fungus — wrong, it's soil bacteria Bt) and 2 (terminator seeds — not applicable here) are incorrect. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Bt brinjal contains Cry1Ac gene from Bacillus thuringiensis (soil bacterium, not fungus)
     - India put moratorium on Bt brinjal in 2010 citing health and biodiversity concerns

### GS3

**[2014]** Q11. Can overuse and free availability of antibiotics without Doctor's prescription, be contributors to the emergence of drug-resistant diseases in India? What are the available mechanisms for monitoring and control? Critically discuss the various issues involved.
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Public health regulation, Drug-resistant diseases, Pharmaceutical governance

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The free availability and overuse of antibiotics without prescription has accelerated the emergence of drug-resistant bacteria, posing a serious public health crisis.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Drivers: self-medication culture, unregulated pharmacy sales, subtherapeutic use in livestock (veterinary overuse)
      • Scale of problem: India accounts for large share of global antibiotic consumption; high AMR burden
      • Drug-resistant TB, CRE, MRSA increasingly common in Indian hospitals
      • Monitoring: ICMR's AMRSN (AMR Surveillance Network), WHO GLASS network
      • Control: Schedule H1 classification restricts some antibiotics; enforcement weak
      • Way forward: stricter regulation, awareness campaigns, antibiotic stewardship in hospitals
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Combating drug-resistant diseases requires a multi-sectoral One Health approach addressing human, animal, and environmental antibiotic use simultaneously.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Science & Technology, GS2 – Health, GS3 – Agriculture
  🌐 Contemporary: COVID-19 secondary bacterial infections drove antibiotic overuse, worsening AMR; WHO's global AMR action plan is urgent.
