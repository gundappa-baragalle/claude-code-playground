**[Q4746]** [2024]
> What are the duties of the Chief of Defence Staff (CDS) as Head of the Department of Military Affairs ?  1. Permanent Chairman of Chiefs of Staff Committee 2. Exercise military command over the three Service Chiefs 3. Principal Military Advisor to Defence Minister on all tri-service matters  Select the correct answer using the code given below :

     A) 1, 2 and 3
     B) 1 and 2 only
     C) 2 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 1 and 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 says CDS exercises military command over Service Chiefs — this is the biggest trap; CDS is NOT in the chain of command over Service Chiefs operationally.
  💡 **Eliminate:** CDS is Permanent Chairman of COSC (1 correct) and Principal Military Advisor to Defence Minister on tri-service matters (3 correct). CDS does NOT exercise operational command over Service Chiefs (2 wrong). Answer is D (1 and 3).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - CDS created in January 2020 is Permanent Chairman of Chiefs of Staff Committee (COSC)
     - CDS is Principal Military Advisor to Defence Minister on tri-service matters but NOT in operational chain of command over individual Service Chiefs

**[Q4747]** [2024]
> Operations undertaken by the Army towards upliftment of the local population in remote areas to include addressing of their basic needs is called :

     A) Operation Sankalp
     B) Operation Maitri
  ✅ C) Operation Sadbhavana
     D) Operation Madad

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Operation Maitri (Nepal earthquake relief) and Operation Sankalp (Gulf safety) sound like humanitarian civil operations — Operation Sadbhavana is the specific term for army's civil outreach in J&K and NE.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Operation Sadbhavana is the Indian Army's long-running humanitarian initiative for upliftment of local population in remote/disturbed areas — primarily J&K and Northeast.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Operation Sadbhavana launched in 1998 for civil outreach in J&K; extended to NE states
     - It covers education, health, women empowerment, and infrastructure in remote conflict-affected areas

**[Q4573]** [2023]
> With reference to Home Guards, consider the following statements :  1. Home Guards are raised under the Home Guards Act and Rules of the Central Government. 2. The role of the Home Guards is to serve as an auxiliary force to the police in maintenance of internal security. 3. To prevent infiltration on the international border/coastal areas, the Border Wing Home Guards Battalions have been raised in some States.  How many of the above statements are correct?

     A) Only one
  ✅ B) Only two
     C) All three
     D) None

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Home Guards Act is a Central Act in template but administered by States — candidates may confuse central vs state jurisdiction.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Home Guards are raised under State Home Guards Acts (not Central), thus Statement 1 is wrong; Statements 2 and 3 are correct — hence 'only two' is right.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Home Guards raised under State Acts, not Central — Statement 1 is incorrect
     - Border Wing Home Guards assist in coastal/international border security

**[Q4738]** [2024]
> Which of the following is/are correctly matched in terms of equivalent rank in the three services of Indian Defence forces ?   Army - Airforce - Navy  1. Brigadier - Air Commodore - Commander 2. Major General - Air Vice Marshal - Vice Admiral 3. Major - Squadron Leader - Lieutenant Commander 4. Lieutenant Colonel - Group Captain - Captain  Select the correct answer using the code given below :

     A) 1 and 4
     B) 1 and 3
     C) 2, 3 and 4
  ✅ D) 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Pair 1 (Brigadier-Air Commodore-Commander) is incorrect as Army Commander = Navy Commodore, not Commander; Pair 2 incorrect as Major General ≠ Vice Admiral.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Only Pair 3 (Major-Squadron Leader-Lieutenant Commander) is correctly matched; use Army Major as anchor — Squadron Leader in AF and Lt. Commander in Navy are correct equivalents.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Major (Army) = Squadron Leader (AF) = Lieutenant Commander (Navy)
     - Brigadier (Army) = Air Commodore (AF) = Commodore (Navy) — not Commander

**[Q4751]** [2024]
> Which of the following statements about 'Exercise Mitra Shakti-2023' are correct ?  1. This was a joint military exercise between India and Bangladesh. 2. It commenced in Aundh (Pune). 3. Joint response during counter-terrorism operations was a goal of this operation. 4. Indian Air Force was a part of this exercise.  Select the answer using the code given below :

     A) 1, 2 and 3
     B) 1, 2 and 4
     C) 1, 3 and 4
  ✅ D) 2, 3 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Exercise Mitra Shakti is between India and Sri Lanka (not Bangladesh); Statement 1 is the key error. Students may confuse it with India-Bangladesh exercise (Sampriti).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Mitra Shakti is India-Sri Lanka bilateral exercise. Statement 1 is wrong. Statements 2 (Pune, Aundh), 3 (counter-terrorism goal), 4 (IAF participation) are correct. Answer: D (2, 3, 4).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Exercise Mitra Shakti is India-Sri Lanka annual bilateral military exercise — NOT India-Bangladesh
     - India-Bangladesh military exercise is called 'Sampriti'; Mitra Shakti 2023 was held at Aundh, Pune

**[Q1975]** [2008]
> Match List-I with List-II and select the correct answer using the code given below the Lists (Service/Agency) | (Country) A. Foreign Intelligence Service | 1. Israel B. Ministry of State Security | 2. Britain C. Secret Intelligence Service | 3. China D. The Mossad | 4. Russia

     A) A-4, B-1, C-2, D-3
     B) A-2, B-3, C-4, D-1
  ✅ C) A-4, B-3, C-2, D-1
     D) A-2, B-1, C-4, D-3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse the foreign intelligence services of major powers — particularly swapping Russia (SVR) with Britain (MI6/SIS) or China (MSS).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Foreign Intelligence Service = SVR = Russia (4); Ministry of State Security = MSS = China (3); Secret Intelligence Service = MI6 = Britain (2); Mossad = Israel (1). Answer: C (A-4, B-3, C-2, D-1).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - SVR = Russia's Foreign Intelligence Service
     - MSS = China's Ministry of State Security
     - MI6/SIS = Britain's Secret Intelligence Service
     - Mossad = Israel's intelligence agency

**[Q1533]** [2005]
> Which pairs is not correctly matched?

     A) South Air Command : Thiruvananthapuram
     B) Eastern Naval Command : Visakhapatnam
  ✅ C) Armoured Corps Centre and School : Jabalpur
     D) Army Medical Corps Centre and School : Lucknow

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Armoured Corps Centre and School is in Ahmednagar (Maharashtra), not Jabalpur — Jabalpur is associated with Infantry School, causing confusion.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The incorrect pair is Armoured Corps Centre=Jabalpur; it is actually in Ahmednagar. Army Medical Corps=Lucknow is correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Armoured Corps Centre and School: Ahmednagar, not Jabalpur
     - Infantry School: Mhow (Madhya Pradesh)
