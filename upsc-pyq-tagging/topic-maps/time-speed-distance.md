**[Q2268]** [2010]
> Two trains leave New Delhi at the same time. One travels north at 60 kmph and the other travels south at 40 kmph. After how many hours will the trains be 150 km apart ?

  ✅ A) 3/2
     B) 4/3
     C) 3/4
     D) 15/2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Trains travel in opposite directions so their speeds add up; students may try to subtract speeds instead of adding them.
  💡 **Eliminate:** When objects move in opposite directions, relative speed = sum of speeds = 60+40=100 kmph; time = distance/relative speed = 150/100 = 3/2 hours.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Relative speed = sum of individual speeds when objects move in opposite directions
     - Distance = Relative Speed × Time

**[Q2274]** [2010]
> Running at a speed of 60 km per hour, a train passed through a 1-5 km long tunnel in two minutes. What is the length of the train ?

     A) 250 m
  ✅ B) 500 m
     C) 1000 m
     D) 1500 m

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates often forget to convert km/hr to m/s or confuse total distance (tunnel + train) with just tunnel length.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Distance covered = speed × time = 60 km/h × (2/60) h = 2 km = 2000 m. Train length = 2000 − 1500 = 500 m.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Total distance = tunnel + train length
     - Speed × time = total distance

**[Q2307]** [2010]
> A person travelled a distance of 50 km in 8 hours. He covered a part of the distance on food at the rate of 4 km per hour and a part on a bicycle at the rate of 10 km per hour. How much distance did he travel on foot?

     A) 10km
  ✅ B) 20km
     C) 30km
     D) 40km

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may set up two equations but make errors in signs; let foot distance=x and bicycle distance=50-x.
  💡 **Eliminate:** x/4 + (50-x)/10 = 8; multiply by 20: 5x + 2(50-x) = 160; 5x + 100 - 2x = 160; 3x = 60; x = 20 km on foot.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Distance on foot = 20 km; on bicycle = 30 km
     - Simple simultaneous equations: d=4t (foot), D=10T (bicycle), t+T=8, d+D=50

**[Q1866]** [2007]
> Amit starts from a point A and walks to another point B, and then returns from B to A by his car and thus takes a total time of 6 hours and 45 minutes. If he had driven both ways in his car, he would have taken 2 hours less. How long would it take for him to walk both ways?

     A) 7 hours 45 minutes
     B) 8 hours 15 minutes
     C) 8 hours 30 minutes
  ✅ D) 8 hours 45 minutes

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates rush to add 2 hours to 6h45m giving 8h45m without checking the logic properly.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Walk+Car = 6h45m; Car+Car = 6h45m - 2h = 4h45m → car one way = 2h22.5m; Walk one way = 6h45m - 2h22.5m = 4h22.5m; Walk both ways = 8h45m. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Total time (walk+drive) = 6h45m; drive both = 4h45m
     - One way drive = 2h22.5m; one way walk = 4h22.5m
     - Both ways walking = 8h45m

**[Q1681]** [2006]
> There are 8 equidistant points A, B, C, D, E, F, G and H in the clock wise direction on the periphery of a circle. In a time interval t, a person reaches from A to C with uniform motion while another person reaches the point E from the point B during the same time interval with uniform motion. Both the persons move in the same direction along the circumference of the circle and start at the same instant. How much time after the start, will the two persons meet each other?

     A) 4t
  ✅ B) 7t
     C) 9t
     D) Never

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse circular motion speeds and not correctly calculate relative velocities on the circle. The equidistant points setup requires careful arc-length speed calculation.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Person 1: covers 2 segments in time t (A to C). Person 2: covers 3 segments in time t (B to E). Speeds are in ratio 2:3. Find time to meet using relative speed = 1 segment/t. They meet after 7t. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 8 equidistant points on circle = 8 equal segments
     - Speed ratio = 2:3 (person 1 covers 2 units, person 2 covers 3 units per t)
     - Relative speed = 1 unit per t; gap = 7 units → meet at 7t

**[Q1531]** [2005]
> Aryan runs at a speed on 40 metre/ minute. Rahul follows him after an interval of 5 minutes and runs at a speed of 50 metre/minute. Rahul’s dog runs at a speed of 60 metre/minute and starts along with Rahul. The dog reaches Aryan and then comes back to Rahul, and continues to do so till Rahul reaches Aryan. What is the total distance covered by the dog?

     A) 600 metres
     B) 750 metres
     C) 980 metres
  ✅ D) 1200 metres

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The dog covers multiple back-and-forth trips; candidates mistakenly try to calculate each trip separately instead of using total time × dog speed.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Find time for Rahul to catch Aryan: gap=200m at closing speed 10m/min = 20 min. Dog speed × time = 60 × 20 = 1200 m.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Dog problem shortcut: total time × dog speed
     - Rahul catches Aryan in 20 minutes

**[Q1390]** [2004]
> Two cars X and Y start from two places A and B respectively which are 700 km apart at 9 a.m. Both the cars run at an average speed of 60 km/hr. Car X stops at 10 a.m. and again starts at 11 a.m. while the other-car Y continues to run without stopping. When do the two cars cross each other?

     A) 2 : 40 p.m.
  ✅ B) 3 : 20 p.m.
     C) 4 : 10 p.m.
     D) 4 : 20 p.m.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Car X stops for 1 hour (10-11 am) — students may forget to add this lost hour when calculating effective distance covered by X; treating both as continuously moving leads to wrong answer.
  💡 **Eliminate:** By 9am+t hours: X covers 60(t-1) km [stopped 1hr], Y covers 60t km; they meet when 60(t-1)+60t=700; 120t-60=700; 120t=760; t=6.33hrs ≈ 3:20pm.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - When two objects move toward each other, add their speeds for closure rate
     - Account for stop time: Car X effectively travels at 60km/h but loses 1 hour of travel

**[Q1204]** [2003]
> ‘A’ walks around a circular field at the rate of one round per hour while ‘B’ runs around it at the rate of six rounds per hour. They start in the same direction from the same point at 7.30 a.m. They shall first cross each other at

     A) 8.30 a.m.
     B) 8.10 a.m.
     C) 7.48 a.m.
  ✅ D) 7.42 a.m.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may calculate meeting time as 1 hour after start (8:30 am) thinking B gains 5 rounds per hour; actual meeting is when relative gain = 1 round.
  💡 **Eliminate:** B gains 5 rounds/hour over A. To lap A once (gain 1 round), time = 1/5 hour = 12 minutes. Start 7:30 am + 12 min = 7:42 am. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Relative speed for circular track: subtract speeds for same direction
     - Time to first crossing = 1/(speed difference in rounds/hour)

**[Q1334]** [2003]
> Total time taken by a person in going to a place by walking and returning on cycle is 5 hours 45 minutes. He would have gained 2 hours by cycling both ways. The time taken by him to walk both ways is

     A) 6 hours 45 minutes
  ✅ B) 7 hours 45 minutes
     C) 8 hours 15 minutes
     D) 8 hours 30 minutes

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse 'gained 2 hours by cycling both ways' with 'saves 2 hours per trip'. The gain of 2 hours is for both ways, meaning cycling one way saves 1 hour vs walking.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Walk+cycle = 5h45m = 23/4 hrs. Cycling both ways saves 2 hrs → cycle+cycle = 23/4 − 2 = 15/4 hrs. So cycle one way = 15/8 hrs; walk one way = 23/4 − 15/8 = 31/8 hrs. Walk both ways = 31/4 hrs = 7h45m. Answer B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Walk one way + cycle one way = 5h45min; gain 2hr by cycling both → walk both ways = 7h45min

**[Q1127]** [2002]
> A bus is moving with a speed of 30 km/hr ahead of a car with a speed of 50 km/hr. How many kilometers apart are they if it takes 15 minutes for the car to catch up with the bus ?

  ✅ A) 5 km
     B) 7.5 km
     C) 12.5 km
     D) 15 km

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse total distance from start vs gap between them at start, leading to calculating 7.5 km instead of 5 km.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Relative speed of car over bus = 50-30 = 20 km/hr. Time to catch = 15 min = 0.25 hr. Initial gap = 20 × 0.25 = 5 km.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Relative speed = speed difference for same-direction objects
     - Gap = relative speed × time

**[Q1185]** [2002]
> Two ladies simultaneously leave cities A and B connected by a straight road and travel towards each other. The first lady travels 2 km/hr faster than the second lady and reaches B one hour before the second lady reaches A. The two cities A and B are 24 km apart. How many kilometers does each lady travel in one hour?

     A) 5 km, 3 km
     B) 7 km, 5 km
  ✅ C) 8 km, 6 km
     D) 16 km, 14 km

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Setting up two equations is key. Speed difference=2; time difference=1 hour (first lady reaches 1 hour before second). Distance=24 km. Let speeds be v and v+2.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Let first lady's speed=v+2, second=v. Time for first: 24/(v+2); for second: 24/v. Time difference: 24/v − 24/(v+2) = 1. Solving: 24(v+2−v)/(v(v+2))=1 → 48=v²+2v → v²+2v−48=0 → (v+8)(v−6)=0 → v=6. Speeds: 8 km/hr and 6 km/hr. Answer C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Speed equation: 24/v − 24/(v+2)=1 gives v=6, v+2=8; ladies travel 6 km/hr and 8 km/hr

**[Q710]** [1999]
> The average speed of a train in the onward journey is 25% more than that of the return journey. The train halts for one hour on reaching the destination. The total time taken for the complete to and fro journey is 17 hours covering a distance of 800 km. The speed of the train in the onward journey is

     A) 45 km per hour
     B) 47.06 km per hour
     C) 50.00 km per hour
  ✅ D) 56.25 km per hour

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The 1-hour halt and 25% speed difference require careful simultaneous equation setup; candidates often forget the halt when summing travel time.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Let return speed = s; onward speed = 1.25s. Time = 800/(1.25s) + 800/s + 1 = 17. Solve: 800/s(0.8+1)=16; 1.8×800/s=16; s=90; onward=1.25×90=112.5? Recalc: 16s=1440; s=90 km/h; onward = 112.5 — but answer options suggest D (56.25). Let return speed = v; onward = 1.25v; 800/1.25v + 800/v + 1 = 17 → 640/v + 800/v = 16 → 1440/v = 16 → v = 90; onward = 112.5. Answer D (56.25) may reflect different distance interpretation.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Speed-time-distance: D=ST; when speeds differ, set up equations for each leg separately
     - Remember to account for halts/stops in total time calculations

**[Q453]** [1998]
> One local and another express train were proceeding in the same direction on parallel tracks at 29 km/hour and 65 km/hour respectively. The driver of the former noticed that it took exactly 16 seconds for the faster train to pass by him. What is the length of the faster train?

     A) 60 m
     B) 120 m
  ✅ C) 160 m
     D) 240 m

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students forget to take relative speed (65-29=36 km/h) for same-direction motion and instead use total speed. Converting km/h to m/s is another error point.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Relative speed = 65-29 = 36 km/h = 10 m/s. Length = speed × time = 10 × 16 = 160 m. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Relative speed (same direction) = difference of speeds
     - Convert km/h to m/s: divide by 3.6

**[Q48]** [1995]
> A person travelled from one place to another at an average speed of 40 kilometres/hour and back to the original place at an average speed of 50 kilometres/hour. What is his average speed in kilometres/hour during the entire round-trip ?

     A) 45
     B) 20
  ✅ C) 400/9
     D) Impossible to find out unless the distance between the two places is known

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The naive answer of 45 (simple average) is extremely tempting. Students forget that average speed = total distance / total time, requiring the harmonic mean formula.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Eliminate 45 immediately as it is the arithmetic mean, not harmonic mean. Use 2*40*50/(40+50) = 4000/90 = 400/9.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Average speed for equal distances = 2ab/(a+b) (harmonic mean)
     - 400/9 ≈ 44.4 km/hr, always less than arithmetic mean

**[Q49]** [1995]
> A thief running at 8 km/hr is chased by a policeman whose speed is 10 km/hr. If the thief is 100 metres ahead of the policeman, then the time required for the policeman to catch the thief will be

     A) 2 minutes
     B) 6 minutes
     C) 10 minutes
  ✅ D) 3 minutes

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may try to convert 100 metres to km or confuse relative speed direction. The relative speed approach is straightforward but unit conversion (km/hr to minutes) trips many.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Relative speed = 10-8 = 2 km/hr. Gap = 100 m = 0.1 km. Time = 0.1/2 hr = 0.05 hr = 3 minutes. Eliminate all other options.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Relative speed when chasing = difference of speeds
     - 100 m = 0.1 km; 1 hr = 60 min

**[Q2342]** [2010]
> Three men start together to travel the same way around a circular track of 11 km. Their speeds are 4, 5-5 and 8 kmph respectively. When will they meet at the starting point for the first time?

     A) After 11 hours
     B) After 21 hours
  ✅ C) After 22 hours
     D) After 33 hours

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may try to find LCM of speeds (4, 5.5, 8) which are not integers, making direct LCM complex. The correct approach is finding time for each to complete one lap and then LCM of those times.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Time for one lap: A=11/4=2.75hr, B=11/5.5=2hr, C=11/8=1.375hr. LCM of 2.75, 2, 1.375 = LCM(11/4, 2, 11/8) = LCM(11,2,11)/GCD(4,1,8) = 22/1 = 22 hours. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Meeting time on circular track = LCM of individual lap times
     - LCM of fractions = LCM of numerators / GCD of denominators

**[Q1771]** [2006]
> A watch showed a time of fourteen minutes past nine (9 hrs and 14 mins). The positions of the hour-hand and the minute-hand of the watch are exactly interchanged. The new time shown by the watch is closest to which one of the following?

     A) Twelve minutes to three
     B) Thirteen minutes to three
  ✅ C) Fourteen minutes to three
     D) Fifteen minutes to three

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Clock hand interchange problems require careful calculation of exact positions; students may approximate incorrectly or confuse hour-hand and minute-hand positions.
  💡 **Eliminate:** At 9:14, minute hand is at 14×6°=84°; hour hand at 9×30°+14×0.5°=277°. After swap: minute hand at 277° → time ~46 min; hour hand at 84° → ~2hr 48min ≈ 14 min to 3.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Clock hand swap problems use position equations: minute=6° per min, hour=0.5° per min
     - 9:14 hands swapped gives approximately 2:46 = 14 minutes to 3

**[Q1464]** [2004]
> A and B start from the same point and in the same direction at 7 a.m. to walk around a rectangular field 400 m x 300 m. A and B walk at the rate of 3 km/hr and 2.5 km/hr respectively. How many times shall they cross each other if they continue to walk till 12 : 30 p.m?

     A) Not even once
  ✅ B) Once
     C) Twice
     D) Thrice

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may miscalculate the perimeter or the relative speed, or forget to account for them moving in the same direction (not opposite).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Perimeter = 2(400+300) = 1400m. Time = 5.5 hrs. A covers 5.5×3000 = 16500m; B covers 5.5×2500 = 13750m. Difference = 2750m = 1 full lap + 1350m. They cross once when A laps B by exactly 1 perimeter (14000m gap needed for one crossing... recalculate: gap = 2750m < 1400m × 2 = they cross once).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - For same-direction motion, crossing occurs when faster person completes exactly one more lap than slower
     - Relative speed = 3-2.5 = 0.5 km/hr; distance for one crossing = 1400m = 1.4 km; time = 1.4/0.5 = 2.8 hrs

**[Q1297]** [2003]
> A car travels the first one-third of a certain distance with a speed of 10 km/hr, the next one-third distance with a speed of 20 km/hr and the last one-third distance with a speed of 60 km/hr. The average speed of the car for the whole journey is

  ✅ A) 18 km/hr
     B) 24 km/hr
     C) 30 km/hr
     D) 36 km/hr

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Simple average of 10+20+60 gives 30 km/hr — a classic wrong answer. Average speed requires harmonic mean formula when equal distances are covered.
  💡 **Eliminate:** For equal distances with different speeds, average speed = 3/(1/v1 + 1/v2 + 1/v3) = 3/(1/10+1/20+1/60) = 3/(6/60+3/60+1/60) = 3/(10/60) = 18 km/hr. Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Harmonic mean formula for equal distance speed problems
     - Simple arithmetic mean gives wrong answer when distances are equal but speeds differ

**[Q1158]** [2002]
> Assume that the moon takes exactly 30 days to complete the cycle and also assume that it rises in the East exactly at 6.48 p.m. on the first day. On the fourth day, at what time will it rise?

     A) 8.24 p.m.
  ✅ B) 9.12 p.m.
     C) 10.00 p.m.
     D) 11.48 p.m.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse the moon's daily rising delay with simple arithmetic instead of calculating the lunar cycle delay per day (30-day cycle = 12 mins/day). Choosing 10 p.m. is a common error from rough estimation.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Moon completes one cycle in 30 days, so it rises ~48 min later each day (24 hrs/30 days × 60 min). On day 4, delay = 3 × 48 min = 144 min = 2 hrs 24 min added to 6:48 p.m. = 9:12 p.m.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Moon rises ~48 minutes later each day in a 30-day cycle
     - 3 days × 48 min = 144 min delay from day 1

**[Q962]** [2001]
> A person travels from X to Y a speed of 40 kmph and returns by increasing his speed by 50%. What is his average speed for both the trips ?

     A) 36 kmph
     B) 45 kmph
  ✅ C) 48 kmph
     D) 50 kmph

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may compute simple average (40+60)/2 = 50 kmph, forgetting that average speed for same distance must use harmonic mean — the simple average overestimates.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Return speed = 40 × 1.5 = 60 kmph; average speed = 2 × (40 × 60)/(40 + 60) = 4800/100 = 48 kmph — use harmonic mean formula for equal distances, not arithmetic mean.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Average speed for equal distances = 2v₁v₂/(v₁+v₂) — harmonic mean, not arithmetic mean
     - If speeds for equal distances are 40 and 60, average = 48, not 50

**[Q712]** [1999]
> If it is 10.00 a.m. IST., then what would be the local time at Shillong on 92°E longitude ?

     A) 9.38 a.m.
  ✅ B) 10.38 a.m.
     C) 10.22 a.m.
     D) 9.22 a.m.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse IST (82.5°E) with 92°E and calculate incorrectly; the key is that 92°E is east of IST meridian, so local time is ahead.
  💡 **Eliminate:** IST is based on 82.5°E. Shillong is at 92°E, which is 9.5° east of IST. Time difference = 9.5 × 4 = 38 minutes ahead. So 10:00 AM IST = 10:38 AM local — answer B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Each degree of longitude = 4 minutes of time
     - IST is based on 82.5°E; places east of this meridian have a local time ahead of IST

**[Q525]** [1998]

     A) 17° 30’ E
     B) 7° 30’ W

  ✅ D) 90° W

**[Q445]** [1997]
> A boat which has a speed of 5 km/hr in still water crosses a river of width 1 km along the shortest possible path in 15 minutes. The velocity of the river water in km/hr is

     A) 1
  ✅ B) 3
     C) 4
     D) 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may forget to use the Pythagorean relationship between boat speed, river speed and resultant speed for shortest path problems.
  💡 **Eliminate:** For shortest path crossing: resultant speed = width/time = 1km/0.25hr = 4 km/hr. Boat speed = 5, resultant = 4, so river speed = √(5²-4²) = √9 = 3 km/hr.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Shortest path crossing: boat aims perpendicular, uses Pythagorean theorem
     - v_river = √(v_boat² - v_resultant²)
