**[Q1935]** [2007]
> A and B can complete work together in 5 days. If A works at twice his speed and B at half of his speed, this work can be finished in 4 days. How many days would it take for A alone to complete the job?

  ✅ A) 10
     B) 12
     C) 15
     D) 18

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Setting up equations with A's and B's rates is straightforward but candidates often make errors when doubling/halving rates — must keep track of individual rates carefully.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Let A = a, B = b work/day; a+b = 1/5; 2a + b/2 = 1/4. Solve: from eq1, b = 1/5 - a; substitute: 2a + (1/5-a)/2 = 1/4 → 3a/2 = 1/4 - 1/10 = 3/20 → a = 1/10; A alone takes 10 days — answer A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Work-time problems: set up individual rates and use given conditions as simultaneous equations

**[Q579]** [1998]
> If 15 pumps of equal capacity can fill a tank in 7 days, then how many extra pumps will be required to fill the tank in 5 days?

  ✅ A) 6
     B) 7
     C) 14
     D) 21

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates sometimes solve for total pumps needed (21) and forget to subtract the original 15, giving 21 instead of 6.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Total pump-days = 15×7 = 105. Pumps needed for 5 days = 105/5 = 21. Extra pumps = 21 − 15 = 6.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Inverse proportion: pumps × days = constant
     - Extra pumps = new total − original

### 🟡 MEDIUM Priority — Full Options

**[Q1945]** [2008]
> Carpenter A can make a chair in 6 hours, carpenter B in 7 hours and carpenter C is 8 hours. If each carpenter works for 8 hours per day, how many chairs will be made in 21 days?

     A) 61
     B) 67
  ✅ C) 73
     D) 79

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often compute each carpenter's daily chairs as a fraction and add, but forget that chairs per day is fractional and must be accumulated over 21 days correctly.
  💡 **Eliminate:** A makes 8/6=4/3 chairs/day; B makes 8/7; C makes 8/8=1 chair/day. Total per day = 4/3 + 8/7 + 1 = 28/21 + 24/21 + 21/21 = 73/21. Over 21 days = 73 chairs. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Chairs per day = hours worked / hours per chair
     - Total chairs = (chairs/day) × number of days

### 🟢 LOW Priority — Full Options

**[Q1319]** [2003]
> Four different candles, which can last for 5 hours, 4 hours. 3 hours and 2 hours of burning, respectively, are lit in a room at the same instant and allowed to burn till such time that the three candles in the room get extinguished. The cost of burning each one of these candles is 75 paise per hour. The cost involved is:

     A) Rs. 2.75
     B) Rs. 3.75
  ✅ C) Rs. 9.75
     D) Rs. 12.50

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates confuse 'three candles get extinguished' with 'all four candles.' The 3-hour candle is the third to extinguish, so burning continues until the 3-hour mark; the 5-hour candle is still burning at that point.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Candles extinguish at 2, 3, 4 and 5 hours. Three get extinguished when the 4th candle (4-hour) goes out — but the 2h and 3h candles go first. All four burn for the shortest duration of 2h. Calculate cumulative burn: 4 candles × 2h + 3 candles × 1h + 2 candles × 1h + 1 candle × 1h = 8+3+2+1 = 14 hours total. 14 × 0.75 = Rs 10.50? Re-check: sum of burn-hours = 2+3+4+5 — wait, all burn from start until extinguished: 5+4+3+2=14 hours total burn. 14×0.75=10.50. Check answer C=9.75. Actually three candles extinguished means burn stops when 3rd candle dies (3-hour mark): only the 5h candle remains. Total burn until 3h: 4 candles×2h=8 (first 2h) + 3 candles×1h=3 (2h to 3h) = 11h. But 5h and 4h candles still burn... Re-read: 'allowed to burn till three candles get extinguished' = stop when 3rd candle goes out = at 4th hour mark (4h candle dies, already 2h and 3h extinguished). Burn: 4×2+3×1+2×1=8+3+2=13h. 13×0.75=9.75. Answer C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Three candles extinguished = at the 4-hour mark when the 4-hour candle burns out
     - Total burn-hours = 4×2 + 3×1 + 2×1 = 13 hours
