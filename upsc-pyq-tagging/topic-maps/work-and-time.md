**[Q4964]** [2007]
> A and B can complete work together in 5 days. If A works at twice his speed and B at half of his speed, this work can be finished in 4 days. How many days would it take for A alone to complete the job?

  ✅ A) 10
     B) 12
     C) 15
     D) 18

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Setting up equations with A's and B's rates is straightforward but candidates often make errors when doubling/halving rates — must keep track of individual rates carefully.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Let A = a, B = b work/day; a+b = 1/5; 2a + b/2 = 1/4. Solve: from eq1, b = 1/5 - a; substitute: 2a + (1/5-a)/2 = 1/4 → 3a/2 = 1/4 - 1/10 = 3/20 → a = 1/10; A alone takes 10 days — answer A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Work-time problems: set up individual rates and use given conditions as simultaneous equations

**[Q4637]** [1998]
> If 15 pumps of equal capacity can fill a tank in 7 days, then how many extra pumps will be required to fill the tank in 5 days?

  ✅ A) 6
     B) 7
     C) 14
     D) 21

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates sometimes solve for total pumps needed (21) and forget to subtract the original 15, giving 21 instead of 6.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Total pump-days = 15×7 = 105. Pumps needed for 5 days = 105/5 = 21. Extra pumps = 21 − 15 = 6.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Inverse proportion: pumps × days = constant
     - Extra pumps = new total − original

**[Q1309]** [2008] Carpenter A can make a chair in 6 hours, carpenter B in 7 hours and carpenter C is 8 hours. If each carpenter works for 8 hours per day, how many chairs will be made in 21 days?

     A) 61
     B) 67
  ✅ C) 73
     D) 79

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often compute each carpenter's daily chairs as a fraction and add, but forget that chairs per day is fractional and must be accumulated over 21 days correctly.
