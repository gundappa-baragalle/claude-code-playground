**[Q4576]** [2023]
> Consider the following statements :  1. Statement-I : Israel has established diplomatic relations with some Arab States. 2. Statement-II: The 'Arab Peace Initiative' mediated by Saudi Arabia was signed by Israel and Arab League.  Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

     A) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct and Statement-II is the correct explanation for Statement-I
     B) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct and Statement-II is not the correct explanation for Statement-I
  ✅ C) Statement-I is correct but Statement-II is incorrect
     D) Statement-I is incorrect but Statement-II is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement II is wrong: the Arab Peace Initiative (2002) was proposed by Saudi Arabia and endorsed by the Arab League — Israel never signed it. Students may confuse the Abraham Accords with the Arab Peace Initiative.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Statement I is correct (Israel normalized relations with UAE, Bahrain, Morocco, Sudan via Abraham Accords 2020); Statement II is wrong (Israel did not sign the Arab Peace Initiative). Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Abraham Accords (2020): Israel normalized ties with UAE, Bahrain, Morocco, Sudan
     - Arab Peace Initiative (2002): Saudi-proposed but never signed by Israel; offered normalization in exchange for Palestinian state

**[Q4381]** [2022]
> Which one of the following statements best reflects the issue with Senkaku Islands, sometimes mentioned in the news?

     A) It is generally believed that they are artificial islands made by a country around South China Sea.
  ✅ B) China and Japan engage in maritime disputes over these islands in East China Sea.
     C) A permanent American military base has been set up there to help Taiwan to increase its defence capabilities.
     D) Though International Court of Justice declared them as no man's land, some South-East Asian countries claim them.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Options A (artificial islands in South China Sea) and D (ICJ no-man's land) contain plausible-sounding misinformation; Senkaku/Diaoyu Islands dispute is a bilateral Japan-China matter.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Senkaku (Japan's name) / Diaoyu (China's name) islands are in the East China Sea and are the subject of an ongoing Japan-China sovereignty dispute — option B is correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Senkaku/Diaoyu Islands: administered by Japan but claimed by China and Taiwan; located in East China Sea near Okinawa
     - Dispute intensified after 2010; Chinese coast guard regularly operates in disputed waters

**[2024]** Q20. Discuss the geopolitical and geostrategic importance of Maldives for India with a focus on global trade and energy flows. Further also discuss how this relationship affects India's maritime security and regional stability amidst international competition?
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: IMPACT | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Maritime security, Neighbourhood diplomacy, Indian Ocean geopolitics

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Maldives occupies a critical geostrategic position on India's maritime frontier, and its relationship with India directly shapes Indian Ocean security dynamics.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Geopolitical importance: along critical SLOC connecting Gulf to Southeast Asia
      • Energy flow: India's oil imports pass through this region; Maldives proximity critical for energy security
      • Security: China's infrastructure investments ('string of pearls') threaten India's maritime dominance
      • Bilateral: India's 'Neighbourhood First' — financial assistance, vaccine diplomacy, hydrography cooperation
      • Challenge: Maldives' 'India Out' campaign (2023) and China tilt under Muizzu government
      • India's response: diplomatic recalibration, continuing aid and security cooperation
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India must sustain a strategic engagement with Maldives balancing security imperatives with economic generosity to counterbalance China's growing influence.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: IR, GS3: Internal security, Economy: Trade routes
  🌐 Contemporary: Muizzu government's India-Out stance (2023-24) and Indian military withdrawal from Maldives is directly relevant.

**[2022]** Q10. Do you think that BIMSTEC is a parallel organisation like the SAARC? What are the similarities and dissimilarities between the two? How are Indian foreign policy objectives realized by forming this new organisation?
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: VS | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Regional cooperation, India foreign policy, BIMSTEC vs SAARC

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** BIMSTEC has emerged as a functional alternative to the politically paralyzed SAARC, reflecting India's strategic pivot to sub-regional and Bay of Bengal connectivity.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Similarities: both South Asia-focused; overlapping members (India, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan, Sri Lanka); connectivity, trade, and economic cooperation goals
      • Dissimilarities: BIMSTEC includes Thailand and Myanmar (ASEAN bridge); SAARC includes Pakistan and Maldives; BIMSTEC is Bay of Bengal-centric; SAARC is broader South Asian
      • Why BIMSTEC: SAARC paralyzed by India-Pakistan tensions (2016 SAARC Summit cancelled); BIMSTEC functions without Pakistan vetoing India's agenda
      • India's interests: BIMSTEC advances Neighbourhood First + Act East policy simultaneously; Myanmar-Thailand link enables connectivity to SE Asia
      • Challenges: BIMSTEC lacks institutional depth; no permanent secretariat strength; overlapping with other regional bodies
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** BIMSTEC is not a parallel SAARC but a more agile and geographically purposeful platform — India's strategic preference reflects a pragmatic shift toward functional regionalism.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 IR, Neighbourhood First, Act East Policy
  🌐 Contemporary: 5th BIMSTEC Summit (2022) in Colombo adopted new charter and admitted new pillars of cooperation.

**[2020]** Q20. What is the significance of Indo-US defence deals over Indo-Russian defence deals? Discuss with reference to stability in the Indo-Pacific region.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: VS | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Defence procurement, Strategic autonomy, Indo-Pacific stability

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's defence partnerships with both the US and Russia reflect its strategic autonomy doctrine, though the comparative significance of each shapes its role in the Indo-Pacific.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Indo-US deals: advanced technology, interoperability, intelligence sharing (COMCASA, LEMOA, BECA)
      • Russia deals: cost-effectiveness, proven platforms (S-400, INS Chakra, MiG series)
      • Significance of US deals for Indo-Pacific: Quad, shared maritime surveillance
      • Russia deals: vulnerability to sanctions, spare parts dependency
      • India's challenge: balancing both without triggering CAATSA
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** While Indo-Russian defence ties remain historically significant, Indo-US deals are increasingly critical for India's strategic positioning in the Indo-Pacific.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: IR, GS3: Internal security and defence, Economy: Defence sector
  🌐 Contemporary: Ongoing Russia-Ukraine war and its impact on Indian defence supplies makes this extremely relevant.

**[2019]** Q9. 'The time has come for India and Japan to build a strong contemporary relationship, one involving global and strategic partnership that will have a great significance for Asia and the world as a whole.' Comment.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: WHY | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: India-Japan strategic partnership in Indo-Pacific context, Economic and technological cooperation, Shared democratic values and security concerns

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India and Japan, as the world's largest democracies in Asia, share strategic convergence that transcends bilateral interests into a partnership shaping regional and global order.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Strategic convergence: Both wary of China's assertiveness — Quad, FOIP alignment
      • Economic cooperation: Japan's ODA, bullet train project (Mumbai-Ahmedabad), semiconductor and defence tech transfer
      • Security partnership: India-Japan 2+2 format, ACSA (logistics agreement), joint exercises
      • Technology and innovation: cooperation in AI, quantum computing, clean energy
      • Asia's significance: combined GDP, demographic dividend, democratic models provide alternative to authoritarian development
      • Challenges: trade imbalance, pace of defence deals, Japan's pacifist constitution constraints
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** A robust India-Japan partnership anchored in shared values, complementary strengths, and strategic alignment has the potential to serve as a stabilizing force for Asia and a model for democratic cooperation globally.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 International Relations, GS3 Economy — infrastructure, Internal Security — defence cooperation
  🌐 Contemporary: India-Japan relations are central to Quad and Indo-Pacific strategy amid China's rise.

**[2018]** Q9. "India's relations with Israel have, of late, acquired a depth and diversity, which cannot be rolled back." Discuss.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: India-Israel strategic partnership, India's dual-track Middle East policy, Defence cooperation

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India-Israel relations have undergone a fundamental transformation since normalization in 1992, acquiring strategic depth through defence cooperation, technology partnerships and counter-terrorism collaboration that creates strong institutional momentum against reversal.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Defence: Israel is India's top defence supplier (Barak missiles, surveillance UAVs, Spyder systems)
      • Agriculture and water: drip irrigation technology, Arava model agriculture — grassroots impact
      • Counter-terrorism: intelligence sharing, joint training — post-26/11 intensified
      • Technology: cybersecurity, space, pharmaceutical cooperation
      • Political symbolism: PM Modi's visit to Israel (2017) — first by Indian PM
      • Balancing act: India maintains strong Arab/Palestine ties — voted against Hamas resolution initially, then abstained
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India-Israel relations have acquired structural depth and institutional momentum that transcend political changes, as both countries share strategic interests in counterterrorism, technology and regional stability that no future government can easily unwind.
  🔗 Interlinkages: IR — India's Middle East policy, Internal Security — counterterrorism, Economy — defence industrial cooperation
  🌐 Contemporary: Israel-Gaza conflict (2023-24) tested India's balancing act between Israel partnership and Palestinian solidarity.

**[2017]** Q19. The question of India's Energy Security constitutes the most important part of India's economic progress. Analyze India's energy policy cooperation with West Asian countries.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: India's energy security, India-West Asia relations, Energy diplomacy

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's energy security imperative — with 85% import dependence on crude oil — makes West Asian countries' cooperation not merely a foreign policy preference but an economic and strategic necessity.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • West Asia supplies ~65% of India's crude needs — Saudi Arabia, UAE, Iraq, Kuwait are key suppliers
      • Strategic petroleum reserves: Mangaluru, Padur, Visakhapatnam — 5.3 MMT capacity, 9 days backup
      • Bilateral energy cooperation: India-Saudi ARAMCO investment discussions; UAE NDF investments
      • Post-Arab Spring: India diversified — African sources (Nigeria, Angola) and US shale oil
      • Diaspora dividend: 9 million Indians in Gulf remit ~$35 billion annually
      • I2U2 (India-Israel-UAE-US): emerging West Asia quad with clean energy agenda
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India's energy policy cooperation with West Asia must evolve from transactional oil import dependence to comprehensive strategic energy partnerships encompassing renewables, hydrogen and broader economic integration.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Economy — energy imports and current account deficit, IR — West Asia geopolitics, Environment — renewable energy transition
  🌐 Contemporary: Russia oil imports (post-2022), I2U2 formation and Saudi Arabia ARAMCO investment discussions are current.
