**[Q4676]** [2024]
> Consider the following statements:  1. Statement-I: Rainfall is one of the reasons for weathering of rocks 2. Statement-II: Rain water contains carbon dioxide in solution. 3. Statement-III: Rain water contains atmospheric oxygen.  Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

  ✅ A) Both Statement-II and Statement-III are correct and both of them explain Statement-I
     B) Both Statement-II and Statement-III are correct, but only one of them explains Statement-I
     C) Only one of the Statements II and III is correct and that explains Statement-I
     D) Neither Statement-II nor Statement-III is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may think only one factor (CO₂ dissolved in water forming carbonic acid) explains weathering — but atmospheric oxygen also oxidizes iron minerals, contributing to weathering.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Both Statement-II (CO₂ dissolves in rainwater, forms carbonic acid — chemical weathering) and Statement-III (O₂ in rainwater causes oxidation of minerals) explain Statement-I. Answer: A (both explain).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Carbonic acid (H₂CO₃) from dissolved CO₂ weathers limestone and silicate minerals (carbonation)
     - Dissolved oxygen causes oxidation of iron and other minerals — another form of chemical weathering

**[Q4547]** [2023]
> Consider the following statements :  1. In a seismograph, P waves are recorded earlier than S waves. 2. In P waves, the individual particles vibrate to and fro in the direction of wave propagation whereas in S waves, the particles vibrate up and down at right angles to the direction of wave propagation.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse P-waves and S-waves — P (primary) waves travel faster and arrive first; S (secondary) waves are transverse and arrive later.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Both statements are correct: P waves are recorded before S waves (statement 1) and P waves are longitudinal (to-and-fro) while S waves are transverse (up-and-down, perpendicular) — answer is 'Both 1 and 2'.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - P waves (compressional/primary) travel at ~6-8 km/s; S waves (shear/secondary) at ~3.5-4.5 km/s — hence P arrives first
     - P waves = longitudinal (particle motion parallel to propagation); S waves = transverse (particle motion perpendicular)

### GS1

**[2025]** Q16. Discuss how the changes in shape and sizes of continents and ocean basins of the planet take place due to tectonic movements of the crustal masses.
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Plate tectonics, Geomorphology, Continental evolution

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The shapes and sizes of Earth's continents and ocean basins are not static features but dynamic products of tectonic plate movements — a continuous geological process that has been reshaping Earth's surface for billions of years.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Divergent boundaries: sea floor spreading at mid-ocean ridges creates new oceanic crust — Atlantic Ocean widening at ~2.5cm/year
      • Convergent boundaries: subduction of oceanic plate under continental — ocean basins shrink (Pacific); mountains form (Himalayas — Indian and Eurasian plate collision)
      • Transform boundaries: lateral movement — no creation/destruction of crust; San Andreas Fault
      • Continental drift: Pangaea (300 mya) split into Laurasia and Gondwana — then into current continents
      • Ocean basin changes: Tethys Sea closed as India moved north; Mediterranean shrinking as Africa moves north
      • Evidence: matching coastlines, identical fossils (Glossopteris) on separated continents, paleomagnetism
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Tectonic movements have been Earth's great sculptor — continuously creating and destroying ocean basins, building and eroding continents, and shaping the planet's surface in ways that remain geologically dynamic today.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1: Physical geography, Disaster management: earthquakes and volcanoes, Natural resources
  🌐 Contemporary: Understanding tectonic movements is essential for earthquake hazard mapping — critical for India given its seismic vulnerability in Himalayan and Andaman regions.

  - 📋 **Body:**

      • Mountain building: Andes (Americas) and island arcs (Japan, Philippines) formed by ongoing subduction

**[2013]** Q19. (a) What do you understand by the theory of 'continental drift'? Discuss the prominent evidences in its support.

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~100 words: intro 30w / body 80w / conclusion 25w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Alfred Wegener's continental drift theory (1912) revolutionized understanding of Earth's dynamic surface, supported by compelling geological and paleontological evidence.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Theory: all continents once formed a single landmass (Pangaea) that drifted apart ~200 million years ago
