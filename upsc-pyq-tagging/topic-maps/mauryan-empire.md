**[Q3951]** [2020]
> Who among the following rulers advised his subjects through this inscription ? "Whosoever praises his religious sect or blames other sects out of excessive devotion to his own sect, with the view of glorifying his own sect, he rather injures his own sect very severely."

  ✅ A) Ashoka
     B) Samudragupta
     C) Harshavardhana
     D) Krishnadeva Raya

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students might consider Harshavardhana (known for religious tolerance) or Krishnadeva Raya (known for patronage of multiple religions) instead of Ashoka.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The inscription's philosophy of religious tolerance and warning against sectarian pride is characteristic of Ashoka's Rock Edicts, particularly Edict XII. Answer is A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Ashoka's Rock Edict XII advocates religious tolerance and condemns excessive sectarian devotion
     - Ashoka propagated Dhamma — a universal moral code transcending religious boundaries

**[Q3213]** [2016]
> Who of the following had first deciphered the edicts of Emperor Ashoka?

     A) Georg Bühler
  ✅ B) James Prinsep
     C) Max Müller
     D) William Jones

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse James Prinsep with William Jones (who translated Sanskrit texts) or Max Müller; Georg Bühler also worked on ancient scripts.
  💡 **Eliminate:** James Prinsep (1837) first deciphered Brahmi and Kharosthi scripts, enabling reading of Ashokan edicts. William Jones = Sanskrit/comparative linguistics; Max Müller = Vedic texts translation.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - James Prinsep deciphered Ashokan edicts (Brahmi script) in 1837
     - William Jones founded Asiatic Society; Max Müller translated Vedas

**[Q2648]** [2013]
> Consider the following historical places:  1. Ajanta Caves 2. Lepakshi Temple 3. Sanchi Stupa  Which of the above places is/are also known for mural paintings?

     A) 1 only
  ✅ B) 1 and 2 only
     C) 1, 2 and 3
     D) None

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Sanchi Stupa is famous for its sculptures/gateways (toranas), NOT mural paintings. Ajanta and Lepakshi are known for murals — students may mistakenly include Sanchi.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Ajanta has famous Buddhist murals (2nd century BCE–6th century CE); Lepakshi (Vijayanagara era) has ceiling murals; Sanchi is famous for stone sculpture/toranas, not murals. Answer is B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Ajanta Caves have UNESCO-listed Buddhist murals from 2nd century BCE to 6th century CE
     - Lepakshi temple (Andhra Pradesh) has 16th century Vijayanagara mural paintings on ceiling
     - Sanchi Stupa is famous for sculptural toranas (gateways), not mural paintings

**[Q1097]** [2002]
> The ancient Indian play Mudrarakshasa of Visakhadutt has its subject on

     A) a conflict between Gods and Demons of ancient Hindu lore
     B) a romantic story of an Aryan prince and a tribal woman
     C) the story of the power struggle between two Aryan tribes
  ✅ D) the court intrigues at the time of Chandragupta Maurya

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The name Mudrarakshasa contains 'rakshasa' which may suggest demonic or mythological content; the play is actually about political intrigue at Chandragupta's court, not mythology.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Mudrarakshasa by Visakhadatta is a Sanskrit play about the political machinations of Chanakya (Kautilya) in installing and securing Chandragupta Maurya's throne. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Mudrarakshasa = 'The Signet Ring of the Rakshasa (minister)' — political drama about Chandragupta's court
     - Visakhadatta (Vishakhadatta) was the playwright; Chanakya is the protagonist

**[Q761]** [2000]
> Which one of the following dynasties was ruling over North India at the time of Alexander’s invasion ?

  ✅ A) Nanda
     B) Maurya
     C) Sunga
     D) Kanva

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students confuse Maurya with Nanda — Chandragupta Maurya defeated the Nandas after Alexander's invasion; Alexander's invasion (327-325 BCE) predates Mauryan Empire (321 BCE).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Alexander invaded around 326 BCE; the Nanda dynasty ruled North India at that time. Chandragupta Maurya overthrew the Nandas only after Alexander departed.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Nanda dynasty ruled at time of Alexander's invasion (326 BCE)
     - Chandragupta Maurya overthrew Nandas c.321 BCE to establish Mauryan Empire
     - Alexander retreated due to his army's refusal to advance further east

**[Q796]** [2000]
> Assertion (A) : Ashoka annexed Kalinga to the Mauryan Empire. Reason (R) : Kalinga controlled the land and sea routes to South India.

  ✅ A) Both A and R are true, and R is the correct explanation of A
     B) Both A and R are true, but R is not a correct explanation of A
     C) A is true, but R is false
     D) A is false, but R is true

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Both statements are true and well-known facts; the challenge is judging if R correctly explains A. Kalinga's control of trade routes IS the reason Ashoka annexed it.
  💡 **Eliminate:** A: Ashoka annexed Kalinga (true). R: Kalinga controlled land and sea routes to South India (true, and this IS the strategic reason for annexation). Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Kalinga War: 261 BCE, most devastating conquest of Ashoka
     - Kalinga controlled vital trade routes to South India and Southeast Asia
     - Led to Ashoka's conversion to Buddhism and policy of Dhamma

**[Q332]** [1997]
> Which one of the following statements regarding Asokan stone pillars is incorrect ?

     A) These are highly polished
     B) These are monolithic
     C) The shaft of pillars is tapering in shape
  ✅ D) These are parts of architectural structures

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may think Ashokan pillars are part of architectural structures (temple bases etc.) since they look like columns. The key fact is they are freestanding monolithic pillars, not structural supports.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Ashokan pillars are highly polished (A — true), monolithic (B — true), with tapering shafts (C — true), but NOT parts of architectural structures — they are freestanding. Select D (incorrect statement).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Ashokan pillars — freestanding monolithic polished sandstone, not structural columns
     - Chunar sandstone used; bell-shaped capital; edicts inscribed in Brahmi/Kharoshti

**[Q7]** [1995]
> The name by which Asoka is generally referred to in his inscriptions is

     A) Chakravarti
     B) Dharmadeva
     C) Dharmakirti
  ✅ D) Priyadarsi

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Chakravarti means 'universal ruler' and sounds like a regal title Asoka might use; Dharmadeva sounds plausible given his Buddhist leanings. But inscriptions consistently use 'Priyadarsi' (beloved of the gods).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Asoka used 'Priyadarsi' (Devanampiya Piyadassi) in his inscriptions — this is a fundamental Mauryan history fact. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Asoka called himself 'Priyadarsi' (Devanampiya) in inscriptions
     - The name 'Asoka' rarely appears in inscriptions themselves

**[Q4875]** [2025]
> Ashokan inscriptions suggest that the 'Pradeshika', Rajuka' and Yukta' were important officers at the

     A) village-level administration
  ✅ B) district-level administration
     C) provincial administration
     D) level of the central administration

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Pradeshika sounds like a village-level officer; Yukta sounds central — but Ashokan inscriptions clearly place all three at the district (janapada/ahara) level.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Ashokan inscriptions identify Pradeshika, Rajuka, and Yukta as district-level officers responsible for welfare and justice at the district level. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Pradeshika, Rajuka, Yukta = district-level Mauryan officers
     - Rajukas were responsible for welfare of rural people in districts

**[Q4355]** [2022]
> According to Kautilya's Arthashastra, which of the following are correct?  1. A person could be a slave as a result of a judicial punishment. 2. If a female slave bore her master a son, she was legally free. 3. If a son born to a female slave was fathered by her master, the son was entitled to the legal status of the master's son.  Which of the statements given above are correct?

     A) 1 and 2 only
  ✅ B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 3 says the son fathered by the master is entitled to master's son status — Arthashastra actually says such a son is free but not necessarily fully entitled to master's son legal status; statement 2 alone about female slave bearing master's son = legally free is the more precise Arthashastra provision.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Arthashastra: statement 1 (judicial punishment → slavery) and statement 2 (female slave bearing master's son = free) are correct; statement 3 overreaches — answer is A (1 and 2 only). But given answer is B (2 and 3 only) — use given answer B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Kautilya's Arthashastra: details multiple sources of slavery including debt, capture in war, and judicial punishment
     - Arthashastra's provisions on slavery show relatively humane treatment compared to other ancient systems
