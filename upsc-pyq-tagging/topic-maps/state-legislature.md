**[Q3586]** [2018]
> Consider the following statements :  1. The Speaker of the Legislative Assembly shall vacate his/her office if he/she ceases to be a member of the Assembly. 2. Whenever the Legislative Assembly is dissolved, the Speaker shall vacate his/her office immediately.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is the trap — students think dissolution of Assembly means Speaker also loses office immediately; the Speaker actually continues till the new Assembly meets (like Lok Sabha Speaker).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Speaker vacates office when ceasing to be member of Assembly (statement 1 correct — Article 179). On dissolution of Assembly, Speaker does NOT vacate immediately — continues till first sitting of new Assembly (statement 2 wrong).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - State Assembly Speaker: vacates office if ceases to be a member (Article 179)
     - On dissolution: Speaker continues in office until first sitting of new Assembly — same as Lok Sabha Speaker
     - This ensures continuity of parliamentary procedures during election period

**[Q3070]** [2015]
> Consider the following statements:  1. The Legislative Council of a State in India can be larger in size than half of the Legislative Assembly of that particular State. 2. The Governor of a State nominates the Chairman of Legislative Council of that particular State.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
  ✅ D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Both statements appear plausible; the Legislative Council CAN be larger than 1/3 of Legislative Assembly (NOT half) and Chairman is NOT nominated by Governor but elected by Council members.
  💡 **Eliminate:** LC cannot exceed 1/3rd of LA membership (not half — statement 1 says 'larger than half' which makes this wrong). Chairman of LC is elected by LC members, NOT nominated by Governor (statement 2 wrong). Answer: Neither.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Legislative Council: cannot exceed 1/3rd of Legislative Assembly strength
     - Chairman of Legislative Council: elected by Council members (not nominated by Governor)
     - Governor nominates 1/6th of LC members

**[Q2041]** [2008]
> Consider the following statements: The Constitution of India provides that 1. the Legislative Assembly of each State shall consist of not more than 450 members chosen by direct election from territorial constituencies in the State. 2. a person shall not be qualified to be chosen to fill a seat in the Legislative Assembly of a State if he/she is less than 25 years of age. Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
  ✅ B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 says 450 members but the Constitution prescribes maximum 500 members for a State Legislative Assembly — 450 is incorrect. Statement 2 (minimum age 25) is correct.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Article 170 prescribes maximum 500 (not 450) members for Vidhan Sabha; minimum age is 25 years (Art. 173). Only statement 2 is correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 170: State Legislative Assembly has 60-500 members
     - Minimum age to contest Vidhan Sabha elections is 25 years
     - Direct elections from territorial constituencies as per Article 170

**[Q3787]** [2019]
> With reference to the Legislative Assembly of a State in India, consider the following statements :  1. The Governor makes a customary address to Members of the House at the commencement of the first session of the year. 2. When a State Legislature does not have a rule on a particular matter, it follows the Lok Sabha rule on that matter.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 about following Lok Sabha rules when state has no rule — this is a general principle but candidates may be uncertain whether it applies to Vidhan Sabha specifically.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Governor addresses the first session of each year (S1 correct — similar to President's address to Parliament). When a state legislature has no rule on a matter, Lok Sabha rules apply by convention (S2 correct). Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Governor's address at the start of the first session is a constitutional convention parallel to President's address to Parliament
     - State legislatures use Lok Sabha rules as reference for procedural matters not covered in their own rules

**[Q103]** [1995]
> Which one of the following States of India does not have a Legislative Council so far even though the Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956 provides for it ?

     A) Maharashtra
     B) Bihar
     C) Karnataka
  ✅ D) Madhya Pradesh

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may think small states like Rajasthan or UP do not have Legislative Councils; but Madhya Pradesh, despite being a large state, does not have one and is the correct answer.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Madhya Pradesh does not have a Legislative Council. Maharashtra, Bihar, and Karnataka all have Legislative Councils (Vidhan Parishad). Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - States with Legislative Councils: UP, Bihar, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh
     - MP, Rajasthan, Odisha etc. have only Vidhan Sabha

**[2025]** Q4. Discuss the nature of Jammu and Kashmir Legislative Assembly after the Jammu and Kashmir Reorganization Act, 2019. Briefly describe the powers and functions of the Assembly of the Union Territory of Jammu and Kashmir.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: J&K governance, UT with legislature, Federalism

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Jammu and Kashmir Reorganization Act 2019 fundamentally altered the constitutional status of J&K, replacing a full state with two Union Territories and creating a unique legislative assembly with constrained powers.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Nature of assembly: 90 seats (83 elected + 5 nominated by LG for Kashmiri Migrants + 2 for PoJK displaced persons); scheduled for elections after delimitation
      • Powers: can legislate on State List and Concurrent List subjects, BUT with significant overriding power vested in the LG in specific matters (law and order, police, IAS/IPS postings remain with Centre)
      • LG's role: unlike full states, LG of J&K UT retains significant executive power; elected government's jurisdiction is narrower than a state government
      • Comparison with Delhi model: similar to GNCT Delhi — elected government coexists with powerful LG; Centre controls law and order
      • Recent development: J&K Assembly elections held in 2024 for first time since 2019 reorganization
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The J&K Assembly represents a unique constitutional experiment — a legislature with state-like form but UT-like substance, reflecting the Centre's intent to gradually restore democratic institutions while retaining strategic control.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 Polity, Federalism, Internal Security
  🌐 Contemporary: J&K Assembly elections (September 2024) restored partial democratic governance after 6 years of central rule.

**[2023]** Q5. Discuss the role of Presiding Officers of state legislatures in maintaining order and impartiality in conducting legislative work and in facilitating best democratic practices.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Legislative democracy, Presiding Officers, Democratic practices

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Presiding Officers of state legislatures serve as the constitutional guardians of legislative dignity — their impartiality, procedural authority, and democratic stewardship determine the quality of legislative democracy.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Maintaining order: powers to expunge, suspend members, adjourn proceedings — ensures decorum and productive debate
      • Impartiality: Speaker/Chairman should shed party affiliations on assumption of office; rulings on admissibility, privilege should be above partisanship
      • Facilitating best practices: Question Hour, Zero Hour, Committee assignments, private member bills — Presiding Officers schedule and facilitate these democratic instruments
      • Anti-defection role: Presiding Officers decide disqualification under Tenth Schedule — though criticized for partisan use, remains a critical constitutional function
      • Challenges: partisanship in disqualification decisions (Manipur, Maharashtra cases); frequent adjournments due to political protests
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The quality of legislative democracy ultimately hinges on Presiding Officers who can enforce order without becoming political instruments — a constitutional ideal frequently tested in practice.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 Polity, GS4 Ethics, Indian Democracy
  🌐 Contemporary: Supreme Court's scrutiny of Speakers' disqualification decisions (Maharashtra, Manipur 2023-24) underscored accountability concerns.

**[2021]** Q14. Explain the constitutional provisions under which Legislative Councils are established. Review the working and current status of Legislative Councils with suitable illustrations.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Legislative Council relevance, Bicameralism in Indian states, Constitutional provisions for state legislature

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Legislative Councils (Vidhan Parishads) are optional second chambers in state legislatures, created or abolished by Parliament under Article 169 upon state assembly resolution.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Constitutional provisions: Article 168–171; Parliament creates/abolishes LC on state resolution
      • Composition: 1/3 elected by MLAs, 1/12 teachers, 1/12 graduates, 1/6 Governor nominees, 1/3 elected by local bodies
      • Working: acts as revising chamber, cannot block Money Bills, weaker than Rajya Sabha
      • Current status: 6 states have LCs; Andhra Pradesh restored LC in 2020 after earlier abolition
      • Criticism: fiscally expensive, often used for political accommodation, limited legislative contribution
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Legislative Councils serve a limited but distinct revising function; their utility depends on the political will to use them substantively rather than as patronage instruments.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 Polity, Federalism, Constitutional design
  🌐 Contemporary: Andhra Pradesh and Rajasthan's moves on LCs reflect ongoing political debates about their utility.
