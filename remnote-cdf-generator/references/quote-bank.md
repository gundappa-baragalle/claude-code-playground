# Quote Bank — GS4 Ethics and Essay

Use quotes **only from this file**. Every entry has been verified against a named source.
Never paraphrase a quote and present it as verbatim. Never use a quote marked ⚠️ without
the caveat noted there.

If you need a quote on a theme not listed here, use a *~governance link* ;- (Nolan Principle
or constitutional article) instead — those are always safe to cite.

## How to Use in Notes

```
*~thinker quote* ;- "Quote text here." — Thinker Name, Source Title (Year)
                     GS4 use: [one line on which ethical concept this illustrates]
```

Always `;-` (disabled) — quotes are context for recall, not atomic flashcard targets.
Never `;;` on a quote — quotes are not directly testable facts in UPSC Prelims.

## 1. Duty and the Categorical Imperative

**Kant — Universal Law formulation**
> "Act only according to that maxim by which you can at the same time will that it should
> become a universal law."
— Immanuel Kant, *Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals* (1785)
GS4 use: Deontological ethics; integrity; why a civil servant should not take bribes even once.

**Kant — Humanity as End**
> "Act so that you treat humanity, whether in your own person or in that of another, always as
> an end and never as a means only."
— Immanuel Kant, *Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals* (1785)
GS4 use: Human dignity; why using beneficiaries as vote-banks or statistics is unethical.

**Bhagavad Gita — Nishkama Karma**
> "You have a right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits
> of your actions."
— Bhagavad Gita, Chapter 2, Verse 47
GS4 use: Selfless public service; why a civil servant should act without attachment to credit
or career outcomes; nishkama karma as governance ideal.

## 2. Integrity and Honesty

**Socrates — The Examined Life**
> "The unexamined life is not worth living."
— Socrates, in Plato's *Apology* (399 BCE)
GS4 use: Self-awareness; introspection as foundation of ethical conduct; why a civil servant
must constantly audit their own motivations.

**Lincoln — Power and Character**
> "Nearly all men can stand adversity, but if you want to test a man's character, give him
> power."
— Attributed to Abraham Lincoln (⚠️ exact source disputed — do not cite with a specific text)
GS4 use: Power corrupts; why checks on bureaucratic discretion matter; character as test of
ethical fitness for office.

**Gandhi — Be the Change**
> "Be the change you wish to see in the world."
— Mahatma Gandhi (⚠️ simplified paraphrase, widely attributed; not verbatim from a single text)
GS4 use: Personal integrity before systemic reform; leading by example in public service.

**Gandhi — Means and Ends**
> "The means and the end are so intertwined that, to separate them as we often do, is to make
> an error in reasoning."
— Mahatma Gandhi, *Young India* (1924)
GS4 use: Ends-vs-means debate; why corrupt means cannot produce ethical outcomes even for
noble goals; Gandhian ethics.

## 3. Justice and Fairness

**Rawls — Justice as First Virtue**
> "Justice is the first virtue of social institutions, as truth is of systems of thought."
— John Rawls, *A Theory of Justice* (1971), opening sentence
GS4 use: Social justice; why institutions must be designed to be fair before efficient; Rawlsian
difference principle.

**Rawls — Veil of Ignorance (paraphrase)**
> "The principles of justice are chosen behind a veil of ignorance" — no one knows their place
> in society when choosing the rules that will govern it.
— John Rawls, *A Theory of Justice* (1971) (⚠️ paraphrase, not verbatim — cite as "Rawls argues")
GS4 use: Impartiality; why civil servants should design policies as if they did not know which
group they belonged to; procedural fairness.

**Aristotle — Distributive Justice**
> "The worst form of inequality is to try to make unequal things equal."
— Attributed to Aristotle (⚠️ no verified exact source in his texts — cite as "Aristotle held" not
as a direct quote; the idea is from *Nicomachean Ethics* Book V)
GS4 use: Substantive equality; why treating everyone identically ignores structural disadvantage.

**Martin Luther King Jr. — Injustice Anywhere**
> "Injustice anywhere is a threat to justice everywhere. We are caught in an inescapable network
> of mutuality, tied in a single garment of destiny."
— Martin Luther King Jr., *Letter from Birmingham Jail* (April 16, 1963)
GS4 use: Systemic injustice; why staying silent about corruption in other departments is
ethically untenable; solidarity in public service.

**Ambedkar — Measure of Progress**
> "I measure the progress of a community by the degree of progress which women have achieved."
— B.R. Ambedkar, speech at the All India Depressed Classes Women's Conference, Nagpur (1942)
GS4 use: Social justice; gender equity; why development metrics must disaggregate by gender.

## 4. Compassion and Non-violence

**Gandhi — Non-violence**
> "Non-violence is the greatest force at the disposal of mankind. It is mightier than the
> mightiest weapon of destruction devised by the ingenuity of man."
— Mahatma Gandhi, *Young India* and various writings (1920s)
GS4 use: Ahimsa as a governance value; de-escalation in conflict situations; use of force by
the state.

**Gandhi — Forgiveness**
> "The weak can never forgive. Forgiveness is the attribute of the strong."
— Mahatma Gandhi, *Young India* (1919)
GS4 use: Conflict resolution; restorative justice; magnanimity in positions of power.

**Buddha — Non-hatred**
> "Hatred is never appeased by hatred in this world. By non-hatred alone is hatred appeased.
> This is a law eternal."
— *Dhammapada*, Verse 5
GS4 use: Ahimsa; why punitive responses to grievances deepen conflict; compassionate
administration.

**Buddha — Truth**
> "Three things cannot be long hidden: the sun, the moon, and the truth."
— Attributed to Buddha (⚠️ no exact verified Pali source — widely used; cite as "the Buddhist
tradition holds" or "attributed to the Buddha")
GS4 use: Transparency; accountability; why cover-ups in public service are unsustainable.

**Mahavira — Non-violence to All**
> "All breathing, existing, living, sentient creatures should not be slain, nor treated with
> violence, nor abused, nor tormented, nor driven away."
— Mahavira, *Acaranga Sutra* (Jain text, ~500 BCE)
GS4 use: Ahimsa; reverence for all life; environmental ethics (GS3-GS4 link).

## 5. Courage and Moral Courage

**Aristotle — Courage as Mean**
> "Courage is the first of human virtues, because it makes all others possible."
— Attributed to Aristotle (⚠️ not a verbatim Aristotle quote; the idea is in *Nicomachean Ethics*
Book III but not in these exact words — cite as "Aristotle argued" not as a direct quote)
GS4 use: Moral courage; whistleblowing; why other virtues collapse without the courage to act.

**Mandela — Education and Courage**
> "Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world."
— Nelson Mandela, speech at launch of Mindset Network, Johannesburg (July 16, 2003)
GS4 use: Role of education in ethical development; long-term governance investment; human
capital.

**Vivekananda — Fearlessness**
> "Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached."
— Swami Vivekananda, adapting the *Katha Upanishad* (Nachiketa verse)
GS4 use: Perseverance in public service despite obstacles; moral courage to pursue reforms.

## 6. Good Governance and Public Service

**Kautilya — King's Happiness**
> "In the happiness of his subjects lies the king's happiness; in their welfare his welfare.
> He shall not consider as good only that which pleases him but treat as beneficial to him
> whatever pleases his subjects."
— Kautilya, *Arthashastra*, Book I
GS4 use: Public-service orientation; why a civil servant's satisfaction must derive from
citizen outcomes, not career advancement; servant-leadership.

**Kautilya — Corruption**
> "Just as it is impossible not to taste the honey or the poison that finds itself at the tip
> of the tongue, so it is impossible for a government servant not to eat up, at least, a bit
> of the king's revenue."
— Kautilya, *Arthashastra*, Book II (⚠️ this is descriptive, not prescriptive — Kautilya is
acknowledging corruption as a reality, not endorsing it; always note this context)
GS4 use: Systemic corruption analysis; institutional safeguards; why incentive structures
and monitoring systems are as important as personal ethics.

**J.S. Mill — Liberty**
> "The only freedom which deserves the name is that of pursuing our own good in our own way,
> so long as we do not attempt to deprive others of theirs, or impede their efforts to obtain
> it."
— John Stuart Mill, *On Liberty* (1859), Chapter I
GS4 use: Harm principle; limits of state paternalism; individual freedom vs public order;
civil liberties in administration.

**Churchill — Democracy**
> "Democracy is the worst form of government, except for all those others that have been tried."
— Winston Churchill, speech in the House of Commons (November 11, 1947)
Essay use: Democratic governance; imperfection of institutions; why process legitimacy
matters despite inefficiency.

## 7. Constitutional Morality and Civic Values

**Ambedkar — Constitutional Morality**
> "Constitutional morality is not a natural sentiment. It has to be cultivated. We must realise
> that our people have yet to learn it. Democracy in India is only a top-dressing on an Indian
> soil which is essentially undemocratic."
— B.R. Ambedkar, Constituent Assembly Debates, November 4, 1948
GS4 use: Constitutional values vs social morality; why legal compliance alone is not ethical
conduct; the duty of civil servants to uphold constitutional spirit.

**Nehru — Tryst with Destiny**
> "At the stroke of the midnight hour, when the world sleeps, India will awake to life and
> freedom. A moment comes, which comes but rarely in history, when we step out from the old
> to the new."
— Jawaharlal Nehru, *Tryst with Destiny* speech, August 14–15, 1947
Essay use: Nation-building; civilizational transformation; freedom and responsibility; use in
Essay introductions on governance, democracy, or India's development narrative.

**Tagore — Freedom of Mind**
> "Where the mind is without fear and the head is held high / Where knowledge is free /
> Where the world has not been broken up into fragments by narrow domestic walls..."
— Rabindranath Tagore, *Gitanjali*, Poem 35 (1910)
Essay use: Intellectual freedom; inclusive governance; education; national vision; strong as
an Essay opening or closing image.

## 8. Nolan Principles of Public Life (UK, 1995)

These are the 7 principles for public officials — widely cited in GS4 answers on integrity.
**Not quotes; principles.** Cite as "the Nolan Committee identified seven principles of public life."

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Selflessness** | Decisions solely in terms of public interest |
| **Integrity** | No financial obligations to outside individuals/organisations |
| **Objectivity** | Decisions on merit; no favouritism |
| **Accountability** | Subject to scrutiny; answerable to the public |
| **Openness** | Reasons for decisions should be transparent |
| **Honesty** | Declare conflicts of interest; be truthful |
| **Leadership** | Promote and support these principles by example |

GS4 use: Tag each ethical concept in a GS4 note to the relevant Nolan Principle(s). Every
`*~governance link*` descriptor should cite at least one Nolan Principle where applicable.

## 9. Short Attribution-Safe Lines (for Essay openings)

These are short, quotable lines where attribution and accuracy are both reliable:

- **"The ends do not justify the means."** — Principle traceable to Kant and Gandhian ethics;
  safe to use without pinning to a single thinker.

- **"Power tends to corrupt, and absolute power corrupts absolutely."** — Lord Acton, letter to
  Bishop Mandell Creighton (April 5, 1887). Safe to cite as "Lord Acton wrote."

- **"A society grows great when old men plant trees whose shade they know they shall never
  sit in."** — Ancient Greek proverb (⚠️ attribution uncertain — do NOT attribute to a named
  thinker; use as "a Greek proverb holds")

- **"The measure of a man is what he does with power."** — Attributed to Plato (⚠️ disputed;
  use as "Plato reportedly held" or just use the idea without the attribution)

## ⚠️ Quotes to AVOID — Commonly Invented or Misattributed

These appear in UPSC coaching material but cannot be safely attributed. Do not use them
as direct quotes in notes.

| Quote | Why to avoid |
|-------|-------------|
| "We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence is not an act but a habit." | Actually a Will Durant paraphrase of Aristotle, not Aristotle's own words |
| "The good man is the friend of all living things." | Gandhi, not Aristotle — misattributed in much coaching material |
| "Be the change..." (verbatim) | Widely attributed to Gandhi but no exact source found — safe as paraphrase not verbatim |
| Any Chanakya Niti quote without chapter/verse | Chanakya Niti is a medieval compilation; many attributed quotes are not from the Arthashastra and have no reliable source |
| "The only thing necessary for the triumph of evil is for good men to do nothing." | Attributed to Edmund Burke but no verified Burke text contains this — safe as a principle, not as a Burke quote |
