**[Q3639]** [2025]
> Consider the following statements about the Rashtriya Gokul Mission :  I. It is important for the upliftment of rural poor as majority of low producing indigenous animals are with small and marginal farmers and landless labourers. II. It was initiated to promote indigenous cattle and buffalo rearing and conservation in a scientific and holistic manner.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) I only
     B) II only
  ✅ C) Both I and II
     D) Neither I nor II

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may think Statement I is wrong because the scheme is about cattle breed improvement, not poverty — but the scheme explicitly targets small/marginal farmers who own low-productivity indigenous breeds.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Both statements are correct: Rashtriya Gokul Mission targets indigenous cattle/buffalo conservation (II) and benefits small/marginal farmers and landless laborers (I). Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Rashtriya Gokul Mission launched 2014 for indigenous cattle breed development
     - Focuses on conservation and development of indigenous breeds like Gir, Sahiwal

**[Q2914]** [2019]
> With reference to the management of minor minerals in India, consider the following statements :  1. Sand is a 'minor mineral' according to the prevailing law in the country. 2. State Governments have the power to grant mining leases of minor minerals, but the powers regarding the formation of rules related to the grant of minor minerals lie with the Central Government. 3. State Governments have the power to frame rules to prevent illegal mining of minor minerals.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 and 3 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is wrong — State Governments have powers to both grant mining leases AND frame rules for minor minerals; the Centre does not hold the rule-making power for minor minerals. Candidates may assume Centre controls rule-making.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Sand is a minor mineral (statement 1 correct); State Governments have full power — both leasing AND rule-making — for minor minerals (statement 2 wrong); states can frame anti-illegal mining rules (statement 3 correct). Answer is A (1 and 3 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Minor minerals include sand, gravel, clay, building stones — defined under MMDR Act
     - State Governments have complete jurisdiction over minor minerals including leasing and rule-making
     - Illegal sand mining is a major governance problem in India

**[Q2548]** [2016]
> Regarding 'Atal Pension Yojana', which of the following statements is/are correct?  1. It is a minimum guaranteed pension scheme mainly targeted at unorganized sector workers. 2. Only one member of a family can join the scheme. 3. Same amount of pension is guaranteed for the spouse for life after subscriber's death.  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
  ✅ C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 (only one family member) and Statement 3 (same pension for spouse) are both tricky — APY allows spouse continuation but at same guaranteed amount, while family restriction is not strict.
  💡 **Eliminate:** APY guarantees minimum pension for unorganized workers (S1 correct). Spouse receives same guaranteed pension after subscriber's death (S3 correct). S2 is incorrect — both spouses can join. Answer: C (1 and 3 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - APY guarantees pension of ₹1000–₹5000/month at age 60 for unorganized sector
     - Spouse continues to receive same pension after subscriber's death

**[Q2978]** [2012]
> The endeavour of Janani Suraksha Yojana' Programme.is  1. te promote institutional deliveries 2. to provide monetary assistance to the mother to meet the cost of delivery 3. to provide for wage loss due to 'pregnancy and confinement.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 2 only
     C) 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 3 is the trap — JSY does not provide for wage loss due to pregnancy; that is a different provision (Maternity Benefit Act). JSY focuses on institutional delivery and monetary incentive for delivery costs.
  💡 **Eliminate:** JSY aims at promoting institutional deliveries (statement 1) and provides cash assistance for delivery costs (statement 2). Wage loss compensation is NOT part of JSY (statement 3 wrong). Answer is A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - JSY = Janani Suraksha Yojana launched 2005 under NHM
     - Provides cash incentive to BPL pregnant women for institutional delivery
     - Higher incentive in Low Performing States (LPS) to reduce home deliveries

**[Q1625]** [2011]
> Among the following who are eligible to benefit from the "Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act"?

     A) Adult members of only the scheduled caste and scheduled tribe households
     B) Adult members of below poverty line (BPL) households
     C) Adult members of households of all backward communities
  ✅ D) Adult members of any household

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Options A (SC/ST only), B (BPL only), and C (backward communities) are all common but incorrect exclusions; MGNREGA is explicitly universal for all rural adult household members regardless of caste, class, or category.
  💡 **Eliminate:** MGNREGA Section 3 guarantees 100 days employment to 'adult members of any rural household' willing to do unskilled manual work — no caste, income, or category restriction. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - MGNREGA 2005 is a demand-driven right-to-work scheme; any adult rural household member can demand work
     - As of 2023, MGNREGA engaged over 250 million unique workers annually

**[Q4868]** [2006]
> Consider the following statements in respect of the National Rural Employment Guarantee Act, 2005. I. Under the provisions of the Act, 100 days of employment in a year to every household whose adult members volunteer to do unskilled manual work has become a fundamental right. II. Under the provisions of the Act, women are to get priority to the extent that one-half of persons who are given employment are women who have asked for work. Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) I only
     B) II only
     C) Both I and II
  ✅ D) Neither I nor II

  ⚠️ **Trap:** MGNREGA is often misunderstood as a fundamental right guarantee; in reality, it is a legal entitlement under statutory law, not a fundamental right. Also, the women's reservation is one-third, not one-half.
  💡 **Eliminate:** MGNREGA gives a legal right (not fundamental right) to 100 days employment; women's priority is one-third (33%), not one-half — both statements are wrong, making D correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - MGNREGA guarantees 100 days — legal right, not fundamental right
     - Women's reservation under MGNREGA is one-third (33%), not 50%

### GS2

**[2017]** Q6. "To ensure effective implementation of policies addressing water, sanitation and hygiene needs the identification of the beneficiary segments is to be synchronized with the anticipated outcomes." Examine the statement in the context of the WASH scheme. (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: WASH implementation, Beneficiary identification, Behavior change in sanitation

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Effective WASH implementation requires precise beneficiary identification synchronized with behavioral outcomes — without aligning who is targeted with what change is expected, infrastructure investment fails to achieve sanitation security.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • WASH dimensions: safe water access, sanitation facilities, handwashing with soap
      • Challenge: beneficiaries are not homogeneous — tribal areas need different approaches than urban slums
      • Synchronization: identifying school girls (menstrual hygiene), rural women (water safety), disabled persons (accessible toilets)
      • ODF India: SBM achieved 10 crore+ toilets but ODF sustainability requires behavior maintenance
      • JJM: 14 crore rural households provided tap water — coverage expanding
      • IEC (Information, Education, Communication) campaigns needed alongside infrastructure
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Sustainable WASH outcomes require community-centered targeting that matches infrastructure to behavioral goals, supported by sustained IEC campaigns and local-level monitoring rather than one-time construction-centric approaches.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Health — waterborne diseases prevention, Society — women and girls' dignity, Governance — Swachh Bharat Mission
  🌐 Contemporary: Jal Jeevan Mission and ODF Plus phase of SBM keep WASH governance current.

### GS3

**[2020]** Q8. What are the salient features of the Jal Shakti Abhiyan launched by the Government of India for water conservation and water security? (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Water security, Government conservation initiatives, Decentralized water management

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Jal Shakti Abhiyan reflects India's urgent need to address groundwater depletion and water stress through a time-bound campaign approach.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Water conservation and rainwater harvesting through check dams, ponds, and recharge wells
      • Renovation of traditional water bodies (talabs, stepwells)
      • Reuse of wastewater and intensive afforestation
      • Block-level nodal officers, citizen participation model
      • Focus on 256 water-stressed districts across India
      • Phase 2 (2021): extended to all districts with monsoon and pre-monsoon phases
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Jal Shakti Abhiyan's success depends on community participation and convergence with other water-related missions.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Water Security, GS2 – Government Schemes, GS1 – Indian Geography
  🌐 Contemporary: India's worsening water crisis makes Jal Shakti Abhiyan's expansion and effectiveness critically important.

**[2016]** Q4. What are 'Smart Cities'? Examine their relevance for urban development in India. Will it increase rural-urban differences? Give arguments for 'Smart Villages' in the light of PURA and RURBAN Mission. (200 words) 12.5
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Urbanization, Balanced regional development, Digital infrastructure

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Smart Cities represent integrated urban development using technology and data, but without parallel attention to rural areas, they risk accentuating India's rural-urban divide.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Smart Cities: ICT-enabled urban services, citizen participation, sustainability
      • 100 Smart Cities selected through competitive challenge process
      • Risk of increased rural-urban migration without rural development
      • PURA model: providing urban-level amenities in rural clusters
      • RURBAN Mission: developing rural growth clusters with economic activities and infrastructure
      • Need for integrated approach linking smart cities to smart villages
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** A balanced development paradigm combining Smart Cities with Smart Villages under PURA and RURBAN frameworks can ensure inclusive urbanization.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Urban governance, Rural development, Digital India
  🌐 Contemporary: Smart Cities Mission has shown mixed results; rural digital connectivity remains a gap.

### Common Trap Patterns on This Topic
- Gujarati (1) has NOT been declared a classical language — only Tamil, Sanskrit, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, and Odia have been recognized as classical languages.
- This is an obscure 1997 scheme detail; PM's Integrated Urban Poverty Eradication Programme (PMIUPEP) was not subsumed — it is the one NOT included in SJSRY.
- The 50% guarantee (options A and B) sounds more inclusive and thus appealing; the actual scheme targeted one man and one woman per below-poverty-line rural family.
- Options 1 and 2 sound plausible as Women & Child under Health, and Official Language under HRD; however, the question asks which is CORRECTLY matched — only option 3 is correct.
- Students may wrongly assume rural sanitation is in the Concurrent List. It is actually a State subject. The Assertion (launch in 1986) is true but the Reason is false.
