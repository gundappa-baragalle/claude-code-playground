**[Q2370]** [2015]
> With reference to the Fourteenth Finance Commission, which of the following statements is/are correct?  1. It has increased the share of States in the central divisible pool from 32 percent to 42 percent. 2. It has made recommendations concerning sector-specific grants.  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

  ✅ A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is wrong — 14th Finance Commission (Y.V. Reddy Commission) specifically moved away from sector-specific grants to untied grants, giving states more fiscal autonomy.
  💡 **Eliminate:** 14th FC's landmark recommendation: increased states' share from 32% to 42% (correct); it ABOLISHED sector-specific grants in favour of untied grants (Statement 2 wrong) — answer is A (1 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 14th Finance Commission (Y.V. Reddy): increased states' devolution from 32% to 42% of divisible pool
     - 14th FC removed sector-specific tied grants; gave states more untied fiscal transfers

**[Q1628]** [2011]
> With reference to the Finance Commission of India, which of the following statements is correct ?

     A) It encourages the inflow of foreign capital for infrastructure development
     B) It facilitates the proper distribution of finances among the Public Sector Undertakings
     C) It ensures transparency in financial administration
  ✅ D) None of the statements (a), (b) and (c) given above is correct in this context

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option C (ensures transparency in financial administration) sounds vaguely related to Finance Commission's oversight role; however, transparency is the domain of CAG and RTI, not Finance Commission. None of options A, B, C correctly describe the Finance Commission's mandate.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Finance Commission (Article 280) distributes taxes between Centre and States and recommends grants-in-aid — it does NOT attract FDI (A), manage PSUs (B), or ensure transparency (C). All three are incorrect, making D correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Finance Commission's mandate: determine the share of central taxes for states (devolution), recommend grants-in-aid to states under Article 280
     - Finance Commission does NOT manage foreign investment, PSUs, or financial transparency — those are other institutions

**[Q1433]** [2010]
> Who of the following shall cause every recommendation made by the Finance commission to be laid before each House of Parliament?

  ✅ A) The president of India
     B) The speaker of Lok Sabha
     C) The prime minister of India
     D) The Union Finance Minister

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Union Finance Minister or PM seems likely to table Finance Commission recommendations in Parliament, but the constitutional duty falls on the President.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Under Article 281, the President of India shall cause every recommendation of the Finance Commission, together with an explanatory memorandum, to be laid before each House of Parliament. Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 281 — President causes Finance Commission recommendations to be laid before Parliament
     - Finance Commission is constituted under Article 280 every 5 years

**[Q859]** [2003]
> Consider the following statements: The function (S) of the Finance Commission is/are 1. to allow the withdrawal of money out of the Consolidated Fund of India. 2. to allocate between the States the shares of proceeds of taxes. 3. to consider applications for grants-in-aid from States. 4. to supervise and report on whether the Union and State governments are levying taxes in accordance with the budgetary provisions. Which of these statements is/are correct?

     A) Only 1
  ✅ B) 2 and 3
     C) 3 and 4
     D) 1, 2 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students confuse Finance Commission with CAG (audit) or Finance Ministry (budget). Statement 1 about Consolidated Fund withdrawal and Statement 4 about supervising tax collection are outside Finance Commission's mandate.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Finance Commission's role (Article 280): allocate taxes between Union and States (2) and consider grants-in-aid to States (3). It does NOT authorize withdrawals (CAG/Parliament does) or supervise tax compliance.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Finance Commission: Article 280 of Constitution
     - Recommends tax devolution percentages and grants-in-aid to states
     - Does NOT control Consolidated Fund withdrawals (Parliament does that)

**[Q658]** [2002]
> Which one of the following authorities recommends the principles governing the grants-in-aid of the revenues to the states out of the Consolidated Fund of India?

  ✅ A) Finance Commission
     B) Inter-State Council
     C) Union Ministry of Finance
     D) Public Accounts Committee

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Union Ministry of Finance seems directly logical for grants-in-aid decisions; Inter-State Council deals with centre-state issues but not grants-in-aid principles — Finance Commission is the constitutional body for this.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Article 275 and Article 280: Finance Commission recommends principles governing grants-in-aid from Consolidated Fund of India to states — this is its core constitutional mandate; option A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Finance Commission (Article 280): recommends tax devolution AND grants-in-aid principles for states
     - Grants-in-aid (Article 275) are given to states with specific needs as recommended by Finance Commission

**[Q520]** [2000]
> The primary function of the Finance Commission in India is to

  ✅ A) distribute revenue between the Centre and the States
     B) prepare the Annual Budget
     C) advise the President on financial matters
     D) allocate funds to various ministries of the Union and State Governments

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Options B and D confuse Finance Commission with Ministry of Finance/Planning Commission roles; option C (advise President) is partially true but not the primary function.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Finance Commission under Article 280 is primarily tasked with recommending distribution of tax revenues between Centre and States — vertical and horizontal devolution.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Finance Commission: Article 280 of Constitution
     - Recommends vertical devolution (Centre-State) and horizontal devolution (inter-state)
     - Also recommends grants-in-aid to states

### GS2

**[2021]** Q3. How have the recommendations of the 14th Finance Commission of India enabled the States to improve their fiscal position? (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Fiscal federalism, State fiscal capacity, Finance Commission evolution

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The 14th Finance Commission's landmark 42% devolution recommendation fundamentally strengthened states' fiscal position by increasing untied resources available for state-specific development priorities.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • 42% devolution: largest ever increase from ~32%; gave states predictable, untied resources for planning
      • Rationalization of grants: reduced sector-specific grants, allowing states to allocate based on local needs
      • Horizontal distribution: improved formula with 7.5% weight to forest cover (incentivized conservation) and 15% to population control achievement
      • State FRBM strengthening: recommended states maintain fiscal deficit within 3% of GSDP
      • Impact: states with better fiscal management (like Tamil Nadu, Gujarat) used enhanced devolution for capital expenditure; weaker states still depended on CSS
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The 14th FC's devolution expansion represented a structural shift toward cooperative fiscal federalism, empowering states to pursue context-specific development while maintaining fiscal discipline.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: Polity — Finance Commission, GS3: Economy — fiscal federalism, GS2: Federalism
  🌐 Contemporary: 15th FC reduced devolution to 41% (adding 1% for J&K/Ladakh), reflecting ongoing negotiation between fiscal federalism and national priorities.

**[2018]** Q14. How is the Finance Commission of India constituted?  What do you know about the terms of reference of the recently constituted Finance Commission? Discuss. (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Fiscal federalism, Finance Commission role, Centre-State financial relations

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Finance Commission serves as India's primary constitutional mechanism for achieving fiscal federalism, with each successive commission refining devolution principles to balance equity, efficiency and cooperative governance.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Constitution: appointed by President, 5-year term, quasi-judicial character
      • Composition: Chairman + 4 members, qualified persons in finance/economics
      • Functions: recommend tax devolution, grants-in-aid, measures to augment consolidated fund of states
      • 15th Finance Commission: 41% devolution (1% less than 14th's 42% — due to J&K restructuring)
      • Novel terms: revenue deficit grants, sector-specific grants, disaster management grants
      • Performance incentives: grants linked to states' own tax effort, ease of doing business
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The Finance Commission's evolving terms of reference reflect India's changing federal landscape — from simple resource distribution to outcome-linked fiscal federalism that incentivizes good governance at the state level.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Economy — public finance and fiscal transfers, Polity — federalism, Governance — state development financing
  🌐 Contemporary: 15th Finance Commission recommendations (2021-26) are being implemented; 16th FC will be constituted soon.

**[2013]** Q7. Discuss the recommendations of the 13th Finance Commission which have been a departure from the previous commissions for strengthening the local government finances. (200 words) 10
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Fiscal federalism, Local government finance, Finance Commission evolution

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The 13th Finance Commission marked a departure from precedent by directly addressing local government finances and incentivizing fiscal discipline at state level.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Departure in local body grants: first FC to provide performance-based grants directly to PRIs and ULBs conditional on audit and accounting reforms
      • Increased devolution: maintained states' share at ~32% of central taxes (14th FC subsequently raised to 42%)
      • Fiscal consolidation incentives: states maintaining FRBM targets rewarded with additional grants
      • Grants to disaster management fund and specific sector grants for health, education
      • Departure from earlier FCs in recommending a formal roadmap for GST introduction
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The 13th FC's direct local body grants and fiscal discipline incentives represented meaningful steps toward comprehensive fiscal federalism, though full realization awaited subsequent commissions.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: Federalism — fiscal devolution, GS2: Local government, GS3: Economy — public finance
  🌐 Contemporary: 15th Finance Commission (2021-26) recommendations and upcoming 16th FC terms continue this evolution.
