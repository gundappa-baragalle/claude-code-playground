**[Q441]** [1997]
> The length, breadth and height of a room are 1, b and h respectively. The perimeter of the ceiling expressed as a percentage of the total area of the four walls is

     A) 100 h
  ✅ B) 100/h
     C) h
     D) h/100

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse perimeter of ceiling with area of ceiling, or incorrectly compute total wall area; dimensional analysis is key.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Ceiling perimeter = 2(l+b); total wall area = 2(l+b)h; ratio as percentage = [2(l+b)/2(l+b)h] × 100 = 100/h — answer is B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Perimeter of ceiling = 2(l+b)
     - Total area of four walls = 2(l+b)h

**[Q1092]** [2002]
> A solid cube just gets completely immersed in water when a 0.2 kg mass is placed on it. If the mass is removed, the cube is 2 cm about the water level. What is the length of each side of the cube?

     A) 12 cm
  ✅ B) 10 cm
     C) 8 cm
     D) 6 cm

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates must set up two equations: one for full immersion with 0.2kg mass, another for floating 2cm above water without mass. Confusing which condition applies to which equation is the main trap.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Let side = a cm. Volume of cube = a³ cm³. When 2cm above water, submerged volume = a²(a-2). Buoyancy = a²(a-2)g = a³×ρ_cube×g. With 0.2kg: a³ρ_cube×g + 0.2×1000g = a³g → solving gives a = 10cm. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Archimedes principle: buoyancy = weight of fluid displaced
     - When cube floats 2cm above water: buoyancy equals weight of cube

**[Q557]** [1998]
> LMNOP, is a semi-circle with centre at R and diameter LP, LSR and RQP are also semi-circles with centres at T and U and diameters LR = RP = 1/2 LP. The ratio of perimeters of LMNOP and LSRQP is

     A) 0.75 : 1
  ✅ B) 1 : 1
     C) 1 : 0.75
     D) 1.25 : 1

  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Perimeter of large semicircle LMNOP = π×LP/2 + LP; Perimeter of LSRQP = 2 small semicircles + LP
     - Ratio equals 1:1 since total curved perimeter of 2 small semicircles = 1 large semicircle perimeter

**[Q415]** [1997]
> A rectangular plot of lawn shown in the figure has dimensions x and y and is surrounded by a gravel pathway of width 2 m. What is the total area of the pathway ?

     A) 2x + 2y + 4
     B) 2x + 2y + 8
     C) 4x+ 4y + 8
  ✅ D) 4x + 4y + 16

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often calculate only the border area as 2×(length + width)×width, forgetting the four corner squares; the correct formula accounts for the full outer rectangle minus the inner lawn.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Outer dimensions: (x+4) × (y+4). Pathway area = outer area - lawn area = (x+4)(y+4) - xy = 4x + 4y + 16 — answer D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Pathway area = Outer rectangle area − Inner lawn area = (x+4)(y+4) − xy = 4x + 4y + 16
     - Always add 2×width to each dimension (both sides) when adding a border

**[Q46]** [1995]
> In track meets both 100 yards and 100 metres are used as distances. By how many metres is 100 metres longer than 100 yards ?

     A) 0.856 m
  ✅ B) 8.56 m
     C) 0.0856 m
     D) 1.0 m

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse yards and metres; 1 yard = 0.9144 metres, so 100 yards = 91.44 m; difference = 100 - 91.44 = 8.56 m.
  💡 **Eliminate:** 100 metres − 100 yards (91.44 m) = 8.56 m; select B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 1 yard = 0.9144 metres
     - 100 yards = 91.44 metres
     - Difference: 100 m − 91.44 m = 8.56 m

**[Q722]** [1999]
> The surface area of a spherical dome-shaped roof of a cylindrical water tank shown in the figure is

     A) 60pi m2
  ✅ B) 109pi m2
     C) 120 pi m2
     D) 300pi m2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Without the figure, candidates must reconstruct the geometry; if the dome is a hemisphere of radius r and the cylinder has given dimensions, the surface area formula requires careful separation of dome from flat surfaces.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Surface area of a spherical dome (hemisphere): 2πr². If the dome has radius r=√(some given value), compute accordingly. The answer given is 109π m², implying specific dimensions in the original figure.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Surface area of hemisphere = 2πr²
     - Total surface area = curved surface of dome + any additional surfaces specified

**[Q279]** [1996]
> A rectangle has a perimeter of 50 meters. If its length is 13 metres more than its breadth, then its area is

     A) 124 m2
     B) 144 m2
  ✅ C) 114 m2
     D) 104 m2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students sometimes use diameter instead of breadth in the area formula; set up equations carefully.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Perimeter = 2(l+b) = 50, so l+b = 25. l = b+13. So b+13+b = 25, 2b = 12, b = 6, l = 19. Area = 19×6 = 114 m². Answer C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Perimeter of rectangle = 2(l+b)
     - Area of rectangle = l × b
