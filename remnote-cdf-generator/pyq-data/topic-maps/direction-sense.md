**[Q2359]** [2010]
> In a meeting, the map of a village was placed in such a manner that south-east becomes north, north-east becomes west and so on. What will south become ?

     A) North
  ✅ B) North-east
     C) North-west
     D) West

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students confuse the rotation angle; the map is rotated 135° clockwise, making south-east become north. Candidates may miscalculate which direction south maps to.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Identify the rotation: SE→N means a 135° clockwise rotation. Apply the same rotation to South (180°) to get North-East (45°).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - SE becomes N means 135° clockwise rotation
     - Each compass direction shifts by 135°

**[Q851]** [2000]
> A person starts from a point A and travels 3 km eastwards to B and then turns left and travels thrice that distance to reach C. He again turns left and travels five times the distance he covered between A and B and reaches his destination D. The shortest distance between the starting point and destination is

     A) 18 km
     B) 16 km
  ✅ C) 15 km
     D) 12 km

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The turn direction (left from eastward travel = northward) and the 5x eastward distance for final leg catches candidates who misread compass directions.
  💡 **Eliminate:** A→B: 3km East. B→C: left=North, 9km. C→D: left=West, 15km. D is at (-12, 9) relative to A. Shortest distance = √(144+81)=√225=15km. Answer C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Direction sense: turning left when facing East means turning North
     - Shortest distance by Pythagoras: √(horizontal² + vertical²)

**[Q2247]** [2010]
> A cuboid has six sides of different colours. The red side is opposite to black. The blue side is adjacent to white. The brown side is adjacent to blue. The red side is face down. Which one of the following would be the opposite to brown?

     A) Red
     B) Black
  ✅ C) White
     D) Blue

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often confuse 'adjacent' with 'opposite'. The cuboid has 3 pairs of opposite faces; given red-black and the positional clues, deriving brown's opposite requires careful spatial reasoning.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Red (down) opposite Black (up). Blue adjacent to white; brown adjacent to blue. The face opposite brown must be white (since blue-brown-white form one axis and red-black form another). Answer: C (White).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - On a cuboid, each face has exactly one opposite face
     - Adjacent means sharing an edge, not opposing

**[Q2161]** [2009]
> A person travels 12 km due North, then 15 km due East, after that 15 km due West and then 18 km due South. How far is he from the starting point?

  ✅ A) 6 km
     B) 12 km
     C) 33 km
     D) 60 km

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may add all distances; the east and west movements (15 km each) cancel out; net displacement is from 12 km North and 18 km South = 6 km South of start.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Net North-South: 12N - 18S = 6 km South; Net East-West: 15E - 15W = 0; Total displacement = 6 km South from starting point — answer is A (6 km).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - East and west distances cancel: 15E - 15W = 0
     - Net vertical displacement: 12N - 18S = 6 km South

**[Q260]** [1996]
> A man starts walking in the north-easterly direction from a particular point. After walking a distance of 500 metres, he turns southward and walks a distance of 400 metres. At the end of this walk he is situated

     A) 300 metres north of the starting point
     B) 100 metres north-east of the starting point
  ✅ C) 300 metres east of the starting point
     D) 100 metres north of the starting point

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Walking north-east at 500m then turning south 400m — candidates must resolve NE displacement into N and E components (500/√2 each ≈ 354m N and 354m E), then subtract 400m south.
  💡 **Eliminate:** NE 500m → N component = 500/√2 ≈ 354m, E component = 354m. After walking 400m south → final N = 354−400 = −46m (south), E = 354m east. Position ≈ 300m east. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - NE direction = 45° — resolve into N and E components using trigonometry (each = distance/√2)
     - Direction sense problems require vector decomposition when diagonal directions are involved
