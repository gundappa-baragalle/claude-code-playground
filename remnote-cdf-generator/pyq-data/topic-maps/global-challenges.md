**[Q3439]** [2023]
> Consider the following pairs : Regions often mentioned in news - Reason for being in news  1. North Kivu and Ituri - War between Armenia and Azerbaijan 2. Nagorno-Karabakh - Insurgency in Mozambique 3. Kherson and Zaporizhzhia - Dispute between Israel and Lebanon  How many of the above pairs are correctly matched?

     A) Only one
     B) Only two
     C) All three
  ✅ D) None

  ⚠️ **Trap:** All three pairings are wrong: North Kivu/Ituri = DRC conflict (not Armenia-Azerbaijan); Nagorno-Karabakh = Armenia-Azerbaijan conflict (not Mozambique); Kherson/Zaporizhzhia = Ukraine-Russia conflict (not Israel-Lebanon).
  💡 **Eliminate:** North Kivu/Ituri = DRC civil conflict; Nagorno-Karabakh = Armenia-Azerbaijan; Kherson/Zaporizhzhia = Ukraine war. All pairs are incorrectly matched. Answer: D (None).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - North Kivu & Ituri: eastern DRC, site of long-running armed group conflict
     - Nagorno-Karabakh: disputed between Armenia and Azerbaijan
     - Kherson & Zaporizhzhia: Ukrainian oblasts contested in Russia-Ukraine war

**[Q3308]** [2022]
> With reference to the United Nations Convention on the Law of Sea, consider the following statements :  1. A coastal state has the right to establish the breadth of its territorial sea up to a limit not exceeding 12 nautical miles, measured from baseline determined in accordance with the convention. 2. Ships of all states, whether coastal or land-locked, enjoy the right of innocent passage through the territorial sea. 3. The Exclusive Economic Zone shall not extend beyond 200 nautical miles from the baseline from which the breadth of the territorial sea is measured.  Which of the statements given above are correct?

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** All three UNCLOS provisions are standard and well-established; this question tests recall of specific UNCLOS articles rather than conceptual confusion.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three statements accurately reflect UNCLOS provisions: 12 nm territorial sea (Art 3), innocent passage for all ships (Art 17-26), 200 nm EEZ (Art 57). Answer: all three correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - UNCLOS: Territorial sea = max 12 nm; Contiguous Zone = 24 nm; EEZ = 200 nm; Continental Shelf up to 350 nm
     - Innocent passage: all ships including warships enjoy this right through territorial seas

**[Q2798]** [2018]
> Consider the following pairs : Regions sometimes mentioned in news - Country  1. Catalonia - Spain 2. Crimea - Hungary 3. Mindanao - Philippines 4. Oromia - Nigeria  Which of the pairs given above are correctly matched?

     A) 1, 2 and 3
     B) 3 and 4 only
  ✅ C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 2 and 4 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Crimea is associated with Russia (annexed 2014), not Hungary; Oromia is Ethiopia's largest region, not Nigeria — two classic traps in this question.

  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Crimea was annexed by Russia in 2014 after a disputed referendum; Ukraine and Western nations consider it occupied Ukrainian territory

**[Q2568]** [2016]
> Consider the following statements:  1. The Sustainable Development Goals were first proposed in 1972 by a global think tank called the 'Club of Rome'. 2. The Sustainable Development Goals have to be achieved by 2030.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
  ✅ B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is the trap — SDGs were proposed at Rio+20 (2012) by UN member states, not in 1972 by Club of Rome (which published 'Limits to Growth' but did not propose SDGs).
  💡 **Eliminate:** SDGs were adopted by the UN in September 2015 at the UNGA, to be achieved by 2030. Statement 1 is factually wrong. Answer: B (2 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 17 SDGs adopted by UN in 2015 at UNGA, to be achieved by 2030
     - Club of Rome (1972) published 'Limits to Growth' — NOT the same as SDGs

**[Q134]** [2015]
> Consider the following countries:  1. China 2. France 3. India 4. Israel 5. Pakistan  Which among the above are Nuclear Weapons States as recognized by the Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons, commonly known as Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty (NPT)?

  ✅ A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 1, 3, 4 and 5 only
     C) 2, 4 and 5 only
     D) 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5

  ⚠️ **Trap:** India (3), Israel (4), and Pakistan (5) are nuclear-armed states but are NOT recognized as Nuclear Weapons States (NWS) under NPT. Only 5 states are officially recognized NWS under NPT.
  💡 **Eliminate:** NPT Nuclear Weapons States (NWS): USA, Russia, UK, France, China — the P5 (UN Security Council permanent members). India, Pakistan, Israel are de facto nuclear states but NOT NPT NWS. Answer: A (China and France only from the given list).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - NPT NWS: USA, Russia, UK, France, China (only these 5)
     - India, Pakistan, Israel: nuclear-armed but not NPT parties/NWS
     - North Korea withdrew from NPT in 2003

**[2016]** Q16. South China Sea has assumed great geopolitical significance in the present context. Comment. (200 words) 12.5
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHY | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Geopolitical significance of South China Sea, India's strategic interests, Multilateral vs bilateral dispute resolution

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The South China Sea, straddling critical trade routes and rich in resources, has become a flashpoint of 21st century geopolitical contestation.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Strategic importance: 30% of global trade transits through it; abundant hydrocarbon reserves
      • Overlapping claims: China's 9-dash line vs UNCLOS-based claims of Vietnam, Philippines, Malaysia
      • 2016 Permanent Court of Arbitration ruling against China's claims — rejected by Beijing
      • India's stakes: freedom of navigation, energy security, ASEAN partnerships, and countering Chinese assertiveness
      • US Freedom of Navigation Operations (FONOPs) add great power dimension
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The South China Sea exemplifies the tension between revisionist claims and rules-based international order, with India's strategic interests demanding active diplomatic engagement.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 International Relations, GS3 Internal Security, India-China relations
  🌐 Contemporary: South China Sea tensions remain active with Chinese island-building and increased US-China rivalry post-2016.

**[2014]** Q18. Some of the International funding agencies have special terms for economic participation stipulating a substantial component of the aid to be used for sourcing equipment from the leading countries. Discuss on merits of such terms and if, there exists a strong case not to accept such conditions in the Indian context.  (200 words) 12.5
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: IMPACT | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Aid conditionality, Economic sovereignty, International financial institutions

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Tied aid conditionalities that mandate procurement from donor countries raise fundamental questions about the true developmental intent of international funding and recipient country sovereignty.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Nature of such terms: equipment/services must be sourced from donor country — prevalent in bilateral ODA and some multilateral loans
      • Merits argued by donors: ensures quality standards, reduces fiduciary risk, promotes donor country exports
      • Demerits / Indian context: inflates costs by 15-30%, limits competition, creates technology lock-in, undermines Make in India
      • Case for rejection: Paris Declaration 2005 emphasized untying aid; India's own procurement preferences conflict with such conditions
      • India's stance: generally prefers untied assistance, has declined some bilateral aid with onerous conditions
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India should assertively negotiate against tied-aid conditionalities that undermine sovereignty and cost-efficiency, leveraging its growing economic stature and South-South cooperation alternatives.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: International organizations, GS3: External sector and trade policy, GS2: India's foreign policy
  🌐 Contemporary: Debates around conditions attached to IMF/World Bank assistance and China's debt-trap diplomacy make aid conditionality perpetually relevant.
