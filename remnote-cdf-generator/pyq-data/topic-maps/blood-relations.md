**[Q2319]** [2010]
> In a group of five persons A, B, C, D and E, there is a professor, a doctor and lawyer. A and D are unmarried ladies, and do not work. Of the married couple in the Group, E is the husband. B is the brother of A and is neither a doctor nor a lawyer. Who is the professor?

  ✅ A) B
     B) C
     C) A
     D) Cannot be determined with the available data

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may get confused between who is married and who works; the puzzle requires careful elimination using all given constraints simultaneously.
  💡 **Eliminate:** B is brother of A; A, D don't work; E is husband in married couple. Among working persons (B, C, E), B is neither doctor nor lawyer → B is professor. Answer: A (B).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Elimination: B not doctor, not lawyer → B must be professor
     - A and D don't work, so the three professionals are B, C, and E

**[Q2457]** [2011]
> A married couple adopted a male child. A few years later, twin boys were born to them. The blood group of the couple is AB positive and O negative. The blood group of the three sons is A positive, B positive, and O positive. The blood group of the adopted son is

  ✅ A) O positive
     B) A positive
     C) B positive
     D) Cannot be determined on the basis of the given data

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may incorrectly try to determine the adopted son's blood group from the parents' blood groups without realizing the twin sons together exhaust the biological possibilities, leaving the adopted son as O positive.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Parents are AB+ and O−; biological twins can be A or B; since twins are A+ and B+, the remaining son with O+ must be the adopted one since O cannot come from AB parent.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - AB × O cross can produce A or B children but not AB or O
     - O blood group requires both alleles to be recessive (i, i) — cannot come from an AB parent

**[Q2194]** [2009]
> There is a family of 6 persons A, B, C, D, E and F. There are two married couples in the family. The family members are lawyer, teacher, salesman, engineer, accountant and doctor. D, the salesman is married to the lady teacher. The doctor is married to the lawyer. F, the accountant is the son of B and brother of E. C, the lawyer is the daughter-in-law of A. E is the unmarried engineer. A is the grandmother of F. How is E related to F ?

     A) Brother
     B) Sister
     C) Father
  ✅ D) Cannot be established (cannot be determined)

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The puzzle establishes E is unmarried engineer and F's brother; but the gender of E is never explicitly stated — E could be brother or sister of F; hence relationship cannot be determined.
  💡 **Eliminate:** E is stated as F's brother (implied) and unmarried; however, the gender of E is not explicitly confirmed from the puzzle — since E is unmarried engineer and gender not given, the relationship 'brother or sister' cannot be determined — answer is D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - When gender of a person in blood relation puzzle is ambiguous/unstated, 'cannot be determined' is the answer
     - E is F's sibling but gender not definitively established from given clues

**[Q766]** [2000]
> Examine the following relationships among members of a family of six persons A, B, C, D, E and F. I. The number of males equals that of females. II. A and E are sons of F. III. D is the mother of two, one boy and one girl. IV. B is the son of A. V. There is only one married couple in the family at present. Which one of the following inferences can be drawn from the above ?

     A) A, B and C are all females
  ✅ B) A is the husband of D
     C) E and F are children of D
     D) D is the grand-daughter of F

  ⚠️ **Trap:** With 3 males and 3 females and only one married couple, it's easy to misassign genders; the clue that B is son of A and A and E are sons of F helps establish the male line.
  💡 **Eliminate:** A, E = sons of F (males). B = son of A (male). D = mother of one boy, one girl = female. One married couple among adults = A married to D. F is likely female (grandmother). Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Blood relation puzzles: draw family tree, establish gender first
     - One married couple constraint limits possibilities significantly

**[Q532]** [1998]
> A, B, C, D, E, F and G are members of a family consisting of 4 adults and 3 children, two of whom, F and G are girls, A and D are brothers and A is a doctor. E is an engineer married to one of the brothers and has two children. B is married to D and G is their child. Who is C ?

     A) G’s brother
     B) F’s father
     C) E’s daughter
  ✅ D) A’s son

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The family tree has many members making it easy to lose track; students may confuse which children belong to which couple, especially with F and G both being girls.
  💡 **Eliminate:** E is married to A (doctor), D is married to B with child G. F is a girl child of E and A. C must be the remaining adult or child — trace systematically: A-E couple has 2 children, one is F (girl), so C could be F's sibling or related to A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 4 adults and 3 children in the family
     - F and G are girls among children
     - A and D are brothers; E married to one brother (A), B married to D
