**[Q790]** [2003]
> Assertion (A) : Marathas emerged as the strongest native power in India after the decline of Mughal empire. Reason (R) : Marathas were the first to have clear concept of a united Indian nation.

     A) Both A and R are individually true and R is the correct explanation of A
     B) Both A and R are individually true but R is NOT the correct explanation of A
  ✅ C) A is true but R is false
     D) A is false but R is true

  ⚠️ **Trap:** R (Marathas had concept of united Indian nation) seems historically debatable; Marathas were the strongest post-Mughal power (A true) but their motivation was Hindu Pad Padshahi, not united India.
  💡 **Eliminate:** A is true (Marathas were strongest native power after Mughals); R is false (they had no clear concept of united Indian nation — that's a modern nationalist retrospection). Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Marathas emerged as dominant power after Mughal decline in 18th century
     - Maratha confederacy: Peshwas, Sindhia, Holkar, Bhonsle, Gaekwad — but no unified national vision

**[Q56]** [1998]
> The member of Shivaji’s Astha Pradhana who looked after foreign affairs was

     A) Peshwa
     B) Sachiv
     C) Pandit Rao
  ✅ D) Sumant

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Peshwa (PM) is the most remembered Ashta Pradhan minister; students guess Peshwa for foreign affairs. Sachiv (secretary) sounds administrative. Sumant/Dabir is the least-known minister name.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Peshwa = Prime Minister. Sachiv = correspondence/letters. Pandit Rao = religious affairs. Sumant = foreign affairs/protocol (also called Dabir). Senapati = military. Lock Peshwa=PM first, then Sumant=Foreign by elimination.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Sumant (Dabir): Shivaji's foreign affairs minister in Ashta Pradhan
     - Peshwa: PM of Maratha council
     - Pandit Rao: head of religious/charity affairs
     - Senapati: commander-in-chief
