**[Q2737]** [2018]
> Regarding Wood's Dispatch, which of the following statements are true?  1. Grants-in-Aid system was introduced. 2. Establishment of universities was recommended. 3. English as a medium of instruction at all levels of education was recommended.  Select the correct answer using the code given below:

  ✅ A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 3 is the key trap — Wood's Dispatch (1854) did NOT recommend English as medium at ALL levels; it recommended vernacular at primary and English at higher levels.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Wood's Dispatch 1854: introduced grants-in-aid (statement 1 correct), recommended establishing universities (statement 2 correct), but recommended vernaculars for primary education, not English throughout (statement 3 wrong). Answer: 1 and 2.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Wood's Dispatch 1854 (Magna Carta of Indian Education): grants-in-aid, university establishment (Bombay, Calcutta, Madras)
     - Did NOT prescribe English at all levels — recommended vernaculars at primary level
     - Considered turning point in British India's education policy

**[Q1510]** [2010]
> With reference to Simon Commission’s recommendations, which one of the following statement is correct?

  ✅ A) It recommended the replacement of diarchy with responsible govt. in the provinces
     B) It proposed the setting up of interprovincial council under the Home Department
     C) It suggested the abolition of bicameral legislature at the centre
     D) It recommended the creation of Indian Police Service with a provision for increased pay and allowances for British recruits as compared to Indian recruits

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Simon Commission's recommendations included responsible government in provinces, not immediate independence; students may confuse it with full self-rule proposals.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The Simon Commission recommended replacement of diarchy (dual control) with full responsible government in provinces — this is its most important recommendation; focus on provincial autonomy as the key change.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Simon Commission was appointed in 1927 and submitted report in 1930
     - Commission had no Indian members, triggering widespread protests and Simon Go Back movement

**[Q1294]** [2008]
> Which one of the following suggested the reconstitution of the Viceroy’s Executive Council in which all the portfolios including that of War Members were to be held by the Indian leaders?

     A) Simon Commission
  ✅ B) Simla Conference
     C) Cripps Proposal
     D) Cabinet Mission

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Cabinet Mission and Cripps Proposal both dealt with Executive Council changes, making them easy to confuse with Simla Conference. The specific detail about 'War Member portfolio' distinguishes Simla.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The Simla Conference (1945) under Wavell Plan proposed that the reconstituted Executive Council would include Indian leaders in all portfolios including War Member. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Simla Conference 1945 — Wavell Plan proposed all portfolios including War Member to be held by Indians
     - Cabinet Mission (1946) dealt with constitutional arrangements, not just executive council

**[Q4827]** [2001]
> The Hunter Commission was appointed after the

     A) Black hole incident
  ✅ B) Jalianwallabagh massacre
     C) Uprising of 1857
     D) Partition of Bengal

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse Hunter Commission (1882, education) with Hunter Committee (1919, Jallianwala Bagh). There were two Hunter Commissions — the 1919 one investigated the massacre.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The Hunter Commission appointed to investigate the Jallianwala Bagh massacre (1919) is the relevant one. The 1882 Hunter Commission was about education. Answer B (Jallianwala Bagh).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Hunter Committee (1919) was appointed to inquire into the Jallianwala Bagh massacre and Punjab disturbances
     - The 1882 Hunter Commission investigated Indian education — a different Hunter Commission
