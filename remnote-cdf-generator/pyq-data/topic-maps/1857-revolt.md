**[Q1138]** [2006]
> Who was the Governor-General of India during the Sepoy Mutiny?

  ✅ A) Lord Canning
     B) Lord Dalhousie
     C) Lord Hardinge
     D) Lord Lytton

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Lord Dalhousie is associated with the period just before 1857 (Doctrine of Lapse, annexations) leading to the revolt, making him a plausible wrong answer.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Lord Canning was Governor-General during the 1857 Sepoy Mutiny and became the first Viceroy of India in 1858. Dalhousie's provocative policies preceded it but he left India before 1857.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Lord Canning was Governor-General during 1857 Revolt
     - Canning became first Viceroy of India (1858)
     - Lord Dalhousie's Doctrine of Lapse preceded 1857

**[Q1099]** [2005]
> Which one of the following places did Kunwar Singh, a prominent leader of the Revolt of 1857 belong to?

  ✅ A) Bihar
     B) Madhya Pradesh
     C) Rajasthan
     D) Uttar Pradesh

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse Kunwar Singh with UP leaders since the 1857 revolt is often associated with UP centers like Lucknow and Kanpur.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Kunwar Singh of Jagdishpur, Bihar — an 80-year-old zamindar who led the revolt in Bihar — is a well-known figure. Answer is A (Bihar).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Kunwar Singh: leader from Jagdishpur, Arrah district, Bihar in 1857 revolt
     - Led revolt at 80 years of age — known for military strategy against British

**[Q1100]** [2005]
> Which one of the following territories was not affected by the Revolt of 1857?

     A) Jhansi
  ✅ B) Chittor
     C) Jagdishpur
     D) Lucknow

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may include Chittor (Chittorgarh) in 1857 revolt as it was a historical Rajput stronghold, but Rajputana generally stayed loyal to the British in 1857.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Chittor/Chittorgarh (Rajasthan) was not affected by the 1857 revolt — Rajputana rulers largely supported the British. Jhansi, Jagdishpur (Bihar), and Lucknow were all major centres. Answer is B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 1857 revolt major centres: Delhi, Meerut, Kanpur, Lucknow, Jhansi, Arrah/Jagdishpur
     - Rajputana: largely remained loyal to British in 1857

**[Q4811]** [1999]
> Consider the following events : I. Indigo Revolt II. Santhal Rebellion III. Deccan Riot IV. Mutiny of the Sepoys The correct chronological sequence of these events is :

     A) IV, II, I, III
     B) IV, II, III, I
     C) II, IV, III, I
  ✅ D) II, IV, I, III

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Indigo Revolt (1859–60) vs Deccan Riot (1875) order is commonly confused. Santhal Rebellion (1855) predates the 1857 Mutiny — candidates often assume Mutiny came first.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Santhal Rebellion = 1855 (II); Sepoy Mutiny = 1857 (IV); Indigo Revolt = 1859–60 (I); Deccan Riot = 1875 (III). Chronological order: II, IV, I, III = Answer D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Santhal Rebellion (1855) preceded the 1857 Sepoy Mutiny by two years
     - Indigo Revolt (Neel Bidroha) was 1859–60 in Bengal; Deccan Riots were 1875 against moneylenders in Maharashtra

**[Q434]** [1998]
> The educated middle class in India

     A) opposed the revolt of 1857
     B) supported the revolt of 1857
  ✅ C) remained neutral to the revolt of 1857
     D) fought against native rulers

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may assume the educated middle class supported the revolt due to nationalist sentiment, but historically they remained neutral or even opposed it.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The educated middle class (products of English education) remained neutral in 1857 — they had a stake in British-introduced reforms and did not identify with the feudal uprising.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Educated middle class remained neutral in 1857: beneficiaries of British education and legal reforms
     - 1857 was largely a mutiny of sepoys and dispossessed zamindars, not urban educated class

### GS1

**[2019]** Q2. The 1857 Uprising was the culmination of the recurrent big and small local rebellions that had occurred in the preceding hundred years of British rule. Elucidate. (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHY | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: 1857 as culmination of resistance, Anti-colonial struggle, Causes of 1857

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The 1857 Uprising was not a sudden explosion but the volcanic culmination of a century of accumulated grievances, rebellions, and resistance against British rule.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Pre-1857 rebellions: Sanyasi, Vellore, Paika, Santhal — their common grievances
      • Economic, social, and religious causes converging by 1857
      • Immediate trigger (cartridge controversy) as spark on powder keg
      • Regional spread: proof of cumulative tension, not spontaneous eruption
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The 1857 Uprising stands as testament to the enduring spirit of resistance that had simmered through a century of colonial exploitation.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 — Modern History, History — Rise of Nationalism
  🌐 Contemporary: 1857's legacy informs understanding of tribal and agrarian resistance movements in independent India.

**[2016]** Q3. Explain how the Uprising of 1857 constitutes an important watershed in the evolution of British policies towards colonial India. (200 words) 12.5
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: IMPACT | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: British colonial policy shift, 1857 as watershed, Colonial administration

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Uprising of 1857, though suppressed militarily, fundamentally restructured the British approach to governance in India, marking a decisive departure from the East India Company's aggressive modernisation policies.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Transfer of power from Company to Crown — Queen's Proclamation 1858
      • British abandoned Doctrine of Lapse and policy of annexation of princely states
      • Non-interference in Indian social and religious customs — lessons from greased cartridge controversy
      • Reorganisation of Indian Army: reduced Indian soldiers' proportion, artillery reserved for Europeans
      • Racial policies intensified — increased segregation between British and Indians
      • Indian Councils Act 1861 — token Indian representation to prevent alienation of elites
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The 1857 Uprising succeeded not in ending British rule but in compelling the British to adopt a more cautious, conservative colonialism that paradoxically both entrenched and adapted imperial governance.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1: Modern India, Constitutional development, Rise of nationalism
  🌐 Contemporary: 1857 remains a contested symbol — 'Sepoy Mutiny' vs 'First War of Independence' — reflecting ongoing historical debates.
