**[Q4603]** [1998]
> A man purchases two clocks A and B at a total cost of Rs. 650. He sells A with 20% profit and B at a loss of 25% and gets the same selling price for both the clocks. What are the purchasing prices of A and B respectively ?

     A) Rs. 225; Rs. 425
  ✅ B) Rs. 250; Rs. 400
     C) Rs. 275; Rs. 375
     D) Rs. 300; Rs. 350

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Setting up equations is straightforward but candidates may make errors assuming equal selling prices without properly writing: 1.2A = 0.75B and A + B = 650.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Let price of A = x, then B = 650-x. Selling price equality: 1.2x = 0.75(650-x). Solve: 1.2x = 487.5 - 0.75x → 1.95x = 487.5 → x = 250. So A = Rs.250, B = Rs.400.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Profit-loss simultaneous equation: 1.2A = 0.75B with A+B=650 gives A=250, B=400
     - 20% profit on A, 25% loss on B with equal selling price

**[Q1310]** [2008] A person purchases 100 pens at a discount of 10%. The net amount of money spent by the person to purchase the pens is Rs 600. The selling expenses incurred by the person are 15% on the net cost price. What should be the selling price for 100 pens in order to earn a profit of 25%?

     A) Rs 802.50
     B) Rs 811.25
  ✅ C) Rs 862.50
     D) Rs 875

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often forget to add selling expenses to net cost price before calculating profit, or apply discount incorrectly on the listed vs net price.

**[Q4684]** [2005] Left pan of a faulty balance weights 100 gram more than its right pan. A shopkeeper keeps the weight measure in the left pan while buying goods but keeps it in the right pan while selling his goods. He uses only 1 kg weight measure. If he sells his goods at the listed cost price, what is his gain?

  ✅ A) 200/11%
     B) 100/11%
     C) 100/9%
     D) 200/9%

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Faulty balance problems require understanding that buying with weight in left pan (100g heavier) means getting less goods; selling with weight in right pan means giving less goods — double advantage.

**[Q711]** [2002] A trader fixed the price of an article in such a way that by giving a rebate of 10% on the price fixed he made a profit of 15%. If the cost of the article is Rs 72, the price fixed on it is

     A) Rs 82.80
     B) Rs 90.00
  ✅ C) Rs 92.00
     D) Rs 97.80

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse the chain of percentage calculations — 10% rebate on marked price, 15% profit on cost price; working backwards: SP = Cost × 1.15, and SP = Marked Price × 0.90.
