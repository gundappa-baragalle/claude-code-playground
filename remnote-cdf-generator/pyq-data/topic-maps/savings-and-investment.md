**[Q3602]** [2025]
> Consider the following statements :  Statement I : As regards returns from an investment in a company, generally, bondholders are considered to be relatively at lower risk than stockholders. Statement II : Bondholders are lenders to a company whereas stockholders are its owners. Statement III : For repayment purpose, bond- holders are prioritized over stockholders by a company.  Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

  ✅ A) Both Statement II and Statement III are correct and both of them explain Statement I
     B) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct and Statement I explains Statement II
     C) Only one of the Statements II and III is correct and that explains Statement I
     D) Neither Statement II nor Statement III is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may overthink whether both Statements II and III are needed or just one; the key is understanding WHY bondholders are at lower risk.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Bondholders are lenders (II) AND have repayment priority (III) — both together explain why they are at lower risk than stockholders (I). Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Bondholders are creditors; stockholders are equity owners
     - In liquidation, bondholders are paid before stockholders

**[Q3211]** [2022]
> With reference to Convertible Bonds, consider the following statements:  1. As there is an option to exchange the bond for equity, Convertible Bonds pay a lower rate of interest. 2. The option to convert to equity affords the bondholder a degree of indexation to rising consumer prices.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is subtle — equity indexation link is counterintuitive; students may reject it thinking bonds don't hedge inflation.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Statement 1 is straightforwardly true (conversion option = lower coupon). Statement 2 refers to equity's inflation-hedging property transferred to the bondholder via conversion option.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Convertible bonds pay lower interest because conversion option has intrinsic value
     - Equity acts as inflation hedge; conversion option gives bondholder indirect inflation protection

### GS3

**[2017]** Q1. Among several factors for India's potential growth, savings rate is the most effective one. Do you agree? What are the other factors available for growth potential? (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHY | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Sources of economic growth, Role of savings, Inclusive growth drivers

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** While savings rate is a critical enabler of investment-led growth, India's growth potential rests on a constellation of factors beyond savings alone.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Savings rate importance: high domestic savings reduce dependence on foreign capital and finance investment
      • But limitations: savings alone cannot drive growth without efficient financial intermediation and investment climate
      • Other factors: FDI, TFP improvements, infrastructure quality, human capital, institutional quality
      • Demographic dividend: India's young workforce can drive consumption and labor supply simultaneously
      • GST, IBC reforms, ease of doing business improve investment efficiency independent of savings
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Savings is necessary but not sufficient — sustainable growth requires synergy between savings, investment, human capital and institutional reforms.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Economy, Banking sector, Infrastructure
  🌐 Contemporary: India's savings rate decline post-COVID raises concerns about long-term investment sustainability.
