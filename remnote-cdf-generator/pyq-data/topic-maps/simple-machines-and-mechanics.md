## Prelims Questions

### 🟡 MEDIUM Priority — Full Options

**[Q150]** [1996]
> A simple machine helps a person doing

     A) less work.
  ✅ B) the same amount of work with lesser force.
     C) the same amount of work.
     D) the same amount of work much faster.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option A ('less work') is tempting because machines seem to 'save' effort. Option D ('much faster') is tempting for modern context. The key principle: machines don't reduce work, they redistribute force over distance.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Work = Force × Distance. Simple machines trade force for distance while keeping work constant. Eliminates A (less work), C (same work without context), D (not faster necessarily). Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Simple machines: lever, pulley, inclined plane, wheel, wedge, screw
     - No machine increases total work — work input ≥ work output
     - Mechanical advantage: less force over greater distance
