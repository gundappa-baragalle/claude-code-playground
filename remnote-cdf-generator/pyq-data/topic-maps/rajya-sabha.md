**[Q3032]** [2020]
> Rajya Sabha has equal powers with Lok Sabha in

     A) the matter of creating new All India Services
  ✅ B) amending the Constitution
     C) the removal of the government
     D) making cut motions

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option A (All India Services) sounds like a special Rajya Sabha power but actually Rajya Sabha needs to pass a resolution (not equal powers). Option D (cut motions) is only Lok Sabha's prerogative.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Constitutional amendments require equal powers of both Houses — no joint sitting possible for amendments. Rajya Sabha has no role in cut motions and cannot remove government.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Both Houses have equal powers in constitutional amendments under Article 368
     - Money bills and removal of government are exclusive Lok Sabha powers

**[Q2157]** [2013]
> Consider the following statements :  1. The Chairman and the Deputy Chairman of the Rajya Sabha are not the members of that House. 2. While the nominated members of the two Houses of the Parliament have no voting right in the presidential election, they have the right to vote in the election of the Vice President.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
  ✅ B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 says Chairman and Deputy Chairman are NOT members of Rajya Sabha — this is wrong; the Vice President is Chairman of RS but is NOT a member, however the Deputy Chairman IS a member of Rajya Sabha. Statement 1 is partially wrong.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Vice President (Chairman of RS) is NOT a member of RS; but Deputy Chairman IS elected from among RS members. Statement 1 is incorrect as stated. Statement 2 (nominated members vote in VP election) is correct. Answer is B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Vice President is ex-officio Chairman of Rajya Sabha but NOT its member
     - Deputy Chairman of Rajya Sabha IS an elected member of the House
     - Nominated members of Parliament can vote in VP election but NOT in Presidential election

**[Q1858]** [2012]
> Which of the following special powers have been conferred on the Rajya Sabha by the Constitution of India?

     A) To change the existing territory of a State and to change the name of a State
  ✅ B) To pass a resolution empowering the Parliament to make laws in the State List and to create one or more All India Services
     C) To amend the election procedure of the President and to determine the pension of the President after his/her retirement
     D) To determine the functions of the Election Commission and to determine the number of Election Commissioners

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Options A, C, D contain plausible-sounding but incorrect powers — changing state territory (President's bill + Parliament), determining Election Commission (Parliament, not specially Rajya Sabha) — candidates may get confused.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Rajya Sabha's special powers: (1) Article 249 — pass resolution to enable Parliament to legislate on State List subject; (2) Article 312 — create new All India Services by 2/3rd majority resolution. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 249 — Rajya Sabha resolution (2/3 majority) allows Parliament to make laws on State List for national interest
     - Article 312 — Rajya Sabha can create new All India Services by resolution
     - These are unique powers not shared by Lok Sabha

**[Q4891]** [2012]
> Consider the following statements:  1. Union Territories are not represented in the Rajya Sabha. 2. It is within the purview of the Chief Election Commissioner to adjudicate the election disputes. 3. According to the Constitution of India, the Parliament consists of the Lok Sabha and the Rajya Sabha only.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3
     C) 1 and 3
  ✅ D) None

  ⚠️ **Trap:** All three statements appear plausible; UTs being unrepresented in RS sounds logical but is wrong (Delhi, Puducherry are represented); CEC adjudicating disputes sounds reasonable but is wrong.
  💡 **Eliminate:** UTs like Delhi and Puducherry are represented in Rajya Sabha — Statement 1 wrong. CEC cannot adjudicate disputes (that's election courts/Supreme Court) — Statement 2 wrong. Parliament includes President too (Art.79) — Statement 3 wrong. Answer: D (None).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Parliament = President + Lok Sabha + Rajya Sabha (Article 79)
     - CEC cannot adjudicate election disputes — courts do that

**[Q1001]** [2001]
> Which one of the following statement correctly describes the Fourth Schedule of the Constitution of India ?

     A) It lists the distribution of power between the Union and the States
     B) It contains the languages listed in the Constitution
     C) It contains the provisions regarding the administration of tribal areas
  ✅ D) It allocates seats in the Council of States

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse schedules — Seventh Schedule has Union/State/Concurrent lists; Eighth Schedule has languages. Fourth Schedule specifically allocates Rajya Sabha seats.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Fourth Schedule = allocation of seats in Council of States (Rajya Sabha). Seventh Schedule = distribution of powers. Eighth Schedule = languages. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Fourth Schedule of the Constitution allocates Rajya Sabha seats to states
     - Eighth Schedule lists 22 recognized languages

**[Q227]** [1995] Consider the table given below : Which one of the following will fit in the place marked ‘X’ ?

     A) Ministers who are not Members of Parliament but who have to get themselves elected to either House of Parliament within six months after assuming office
     B) Not more than 20 nominated members
  ✅ C) Not more than 20 representatives of Union Territories
     D) The Attorney-General who has the right to speak and take part in the proceedings of either House of Parliament

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option B (20 nominated members) is plausible but incorrect; Rajya Sabha has 12 nominated members, not 20. Option D (Attorney-General) is wrong as AG is not a member of Parliament.

### GS2

**[2020]** Q14. Rajya Sabha has been transformed from a 'useless stepney tyre' to the most useful supporting organ in past few decades. Highlight the factors as well as the areas in which this transformation could be visible. (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Parliamentary democracy, Bicameralism, Federalism

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Rajya Sabha has evolved from a largely ceremonial body to a meaningful legislative check, especially in an era of hung parliaments and coalition politics.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Historical perception as 'stepney' — government of day could push through legislation easily
      • Post-1989 coalition era: opposition majority in RS checked Lok Sabha overreach
      • Special powers: states reorganization, creation of All India Services (Art 249, 312)
      • Role in passing key legislation and as platform for expert deliberation
      • Recent role in slowing/modifying contentious bills
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Rajya Sabha's transformation reflects the maturation of India's parliamentary democracy and the importance of deliberative bicameralism.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: Parliament, Polity: Federalism, Elections & Political Parties
  🌐 Contemporary: Ongoing debates about Rajya Sabha's role when ruling party lacks majority there remain highly pertinent.
