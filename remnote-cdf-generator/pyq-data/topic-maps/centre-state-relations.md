**[Q3659]** [2025]
> With reference to India, consider the following :  I. The Inter-State Council II. The National Security Council III. Zonal Councils  How many of the above were established as per the provisions of the Constitution of India?

  ✅ A) Only one
     B) Only two
     C) All the three
     D) None

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Inter-State Council sounds constitutional and it IS (Article 263), but Zonal Councils (statutory) and National Security Council (executive order) are NOT constitutional bodies — candidates may think all three are constitutional.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Only the Inter-State Council is established under the Constitution (Article 263). Zonal Councils = States Reorganisation Act 1956 (statutory). NSC = executive order. Only one (ISC) is constitutional. Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Inter-State Council established under Article 263 of the Constitution
     - Zonal Councils are statutory bodies under States Reorganisation Act 1956

**[Q4749]** [2025]
> Consider the following statements :  Statement I : In India, State Governments have no power for making rules for grant of concessions in respect of extraction of minor minerals even though such minerals are located in their territories.  Statement II : In India, the Central Government has the power to notify minor minerals under the relevant law.  Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

     A) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct and Statement II explains Statement I
     B) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct but Statement II does not explain Statement I
     C) Statement I is correct but Statement II is not correct
  ✅ D) Statement I is not correct but Statement II is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement I says states have no power over minor minerals — this is actually incorrect; MMDR Act gives states power to frame rules for minor mineral concessions. Statement II about Central Government notifying minor minerals is correct.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Under MMDR Act 1957: State governments DO have power to regulate minor mineral concessions in their territory (Statement I is wrong). Central Government notifies what constitutes 'minor minerals' (Statement II is correct). Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Under MMDR Act 1957, minor mineral concessions are regulated by State Governments
     - Central Government has power to define and notify which minerals are 'minor minerals'

**[Q2772]** [2018]
> If the President of India exercises his power as provided under Article 356 of the Constitution in respect of a particular State, then

     A) the Assembly of the State is automatically dissolved.
  ✅ B) the powers of the Legislature of that State shall be exercisable by or under the authority of the Parliament.
     C) Article 19 is suspended in that State.
     D) the President can make laws relating to that State.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Article 356 (President's Rule) does NOT automatically dissolve the Assembly — it can be suspended; and Article 19 (freedoms) is not suspended — only Article 358/359 suspensions apply during National Emergency.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Under Article 356, the powers of State Legislature become exercisable by Parliament (statement B) — the Assembly is suspended (not automatically dissolved) and Parliament takes over — answer B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 356 President's Rule: State Assembly may be suspended or dissolved; Parliament assumes state legislative powers
     - SR Bommai case (1994) ruled that dissolution of Assembly under Article 356 requires parliamentary approval

Removal of the Council of Ministers in the State 3. Dissolution of the local bodies  Select the correct answer using the code given below:

     A) 1 and 2 only
  ✅ B) 1 and 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students assume President's Rule automatically dissolves the Assembly and removes CoM; CoM removal is automatic (necessary consequence) but Assembly dissolution is NOT — it may only be suspended.
  💡 **Eliminate:** President's Rule: CoM removal is a NECESSARY consequence (automatic). Assembly dissolution is NOT necessary — it can be kept in suspended animation. Local body dissolution is NOT a consequence. So 1 and 3 are not necessarily consequences.

     - Local bodies (municipalities, panchayats) are NOT affected by President's Rule

### Common Trap Patterns on This Topic
- Students may pick Indian Independence Act 1947 thinking it established the new constitutional framework, or Montagu-Chelmsford 1919 for introducing dyarchy. The 1935 Act's federal scheme is the direct precursor.
- The Inter-State Council (IV) is actually a constitutional body under Article 263, so it is NOT extra-constitutional. Students often include it. Zonal Councils are statutory (not constitutional), and NDC and Governors' Conference are extra-constitutional.
- Students assume state consent is required for Parliament to enact laws implementing international treaties; Article 253 explicitly overrides this requirement.
- Statement II sounds plausible since Lok Sabha is the more powerful house; students forget that Emergency proclamations require approval of both Houses, not just Lok Sabha.
- Articles 355 and 356 are emergency-related; Article 258 transfers Union powers to states; Article 257 is specifically about states not impeding Union executive power.
