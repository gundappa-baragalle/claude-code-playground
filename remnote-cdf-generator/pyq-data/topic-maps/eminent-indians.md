**[Q2569]** [2016] A recent movie titled The Man Who Knew Infinity is based on the biography of

  ✅ A) S. Ramanujan
     B) S. Chandrasekhar
     C) S. N. Bose
     D) C. V. Raman

  ⚠️ **Trap:** S. Chandrasekhar and S.N. Bose are distinguished Indian scientists whose biographies could plausibly be subjects of biographical films, causing confusion.

**[Q54]** [2009] Match List I and List II and select the correct answer using the code given below the lists (Famous Person) | (Well-Known as) A. Anna Hazare | 1. Lawyer B. Deepak Parekh | 2. Banker C. GVK Reddy | 3. Gandhian and Social activist D. Harish Salve | 4. Industrialist

  ✅ A) A-1, B-2, C-4, D-3
     B) A-1, B-4, C-2, D-3
     C) A-3, B-2, C-4, D-1
     D) A-3, B-4, C-2, D-1

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Deepak Parekh (HDFC) and GVK Reddy (industrialist) are often confused — both are business figures. Harish Salve is commonly mistaken for a politician; Anna Hazare's correct description as 'Gandhian' not 'activist/politician' matters.

**[Q2277]** [2009] Match List I and List II and select the correct answer using the code given below the lists (Book) | (Author) A. In custody | 1. Amartya Sen B. Sea of Poppies | 2. Amitav Ghosh C. The Argumentative Indian | 3. Anita Desai D. Unaccustomed Earth | 4. Jhumpa Lahiri

     A) A-4, B-1, C-2, D-3
  ✅ B) A-4, B-2, C-1, D-3
     C) A-3, B-2, C-1, D-4
     D) A-3, B-1, C-2, D-4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse Amartya Sen's work 'In Custody' with Anita Desai — Amartya Sen wrote 'The Argumentative Indian'; 'In Custody' is by Anita Desai.

**[Q5008]** [2009] Consider the following famous names : 1. Amrita Sher-Gil 2. Bikash Bhattacharjee 3. N.S. Bendre 4. Subodh Gupta Who of the above is/are well known as artist (s) ?

     A) 1 only
     B) 1 and 4 only
     C) 2, 3 and 4 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2, 3 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may not know Subodh Gupta (contemporary artist known for metal utensil sculptures) alongside the more famous Amrita Sher-Gil; all four are well-known visual artists.

**[Q3163]** [2007] Who among the following served as the Chief Economist of the International Monetary Fund?

     A) Ashok Lahiri
     B) Sumantra Ghoshal
     C) Saumitra Chaudhuri
  ✅ D) Raghuram Rajan

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Ashok Lahiri was Chief Economic Adviser to Government of India, not IMF; Raghuram Rajan served as IMF Chief Economist before becoming RBI Governor.

**[Q4685]** [2005] Whose autobiography is the book “My music. My Life”?

     A) Pandit Shiv Kumar Sharma
     B) Ustad Amjad Ali Khan
  ✅ C) Pandit Ravi Shankar
     D) Ustad Zakir Hussain

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Zakir Hussain (tabla) and Amjad Ali Khan (sarod) are also great musicians; Pandit Ravi Shankar's autobiography is specifically 'My Music, My Life'.

### Common Trap Patterns on This Topic
- Deepak Parekh (HDFC) and GVK Reddy (industrialist) are often confused — both are business figures. Harish Salve is commonly mistaken for a politician; Anna Hazare's correct description as 'Gandhian' not 'activist/politician' matters.
- This is a highly specific current-affairs question from 2004 about a sports doubles team. Belarus is easily confused with Baltic states (Estonia, Latvia) or Russia.
- Reinhold Messner is a famous mountaineer (first to climb all 14 eight-thousanders), NOT a computer technologist. The question asks for the 'not correctly matched' pair.
- Elizabeth Hawley's association with Nepal/Himalayas is not widely known. Students may confuse her with a wildlife writer or Indian cultural writer.
- G.N. Lewis and Walter Heitler also worked at the molecular level; but Linus Pauling uniquely bridged physics, chemistry, and biology AND promoted Vitamin C.
