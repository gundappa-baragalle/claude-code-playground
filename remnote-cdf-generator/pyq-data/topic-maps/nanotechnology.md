**[Q4719]** [2015]
> With reference to the use of nano-technology in health sector, which of the following statements is/are correct?  1. Targeted drug delivery is made possible by nanotechnology. 2. Nanotechnology can largely contribute to gene therapy.  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may doubt gene therapy contribution of nanotechnology; nanoparticles are used as vectors for gene delivery making Statement 2 also correct.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Both nanotargeted drug delivery (nanoparticles as carriers) and gene therapy (nano-vectors delivering DNA/RNA) are established applications. Answer: C (Both).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Nanotechnology enables targeted drug delivery via nanoparticles attached to specific cell receptors (e.g., cancer cells)
     - Nanocarriers (liposomes, dendrimers) used in gene therapy to deliver genetic material into cells

**[Q4913]** [2014]
> There is some concern regarding the nanoparticles of some chemical elements that are used by the industry in the manufacture of various products. Why?  1. They can accumulate in the environment, and contaminate water and soil. 2. They can enter the food chains. 3. They can trigger the production of free radicals.  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may think nanotechnology concerns are only about accumulation in the environment, missing the food chain entry and free radical production as separate, additional concerns.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three concerns are valid: nanoparticles persist in environment (1), enter food chains due to tiny size (2), can trigger free radical production causing oxidative stress (3). Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Nanoparticles: tiny size allows food chain entry and environmental persistence
     - Nanoparticles can cause oxidative stress by triggering free radical production

**[Q1840]** [2012]
> Graphene is frequently in news recently. What is its importarice?  1. It is a two-dimensional material and has good electrical conductivity. 2. It is one of the thinnest but strongest materials tested so far. 3. It is entirely made of silicon and has high optical transparency. 4. It can be used as conducting electrodes' required for touch screens, LCDs and organic LEDs.  Which of the statements given above are correct?

     A) 1 and 2. only
     B) 3 and 4 only
  ✅ C) 1, 2 and 4 only
     D) 1, 2, 3 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 3 (graphene made entirely of silicon) is the key false statement — graphene is made entirely of CARBON arranged in hexagonal lattice, not silicon (which is used in semiconductors).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Graphene = single layer of carbon atoms in 2D hexagonal lattice; it is NOT silicon-based (silicon is used in conventional semiconductors); statements 1, 2, and 4 are correct; statement 3 is false. Answer: C (1, 2 and 4).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Graphene — single atom thick layer of carbon (not silicon), strongest material tested
     - Graphene has excellent electrical conductivity (better than copper)
     - Used in touch screens, flexible electronics, LCDs due to transparency and conductivity

**[Q3410]** [2023] Consider the following statements :  1. Carbon fibres are used in the manufacture of components used in automobiles and aircrafts. 2. Carbon fibres once used cannot be recycled.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 ('cannot be recycled') is false — carbon fibres can be recycled through pyrolysis, solvolysis, and mechanical grinding; this is an active area of sustainable materials research.

**[Q151]** [2022] Consider the following statements:  1. Other than those made by humans, nanoparticles do not exist in nature. 2. Nanoparticles of some metallic oxides are used in the manufacture of some cosmetics. 3. Nanoparticles of some commercial products which enter the environment are unsafe for humans.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 3 only
     C) 1 and 2
  ✅ D) 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is wrong—nanoparticles occur naturally (sea spray, volcanic ash, forest fires); many candidates assume all nanoparticles are man-made.

**[Q2997]** [2020] With reference to carbon nanotubes, consider the following statements:  1. They can be used as carriers of drugs and antigens in the human body. 2. They can be made into artificial blood capillaries for an injured part of human body. 3. They can be used in biochemical sensors. 4. Carbon nanotubes are biodegradable.  Which of the statements given above are correct?

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 2, 3 and 4 only
  ✅ C) 1, 3 and 4 only
     D) 1, 2, 3 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 4 about biodegradability is the trap — carbon nanotubes are NOT easily biodegradable; this is actually an environmental concern. Statement 2 about artificial blood capillaries is also questionable.

**[Q2147]** [2008] How can the height of a person who is six feet tall be expressed (approximately) in nanometers?

     A) 183 x 106 nanometres
     B) 234 x 106 nanometres
  ✅ C) 183 x 107 nanometres
     D) 234 x 107 nanometres

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse 10^6 (million) nanometres with 10^7 — 1 foot = ~30.48 cm = ~0.3048 m = 304,800,000 nm; 6 feet = 1,828,800,000 nm ≈ 183 × 10^7 nm.

**[2025]** Q15. How does nanotechnology offer significant advancements in the field of agriculture? How can this technology help to uplift the socio-economic status of farmers? (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Technology in agriculture, Sustainable farming, Farmer income enhancement

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Nanotechnology offers a paradigm shift in agriculture by enabling precision delivery of inputs, real-time monitoring, and improved crop protection at nanoscale.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Nano-fertilizers: slow-release nanoparticles improve nutrient use efficiency, reduce soil degradation
      • Nano-pesticides: targeted delivery reduces chemical runoff, environmental harm, and resistance
      • Nano-sensors: real-time monitoring of soil health, moisture, pathogens for precision farming
      • Plant nano-bionics: enhancing photosynthesis efficiency using carbon nanotubes
      • Socio-economic impact: reduced input costs improve farmer margins and land productivity
      • Risks: nano-particle toxicity, environmental persistence, regulatory gaps need attention
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** By enabling precision agriculture with reduced inputs and higher yields, nanotechnology holds transformative potential to improve both agricultural sustainability and farmers' economic wellbeing.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3: Food security, GS2: Agricultural policy, Economy: farmer income doubling
  🌐 Contemporary: Relevant to India's precision farming push and doubling farmer income goals.

**[2020]** Q5. What do you understand by nanotechnology and how is it helping in health sector? (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Science and technology for healthcare, Nanotechnology applications, Future of medicine

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Nanotechnology, operating at the scale of individual atoms and molecules (1–100 nm), is transforming medicine by enabling precise, targeted, and minimally invasive healthcare interventions.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Definition: Manipulation of matter at nanoscale to exploit unique physical, chemical, and biological properties
      • Drug delivery: Nanoparticles carry drugs directly to diseased cells (e.g., cancer tumours), reducing systemic toxicity and improving efficacy of chemotherapy
      • Diagnostics: Quantum dots and nano-biosensors enable early disease detection; nanoparticle contrast agents improve MRI and imaging
      • Wound care: Silver nanoparticle dressings have antimicrobial properties accelerating wound healing
      • Vaccine development: Lipid nanoparticles (LNPs) enabled mRNA COVID-19 vaccines — a landmark nanotechnology success
      • India's initiatives: National Mission on Nano Science and Technology (NanoMission) under DST
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Nanotechnology is poised to revolutionise healthcare by making treatments more precise, diagnostics more sensitive, and therapeutic interventions less invasive.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3: Science and technology, biotechnology, GS2: Health policy, Environment: nano-pollution concerns
  🌐 Contemporary: Lipid nanoparticles enabled COVID-19 mRNA vaccines — a defining nanotechnology breakthrough.

**[2016]** Q13. Why is nanotechnology one of the key technologies of the 21st century? Describe the salient features of Indian Government's Mission on Nanoscience and Technology and the scope of its application in the development process of the country. (200 words) 12.5
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHY | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Technology-led development, Basic sciences application, Innovation ecosystem

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Nanotechnology's ability to manipulate matter at atomic scales confers extraordinary properties to materials, making it transformative across medicine, agriculture, energy, and defence sectors.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Key features of Nano Mission: basic research support, infrastructure, human resource development
      • Applications: targeted drug delivery, nano-fertilizers, water purification membranes, solar cells
      • India's focus areas: healthcare, agriculture, water, energy under Nano Mission
      • Scope for development: affordable diagnostics, durable construction materials, efficient catalysts
      • Challenges: toxicity concerns, regulatory gaps, commercialization barriers
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Realizing nanotechnology's developmental potential requires bridging the gap between laboratory research and industrial application through dedicated policy support.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Biotechnology, Healthcare, Agriculture, Energy
  🌐 Contemporary: Nano-enabled COVID vaccines (lipid nanoparticles) demonstrated real-world impact of nanotechnology.
