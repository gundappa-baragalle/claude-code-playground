**[Q2704]** [2007]
> Consider the following statements: 1. The mode of removal of a Judge of a High Court in India is same as that of removal of a Judge of the Supreme Court. 2. After retirement from the office, a permanent Judge of a High Court cannot plead or act in any court or before any authority in India. Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is a classic trap — retired HC judges are barred from practicing only in THAT High Court, not in all courts/authorities in India.
  💡 **Eliminate:** HC Judge removal: same procedure as SC Judge (by Parliament through impeachment) — statement 1 correct. Retired HC Judge is barred only from practicing in that same HC, not all courts — statement 2 wrong.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - HC Judge removal: same as SC Judge — Address by Parliament (impeachment)
     - Retired permanent HC Judge: barred from practicing ONLY before that High Court
     - Retired HC judge can practice in SC, other HCs, and other authorities

**[Q1119]** [2006]
> Assertion (A) : In India, every State has a High Court in its territory. Reason (R) : The Constitution of India provides for a High Court in each State.

     A) Both A and R are individually true R is the correct explanation of A.
     B) Both A and R are individually true R is not the correct explanation of A.
     C) A is true but R is false.
  ✅ D) A is false but R is true.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Assertion A states every State has a High Court in its territory — this is FALSE as some states share High Courts (e.g., Punjab & Haryana share Chandigarh HC). Reason R states Constitution provides HC in each State — also misleading.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Not every state has its own High Court — some share (Punjab-Haryana, Maharashtra-Goa). Assertion A is false. The Constitution allows for common HCs for two or more states. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 214: Each State shall have a High Court — but Parliament can establish common HC (Article 231)
     - Punjab and Haryana share a common High Court at Chandigarh
     - Some UTs also fall under jurisdiction of nearby State HCs

**[Q4962]** [2005]
> Consider the following statement: 1. There are 25 High Courts in India. 2. Punjab, Haryana and the Union Territory of Chandigarh have a common High Court. 3. National Capital Territory of Delhi has a High Court of its own. Which of the above statements is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 2 and 3
     B) 1 and 2
     C) 1, 2 and 3
     D) 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 about 25 High Courts was false in 2005 (there were 21 then); as of 2024 there are 25, so this statement is now true but was wrong when asked. Statement 2 (Punjab, Haryana, Chandigarh) and 3 (Delhi HC) are correct.
  💡 **Eliminate:** In 2005, there were 21 High Courts (not 25), making statement 1 wrong; statements 2 and 3 are correct — answer is A (2 and 3).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Currently India has 25 High Courts (as of 2019 with Andhra Pradesh HC)
     - Punjab and Haryana share a common High Court at Chandigarh

**[Q1212]** [2004]
> Consider the following statements: 1. The highest criminal court of the district is the Court of District and Sessions Judge. 2. The District Judges are appointed by the Governor in consultation with the High Court. 3. A person to be eligible for appointment as a District Judge should be an advocate or a pleader of seven years’ standing or more, or an officer in judicial service of the Union or the State. 4. When the Sessions Judge awards death sentence, it must be confirmed by the High Court before it is carried out. Which of the statements given above are correct?

     A) 1 and 2
     B) 2, 3 and 4
     C) 3 and 4
  ✅ D) 1, 2, 3, and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 3 says 'seven years standing' — the actual qualification is seven years as advocate; an 'officer in judicial service' requirement may be confused with Union/State distinction.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All four statements are correct: District Judges are highest criminal courts; appointed by Governor with HC consultation; 7-year advocate standing; HC must confirm death sentences — Option D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - District Judge: highest criminal court (Sessions Court) in district
     - Death sentence by Sessions Judge MUST be confirmed by High Court

**[Q1170]** [2003]
> Which one of the following High Courts has the Territorial Jurisdiction over Andaman and Nicobar Islands?

     A) Andhra Pradesh
  ✅ B) Calcutta
     C) Madras
     D) Orissa

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Andaman & Nicobar Islands are geographically close to both the Madras and Calcutta jurisdictions. Students may assume Madras HC (Chennai) due to proximity to South India/Bay of Bengal.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Andaman & Nicobar Islands fall under the territorial jurisdiction of the Calcutta High Court (historically administered from Bengal). Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Andaman & Nicobar Islands: under Calcutta High Court jurisdiction
     - Historical administrative link to Bengal Presidency
     - Lakshadweep: under Kerala High Court; Puducherry: under Madras HC

**[Q725]** [2002]
> The salaries and allowances of the Judges of the High Court are charged do the

     A) Consolidated Fund of India
  ✅ B) Consolidated Fund of the State
     C) Contingency Fund of India
     D) Contingency Fund of the State

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates often confuse this with Supreme Court judges (charged to Consolidated Fund of India). High Court judges' salaries are charged to the Consolidated Fund of the STATE, not India.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Article 202(3): High Court judges' salaries and allowances are charged to the Consolidated Fund of the respective State. Supreme Court judges are charged to Consolidated Fund of India. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - HC judges' salaries → Consolidated Fund of the State (Article 202)
     - SC judges' salaries → Consolidated Fund of India (Article 112)

**[Q2513]** [2016] With reference to the 'Gram Nyayalaya Act', which of the following statements is/are correct?  1. As per the Act, Gram Nyayalayas can hear only civil cases and not criminal cases. 2. The Act allows local social activists as mediators/reconciliators.  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

  ✅ B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 incorrectly limits Gram Nyayalayas to civil cases — they handle both civil and criminal (minor) cases; this misconception is a standard trap.

**[Q3344]** [2009] Consider the following statements : 1. The Advocate General of a State in India is appointed by the President ot India upon the recommendation of the Governor of the concerned State. 2. As provided in Civil Procedure Code, High Courts have original, appellate and advisory jurisdiction at the State level. Which of the statements given above is/are correct ?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
  ✅ D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 says Advocate General is appointed by the President on Governor's recommendation — but it is the Governor (not President) who appoints the Advocate General. Statement 2 says High Courts have advisory jurisdiction under CPC — but advisory jurisdiction is not from CPC.

**[Q2962]** [2008] How many High Courts in India have jurisdiction over more than one State (Union Territories not included)?

     A) 2
  ✅ B) 3
     C) 4
     D) 5

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may think there are more or fewer High Courts with multi-state jurisdiction. The three are specific — Bombay (Maharashtra, Goa, Dadra & NH, Daman & Diu), Kolkata (West Bengal, Andaman & Nicobar — but AN is UT), Guwahati (multiple NE states).

**[Q1150]** [2006] Consider the following statements: I. A person who has held office as a permanent Judge of a High Court cannot pleaded or act in any court or before any authority in India except the Supreme Court. II. A person is not qualified for appointment as a Judge of a High Court in India unless he has for at least five years held a judicial office in the territory of India. Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) I only
     B) II only
     C) Both I and II
  ✅ D) Neither I nor II

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement I about permanent HC judges not pleading before any court except SC sounds correct — but they can practice before other High Courts (not the one from which they retired). Statement II about 5-year judicial office qualification is one of two qualifications (the other is 10-year HC advocate).

**[Q700]** [1996] When the Chief Justice of a High Court acts in an administrative capacity, he is subject to

  ✅ A) the writ jurisdiction of any of the other judges of the High Court
     B) special control exercised by the Chief Justice of India
     C) discretionary powers of the Governor of the state
     D) special powers provided to the Chief Minister in this regard

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may think the Chief Justice of India supervises all High Court CJs or that the Governor has administrative control; the correct answer involves the unique principle that CJ's administrative acts are subject to judicial review by fellow judges.
