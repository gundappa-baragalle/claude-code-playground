**[Q4905]** [2014]
> The Radcliffe Committee was appointed to

     A) solve the problem of minorities in India
     B) give effect to the Independence Bill
  ✅ C) delimit the boundaries between India and Pakistan
     D) enquire into the riots in East Bengal

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse Radcliffe Committee with Boundary Commission; also may confuse it with Mountbatten's role in partition, thinking Radcliffe solved minority problems.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Radcliffe Committee (Boundary Commission) was specifically appointed to demarcate the boundaries between India and Pakistan during partition in 1947; Sir Cyril Radcliffe headed it.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Radcliffe Committee = Boundary Commission for India-Pakistan partition (1947)
     - Sir Cyril Radcliffe drew the Radcliffe Line in 5 weeks

**[Q1020]** [2005]
> Consider the following statements : 1. In the First Round Table Conference, Dr. Ambedkar demanded separate electorates for the depressed classes. 2. In the Poona Act, special provisions for representation of the depressed people in the local bodies and civil services were made. 3. The Indian National Congress did not take part in the Third Round Table Conference. Which of the statements is/are correct?

     A) 1 and 2
     B) 2 and 3
     C) 1 and 3
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 3 is tricky — Congress boycotted the Second Round Table Conference (after Gandhi-Irwin Pact), not the Third. Congress did not participate in the Third RTC as well.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Ambedkar demanded separate electorates at 1st RTC (1 = correct). Poona Pact included civil service provisions (2 = correct). Congress did not attend Third RTC (3 = correct — they boycotted it). All three correct → Answer D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Dr. Ambedkar demanded separate electorates for depressed classes at First RTC (1930)
     - Poona Pact (1932) replaced separate electorates with reserved seats

**[Q689]** [2002]
> With reference to the Indian freedom struggle, which one of the following statements is not correct?

     A) Hakim Ajmal Khan was one of the leaders to start a nations list and militant Ahrar movement
     B) When the Indian National Congress was formed, Sayyid Ahmad Khan opposed it
  ✅ C) The All-India Muslim League which was formed in 1906 vehemently opposed the partition of Bengal and separate electorates
     D) Maulana Barkaullah and Maulana Obeidullah Sindhi were among those who formed a Provisional Government of India in Kabul

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement C is the incorrect one — the All India Muslim League (formed 1906) actually SUPPORTED the partition of Bengal and separate electorates, not opposed them; this is a commonly tested reversal.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Muslim League was formed partly to protect Muslim interests and SUPPORTED separate electorates (Morley-Minto reforms 1909) and did NOT oppose Bengal's partition. Statement C is incorrect. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Muslim League (1906) supported partition of Bengal and demanded separate electorates
     - Khudai Khidmatgars (Red Shirts) under Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan opposed British rule
     - Maulana Barkaullah established Provisional Government of India in Kabul (1915)

**[Q706]** [2002]
> The last opportunity to avoid the partition of India was lost with the rejection of

     A) Cripps Mission
     B) Rajagopalachari Formula
  ✅ C) Cabinet Mission
     D) Wavell Plan

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The Rajagopalachari Formula and Wavell Plan also failed but the Cabinet Mission Plan (1946) is specifically considered the last real opportunity for a united India before partition became inevitable.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The Cabinet Mission Plan (1946) proposed a united India with a three-tier federal structure; its rejection by both Congress and League made partition inevitable. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Cabinet Mission Plan (1946) was the last proposal for a united India with a federal structure
     - Both INC and Muslim League's rejection/acceptance disagreements led to its failure

**[Q1822]** [2002]
> The President of India National Congress as the time of partition of i India was

     A) C Rajagopalachari
  ✅ B) J B Kripalani
     C) Jawaharlal Nehru
     D) Maulana Abul Kalam Azad

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Jawaharlal Nehru and Maulana Azad are more prominent names associated with Congress leadership, leading many to select them; J.B. Kripalani was the Congress president at the time of partition in 1947.
  💡 **Eliminate:** J.B. Kripalani (Acharya Kripalani) was Congress President from 1946 to 1947, at the time of partition; Maulana Azad was President 1940–1946 preceding him; Nehru became PM in 1947.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - J.B. Kripalani was INC President at time of partition (1946-47)
     - Maulana Abul Kalam Azad was Congress President 1940-1946
     - Nehru became India's first Prime Minister, not Congress President at partition

**[Q3195]** [2000]
> As an alternative to the partition of India, Gandhiji suggested to Mountbatten that he

     A) postpone granting of independence
  ✅ B) invite Jinnah to form the government
     C) invite Nehru and Jinnah to form the government together
     D) invite the army to take over for some time

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may choose 'invite Nehru and Jinnah to form government together' as Gandhi's reasonable alternative; but Gandhi specifically suggested inviting Jinnah alone to form government to give him his demand without partition.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Gandhi's proposal to Mountbatten: invite Jinnah to form government at the Centre, giving him effective power without dividing the country — answer is B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Gandhi proposed inviting Jinnah to form the government at Centre as alternative to partition
     - Gandhi believed giving Jinnah power would make Pakistan unnecessary

**[Q397]** [1998]
> When the Indian Muslim League was inducted into the interim government in 1946, Liyaqat Ali Khan was assigned the portfolio of

     A) Foreign affairs
     B) Home
  ✅ C) Finance
     D) Defence

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Finance portfolio is counterintuitive for Muslim League's interests; Home or Defence seems more strategically important, making Finance a trap.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Liaquat Ali Khan took Finance deliberately — it gave him control over budgets and could be used to limit Hindu industrialist funding; this was seen as a strategic move.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Liaquat Ali Khan: Finance portfolio in Interim Government 1946
     - Used Finance to present 'poor man's budget' targeting Hindu capitalists

**[Q402]** [1998]
> The Indian National Congress agreed in 1947 to the partition of the country mainly because

     A) the principle of two-Nation theory was then acceptable to them
     B) it was imposed by the British Government and the Congress was helpless in this regard
  ✅ C) they wanted to avoid large-scale communal riots
     D) India would have otherwise lost the opportunity to attain freedom

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option D (opportunity for freedom) sounds plausible; option B (imposed by British) is a common misconception about the Congress's agency in partition.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Historians and the question's answer key indicate Congress agreed to partition primarily to avoid large-scale communal violence, not because partition was the only path to freedom.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Congress accepted Partition: to prevent large-scale communal riots threatening millions of lives
     - Mountbatten Plan (June 3, 1947) — Congress accepted partition for peace

**[Q1717]** [1998]
> Lord Mountbatten came to India as Viceroy along with specific instructions to

     A) balkanize the Indian sub-continent
  ✅ B) keep India united if possible
     C) accept Jinnah’s demand for Pakistan
     D) persuade the Congress to accept partition

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option A (balkanize) sounds dramatic and matches Britain's 'divide and rule' narrative; option D (persuade Congress) seems logical but misrepresents Mountbatten's actual brief.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Historical records show Mountbatten's instructions from Attlee were to keep India united if possible; only if unity failed was partition to be considered — favour option B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Mountbatten's official brief from Attlee: transfer power keeping India united if possible
     - Mountbatten arrived in India March 1947; partition announced June 1947

**[Q334]** [1997]
> The Poona Pact which was signed between the British Government and Mahatma Gandhi in 1932 provided for

     A) the creation of dominion status for India
     B) separate electorates for the Muslims
     C) separate electorate for the Harijans
  ✅ D) joint electorate with reservation for Harijans

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option C (separate electorates for Harijans) is exactly what MacDonald Award offered and what Gandhi opposed — students may select C thinking the Pact confirmed it.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Poona Pact (1932) reversed the Communal Award: instead of separate electorates for Harijans (which Gandhi opposed), it created JOINT electorates with reserved seats for Harijans. Select D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Poona Pact 1932 — joint electorates with reserved seats for Depressed Classes
     - Gandhi's fast-unto-death forced reversal of MacDonald's Communal Award

**[Q271]** [1996]
> Which one of the following is not correct about the Cabinet Mission Plan ?

     A) Provincial grouping
     B) Interim Cabinet of Indians
  ✅ C) Acceptance of Pakistan
     D) Constitution framing right

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may think the Cabinet Mission Plan accepted Pakistan since it discussed provincial groupings (A, B, C groups) which could seem like partitioning. The plan explicitly rejected Pakistan.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The Cabinet Mission Plan (1946) explicitly rejected Pakistan. It proposed a three-tier federal structure: Union, Groups (A/B/C), and Provinces — without partition. Correct: C (acceptance of Pakistan is NOT part of CMP).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Cabinet Mission Plan 1946 — rejected Pakistan; proposed loose confederation with three-tier structure
     - Three groups: A (Hindu-majority), B (Muslim NW), C (Muslim NE + Assam)

**[Q230]** [1995]
> In the interim government formed in 1946, the Vice-President of the Executive Council was

  ✅ A) Jawaharlal Nehru
     B) Dr. S. Radhakrishnan
     C) C. Rajagopalachari
     D) Dr. Rajendra Prasad

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse Nehru's position with that of the President of Congress in 1946; he was Vice-President of the Executive Council (equivalent to PM), not formally President of Congress at that time.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Jawaharlal Nehru was the Vice-President of the Interim Government's Executive Council (September 1946), effectively India's first Prime Minister before Independence. Correct: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Interim Government formed September 1946; Nehru as VP of Executive Council
     - Viceroy Wavell remained as Governor-General; Nehru headed the council

**[2019]** Q12. Assess the role of British imperial power in complicating the process of transfer of power during the 1940s. (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: British role in partition, Transfer of power 1940s, Communal violence and political failure

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** British imperial power, through deliberate policy choices and strategic miscalculations in the 1940s, significantly complicated the process of power transfer, contributing to the tragic partition of India.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Communal Award 1932 — entrenched communal representation
      • Wavell's pessimism and breakdown plan — 'Operation Madhouse'
      • Mountbatten's rushed timeline — originally June 1948 moved to August 1947
      • British failure to control communal violence during partition
      • Cabinet Mission Plan — structural ambiguity exploited by both Congress and League
      • Radcliffe Award — hasty boundary drawing without field surveys
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** While Indian political divisions provided the context, British imperial decisions in the 1940s turned a difficult transfer of power into a catastrophic partition.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 — Communal politics, GS2 — Constitutional development
  🌐 Contemporary: India-Pakistan relations continue to be shaped by the legacy of partition.
