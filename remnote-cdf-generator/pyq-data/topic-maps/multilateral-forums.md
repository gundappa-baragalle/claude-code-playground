**[Q3445]** [2023]
> Consider the following statements about G-20:  1. The G-20 group was originally established as a platform for the Finance Ministers and Central Bank Governors to discuss the international economic and financial issues. 2. Digital public infrastructure is one of India's G-20 priorities.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Both statements are correct; students may doubt statement 1 (G-20 origins) or statement 2 (India's DPI priority), but both are factually accurate.
  💡 **Eliminate:** G-20 was established in 1999 for Finance Ministers/Central Bank Governors; India's G-20 Presidency (2023) prioritized Digital Public Infrastructure. Both correct. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - G-20 established 1999 for Finance Ministers; elevated to Leaders' Summit level after 2008 financial crisis
     - India's G-20 priorities (2023): Digital Public Infrastructure, green development, inclusive growth, women-led development

**[Q3268]** [2022]

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse G20 Common Framework with HIPC (Heavily Indebted Poor Countries) initiative or Paris Club's standalone debt relief mechanisms.

     - It extends debt treatment to Low Income Countries (LICs) beyond DSSI

**[Q2821]** [2018]
> What is/are the consequence/consequences of a country becoming the member of the 'Nuclear Suppliers Group'?  1. It will have access to the latest and most efficient nuclear technologies. 2. It automatically becomes a member of "The Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons (NPT)".  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is a classic trap — NSG membership does NOT automatically make a country an NPT member; India itself is an NSG supplier group observer but is NOT an NPT signatory.
  💡 **Eliminate:** NSG membership gives access to advanced nuclear technologies (statement 1 correct); NSG membership does NOT automatically confer NPT membership — India is a clear example (statement 2 wrong) — answer A (1 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - NSG (Nuclear Suppliers Group) is a group of 48 nuclear supplier countries controlling nuclear technology exports
     - India is NOT an NPT signatory but received a waiver from NSG in 2008 for civilian nuclear trade (India-US nuclear deal)

**[Q2683]** [2017]

  ✅ A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 1 and 3 only
     C) 2 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 3 is the trap — TFA actually came into force in February 2017 (not January 2016). Candidates may assume it came into force around the time of ratification announcements.
  💡 **Eliminate:** India ratified TFA (statement 1 correct); TFA is part of Bali Ministerial Package 2013 (statement 2 correct); TFA came into force February 22, 2017, not January 2016 (statement 3 wrong). Answer is A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Trade Facilitation Agreement: part of WTO Bali Ministerial Package, December 2013
     - India ratified TFA in April 2016
     - TFA entered into force February 22, 2017 (after 2/3 WTO members ratified)
