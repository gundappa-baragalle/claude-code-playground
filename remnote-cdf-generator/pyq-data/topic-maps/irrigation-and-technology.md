**[Q3047]** [2020]
> What is/are the advantage/advantages of zero tillage in agriculture ?  1. Sowing of wheat is possible without burning the residue of previous crop. 2. Without the need for nursery of rice saplings, direct planting of paddy seeds in the wet soil is possible. 3. Carbon sequestration in the soil is possible.  Select the correct answer using the code given below:

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 about direct paddy seeding without nursery might seem like a separate practice (DSR — Direct Seeded Rice), but zero tillage does facilitate this in appropriate conditions.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Zero tillage advantages: (1) wheat sowing without burning residue — correct; (2) direct paddy planting (DSR technique compatible with zero tillage) — correct; (3) carbon sequestration — correct. All three are genuine advantages.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Zero tillage reduces stubble burning and allows direct wheat sowing after rice harvest
     - Zero tillage promotes carbon sequestration by minimizing soil disturbance

**[Q2656]** [2017]
> Which of the following practices can help in water conservation in agriculture ?  1. Reduced or zero tillage of the land 2. Applying gypsum before irrigating the field 3. Allowing crop residue to remain in the field  Select the correct answer using the code given below:

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 3 only
  ✅ C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 (gypsum application) is the trap — gypsum improves soil structure and reduces waterlogging but doesn't directly conserve irrigation water in the way statements 1 and 3 do.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Zero/reduced tillage conserves soil moisture by reducing evaporation; crop residue improves water retention. Gypsum primarily treats sodic soils, not primarily for water conservation. Statements 1 and 3 are correct — answer is C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Zero tillage reduces soil moisture loss through evaporation
     - Crop residue mulching reduces surface runoff and evaporation
     - Gypsum is used for sodic/alkaline soil reclamation, not water conservation per se

**[Q4919]** [2016]
> Which of the following is/are the advantage/advantages of practising drip irrigation?  1. Reduction in weed 2. Reduction in soil salinity 3. Reduction in soil erosion  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 3 only
  ✅ C) 1 and 3 only
     D) None of the above is an advantage of practising drip irrigation

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 about soil salinity reduction is tricky — drip irrigation actually helps avoid surface evaporation-induced salinity but does not directly reduce existing soil salinity.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Drip irrigation reduces weed growth (1-correct, less water for weeds) and soil erosion (3-correct, no surface runoff); but it does NOT significantly reduce soil salinity (2-wrong). Answer: C (1 and 3 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Drip irrigation reduces weeds (targeted water delivery) and soil erosion (no runoff)
     - Drip irrigation doesn't directly reduce soil salinity; can even concentrate it near roots

**[Q4925]** [2011]
> With reference to micro-irrigation, which of the following statements is/are correct?  1. Fertilizer/nutrient loss can be reduced. 2. It is the only means of irrigation in dry land farming. 3. In some areas of farming, receding of ground water table can be checked.  Select the correct answer using the codes given below :

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
  ✅ C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 — micro-irrigation as the only means in dryland farming — is clearly an overstatement; dryland farming uses rainfall, not just micro-irrigation.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Micro-irrigation reduces fertilizer loss (1-correct, fertigation concept), and helps check groundwater depletion in areas (3-correct); but it is NOT the only irrigation means in dryland farming (2-wrong). Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Micro-irrigation allows fertigation — reduces nutrient loss
     - Micro-irrigation helps check groundwater depletion by efficient water use

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is the trap — Rock Phosphate cannot be efficiently applied through fertigation as it is insoluble in water; only water-soluble fertilizers work in drip fertigation systems.

**[2024]** Q13. What are the major challenges faced by Indian irrigation system in recent times? State the measures taken by the government for efficient irrigation management. (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Agricultural water management, Irrigation efficiency, Water security

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's irrigation system, despite being the world's largest by coverage, faces mounting challenges of inefficiency, inequitable distribution, and deteriorating infrastructure.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Challenges: low water use efficiency (40-60% in surface irrigation), groundwater over-extraction, canal silting, inter-state water disputes, inadequate maintenance
      • Widening gap between potential created and utilized (CAD backlog)
      • Government measures: PMKSY — Accelerated Irrigation Benefits Programme, Har Khet Ko Paani, Per Drop More Crop components
      • National Micro Irrigation Fund, command area development
      • Jal Shakti Abhiyan for water conservation
      • Long-term: water pricing reform, participatory irrigation management
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Modernizing India's irrigation system requires shifting from canal-expansion to efficiency improvement, integrating micro-irrigation, and empowering water user associations.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Water security, Food security, Climate change adaptation
  🌐 Contemporary: Declining groundwater tables and erratic monsoons are making irrigation efficiency an existential agricultural challenge.

**[2021]** Q4. How and to what extent would micro-irrigation help in solving India’s water crisis? (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Water crisis solution, Agricultural water management, Micro-irrigation policy

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Micro-irrigation offers the most effective technological solution to India's water crisis in agriculture, dramatically improving water productivity per unit of land.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • India's water crisis: groundwater depletion (~0.5m/year in Punjab/Haryana), river water over-extraction, monsoon dependency
      • Micro-irrigation contribution: drip reduces water use by 30-70%; sprinkler by 25-50% vs flood irrigation
      • Extent: ~13 million ha under micro-irrigation; potential area ~69 million ha — huge scope
      • PMKSY Per Drop More Crop: subsidized drip and sprinkler installation; preferential for small farmers
      • Fertigation advantage: soluble fertilizers applied through drip reduces fertilizer wastage
      • Limitations: high capital cost, technical maintenance, water quality requirements for drip emitters
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Scaling micro-irrigation to its full potential through subsidies, awareness, and convergence with Atal Bhujal Yojana can significantly ease India's agricultural water crisis.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Agriculture, GS1 – Physical Geography, GS2 – Government Schemes
  🌐 Contemporary: India's groundwater crisis and PMKSY expansion make micro-irrigation a central policy priority.

  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Water conservation, Agricultural efficiency, Sustainable irrigation

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** With India facing increasing water stress and groundwater depletion, improving water storage and irrigation infrastructure is both an agricultural imperative and a sustainability challenge.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Storage measures: revival of traditional water bodies (tanks, stepwells), construction of check dams, watershed management, desiltation of reservoirs

**[2019]** Q4. Elaborate the impact of National Watershed Project in increasing agricultural production from water-stressed areas. (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: IMPACT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Watershed development, Rainfed agriculture, Water conservation and productivity

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** National Watershed Projects have significantly enhanced agricultural production in water-stressed rainfed areas by improving soil moisture, groundwater recharge, and land productivity.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Water conservation: check dams, farm ponds, contour bunding trap rainwater; soil moisture improvement

      • Limitations: maintenance challenges, elite capture in water management committees, irregular rainfall variability
