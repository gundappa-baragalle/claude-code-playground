**[Q3697]** [2025] Who amongst the following are members of the Jury to select the recipient of 'Gandhi Peace Prize"?  I. The President of India II. The Prime Minister of India III. The Chief Justice of India IV. The Leader of Opposition in the Lok Sabha  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) II and IV only
     B) I, II and III
  ✅ C) II, III and IV

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The President is often assumed to be on the jury (as Head of State), but the Gandhi Peace Prize jury actually does NOT include the President as a regular member.

**[Q3703]** [2025] Consider the following statements :  I. In the finals of the 45th Chess Olympiad held in 2024, Gukesh Dommaraju became the world's youngest winner after defeating the Russian player Ian Nepomniachtchi. II. Abhimanyu Mishra, an American chess player, holds the record of becoming world's youngest ever Grandmaster.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) I only
  ✅ B) II only
     C) Both I and II
     D) Neither I nor II

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement I conflates Chess Olympiad with World Chess Championship — Gukesh beat Ding Liren in the World Chess Championship 2024, not at the Olympiad final. Statement II about Abhimanyu Mishra is correct.

**[Q4742]** [2023] Consider the following pairs with regard to sports awards :  1. Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award - For the most spectacular and outstanding performance by a sportsperson over period of last four years 2. Arjuna Award - For the lifetime achievement by a sportsperson 3. Dronacharya Award - To honour eminent coaches who have successfully trained sportspersons or teams 4. Rashtriya Khel Protsahan Puraskar - To recognize the contribution made by sportspersons even after their retirement  How many of the above pairs are correctly matched?

     A) Only one
  ✅ B) Only two
     C) Only three
     D) All four

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Arjuna Award is for outstanding performance over 4 years (not lifetime); Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna is for outstanding performance; Dronacharya is for coaches; Rashtriya Khel Protsahan is for corporate/institutional promotion of sports — not retired sportspersons.

**[Q4350]** [2006] In which Olympics Games did India last win a Gold Medal?

     A) Montreal (1976)
  ✅ B) Moscow (1980)
     C) Los Angeles (1984)
     D) Atlanta (1996)

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Many candidates confuse 1976 Montreal (where India lost in hockey) with 1980 Moscow where India won its last Olympic gold in field hockey.

### Common Trap Patterns on This Topic
- Statement 2 is wrong—Laureus Sportsman of the Year was won mostly by athletes from various sports, not predominantly Formula One; F1's Sebastian Vettel won it a few times.
- This is a highly dated current affairs question (2001 sports achievements) with very specific match-list format; without direct knowledge of each person's specific achievement, all options look plausible.
- Delhi hosted Asian Games in 1951 and 1982; Tokyo and Beijing hosted once each — Bangkok (1966, 1970, 1978, 1998) hosted four times making it the maximum, but students may default to Delhi as 'home advantage'.
- The match-list format with four women and four achievements makes elimination from one correct pair difficult; Mira Nair's Golden Lion connection to Venice Film Festival is the most well-known anchor.
- All options are cricket and sports records; the confusion is that Gavaskar held the record for most runs until Brian Lara surpassed it — so the Gavaskar-highest aggregate pair was formerly correct but not at time of question.
