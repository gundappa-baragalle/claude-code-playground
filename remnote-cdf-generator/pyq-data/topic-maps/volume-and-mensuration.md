**[Q744]** [1999]
> A hemispherical bowl is filled to the brim with a beverage. The contents of a bowl are transferred into a cylindrical vessel whose radius is 50% more than its height. If the diameter is same for both bowl and cylinder, then the volume of the beverage in the, cylindrical vessel will be

     A) 66(2/3)%
     B) 78½%
  ✅ C) 100%
     D) more than 100% (that is, some liquid will still be left in the bowl)

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may assume the cylinder volume is less than hemisphere; the key condition is radius is 50% more than height (r = 1.5h) and diameter equals the bowl's diameter — so r = R (bowl radius), and h = r/1.5 = 2r/3.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Bowl volume = (2/3)πR³; cylinder: r=R, h=2R/3; cylinder volume = πR²·(2R/3) = (2/3)πR³ = exactly equal to bowl — answer is C (100%).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Hemisphere volume = (2/3)πr³
     - Cylinder volume = πr²h; when h = 2r/3 and r = R(bowl), volumes are equal

**[Q747]** [2000]
> A rectangular water tank measures 15 m x 6 m at top and is 10 m deep. It is full of water. If water is drawn out lowering the level by 1 metre, how much of water has been drawn out ?

     A) 45,000 litres
  ✅ B) 90,000 litres
     C) 4,500 litres
     D) 900 litres

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Test-takers often compute 15×6×10 (full tank volume) and then divide by 10, giving 45,000 litres — forgetting that only 1m depth is drawn, not the full 10m.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Volume drawn = 15 × 6 × 1 = 90 m³. Convert: 1 m³ = 1000 litres → 90,000 litres. Eliminate options that use full depth or wrong unit conversion.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 1 cubic metre = 1000 litres
     - volume = length × breadth × depth

**[Q768]** [2000]
> A rectangular piece of iron sheet measuring 50 cm by 100 cm is rolled into a cylinder of height 50 cm. If the cost of painting the cylinder is Rs. 50 per square metre, then what will be the cost of painting the outer surface of the cylinder ?

  ✅ A) Rs. 25.00
     B) Rs. 37.50
     C) Rs. 75.00
     D) Rs. 87.50

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The unit conversion from cm to metres is critical; painting cost per sq metre but dimensions in cm leads to errors if conversion is skipped.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Circumference = 100cm = 1m (width of sheet becomes circumference), height = 50cm = 0.5m. Lateral surface area = 1×0.5 = 0.5 m². Cost = 0.5 × 50 = Rs.25.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Rolling a rectangle into cylinder: length = circumference, width = height
     - Lateral surface area of cylinder = 2πr × h = circumference × height

**[Q852]** [2000]
> What is the maximum number of pieces of 5 cm x 5 cm x 10 cm cake that can be cut from a big cake of 5 cm x 30 cm x 30 cm size ?

     A) 10
     B) 15
  ✅ C) 18
     D) 30

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may divide volumes directly without accounting for piece orientation; a 5×5×10 piece can be oriented differently within a 5×30×30 cake.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Big cake volume = 5×30×30=4500 cm³. Piece volume = 5×5×10=250 cm³. Count = 4500/250=18. Answer C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Maximum pieces from a cake = total volume / piece volume (when cutting is efficient)
     - Verify: 30/5=6 cuts one way, 30/10=3 cuts other way, 5/5=1 layer: 6×3×1=18

**[Q410]** [1997]
> If the number representing volume and surface area of a cube are equal, then the length of the edge of the cube in terms of the unit of measurement will be

     A) 3
     B) 4
     C) 5
  ✅ D) 6

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse volume (a³) with surface area (6a²) formulas or set up the equation incorrectly as a³=6a instead of a³=6a².
  💡 **Eliminate:** Volume = a³; Surface Area = 6a². If a³ = 6a², then a = 6. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Cube: Volume = a³, Surface Area = 6a²
     - When numerically equal: a³ = 6a² → a = 6

**[Q559]** [1998]
> A square pond has 2m sides and is 1m deep. If it is to be enlarged, the depth remaining the same, into a circular pond with the diagonal of the square as diameter as shown in the figure, then what would be the volume of earth to be removed?

  ✅ A) (2*pi-4)m3
     B) (4pi-4) m3
     C) (4pi-2) m3
     D) (2pi-2) m3

  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Square diagonal = 2√2 m; circular radius = √2 m; circular volume = π(√2)²×1 = 2π m³
     - Volume to remove = circular volume - square volume = 2π - 4 m³

**[Q120]** [1995]
> A rectangular sump of dimensions 6 m x 5 m x 4 m is to be built by using bricks to make the outer dimension 6.2 m x 5.2 x 4.2 m. Approximately how many bricks of size 20 cm x 10 cm x 5 cm are required to build the sump for storing water ?

     A) 1500
     B) 3000
  ✅ C) 15408
     D) 30000

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates must calculate volume of bricks needed = volume of sump walls (outer minus inner), then divide by volume of each brick — errors in unit conversion from metres to centimetres are common.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Volume of walls = (6.2×5.2×4.2) − (6×5×4) = 135.408 − 120 = 15.408 m³ = 15,408,000 cm³. Brick volume = 20×10×5 = 1000 cm³. Number = 15408. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Volume of hollow structure = outer volume minus inner volume
     - Unit consistency: always convert all measurements to same unit before computing
