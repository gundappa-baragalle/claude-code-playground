**[Q4980]** [2022]
> With reference to the United Nations General Assembly, consider the following statements:  1. The UN General Assembly can grant observer status to the non-member States. 2. Inter-governmental organisations can seek observer status in the UN General Assembly. 3. Permanent Observers in the UN General Assembly can maintain missions at the UN headquarters.  Which of the statements given above are correct?

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may think only nation-states can have observer status; intergovernmental organisations can also hold it. All three statements are correct — tricky because the question seems like a trap when all are true.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three statements about UN General Assembly observer status are correct: non-member states (Vatican), IGOs (EU, AU), and permanent observers can maintain UNHQ missions — answer is D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Holy See (Vatican) and Palestine have permanent observer state status at UNGA
     - EU and African Union hold permanent observer intergovernmental organisation status at UNGA

**[Q1629]** [2011]
> Consider the following:  1. Right to education. 2. Right to equal access to public service. 3. Right to food.  Which of the above is/are Human Right/Human Rights under "Universal Declaration of Human Rights"?

     A) 1 only
     B) 1 and 2 only
     C) 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option A (only right to education) and B (only 1 and 2) tempt because right to food seems less 'classic'; however, UDHR Article 25 includes right to an adequate standard of living including food.
  💡 **Eliminate:** UDHR (1948) Articles: 26 = right to education, 21 = right to equal access to public service, 25 = right to adequate standard of living (including food). All three are in UDHR. Answer: D (1, 2, and 3).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - UDHR 1948 has 30 articles; adopted by UN General Assembly on December 10, 1948
     - Article 25 UDHR: right to adequate standard of living including food, clothing, housing, medical care

**[Q1649]** [2011]
> Regarding the International Monetary Fund, which one of the following statements is correct?

     A) It can grant loans to any country
     B) It can grant loans to only developed countries
  ✅ C) It grants loans to only member countries
     D) It can grant loans to the central bank of a country

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option A sounds plausible as IMF is often portrayed as a universal institution, but IMF lending is restricted to member countries only.
  💡 **Eliminate:** IMF, like World Bank, only lends to member states — non-members or central banks independently cannot access IMF funds. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - IMF loans are available only to member countries facing balance of payments difficulties
     - IMF has 190 member countries; loans come with conditionalities

**[Q1349]** [2009]
> The Security Council of UN consists of 5 permanent members, and the remaining 10 members are elected by the General Assembly for a term of

     A) 1 year
  ✅ B) 2 years
     C) 3 years
     D) 5 years

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse the 2-year term with 1-year or 3-year terms by analogy with other UN bodies or domestic legislative bodies.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Non-permanent UNSC members are elected for 2-year terms by the General Assembly, with 5 elected each year on a staggered basis. Answer: B.

     - Non-permanent members elected for 2-year terms

**[Q1798]** [2001]
> Consider the following organisations : I. International Bank for Reconstruction and Development II. International Finance Corporation III. International Fund for Agricultural Development IV. International Monetary Fund Which of these are agencies of the United Nations ?

     A) I and II
  ✅ B) II and III
     C) III and IV
     D) I, II, III and IV

  📌 **Key facts:**
     - IFAD is a UN specialized agency focused on rural poverty reduction
     - IFC is part of World Bank Group but is a UN specialized agency
     - IMF and IBRD are Bretton Woods institutions — related to but independent of UN system

**[2025]** Q20. "The reform process in the United Nations remains unresolved, because of the delicate imbalance of East and West and entanglement of the USA vs. Russo-Chinese alliance." Examine and critically evaluate the East-West policy confrontations in this regard. (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: WHY | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: UN reform, Global governance, US-Russia-China geopolitics

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The UN reform impasse reflects the deep structural conflict between the post-WWII order enshrined in the UN Charter and the emerging multipolar reality of the 21st century.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • P5 veto: any reform requires all P5 to agree — China and Russia block UNSC expansion that would dilute their influence
      • US-China rivalry and US-Russia confrontation (Ukraine, Syria) paralyze UNSC on key global issues
      • East-West divide manifests in votes on Ukraine, Gaza, North Korea sanctions
      • India's case for permanent UNSC seat — G4 coalition — faces opposition from China
      • Reform proposals: expanding UNSC, limiting veto, creating intermediate categories
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Meaningful UN reform requires a fundamental rebalancing of great power interests — unlikely without a new global compact transcending current US-Russo-Chinese rivalry.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 IR — international organizations, GS2 IR — India's foreign policy
  🌐 Contemporary: Russia-Ukraine war and Gaza crisis have dramatically illustrated UNSC paralysis, making this question extremely timely.

**[2024]** Q19. 'Terrorism has become a significant threat to global peace and security.' Evaluate the effectiveness of the United Nations Security Council's Counter Terrorism Committee (CTC) and its associated bodies in addressing and mitigating this threat at the international level. (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: IMPACT | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Global terrorism, UN counter-terrorism, International security

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Terrorism threatens global peace, and the UNSC Counter-Terrorism Committee (CTC) is the primary multilateral mechanism to coordinate international response.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • CTC established under UNSC Res 1373 (2001) after 9/11 — mandates member states to adopt anti-terror legislation
      • Supported by CTED (Counter-Terrorism Committee Executive Directorate) for technical assistance
      • Limitations: lacks enforcement powers; relies on state compliance; geopolitical vetoes limit naming state sponsors
      • Complementary bodies: FATF, UN Global Counter-Terrorism Strategy (2006), 1267 Sanctions Committee
      • India's challenge: Pakistan-based terrorism not adequately addressed due to China's UNSC veto
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** While CTC provides a normative and coordination framework, structural UNSC limitations and geopolitical rivalries constrain its effectiveness against state-sponsored terrorism.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Internal Security — Terrorism, IR — India-Pakistan, Governance — International law
  🌐 Contemporary: CTC's role gains relevance as ISIS and Al-Qaeda franchises spread to new theatres including South Asia.

**[2023]** Q20. 'Sea is an important Component of the Cosmos'   Discuss in the light of the above statement the role of the IMO (International Maritime Organisation) in protecting environment and enhancing maritime safety and security. (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Maritime governance, Environmental protection, Global commons

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** As the custodian of global maritime law, the IMO plays an indispensable role in ensuring safe, secure, and environmentally sound shipping across the world's oceans.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • IMO's environmental role: MARPOL — controls oil, chemical, sewage pollution from ships
      • Ballast water convention — prevents invasive species spread

      • Maritime security: ISPS Code post-9/11, counter-piracy frameworks
      • India's role: member of IMO council, implementing MARPOL
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** IMO's comprehensive framework underscores how global commons like the seas require robust multilateral governance to balance economic use with environmental protection.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: IR, GS3: Environment, Economy: Trade and shipping
  🌐 Contemporary: IMO's 2023 revised GHG strategy and Red Sea shipping disruptions make this highly topical.

**[2020]** Q9. Critically examine the role of WHO in providing global health security during the Covid-19 pandemic. (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: IMPACT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Global health governance, WHO reforms, International cooperation in pandemics

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** WHO's response to COVID-19 exposed fundamental tensions between scientific independence, geopolitical pressures and the organization's limited enforcement powers in managing a global health emergency.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Delayed declaration of emergency (January 30, 2020) and pandemic (March 11)
      • Deference to China's narrative initially — criticized for political considerations
      • Led COVAX for equitable vaccine access — distributed 2 billion doses but fell short of targets
      • No enforcement power — depends on member state cooperation under IHR
      • Coordinated ACT-Accelerator for therapeutics and diagnostics
      • Strengths: technical guidance, disease surveillance (GISAID), coordination platform
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** While WHO provided essential global coordination during COVID-19, restoring its credibility requires structural reforms to ensure operational independence, timely communication and equitable access to medical countermeasures.
  🔗 Interlinkages: IR — geopolitics of health (US-China rivalry), Governance — India's health diplomacy and Vaccine Maitri, S&T — vaccine development and mRNA technology
  🌐 Contemporary: Ongoing Pandemic Treaty negotiations at WHO and India's health leadership make this highly relevant.

**[2017]** Q10. What are the main functions of the United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)? Explain different functional commissions attached to it. (150 words) 5+5=10
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~150w*

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** ECOSOC serves as the UN system's central coordinating body for economic, social, and environmental work, linking specialized agencies with member state priorities.
  - 📋 **Body:**

  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: International organizations, GS2: India's multilateral diplomacy, GS3: SDGs and development
  🌐 Contemporary: ECOSOC's High-Level Political Forum on SDGs is central to 2030 Agenda review, especially post-COVID.
