**[Q3643]** [2025]
> Consider the following statements :  Statement I : Article 6 of the Paris Agreement on climate change is frequently discussed in global discussions on sustainable development and climate change. Statement II : Article 6 of the Paris Agreement on climate change sets out the principles of carbon markets. Statement III : Article 6 of the Paris Agreement on climate change intends to promote inter-country non-market strategies to reach their climate targets.  Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

  ✅ A) Both Statement II and Statement III are correct and both of them explain Statement I
     B) Both Statement II and Statement III are correct but only one of them explains Statement I
     C) Only one of the Statements II and III is correct and that explains Statement I
     D) Neither Statement II nor Statement III is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement III says Article 6 promotes inter-country 'non-market' strategies — but Article 6 is primarily about market mechanisms (carbon markets), with Article 6.8 covering non-market approaches.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Article 6 sets principles for carbon markets (II correct) AND includes non-market approaches via Article 6.8 (III also correct) — both explain why Article 6 is widely discussed. Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 6 Paris Agreement: establishes international carbon market rules
     - Article 6.8 covers non-market cooperative approaches

**[2018]** Q7. What is wetland? Explain the Ramsar concept of 'wise use' in the context of wetland conservation. Cite two examples of Ramsar sites from India. (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Wetland conservation, Ramsar wise use, India's Ramsar sites

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Wetlands are among the world's most productive ecosystems and the Ramsar Convention's 'wise use' concept provides a framework for balancing conservation with sustainable human use.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Wetland definition: areas of marsh, fen, peatland or water, natural or artificial, permanent or temporary, with static or flowing water
      • Ecological functions: flood regulation, groundwater recharge, biodiversity habitat, carbon sequestration, water purification
      • Wise use principle: sustainable utilization for human benefit compatible with maintaining the wetland's natural character
      • India's Ramsar sites examples: Chilika Lake (Odisha) — largest coastal lagoon; Keoladeo National Park (Rajasthan) — migratory bird habitat
      • India has 75+ Ramsar sites (as of 2023) — highest in Asia
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Wise use of wetlands integrates ecological conservation with livelihood sustainability, making Ramsar's framework essential for India's wetland governance.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Environment, Biodiversity, International conventions
  🌐 Contemporary: India added record number of Ramsar sites in 2022; wetland conservation remains a flagship environmental priority.
