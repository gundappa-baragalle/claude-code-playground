**[Q4838]** [2002]
> Amit started a business by investing Rs. 30,000. Rahul joined the business after some time and invested Rs. 20,000. At the end of the year, profit was divided in the ratio of 2 : 1. After how many months did Rahul joined the business?

     A) 2
  ✅ B) 3
     C) 4
     D) 5

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Profit is shared in proportion to (capital × time). Rahul joins late so his effective capital-time is lower. Candidates may forget to account for time in profit sharing.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Amit: 30,000×12 months. Rahul joins after x months: 20,000×(12−x). Profit ratio 2:1 → (30,000×12)/(20,000×(12−x))=2/1 → 360,000=40,000×(12−x) → 12−x=9 → x=3. Rahul joined after 3 months. Answer B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Partnership profit sharing: capital × time ratio. Amit=30k×12; Rahul=20k×9 (if x=3); ratio=360:180=2:1 ✓

**[Q1770]** [2000]
> In a class there are 18 boys who are over 160 cm tall. If these boys constitute three-fourths of the boys and the total number of boys is two-third of the number of students in the class, then what is the number of girls in the class?

     A) 6
  ✅ B) 12
     C) 18
     D) 24

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Two-step ratio calculation — candidates who compute boys correctly (24) but forget total students = 24 × 3/2 = 36, then miscalculate girls, are trapped.
  💡 **Eliminate:** 18 boys = 3/4 of total boys → total boys = 24. Total boys = 2/3 of students → total students = 36. Girls = 36 - 24 = 12. Answer B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Fraction/ratio problems: work backwards from given part to find whole
     - Girls = Total students - Total boys

**[Q1773]** [2000]
> A club has 108 members. Two-thirds of them are men and the rest are women. All members are married except for 9 women members. How many married women are there in the club ?

     A) 20
     B) 24
  ✅ C) 27
     D) 30

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse 'all members are married except 9 women' to mean only 9 are unmarried women of total women, missing the calculation of total women first.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Total=108, men=72, women=36. Unmarried women=9. Married women=36-9=27. Answer C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Two-thirds of 108 = 72 men; one-third = 36 women
     - Married women = Total women - Unmarried women = 36 - 9 = 27

**[Q926]** [2000] The monthly income of Komal and Asha are in the ratio of 4 : 3. Their monthly expenses are in the ratio of 3 : 2. However, both save Rs. 600 per month. What is their total monthly income ?

     A) Rs. 8,400
     B) Rs. 5,600
  ✅ C) Rs. 4,200
     D) Rs. 2,800

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may set up equations with incomes and expenses separately but make errors in equating savings; savings = income - expenses, and both save Rs.600 provides the key constraint.

**[Q895]** [1999] Amar, Akbar and Anthony are friends, being looked after by a matron Farah. Amar weighs 50% more than Akbar and Anthony weighs 25% less than Amar. Farah weighs a third of the combined weight of the three boys. All four together weigh 232 kg. The correct arrangement of the persons in the ascending order of their weights is

     A) Anthony, Akbar, Farah, Amar
     B) Anthony, Akbar, Amar, Farah
     C) Akbar, Anthony, Amar, Farah
  ✅ D) Akbar, Anthony, Farah, Amar

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The question asks for ascending order but trap is computing Farah's weight correctly; Farah = (1/3) × total of three boys, not (1/3) of total of all four.

**[Q2825]** [1997] The average monthly income of a person in a certain family of 5 is Rs. 1000. What will be the monthly average income of a person in the same family if the income of one person increased by Rs. 12,000 per year ?

  ✅ A) Rs. 1200
     B) Rs. 1600
     C) Rs. 2000
     D) Rs. 3400

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may compute annual income and divide incorrectly, or misread the increase as per month instead of per year.
