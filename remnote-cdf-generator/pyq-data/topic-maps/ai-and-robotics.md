**[Q3645]** [2025]
> With reference to Direct Air Capture', an emerging technology, which of the following statements is/are correct?  I. It can be used as a way of carbon sequestration. Il. Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     B) III only
  ✅ C) I, II and III
     D) None of the above statements is correct

     - DAC captured CO2 can make synthetic aviation fuels, plastics, and food-grade CO2

**[Q3653]** [2025]
> In the context of electric vehicle batteries, consider the following elements :  I. Cobalt II. Graphite III. Lithium IV. Nickel  How many of the above usually make up battery cathodes?

     A) Only one
     B) Only two
  ✅ C) Only three
     D) All the four

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Graphite is the anode material in most lithium-ion batteries — candidates may include it as a cathode material; only Cobalt, Lithium, and Nickel typically form cathode composition (NMC/NCA cathodes).

  📌 **Key facts:**
     - NMC cathode = Nickel, Manganese, Cobalt + Lithium
     - Graphite is the anode material in lithium-ion batteries

**[2025]** Q16. India aims to become a semiconductor manufacturing hub. What are the challenges faced by the semiconductor industry in India? Mention the salient features of the India Semiconductor Mission. (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Semiconductor manufacturing ambitions, India Semiconductor Mission

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Semiconductors are the foundational technology of the digital economy, and India's bid to become a semiconductor hub reflects its strategic ambition in the global technology value chain.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Challenges: absence of domestic fab experience, huge capital requirements ($10-15 billion per fab), technology availability barriers, water and power requirements, talent gap
      • Supply chain: India depends on imports; COVID supply chain disruptions exposed vulnerability
      • India Semiconductor Mission: ₹76,000 crore incentive, DLI scheme for design ecosystem, fab incentives (50% capital support)
      • Salient features: fiscal support, technology acquisition partnerships, talent development, creation of semiconductor design clusters
      • Semiconductor Design Linked Incentive (DLI): India already has strength in chip design (50,000 engineers)
      • Micron's $825 million ATMP facility and Foxconn-HCL announcement signal growing investor interest
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** India can leverage its design strengths and government incentives to build a semiconductor ecosystem, but sustained policy commitment and global technology partnerships are essential.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 S&T — Emerging Technologies, GS3 Economy — Industry, IR — Global Issues
  🌐 Contemporary: US-China tech decoupling and global chip shortage have created a unique strategic window for India to establish itself in the semiconductor value chain.

**[2023]** Q15. The adoption of electric vehicles is rapidly growing worldwide. How do electric vehicles contribute to reducing carbon emissions and what are the key benefits they offer compared to traditional combustion engine vehicles? (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: IMPACT | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Clean mobility, Climate change mitigation, Technology transition

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Electric vehicles represent a transformative shift in transportation technology, offering significant carbon emission reductions and operational advantages over internal combustion engine vehicles.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Carbon reduction: zero tailpipe emissions; lifecycle emissions 40-70% lower than petrol cars depending on grid
      • Key benefits vs ICE: lower running costs, fewer moving parts (less maintenance), energy efficiency (85% vs 35% for petrol)
      • Grid greening amplifies EV benefits as renewable electricity share increases
      • India-specific: reduced crude oil import dependency, domestic battery manufacturing potential
      • Challenges: higher upfront cost, range anxiety, charging infrastructure, battery disposal
      • FAME II scheme: subsidies for two-wheelers, buses; PLI for advanced battery chemistry
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** EVs' contribution to carbon reduction scales with India's renewable energy transition; simultaneous investments in grid greening and EV infrastructure will maximize climate benefits.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Renewable energy, Air pollution in cities, Crude oil import bill
  🌐 Contemporary: India's EV sales crossed 1.5 million in FY2023, though penetration remains below 5%.

**[2023]** Q5. Introduce the concept of Artificial Intelligence (AI). How does AI help clinical diagnosis? Do you perceive any threat to privacy of the individual in the use of AI in healthcare? (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: AI in healthcare, Clinical diagnosis AI applications, Privacy in digital health

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Artificial Intelligence — the simulation of human cognitive functions by machines — is revolutionising healthcare, particularly clinical diagnosis, while raising significant ethical and privacy concerns.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • AI concept: Machine Learning (pattern recognition from data), Deep Learning (neural networks), NLP (natural language processing), Computer Vision — key subfields powering healthcare AI
