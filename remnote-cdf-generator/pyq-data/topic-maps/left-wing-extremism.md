### GS3

**[2025]** Q10. The Government of India recently stated that Left Wing Extremism (LWE) will be eliminated by 2026. What do you understand by LWE and how are the people affected by it? What measures have been taken by the government to eliminate LWE? (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: LWE elimination strategy, Tribal welfare and security, 2026 elimination target

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Left Wing Extremism exploits tribal grievances in India's forested heartland, and the government's multi-pronged elimination strategy has shown significant progress.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • LWE understanding: CPI(Maoist) armed insurgency based on Maoist ideology; exploits tribal land/forest rights deprivation
      • People affected: tribal communities (adivasis) in Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Odisha; face both Maoist coercion and displacement
      • Government measures: SAMADHAN strategy; security operations (drone surveillance, COBRA units); road connectivity in LWE areas
      • Development initiatives: Aspirational Districts Programme (former LWE districts); Vanbandhu Kalyan Yojana
      • Legal: PESA 1996 and Forest Rights Act 2006 to address tribal land rights; better implementation needed
      • Surrender policy: attractive surrenders and rehabilitation package to deplete Maoist cadre
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** While 2026 elimination target shows ambition, sustainable LWE elimination requires addressing root tribal grievances alongside security operations.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Internal Security, GS1 – Society, GS2 – Governance
  🌐 Contemporary: Recent Maoist leader eliminations in Chhattisgarh and government's 2026 deadline make this actively current.

**[2022]** Q20. Naxalism is a social, economic and developmental issue manifesting as a violent internal security threat. In this context, discuss the emerging issues and suggest a multilayered strategy to tackle the menace of Naxalism. (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Naxalism, Social-economic roots of LWE, Counter-insurgency

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Naxalism is not merely a law-and-order problem but a manifestation of deep socio-economic grievances, requiring a multilayered strategy addressing both its violent expression and underlying causes.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Emerging issues: resilience through forest cover, state-of-the-art weapons, urban presence, tech use for propaganda
      • Social root: land alienation, displacement, forest rights denial, adivasi marginalization
      • Economic root: absence of government services, debt bondage, lack of livelihood options
      • Security layer: intelligence-led operations, specialized forces (CoBRA), anti-IED measures
      • Development layer: Aspirational Districts, PMGSY connectivity, DBT, forest rights implementation
      • Political layer: inter-state coordination, surrender/rehabilitation policies
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** A durable solution to Naxalism requires parallel pressure on the violent movement through security operations and a genuine commitment to addressing the tribal development deficit.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Tribal rights, Forest Rights Act, Centre-state security coordination
  🌐 Contemporary: Recent Chhattisgarh operations have significantly degraded Maoist leadership capacity.

**[2020]** Q19. What are the determinants of left-wing extremism in Eastern part of India? What strategy should Government of India, civil administration and security forces adopt to counter the threat in the affected areas? (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Left-wing extremism, Tribal development, Counter-insurgency strategy

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Left-wing extremism in India's eastern region is rooted in deep developmental deficits and grievances, requiring a dual-track security and development response.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Determinants: displacement by development projects (mining, dams), land alienation, inadequate tribal rights implementation (PESA/FRA), police excesses
      • Governance vacuum: lack of roads, health, education in forested tribal areas exploited by Maoists
      • Socioeconomic marginalization: tribal communities with lowest HDI; unemployment, exploitation
      • Government strategy: SAMADHAN — Smart Leadership, Aggressive Strategy, Motivation, Actionable Intelligence, Dashboard, Harnessing Technology, Action on Finance, No access to LWE areas
      • Civil administration: IAS deployment in LWE districts, Aspirational Districts, Vanbandhu Kalyan Yojana
      • Security forces: CPF deployment, road opening parties, fortified police stations, counter-IED measures
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Defeating LWE requires not just military operations but sustained development investment that addresses the tribal community grievances at its root.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 – Internal Security, GS2 – Governance, GS1 – Society
  🌐 Contemporary: Government's 2026 LWE elimination target and Maoist-affected Chhattisgarh operations are ongoing.

**[2018]** Q10. Left Wing Extremism (LWE) is showing a downward trend, but still affects many parts of the country. Briefly explain the Government of India's approach to counter the challenges posed by LWE.  (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Internal security, LWE, Development-security nexus

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Despite a declining trend, Left Wing Extremism persists in India's tribal heartland, requiring a sophisticated blend of security operations and developmental interventions.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Government approach: SAMADHAN strategy combining security and development
      • Security measures: additional battalions, modern weapons, CoBRA units, anti-mine vehicles
      • Developmental measures: road connectivity, mobile connectivity, banking access in LWE districts
      • Aspirational Districts Programme for 35+ LWE-affected districts
      • Surrender and rehabilitation policy, civic action programmes
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Sustained decline in LWE requires long-term developmental investment rather than purely security operations to address root causes.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Tribal rights, Forest Rights Act, Federal security coordination
  🌐 Contemporary: LWE footprint now restricted to fewer districts mainly in Chhattisgarh, though violence intensity remains high.

**[2015]** Q19. The persisting drives of the Government for development of large industries in backward areas have resulted in isolating the tribal population and the farmers who face multiple displacements. With Malkangiri and Naxalbari foci, discuss the corrective strategies needed to win the Left Wing Extremism (LWE) doctrine affected citizens back into the mainstream of social and economic growth. (200 words) 12.5
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: LWE and development, Tribal rights, Security-development nexus

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Left Wing Extremism in India is rooted in developmental deficits, displacement, and denial of rights to tribal communities, making purely security-driven approaches insufficient.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Forcible displacement from large projects alienates tribal communities
      • SAMADHAN strategy: Smart leadership, Aggressive strategy, Motivation and training, Actionable intelligence, Dashboard-based KPIs, Harnessing technology, Action plan for each theatre, No access to financing
      • Need for full implementation of Forest Rights Act
      • Aspirational Districts Programme for development in LWE areas
      • Livelihood programmes, skill development, and local governance strengthening
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Winning back LWE-affected populations requires a dual approach: zero tolerance for violence combined with genuine development partnerships respecting tribal rights.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Tribal rights, Industrial policy, Governance, Polity (federalism)
  🌐 Contemporary: LWE footprint has shrunk but deep pockets remain in Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand and Odisha.

**[2013]** Q24. Article 244 of the Indian Constitution relates to administration of scheduled areas and tribal areas. Analyse the impact of non-implementation of the provisions of the Fifth schedule on the growth of Left Wing extremism. [200 words] 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: IMPACT | 10m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: LWE, Tribal rights, Development-security nexus

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The failure to implement Fifth Schedule protections has created conditions of alienation and grievance that left-wing extremists exploit to recruit and expand influence.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Fifth Schedule provisions: Governor can modify/apply laws, Tribal Advisory Council, protection against land alienation
      • Non-implementation: land alienation continues despite provisions; forest rights denied
      • PESA non-implementation: gram sabhas bypassed in development projects
      • LWE exploitation: fills governance vacuum — alternative dispute resolution, anti-exploitation narrative
      • Tribal alienation cycle: land alienation → displacement → poverty → LWE recruitment
      • Way forward: strict PESA implementation, Forest Rights Act, PVTG welfare mission
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Addressing LWE requires simultaneously implementing Fifth Schedule protections and PESA while delivering development — treating root causes, not just symptoms.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3: Internal Security, GS2: Governance, Society: Tribal communities
  🌐 Contemporary: PM-JANMAN scheme for PVTGs and declining LWE incidents (record low in 2023) alongside continued concerns make this relevant.
