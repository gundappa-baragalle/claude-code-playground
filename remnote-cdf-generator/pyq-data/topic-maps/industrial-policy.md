**[Q2192]** [2014]
> What does venture capital mean?

     A) A short-term capital provided to industries
  ✅ B) A long-term start-up capital provided to new entrepreneurs
     C) Funds provided to industries at times of incurring losses
     D) Funds provided for replacement and renovation of industries

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Venture capital is not emergency rescue funding (option C) or replacement capital (option D); it is specifically long-term equity funding for new/early stage businesses.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Venture capital = long-term start-up capital for new entrepreneurs with high-risk high-return profile; it is NOT short-term or rescue funding — answer is B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Venture capital provides equity funding to startups with high growth potential
     - VCs take equity stake in exchange for capital; exit through IPO or acquisition

**[Q1656]** [2011]
> Why is the Government of India disinvesting its equity in the Central Public Sector Enterprises (CPSEs) ?  1. The Government intends to use the revenue earned from the disinvestment mainly to pay back the external debt. 2. The Government no longer intends to retain the management control of the CPSEs.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
  ✅ D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Both statements seem plausible — Statement 1 sounds reasonable as revenue could reduce debt, but the government never stated external debt repayment as the primary disinvestment objective; Statement 2 is also incorrect as government retains management control.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Government has consistently stated disinvestment goals as unlocking capital, reducing fiscal deficit, and improving PSE efficiency — NOT for debt repayment or surrendering management control. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Government's disinvestment policy aims to reduce fiscal burden while retaining strategic control in most CPSEs
     - Privatization (full management transfer) differs from disinvestment (partial equity sale)

**[2013]** Q23. (b) Analyze the factors for the highly decentralized cotton textile industry in India. (100 words) 5
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHY | 5m | ~100w*
  🏷️ Themes: Decentralization of cotton textile industry, Location factors for industries

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~100 words: intro 30w / body 80w / conclusion 25w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's cotton textile industry is uniquely decentralized due to a confluence of raw material availability, labour, power, transport and market factors spread across multiple states.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Raw material spread: Cotton cultivation across Maharashtra, Gujarat, Andhra, Punjab allows regional mills
      • Labour availability: Cheap, abundant labour across diverse states removes need for geographic concentration
      • Power loom revolution: Small-scale decentralized power looms spread industry to non-traditional centers like UP and MP
      • Government policy: Reservation of certain products for SSI (small scale industry) encouraged dispersal
      • Market proximity: Consumer demand for textiles is national — decentralized location reduces transport costs
      • Handloom tradition: Regional handloom clusters (silk in Karnataka, Kanjivaram in TN) create natural decentralization
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The decentralization of cotton textiles reflects India's composite advantage in raw material, labour, market access and historical artisan traditions spread across its vast geography.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3 Industrial policy, GEO.IND — Industrial Geography, MSME sector
  🌐 Contemporary: PM MITRA scheme (2022) aims to create integrated textile parks across India to boost exports.

  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Production Linked Incentive scheme represents India's most ambitious attempt to reclaim its manufacturing potential by directly incentivizing output rather than inputs or investments.

  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2 Governance, GS3 Economy, External Sector
  🌐 Contemporary: PLI 2.0 discussions and semiconductor PLI (₹76,000 crore) are central to India's 2030 manufacturing ambitions.

**[2015]** Q8. "Success of 'Make in India' programme depends on the success of 'Skill India' programme and radical labour reforms". Discuss with logical arguments. (200 words) 12.5

  **Answer Framework:**

  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Make in India initiative's potential to transform India into a global manufacturing hub is intrinsically linked to addressing twin supply-side constraints: a skilled workforce and flexible labour regulations.
  - 📋 **Body:**

**[2015]** Q6. What are the impediments in marketing and supply chain management in developing the food processing industry in India? Can e-commerce help in overcoming these bottlenecks? (200 words) 12.5
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: HOW | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Food processing industry, Agriculture supply chain, E-commerce and rural economy

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** India's food processing sector suffers from structural impediments across marketing and supply chain that significantly reduce farm income and increase post-harvest losses.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Marketing impediments: fragmented markets, APMC monopoly, multiple intermediaries reducing producer price
      • Supply chain impediments: inadequate cold chain, poor rural roads, lack of processing clusters near production centers

  🌐 Contemporary: Agri-tech startups, Digital Agriculture Mission, and ONDC for agriculture reflect current policy thrust.
