**[Q3400]** [2023]
> With reference to ancient South India, Korkai, Poompuhar and Muchiri were well known as

     A) capital cities
  ✅ B) ports
     C) centres of iron-and-steel making
     D) shrines of Jain Tirthankaras

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may guess capital cities since these are prominent ancient towns; Korkai, Poompuhar (Kaveripattinam) and Muchiri (Muziri) were actually famous trading ports.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three — Korkai (Pandya), Poompuhar/Kaveripattinam (Chola), and Muchiri/Muziri (Chera) — were important seaports of Sangam Age South India engaged in Roman and Southeast Asian trade.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Poompuhar (Kaveripattinam) was the Chola port; Muchiri (Muziri) was the Chera port — both had Roman trade links
     - Korkai was the Pandya port city, known for pearls; later Sangam ports declined

### GS1

**[2013]** Q1. Though not very useful from the point of view of a connected political history of South India, the Sangam literature portrays the social and economic conditions of its time with remarkable vividness. Comment. (200 words) 10
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Sangam literature's limitations for political history, Vivid social and economic portrayal in Sangam poetry, Cultural richness of ancient Tamil society

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Sangam literature, while fragmentary and non-chronological as a source of political history, offers an unparalleled window into the social fabric, economic life and cultural values of ancient South India.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Political history limitations: Poems are panegyrics, occasional and context-less; no sequential narrative; rulers mentioned episodically without administrative detail
      • Social richness: Caste/class references (Vellala, Paraiya); warrior culture (puram poems); gender roles; sati (early references)
      • Economic life: Trade ports (Puhar/Kaveripattinam), merchant guilds, Roman trade references (amphorae), coins; spices and cotton exports
      • Cultural richness: Five tinai landscapes correlate to emotions; nature-human relationships; religious syncretism (Murugan, Shiva, Vishnu)
      • Archaeological corroboration: Arikamedu findings confirm Sangam references to Indo-Roman trade
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Sangam literature's true historical value lies not in political chronology but as a literary-sociological document — one of humanity's finest records of an ancient civilization's inner life.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 History, GS1 Art & Culture, World History
  🌐 Contemporary: Sangam literature is foundational to Tamil cultural identity and classical language status debates.

### Common Trap Patterns on This Topic
- Kathak and Odissi are also solo forms, so students may second-guess Bharatanatyam. Key: Kuchipudi ORIGINALLY was a GROUP performance by male Brahmin community — it was NOT historically solo unlike the others.
- Yavanapriya literally means 'beloved of Yavanas (Greeks)' suggesting something luxurious; students might guess muslin or ivory, missing that pepper was the main trade commodity.
- Option B (social divisions) seems plausible as caste divisions historically hampered large-scale mobilization; Option A (no iron) is easily eliminated as South India did have iron.
- African trade seems plausible as India had long-standing African connections; Middle-Eastern trade is also historically important for Indian merchants.
- Tamil (Dravidian) seems correct for south India; Ladakhi (Sino-Tibetan) is clearly wrong; Marathi (Indo-Aryan) is also clearly wrong.
