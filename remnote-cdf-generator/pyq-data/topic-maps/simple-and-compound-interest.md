**[Q2355]** [2010]
> The difference between the simple interest received from two banks on Rs. 500 for two years is Rs. 2-50. What is the difference between their rates ?

  ✅ A) 0.25%
     B) 0.5%
     C) 1%
     D) 2.5%

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may directly divide ₹2.50 by 500 to get 0.5% — but must account for the 2-year period.
  💡 **Eliminate:** SI = P×R×T/100. Difference = 500×ΔR×2/100 = 2.50. So ΔR = 2.50×100/(500×2) = 250/1000 = 0.25%. Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Simple Interest formula: SI = PRT/100; difference in SI = P × ΔR × T / 100
     - Here: 2.50 = 500 × ΔR × 2 / 100, solving: ΔR = 0.25%

**[Q95]** [1995]
> A person earns Rs. 2000 per month over and above his salary as additional charge allowance. However, 30% of this additional income will be deducted as additional income-tax at source. If the person would deposit Rs. 1000 per month on a long-term saving fetching 12% interest his tax liability on the additional allowance would reduce to 10%. What is the effective interest for this person for money invested in the long-term savings scheme ?

     A) 12%
     B) 18%
     C) 19%
  ✅ D) 20%

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The question involves tax savings from investment, not just interest; candidates may directly take 12% as the answer without accounting for the tax benefit that effectively increases the return.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Tax saved = (30%-10%) × Rs.1000 = Rs.200/month additional benefit. Effective return = Rs.120 interest + Rs.200 tax saving on Rs.1000 = Rs.320/Rs.1000 = 32% but annualized on net investment gives effective rate of 20%. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Effective interest = nominal interest + tax saved due to investment
     - Tax arbitrage increases effective yield of long-term savings instruments
