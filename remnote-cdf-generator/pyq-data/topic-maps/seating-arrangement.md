**[Q3625]** [2010]
> Six persons M, N, O, P, Q and R are sitting in two rows, three in each. Q is not at the end of any row. P is second to the left of R, O is the neighbour of Q and is sitting diagonally opposite to P. N is the neighbour of R. On the basis of above information, who is facing N ?

     A) R
  ✅ B) Q
     C) P
     D) M

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The two-row, three-each arrangement with diagonal opposite condition is tricky; candidates may misplace P and O, leading to wrong identification of who faces N.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Map positions: Q is middle seat, O is Q's neighbour (diagonally opposite P). N is R's neighbour. Working through constraints places Q facing N. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Seating arrangement: fix Q at centre first since Q is not at any row end
     - Diagonal opposite means across rows and across one seat

**[Q2000]** [2009]
> Six persons A, B, C, D, E and F are standing in a row. C and D are standing close to each other alongside E. B is standing beside A only. A is fourth from P. Who are standing on the extremes ?

     A) A and F
     B) B and D
  ✅ C) B and F
     D) None of the above

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The clue 'A is fourth from P' introduces an undefined person P (likely F from context), creating confusion. Solving step-by-step with constraints resolves it.
  💡 **Eliminate:** CDE are together; B beside A only; working through constraints with 6 persons gives B and F at extremes. Answer: C (B and F).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Seating arrangement: fix known clusters (C, D, E together; B beside A)
     - Extremes determined after placing all clusters

**[Q1769]** [2000]
> A, B, C, D, E and F, not necessarily in that order, are sitting in six chairs regularly placed around a round table. It is observed that A is between D and F. C is opposite D. D and E are not on neighbouring chairs. Which one of the following must be true?

     A) A is opposite B
     B) D is opposite E
     C) C and B are neighbours
  ✅ D) B and E are neighbours

  ⚠️ **Trap:** With 6 people in a circle, 'opposite' means 3 seats apart; candidates may incorrectly evaluate options A, B, C without systematically placing all persons.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Fix C opposite D (3 seats apart). A is between D and F. D and E are not adjacent. With these constraints, B and E end up as neighbors in all valid arrangements — answer D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - In a circular arrangement of 6, opposite means 3 positions apart
     - Systematic constraint application: fix one person and derive rest

**[Q2639]** [2007] Six faces of a cube are numbered from 1 to 6, each face carrying one different number. Further, 1. The face 2 is opposite to the face 6. 2. The face 1 is opposite to the face 5. 3. The face 3 is between the face 1 and the face 5. 4. The face 4 is adjacent to the face 2. Which one of the following is correct?

  ✅ A) The face 2 is adjacent to the face 3
     B) The face 6 is between the face 2 and the face 4
     C) The face 1 is between the face 5 and the face 6
     D) None of the above

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Cube face arrangement is spatial and abstract; candidates may incorrectly assume face 6 is between 2 and 4, or misread the given adjacency clues.

**[Q2589]** [1997] Consider the following : I. Saxena, David. Jain and Kumar were District Collectors at places P, Q, R and S respectively in 1970. II. In 1972 they were transferred. Saxena and Jain interchanged places. Kumar and David also interchanged places. III. One year later in 1973 they were again transferred such that David and Jain interchanged places and Saxena and Kumar were also interchanged. What should be the next round of transfers so that all the four persons could have been posted at all the four places ?

     A) Interchange Saxena and David as well as Jain and Kumar
     B) Interchange Saxena and Kumar as well as David and Jain
  ✅ C) Interchange David and Kumar as well as Saxena and Jain
     D) It is not possible for all the four persons to have been posted at all the four places

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The three rounds of transfers create a cumulative state that is hard to track mentally; candidates may lose track of the current position after each round of swaps.
