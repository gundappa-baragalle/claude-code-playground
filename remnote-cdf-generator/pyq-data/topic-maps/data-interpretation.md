**[Q862]** [2000]
> Answer based on the table : Which one of the following statements is false ? Country | Life Expectancy at Birth (Years) | Infant Mortality Rate | Adult Literacy Rate | 1.995 | 1.996 | 1.995 India | 62.4 | 72 | 52 China | 69.2 | 3.8 | 82 Indonesia | 64 | 4.7 | 84 Malaysia | 71.4 | 1.1 | 84 Thailand | 69.5 | 3.1 | 94 Korea | 71.7 | 6 | 98 Philippines | 67.4 | 3.2 | 95

     A) All countries other than India have over 80% literacy
     B) Malaysia and Korea have life expectancy higher than all other countries
  ✅ C) Higher the adult literacy lower is the infant mortality
     D) The life expectancy at birth in India is almost the same as that of Indonesia

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement C ('higher literacy, lower infant mortality') seems intuitively correct but Indonesia/China data show exceptions — this is the false statement.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Check statement C: China (82% literacy, 3.8 IMR) vs Philippines (95% literacy, 3.2 IMR) vs Korea (98% literacy, 6 IMR) — Korea has higher literacy but higher IMR than others; statement C is false.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Data interpretation requires checking all entries, not assuming correlation
     - Korea's IMR (6) is higher than Philippines (3.2) despite higher literacy — disproves universal correlation

**[Q181]** [1996]
> The table given below depicts the composition of India’s exports between 1992-93 and 1994-95 . The changing composition of the export trade is indicative of structural transformation of Indian economy in favour of modernisation. The best indicator of the trend is the Items | 1992-93 | 1993-94 | 1994-95 Agriculture & allied products | 1.6.9 | 1.8 | 1.5.9 Ores A Minerals | 4. 4 | 3.7 Manufactured floods | 75.5 | 75.6 | 78 Petroleum Products | 2.6 | 1.8 | 1.9

**[Q182]** [1996]
> When the frequency distribution is normal

     A) median, mode and mean are all different from one another
  ✅ B) mean, mode and median are identical
     C) mean is greater than mode
     D) mean is greater than median

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students who only know that mean > median in right-skewed distributions may think D is always true. In a normal distribution specifically, all three measures of central tendency coincide.
  💡 **Eliminate:** In a normal (bell-curve) distribution, mean = median = mode. They are all identical. This is a defining property of the normal distribution. Correct: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Normal distribution — perfectly symmetrical; mean = median = mode
     - Skewed distributions: right-skewed = mean > median > mode; left-skewed = mean < median < mode

**[Q885]** [2000]
> The distribution of 1,00,000 tourists who visited India during a particular year is shown in the given charts. Based on this, the number of Japanese tourists below the age of 39 who visited India in the year concerned is

     A) 10,000
     B) 8,000
     C) 6,000
  ✅ D) 4,000

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Without the actual charts, candidates must apply multi-step calculation — percentage of Japanese tourists × percentage below 39 years; error likely in applying two percentages sequentially.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Apply: (% Japanese of total) × (% below 39 among Japanese) × 1,00,000. Based on answer D=4,000: Japanese = ~20% (20,000) × below-39 = 20% = 4,000.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Data interpretation: apply sequential percentages to base number
     - Always identify the correct base before applying fractions

**[Q166]** [1996]
> Distance-time graph in respect of a race among four persons is shown in the given figure. Consider the following statements in this regard : I. ‘A’ stood first in the race. II. ‘C led all the way. III. ‘D’ ran faster than others in the later part of the race. Of these statements

     A) I and III are false and II is true
     B) I and II are false and III is true
  ✅ C) I and III are true and II is false
     D) I is true and II and III are false

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Without the actual figure, this requires reasoning about distance-time graphs. Statement II ('C led all the way') requires checking if C was always ahead, which may not be true if others overtook.
  💡 **Eliminate:** In a race, whoever reaches the finish line first stands first. D ran faster later means D covered more distance per unit time in later stages. I and III being true (A first, D faster later) while II false (C did not lead all the way) is most internally consistent.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Distance-time graph: steeper slope = faster speed
     - First in race = reached finish line (highest distance) first

**[Q676]** [1999]
> The yield versus fertilizer input is shown in the graph. Consider the following statements based on this graph : I. Yield rate is zero at B and C. II. There is no yield with no fertilizer input. III. The yield is minimum at D. IV. The yield is neither minimum nor maximum at C. Which of the above statements are correct ?

     A) I, II and IV
  ✅ B) III and IV
     C) II and III
     D) I, III and IV

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Without the actual graph, students must reason about yield-fertilizer curves; typical such curves show increasing yield then diminishing returns. 'Minimum at D' means D is a local minimum, not that yield is zero.
  💡 **Eliminate:** In a typical yield-fertilizer curve: B and C are inflection points (not zero yield); D is a local minimum (correct); C is neither min nor max (correct). Statements III and IV are correct — choose B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Yield-fertilizer graphs show law of diminishing returns
     - Inflection points (B, C) are where rate of change changes sign
     - Local minimum (D) exists when excessive fertilizer reduces yield (toxicity)
