**[Q2610]** [2017]
> Consider the following statements:  1. In India, the Himalayas are spread over five States only. 2. Western Ghats are spread over five States only. 3. Pulicat Lake is spread over two States only.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 and 2 only
  ✅ B) 3 only
     C) 2 and 3 only
     D) 1 and 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates often confuse the number of states covered by Himalayas and Western Ghats; statement 1 is wrong (Himalayas span more than 5 states) and statement 2 is also wrong. Pulicat Lake spanning two states (AP and Tamil Nadu) is the only correct one.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Eliminate by recalling Himalayas cover Jammu & Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh, and more — definitely more than 5 states, making statement 1 false.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Pulicat Lake is on AP-Tamil Nadu border
     - Himalayas span more than 5 states including J&K, HP, UK, Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh, and border states
     - Western Ghats span Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Goa, Maharashtra — 5 states, but statement 2 is tricky

**[Q2177]** [2014]
> Which one of the following pairs of islands is separated from each other by the 'Ten Degree Channel?

  ✅ A) Andaman and Nicobar
     B) Nicobar and Sumatra
     C) Maldives and Lakshadweep
     D) Sumatra and Java

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse Ten Degree Channel (separates Andaman from Nicobar) with Eight Degree Channel (separates Minicoy from Maldives/Lakshadweep).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Ten Degree Channel = between Little Andaman (Andaman Islands) and Car Nicobar (Nicobar Islands); named because it lies near 10°N latitude.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Ten Degree Channel separates Andaman Islands from Nicobar Islands
     - Eight Degree Channel separates Minicoy (India) from Maldives

**[Q1768]** [2002]
> Which one of the following is not a lagoon?

     A) Ashtamudi lake
     B) Chilka lake
  ✅ C) Preiyar lake
     D) Pulicat lake

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Periyar Lake in Kerala is sometimes called a reservoir/lake, and candidates may confuse it with coastal lagoons; Ashtamudi, Chilka, and Pulicat are all coastal lagoons.

**[2025]** Q15. How can Artificial Intelligence (AI) and drones be effectively used along with GIS and RS techniques in locational and areal planning? (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: HOW | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Technology in governance, Smart planning, Emerging technologies in geography

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The convergence of Artificial Intelligence, drone technology, GIS, and Remote Sensing is revolutionising spatial planning by enabling real-time, high-resolution, and predictive land-use decision-making.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • AI can process massive GIS and RS datasets to identify optimal sites for infrastructure, forests, water bodies
      • Drones provide centimetre-level aerial imagery — supplementing satellite RS with greater temporal frequency
      • Machine learning models predict urban sprawl patterns and facilitate proactive zoning
      • Flood risk mapping: combining DEM data from RS with AI for disaster-resilient city planning
      • Agricultural planning: crop monitoring, soil health mapping using NDVI indices from satellite RS
      • India: ISRO's Bhuvan, NRSC's geoportals; PM SVAMITVA scheme uses drones for village property mapping
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Integrating AI and drones with GIS and RS creates a powerful planning ecosystem that can transform India's approach to sustainable spatial development, particularly in rapidly urbanising and disaster-prone regions.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3: Science & Technology, Smart cities, Disaster management
  🌐 Contemporary: PM SVAMITVA scheme's drone-based rural property mapping is a landmark application of this technology convergence.

  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Himalayas experience far more frequent and severe landslides than the Western Ghats due to their distinct geological age, tectonic setting, rock composition and anthropogenic pressures.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Young, fragile rocks: Himalayas are geologically young with soft, friable sedimentary rocks; Western Ghats have ancient, hard crystalline rocks
      • Tectonic activity: Himalayas sit on active plate boundaries (India-Eurasian collision); Western Ghats are tectonically stable
      • Steep gradients: Himalayan slopes are steeper, especially in the Greater Himalayas, amplifying mass movement risk
      • Rainfall pattern: Intense monsoonal rains combined with snowmelt saturate Himalayan slopes; Western Ghats receive heavy rain but on more stable terrain
      • Human interference: Road cutting, deforestation, construction in Himalayas destabilizes slopes; Western Ghats better forested
      • Seismic trigger: Earthquakes in Himalayan region (seismic zone IV-V) frequently trigger landslides
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Effective landslide mitigation in the Himalayas requires integrated geological mapping, slope stabilization, sustainable development planning, and early warning systems.
