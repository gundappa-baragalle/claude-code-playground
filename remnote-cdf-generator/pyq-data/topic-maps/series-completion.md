**[Q1951]** [2008]
> March 1, 2008 was Saturday. Which day was it on March 1, 2002?

     A) Thursday
  ✅ B) Friday
     C) Saturday
     D) Sunday

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students forget that 2000 and 2004 are leap years, adding extra days, and may miscalculate by simply counting 6 years × 365 days.
  💡 **Eliminate:** From March 2002 to March 2008 = 6 years with 2 leap years (2004, 2008 partial). Total extra days = 6×365 + 2 = 2192 days. 2192 mod 7 = 5 days back from Saturday = Monday? Recount: 2002→2008 includes leap years 2004, 2008; net day shift = 8 mod 7 = 1. Saturday - 1 = Friday.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Each normal year shifts day by 1; leap year shifts by 2
     - 2004 is a leap year between 2002 and 2008

**[Q308]** [1997]
> In the series POQ, SRT, VUW, ? The blank space refers to

     A) XYZ
     B) XZY
  ✅ C) YXZ
     D) YZX

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Pattern identification: each triplet has a middle letter that is between the outer two in alphabetical sequence but the order is mixed. Students may not notice the consistent middle-last-first pattern within each triplet.
  💡 **Eliminate:** POQ: P(16),O(15),Q(17) — P+2=Q, O is middle. SRT: S(19),R(18),T(20) — same pattern. VUW: V(23),U(21? no,22),W(24) — same. Next: Y(25),X(24? no),Z(26) → YXZ. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Pattern: each group = (n+1), n, (n+2) in letters
     - POQ→SRT→VUW→YXZ skips by +3 each time

**[Q284]** [1996]
> The next pair of letters in the series AZ, CX, FU, ...... is

  ✅ A) JQ
     B) KP
     C) IR
     D) IV

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The gap between first letters of each pair is increasing (1, 2, 3...) and last letters count down from Z (Z, X, U...). Candidates may miss the increasing gap in first letters: A→C(+2), C→F(+3), F→?(+4)=J.
  💡 **Eliminate:** First letters: A(1), C(3), F(6), J(10) — differences are +2,+3,+4 (increasing). Last letters: Z(26), X(24), U(21), ?(21-4=17)=Q. Next pair: JQ. Answer A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Series pattern: first letter gaps +2,+3,+4... → A,C,F,J; last letter gaps -2,-3,-4... → Z,X,U,Q
     - Both sequences have increasing gaps — a double-progression series

**[Q1942]** [2008]
> In the series AABABCABCDABCDE... Which letter occupies the 100th position?

     A) H
  ✅ B) I
     C) J
     D) K

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may miscount cumulative terms or not recognize that each group n contains n letters. Carefully counting to position 100 is needed.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Groups: A(1), AB(2), ABC(3), ABCD(4)... Group n has n letters. Cumulative positions: after group n = n(n+1)/2. n=13: 91 letters total; n=14: 105 letters. Position 100 is in group 14, which has letters A–N (14 letters). Position within group 14: 100–91=9th letter = I. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Cumulative sum of group sizes = n(n+1)/2
     - 100th position: in group 14 (positions 92–105), 9th letter of ABCDEFGHIJKLMN = I

**[Q850]** [2000]
> The following figure represents time vs. learning curves of two students, Q and R for learning a Mathematics lesson : Which one of the following inferences can be drawn from the graph ?

  ✅ A) R started slowly in the beginning but got ahead of Q to complete learning the lesson
     B) Q started slowly and finished learning the lesson earlier than R
     C) R was always faster than Q in learning Mathematics
     D) Q was always faster than R in learning Mathematics

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Without the actual graph image, candidates must reason about learning curves; options C and D (Q/R always faster) are too absolute — graphs with crossover points imply neither started faster throughout.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The graph shows R starting slowly but catching up and finishing before Q — classic crossover learning curve. Answer A: R started slowly but eventually completed learning before Q.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Learning curve crossover: slow start can be compensated by accelerated later learning
     - Reading graphs: intersection of two curves indicates the point where rankings reverse

**[Q745]** [1999]
> In the sequence of numbers 5, 8, 13, X, 34, 55, 89,.......... the value of ‘X’ is

     A) 20
  ✅ B) 21
     C) 23
     D) 29

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may try arithmetic progression (adding 3, 5, 7...) and get 21 but be unsure — actually this is the Fibonacci sequence where each term = sum of two preceding terms.
  💡 **Eliminate:** 5, 8, 13, X, 34, 55, 89 — this is the Fibonacci sequence. 13+8=21 (=X); 21+13=34. So X=21.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Fibonacci sequence: each term = sum of two preceding terms
     - 5, 8, 13, 21, 34, 55, 89 is a Fibonacci-like sequence

**[Q538]** [1998]
> The missing fraction in the series given below is : 4/9, 9/20, ....,39/86

     A) 17/40
  ✅ B) 19/42
     C) 20/45
     D) 29/53

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The series involves fractions — candidates may try arithmetic difference rather than examining numerator-denominator patterns separately.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Series: 4/9, 9/20, ?/?, 39/86. Pattern: numerator: 4,9,19,39 (each ~2n+1); denominator: 9,20,?,86. Differences: numerators +5,+10,+20 (doubling); denominators +11,+?,+? Try 9→20(+11)→42(+22)→86(+44). So missing term = 19/42. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Numerator pattern: 4,9,19,39 (×2+1 each time)
     - Denominator pattern: 9,20,42,86 (each ~×2+2)
     - Series completion requires identifying dual patterns in numerator and denominator

**[Q594]** [1998]
> In below set of figures (I) to (IV), some parts are shown to change their position in a regular direction. Following the same sequence, which one of the following will appear at the fifth stage?

     A) A
     B) B
  ✅ C) C
     D) D

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Without the actual figures being described, candidates must track the pattern of movement/rotation across stages; reading the sequence direction carefully is critical.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Identify the transformation rule (rotation/reflection/shift) applied between each stage and extend to stage 5 — answer is C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Figure-based series questions test spatial reasoning and pattern recognition

  ✅ A) A

**[Q119]** [1995]
> Consider the series given below : 4/12/95, 1/1/96, 29/1/96, 26/2/96,... The next term of the series is

     A) 24/3/96
  ✅ B) 25/3/96
     C) 26/3/96
     D) 27/3/96

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The interval between dates is 28 days (4 weeks), but the last term crosses a month boundary, so candidates must carefully count days in February 1996 (leap year — 29 days) and March.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Series: 4 Dec → 1 Jan (+28 days) → 29 Jan (+28) → 26 Feb (+28) → next = 26 Feb + 28 = 25 March. Answer: B (25/3/96).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Date series with fixed interval of 28 days — must account for varying month lengths
     - 1996 was a leap year — February had 29 days

**[Q124]** [1995]
> X and Y are two variables whose values at any time are related to each other as shown in Fig. (i). X is known to vary periodically with reference to time as shown in Fig. (ii). Which of the following curves depicts correctly the dependence of Y on time?

     A) I
     B) II
  ✅ C) III
     D) IV

  ⚠️ **Trap:** This graph-based question requires understanding how a periodic X function maps through an X-Y relationship to produce Y — without the actual figures the pattern must be inferred logically.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Since X varies periodically and Y depends on X through the Fig.(i) relationship, Y will also be periodic — select the option showing the same periodicity as X but transformed through the given relationship. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Function composition: if Y = f(X) and X = g(t), then Y = f(g(t)) — a composed periodic function
     - Graphical reasoning questions test ability to map functional relationships visually
