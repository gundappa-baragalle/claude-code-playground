**[Q907]** [2001]
> In the figure below, ABCD is a cyclic quadrilateral, AB = BC and angle BAC = 70°, then angle ADC is

     A) 40°
     B) 80°
     C) 110°
  ✅ D) 140°

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often confuse angle in the same segment theorem with the opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral. AB=BC creates an isosceles triangle trap where BAC≠BCA is assumed.
  💡 **Eliminate:** AB=BC → △ABC isosceles → ∠BAC=∠BCA=70°. So ∠ABC=180−140=40°. Opposite angles in cyclic quad sum to 180°: ∠ADC=180−40=140°.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Opposite angles of cyclic quadrilateral sum to 180°
     - Isosceles triangle: base angles equal
