**[Q3261]** [2022]
> Consider the following statements :  1. The Constitution of India classifies the ministers into four ranks viz. Cabinet Minister, Minister of State with Independent Charge, Minister of State and Deputy Minister. 2. The total number of ministers in the Union Government, including the Prime Minister, shall not exceed 15 percent of the total number of members in the Lok Sabha.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
  ✅ B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is the trap — the Constitution does NOT classify ministers into four ranks; the ranking (Cabinet, MoS Independent, MoS, Deputy) is a convention, not constitutional provision.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Only Statement 2 is correct: 91st Constitutional Amendment limits Council of Ministers to 15% of Lok Sabha strength. Statement 1 is wrong — Constitution doesn't enumerate four ministerial ranks.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 91st Constitutional Amendment (2003) capped Council of Ministers at 15% of Lok Sabha seats
     - The Constitution mentions 'Council of Ministers' but does not classify them into specific ranks

**[Q2411]** [2015]
> Consider the following statements :  1. The Executive Power of the Union of India is vested in the Prime Minister. 2. The Prime Minister is the ex officio Chairman of the Civil Services Board.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
  ✅ D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Both statements seem plausible: executive power vested in PM is a common misconception (it is vested in President); PM as Chairman of Civil Services Board is also incorrect (Cabinet Secretary chairs it).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Under Article 53, executive power is vested in the President, not PM. PM is ex officio Chairman of NITI Aayog, not Civil Services Board (Cabinet Secretary chairs it). Both statements are wrong.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Executive power vested in President of India (Article 53), PM exercises it on President's behalf
     - PM is ex officio Chairman of NITI Aayog, not Civil Services Board
     - Civil Services Board is chaired by Cabinet Secretary

**[Q2140]** [2013]
> In the context of India, which of the following principles is/are implied institutionally in the parliamentary government?  1. Members of the Cabinet are Members of the Parliament. 2. Ministers hold the office till they enjoy confidence in the Parliament. 3. Cabinet is headed by the Head of the State.  Select the correct answer using the codes given below.

  ✅ A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 3 only
     C) 2 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 3 says 'Cabinet is headed by Head of State (President)' — this is wrong; Cabinet is headed by PM (Head of Government), not President (Head of State).
  💡 **Eliminate:** In parliamentary democracy: Cabinet members must be MPs (statement 1 correct), ministers resign if they lose Parliament's confidence (statement 2 correct), but Cabinet is headed by PM not President — statement 3 wrong.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Parliamentary government requires Cabinet members to be Parliament members
     - Collective responsibility means Cabinet falls if Parliament withdraws confidence
     - PM heads the Cabinet, not the President (who is a constitutional head)

**[Q2153]** [2013]
> Consider the following statements :  1. The Council of Ministers in the Centre shall be collectively responsible to the Parliament. 2. The Union Ministers shall hold the office during the pleasure of the President of India. 3. The Prime Minister shall communicate to the President about the proposals for legislation.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
  ✅ B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 says collective responsibility is to Parliament — actually it is to Lok Sabha (lower house), not Parliament as a whole. Statement 3 says PM communicates proposals for 'legislation' — Article 78 says PM communicates proposals for 'consideration of the Council of Ministers', not legislation.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to Lok Sabha (Art. 75), not Parliament. Ministers hold office at President's pleasure (Art. 75). PM's Article 78 duty is to communicate Council decisions to President, not specifically legislation. Only statement 2 is fully correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 75: Collective responsibility to Lok Sabha (not Parliament)
     - Article 75(2): Ministers hold office during President's pleasure
     - Article 78: PM's duty to communicate proposals considered by Council of Ministers to President

**[Q1880]** [2012]
> The Prime Minister of India, at the time of his/her appointment

  ✅ A) need not necessarily be a member of one of the Houses of the Parliament but must become a member of one of the Houses within six months
     B) need not necessarily be a member of one of the Houses of the Parliament but must become a member of the Lok Sabha within six months
     C) must be a member of one of the Houses of the Parliament
     D) must be a member of the Lok Sabha

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students often confuse the rule — the PM need not be a Lok Sabha member at time of appointment but must become a member of either House (not necessarily Lok Sabha) within 6 months.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Constitutional convention + Article 75: PM need not be a MP at appointment but must secure membership of either House within 6 months. This eliminates options B, C, D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - PM can be from Rajya Sabha — e.g., Manmohan Singh was PM from RS
     - Must become MP of either house within 6 months of appointment

**[Q2325]** [2009]
> With reference to Union Government, consider the following statements : 1. The Ministries/Departments of the Government of India are created by the Prime Minister on the advice of the Cabinet Secretary. 2. Each of the Ministries is assigned to a Minister by the President of India on the advice of the Prime Minister. Which of the statements given above is/are correct ?

     A) 1 only
  ✅ B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is wrong — Ministries are created by the President on PM's advice (not by PM on Cabinet Secretary's advice); Statement 2 is correct (President assigns ministries on PM's advice).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Ministries are created by Presidential order (on PM's advice), not by PM on Cabinet Secretary's advice; Statement 2 is constitutionally correct (Article 77) — answer is B (2 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Ministries/Departments created by President on PM's advice under Government of India (Allocation of Business) Rules
     - Each Ministry assigned to a Minister by President on PM's advice

**[Q3360]** [2009]
> With reference to Union Government, consider the following statements: 1. The Constitution of India provides that all Cabinet Ministers shall be compulsorily the sitting members of Lok Sabha only. 2. The Union Cabinet Secretariat operates under the direction of the Ministry of Parliamentary Affairs. Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
  ✅ D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 wrongly restricts Cabinet ministers to Lok Sabha only — ministers can be from Rajya Sabha too; statement 2 is wrong as Cabinet Secretariat is under PM, not Ministry of Parliamentary Affairs.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Both statements are false: Constitution allows ministers from either House (Article 75); Cabinet Secretariat functions under PM/Cabinet not Ministry of Parliamentary Affairs — answer is 'Neither 1 nor 2'.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 75: Ministers are appointed by President on PM's advice and must be members of either House of Parliament
     - Cabinet Secretariat operates under the PM and facilitates Cabinet Committee meetings

**[Q350]** [1997]
> If the Prime Minister of India belonged to the Upper House of Parliament

  ✅ A) he will not be able to vote in his favour in the event of a no-confidence motion
     B) he will not be able to speak on the Budget in the Lower House
     C) he can make statements only on the Upper House
     D) he has to become a member of the Lower House within six months after being sworn-in as the Prime Minister

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option D (must become LS member within 6 months) sounds like constitutional requirement but is incorrect — there is no such compulsion. Options B and C (can't speak in LS) are wrong since PM can address both Houses.
  💡 **Eliminate:** PM from Rajya Sabha cannot vote in Lok Sabha no-confidence motion since he is not a Lok Sabha member. He can speak in LS but cannot vote. Select A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - PM from RS can speak in Lok Sabha but cannot vote — not an LS member
     - No-confidence motion in Lok Sabha: RS member PM cannot vote

**[Q735]** [1996]
> Which one of the following statements is correct ? The Prime Minister of India

     A) is free to choose his ministers only from among those who are members of either House of the Parliament
     B) can choose his cabinet colleagues after due counselling by the President of India in this regard
  ✅ C) has full discretion in the choice of persons who are to serve as ministers in his cabinet
     D) has only limited powers in the choice of his cabinet colleagues because of the discretionary power vested with the President of India

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option A tempts candidates who confuse the convention with a constitutional requirement; option D misleads those who overestimate presidential discretion.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Article 75 only requires ministers to be members of Parliament within six months; the PM has full discretion in choosing them, making C correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Article 75: PM chooses ministers, President appoints on PM's advice
     - Ministers must become MPs within 6 months of appointment

**[Q569]** [1995]
> Which one of the following is not explicitly stated in the Constitution of India but followed as a convention ?

     A) The Finance Minister is to be a Member of the Lower House
  ✅ B) The Prime Minister has to resign if he loses majority in the Lower House
     C) All the parts of India are to be represented in the Council of Ministers
     D) In the event of both the President and the Vice-President demitting office simultaneously before the end of their tenure the Speaker of the Lower House of the Parliament will officiate as the President

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students confuse constitutional provisions with conventions; many rules about the PM's position (must resign if loses majority) are conventions, not explicit constitutional text.
  💡 **Eliminate:** The PM's obligation to resign on losing Lok Sabha majority is a constitutional convention, not written in the Constitution. The other options either have statutory basis or are explicitly provided. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - PM must resign if loses Lok Sabha majority: convention, not written law
     - Constitutional convention: unwritten but binding political practice
     - Finance Minister being from Lower House is also a convention

**[2014]** Q6. The size of the cabinet should be as big as governmental work justifies and as big as the Prime Minister can manage as a team. How far is the efficacy of a government then inversely related to the size of the cabinet? Discuss. (200 words) 12.5
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: IMPACT | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Cabinet governance, Executive efficiency, Constitutional provisions on cabinet size

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The size and composition of a cabinet directly shapes governmental efficiency, as coordination costs rise with numbers while political imperatives often demand inclusivity.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Constitutional provision: 91st Amendment 2003 — cabinet size capped at 15% of Lok Sabha strength to prevent bloating
      • Efficacy inverse relationship: larger cabinets face coordination failures, policy incoherence, and collective responsibility dilution
      • Political compulsions: coalition governments require larger cabinets to accommodate allies — India's experience with NDA/UPA
      • Arguments against simple inverse: domain expertise, regional representation, and workload may justify size
      • Best practices: Cabinet Committees allow smaller decision-making cores even within larger cabinets
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Optimal cabinet size balances representational imperatives with managerial efficiency, with constitutional caps and cabinet committees providing structural solutions.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: Parliament, GS2: Federalism and coalition politics, Ethics: Good governance principles
  🌐 Contemporary: India's cabinet size debates under successive coalition and majority governments remain relevant to democratic governance.
