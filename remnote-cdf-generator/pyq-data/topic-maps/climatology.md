**[Q4883]** [2025]
> Consider the following statements :  Statement I : In January, in the Northern Hemisphere, the isotherms bend equatorward while crossing the landmasses, and poleward while crossing the oceans. Statement II : In January, the air over the oceans is warmer than that over the landmasses in the Northern Hemisphere.  Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

  ✅ A) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct and Statement II explains Statement I
     B) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct but Statement II does not explain Statement I
     C) Statement I is correct but Statement II is not correct
     D) Statement I is not correct but Statement II is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The direction of isotherm bending (equatorward over land in January) is counterintuitive — in winter (January NH), land is colder so isotherms bend toward the equator over continents.
  💡 **Eliminate:** In January (NH winter), land cools faster than oceans; oceans are warmer (Statement II correct), causing isotherms to bend equatorward over land and poleward over oceans (Statement I correct); II explains I. Answer: A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - In January NH: isotherms bend equatorward over continents (colder), poleward over oceans (warmer)
     - Land heats and cools faster than water — differential heating principle

**[Q4683]** [2024]
> With reference to "Coriolis force", which of the following statements is/are correct?  1. It increases with increase in wind velocity. 2. It is maximum at the poles and is absent at the equator.  Select the answer using the code given below:

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 (Coriolis increases with wind velocity) is a common misconception — Coriolis force depends on wind speed (velocity) as well as latitude, so Statement 1 is correct.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Both statements are correct: Coriolis force = 2mΩv sinφ (increases with wind speed v, Statement 1) and is maximum at poles (sinφ=1) and zero at equator (sinφ=0, Statement 2). Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Coriolis force formula: F = 2mΩv sinφ — proportional to wind velocity (v) and sin of latitude (φ)
     - Coriolis force is zero at equator (sin 0° = 0) and maximum at poles (sin 90° = 1)

  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** A cloudburst is an extreme localised rainfall event (≥100mm/hour) with devastating flash flood potential, increasingly common in India's mountain regions due to climate change.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Definition: IMD defines cloudburst as rainfall ≥100mm/hour over ~10km² — far exceeding normal heavy rainfall (≥64.5mm/day)

      • Seasonality: spring peak — March to June — when polar and tropical air masses interact most vigorously
      • Tornado Alley geography: Texas, Oklahoma, Kansas — no mountain barrier between Gulf and Arctic air masses
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** The Gulf of Mexico region's unique geography — warm moisture source, cold air proximity, flat terrain, and wind shear — creates the ideal atmospheric conditions for tornadic storm development.

**[2013]** Q20. (b) What do you understand by the phenomenon of 'temperature inversion' in meteorology? How does it affect weather and the habitants of the place?
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHAT | 5m | ~100w*
  🏷️ Themes: Temperature inversion, Weather effects, Air quality

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~100 words: intro 30w / body 80w / conclusion 25w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Temperature inversion — a meteorological anomaly where atmospheric temperature rises with altitude instead of falling — fundamentally alters weather patterns and poses serious challenges for air quality and human health.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Mechanism: normally temperature decreases ~6.5°C/km with altitude; in inversion, a warmer air layer sits above cooler surface air, creating a 'lid' that traps pollutants

      • Effects on inhabitants: respiratory diseases from trapped pollutants; visibility reduction (aviation hazard); crop damage from frost pockets in valleys
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Temperature inversion transforms the atmosphere into a trap — understanding its mechanics is critical for air quality management, urban planning, and weather forecasting.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1 Geography, GS3 Environment, Disaster Management
  🌐 Contemporary: Delhi's winter air quality crisis is directly linked to temperature inversions trapping stubble-burning smoke.
