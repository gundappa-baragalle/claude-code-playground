**[Q4701]** [2011]
> What is the difference between asteroids and comets ?  1. Asteroids are small rocky planetoids, while comets are formed of frozen gases held together by rocky and metallic material. 2. Asteroids are found mostly between the orbits of Jupiter and Mars, while comets are found mostly between Venus and Mercury. 3. Comets show a perceptible glowing tail, while asteroids do not.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 and 2 only
  ✅ B) 1 and 3 only
     C) 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 incorrectly says comets are between Venus and Mercury — comets actually originate from the Oort Cloud/Kuiper Belt far beyond the planets.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Statements 1 (composition) and 3 (glowing tail from sublimating ice) are correct. Statement 2 is wrong — comets originate from Kuiper Belt/Oort Cloud, not between Venus and Mercury. Answer: B (1 and 3).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Comets: dirty snowballs — frozen gases + rock/dust; tail forms when near Sun due to sublimation
     - Asteroids: rocky/metallic planetoids mostly in asteroid belt (Mars-Jupiter); comets from Oort Cloud

**[Q1118]** [2006]
> Assertion (A) : To orbit around the Sun, the planet Mars takes lesser time than the time taken by the Earth. Reason (R) : The diameter of the planet Mars is less than that of the Earth.

     A) Both A and R are individually true R is the correct explanation of A.
     B) Both A and R are individually true R is not the correct explanation of A.
     C) A is true but R is false.
  ✅ D) A is false but R is true.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Assertion (Mars takes less time to orbit than Earth) is FALSE — Mars is farther from the Sun and takes ~687 Earth days. The Reason about Mars's smaller diameter is TRUE but irrelevant to orbital period (which depends on orbital radius).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Orbital period depends on distance from Sun (Kepler's third law), not diameter. Mars is farther from Sun → longer orbital period. A is false, R is true → Answer D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Mars orbital period = 687 Earth days (longer than Earth's 365 days)
     - Kepler's Third Law: orbital period increases with distance from Sun
     - Mars diameter ≈ 53% of Earth's diameter (R is true)

**[Q797]** [2003]
> Among the following which planet takes maximum time for one revolution around the Sun?

     A) Earth
  ✅ B) Jupiter
     C) Mars
     D) Venus

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Saturn is often thought to have the longest orbital period; candidates may forget Jupiter is farther from Sun than Mars and Earth.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Among given options (Earth, Jupiter, Mars, Venus), Jupiter is farthest from Sun — by Kepler's third law, farther planets take longer (Jupiter: ~12 years).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Jupiter's orbital period: ~11.9 years (longest among given options)
     - Orbital periods: Venus (225 days) < Earth (365 days) < Mars (687 days) < Jupiter (12 years)

**[Q57]** [1998]
> Match List I with List II and select the correct answer by using the codes given below the lists List I (Special Characteristic) | List II (Name of Planet) I. Farthest planet from the sun | (A) Mercury II. Largest planet of the solar system | (B) Venus III. Planet second from the Sun in the solar system | (C) Jupiter IV. Planet nearest to the Sun | (D) Pluto | (E) Saturn

     A) I-B, II-C, III-E, IV-A
  ✅ B) I-C, II-E, III-A, IV-B
     C) I-D, II-A, III-B, IV-C
     D) I-D, II-C, III-B, IV-A

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Pluto was a planet in 1998 (question year) but was reclassified as dwarf planet in 2006 — students with modern knowledge may skip Pluto as farthest. Also Jupiter vs Saturn as 'largest' confuses some.
  💡 **Eliminate:** In 1998 context: Farthest=Pluto (D), Largest=Jupiter (C), Second from Sun=Venus (B), Nearest=Mercury (A). Match: I-D, II-C, III-B, IV-A. Note: Neptune is now considered farthest after Pluto's reclassification.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Mercury: closest to Sun; Venus: 2nd planet
     - Jupiter: largest planet (318× Earth's mass)
     - Pluto: farthest (as of 1998); reclassified as dwarf planet 2006
     - Saturn: 2nd largest, known for rings

**[Q439]** [1998]
> One Astronomical Unit is the average distance between

  ✅ A) Earth and the Sun
     B) Earth and the Moon
     C) Jupiter and the Sun
     D) Pluto and the Sun

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Earth-Moon distance is another well-known astronomical unit that students may confuse with AU; Pluto-Sun distance is sometimes associated with AU in general solar system context.
  💡 **Eliminate:** 1 AU = average Earth-Sun distance (~150 million km); it is by definition tied to Earth-Sun, not any other pair.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - 1 AU ≈ 149.6 million km (Earth-Sun average distance)
     - AU is the baseline unit for measuring distances within the solar system

**[Q3487]** [2024] Consider the following statements:  1. Statement-I: Giant stars live much longer than dwarf stars. 2. Statement-II: Compared to dwarf stars, giant stars have a greater rate of nuclear reactions.  Which one of the following is correct in respect of the above statements?

     A) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct and Statement-II explains Statement-I
     B) Both Statement-I and Statement-II are correct, but Statement-II does not explain Statement-I
     C) Statement-I is correct, but Statement-II is incorrect
  ✅ D) Statement-I is incorrect, but Statement-II is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement I is wrong: giant stars actually live SHORTER lives than dwarf stars because their faster nuclear reaction rates exhaust fuel quickly. Statement II is correct — giant stars have higher nuclear reaction rates.

**[Q1308]** [2008] In order to their distances from the Sun, which of the following planets lie between Mars and Uranus?

     A) Earth and Jupiter
  ✅ B) Jupiter and Saturn
     C) Saturn and Earth
     D) Saturn and Neptune

**[Q1483]** [2008] Consider the following statements: 1. The albedo of an object determines its visual brightness when viewed with reflected light. 2. The albedo of Mercury is much greater than the albedo of the Earth. Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 about Mercury having much greater albedo than Earth sounds plausible since Mercury is closer to the Sun and appears bright. In fact Mercury has very low albedo (~0.07) because it has no atmosphere to reflect light.

**[Q1163]** [2006] What is the approximate mean velocity with which the Earth moves round the Sun in its orbit?

     A) 20 km/s
  ✅ B) 30 km/s

**[Q1027]** [2005] Assertion (A) : The same face of the Moon is always presented to the Earth. Reason (R) : The Moon rotates about its own axis in 23 days which is about the same time that takes to orbit the Earth.

     A) Both (A) and (R) are individually true and R is the correct explanation of A
     B) Both (A) and (R) are individually true and R is not the correct explanation of A
  ✅ C) A is true but R is false
     D) A is false but R is true

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Assertion (same face of Moon always faces Earth) is correct but Reason gives wrong period — the Moon rotates on its axis in ~27.3 days (same as its orbital period), not 23 days.

**[Q1028]** [2005] Assertion (A) : Existence of human life on Venus is highly improbable. Reason (R) : Venus has extremely high level of carbon dioxide in its atmosphere.

  ✅ A) Both (A) and (R) are individually true and R is the correct explanation of A
     B) Both (A) and (R) are individually true and R is not the correct explanation of A
     C) A is true but R is false
     D) A is false but R is true

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Both A and R are straightforwardly true. Venus has ~96% CO₂ atmosphere causing extreme greenhouse effect (~465°C surface temperature) making life highly improbable — R correctly explains A.

**[Q329]** [1997] The tail of a comet is directed away from the sun because

     A) as the comet rotates around the sun, the lighter mass of the comet is pushed away due to the centrifugal force alone
     B) as the comet rotates, the lighter mass of the comet is attracted by some star situated in the direction of its tail
  ✅ C) the radiation emitted by the sun exerts a radial pressure on the comet throwing its tail away from the sun
     D) the tail of the comet always exists in the same orientation

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option A (centrifugal force) sounds plausible for orbital mechanics. Option B (attraction from another star) sounds mysterious and plausible to non-science students.

**[Q352]** [1997] The group of small pieces of rock - revolving round the sun between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter are called

     A) meteors
     B) comets
     C) meteorites
  ✅ D) asteroids

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Meteors ('shooting stars') are commonly confused with meteorites (landed on Earth) and asteroids (orbiting rocks). The 'belt between Mars and Jupiter' is the key distinguishing descriptor.

**[Q752]** [1997] Which one of the following stars is nearest to the earth ?

     A) Polaris
     B) Alpha Centauri
  ✅ C) Sun
     D) Sirius

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Alpha Centauri is the nearest star system after the Sun; candidates who consider the Sun separately from 'stars' may pick Alpha Centauri.

**[Q5019]** [1997] The planet Mercury is revolving in an elliptical orbit around the sun as shown in the given figure. The kinetic energy of mercury is greatest at the point labelled

  ✅ A) A
     B) B
     C) C
     D) D

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may think kinetic energy is greatest at aphelion (farthest point) where speed seems 'stored up' — but Kepler's second law and conservation of energy indicate opposite.

**[Q256]** [1996] Consider the following statements : A person in a spaceship located half way between the earth and the sun will notice that the I. Sky is jet black. II. Stars do not twinkle. III. Temperature outside the spaceship is much higher than that on the surface of the earth. Of these statements

     A) Only III is correct
     B) I and II are correct
     C) I and III are correct
  ✅ D) I, II and III are correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement III (much higher temperature outside) seems intuitive near the Sun, but in the vacuum of space, temperature depends on what is measured — the vacuum itself has near absolute zero background, though solar radiation heats objects in direct sunlight to very high temperatures.

### GS3

**[2024]** Q16. What are asteroids? How real is the threat of them causing extinction of life? What strategies have been developed to prevent such a catastrophe? (250 words) 15
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 15m | ~250w*
  🏷️ Themes: Asteroid threat, Planetary defense strategies, Space technology

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~250 words: intro 37w / body 162w / conclusion 50w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Asteroids, rocky remnants of the solar system's formation, pose a low-probability but high-consequence threat to life on Earth, driving the development of global planetary defense strategies.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Definition: rocky/metallic bodies orbiting the Sun, mostly in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter; Near-Earth Asteroids (NEAs) are the threat
      • Extinction threat: Chicxulub impactor (~10 km) caused Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction (~66 million years ago) — real precedent
      • Current threat assessment: no confirmed extinction-level asteroid in near future; probabilistic monitoring ongoing (NASA's Planetary Defense Coordination Office)
      • Prevention strategies: Kinetic impact (DART mission, 2022 — successful); gravity tractor concept; nuclear deflection (for large threats); early detection (telescopes like LSST)
      • International cooperation: UN's Space Mission Planning Advisory Group, International Asteroid Warning Network (IAWN)
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** While the probability of a civilization-ending asteroid impact is low, the consequence is absolute — justifying sustained investment in detection, tracking, and deflection technologies.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3: Science and technology, GS3: Disaster management (cosmic)
  🌐 Contemporary: NASA's DART mission success (2022) and ESA's Hera follow-up mission make this highly topical.
