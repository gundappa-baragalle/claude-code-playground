**[Q3014]** [2020]
> With reference to "Blockchain Technology", consider the following statements:  1. It is a public ledger that everyone can inspect, but which no single user controls. 2. The structure and design of blockchain is such that all the data in it are about cryptocurrency only. 3. Applications that depend on basic features of blockchain can be developed without anybody's permission.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 1 and 2 only
     C) 2 only
  ✅ D) 1 and 3 only

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is the main trap — blockchain stores all types of data (contracts, records, etc.), not just cryptocurrency data; confusing blockchain's origin with its full scope.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Statement 1 (public ledger, no single controller) and Statement 3 (permissionless application development) are both correct. Statement 2 is wrong — blockchain is not limited to cryptocurrency.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Blockchain is a distributed public ledger without central control
     - Blockchain stores any data (contracts, records, votes), not just cryptocurrency transactions

**[Q2538]** [2016]
> With reference to Bitcoins', sometimes seen in the news, which of the following statements is/are correct?  1. Bitcoins are tracked by the Central Banks of the countries. 2. Anyone with a Bitcoin address can send and receive Bitcoins from anyone else with a Bitcoin address. 3. Online payments can be sent without either side knowing the identity of the other.  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1 and 2 only
  ✅ B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 is the classic trap — Bitcoin operates on decentralized blockchain; no central bank tracks or controls it. Candidates may confuse regulatory interest with actual tracking.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Bitcoin is decentralized and untracked by central banks (S1 false). S2 and S3 are core features of Bitcoin — peer-to-peer and pseudonymous transactions. Answer: B (2 and 3 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Bitcoin uses decentralized blockchain — no central bank oversight
     - Bitcoin transactions are pseudonymous, not anonymous

**[Q2219]** [2014]
> In addition to fingerprint scanning, which of the following can be used in the biometric identification of a person?  1. Iris scanning 2. Retinal scanning 3. Voice recognition  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may think only fingerprint and iris are biometric identifiers; voice recognition, retinal scanning, and iris scanning are all valid biometric methods.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three — iris scanning, retinal scanning, and voice recognition — are established biometric identification methods; answer is D (1, 2 and 3).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Biometric identifiers: fingerprint, iris, retina, voice, face, palm vein, gait
     - Iris and retina are different — iris (colored ring) vs retina (back of eye)

**[Q2929]** [2019] With reference to communication technologies, what is/are the difference/differences between LTE (Long-Term Evolution) and VoLTE (Voice over Long-Term Evolution)?  1. Select the correct answer using the code given below.

     A) 1 only

     C) Both 1 and 2
  ✅ D) Neither 1 nor 2

### GS3

**[2024]** Q10. Describe the context and salient features of the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023.  (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Digital governance, Privacy as fundamental right, Data protection framework

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 marks India's first standalone legislation on personal data, enacted against the backdrop of a booming digital economy and the Supreme Court's Puttaswamy judgment.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Context: rapid digitization, rise of Big Tech, SC's 2017 privacy ruling made a data law imperative
      • Key features: consent-based processing, Rights of Data Principal (access, correction, erasure, grievance redressal)
      • Data Fiduciary obligations: purpose limitation, data minimisation, security safeguards
      • Data Protection Board: independent adjudicatory body with penalty powers up to ₹250 crore
      • Exemptions for state security and legitimate state purposes drew criticism from privacy advocates
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** While the Act is a landmark step toward data governance, its effectiveness will depend on the strength and independence of the Data Protection Board and secondary rules.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: Polity and governance, GS3: Cybersecurity and technology, Fundamental Rights (Article 21)
  🌐 Contemporary: The DPDP Act 2023 is directly relevant to digital India governance and cybersecurity debates.
