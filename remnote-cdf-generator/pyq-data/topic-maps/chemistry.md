**[Q3168]** [2021]
> Water can dissolve more substances than any other liquid because

  ✅ A) it is dipolar in nature
     B) it is a good conductor of heat
     C) it has high value of specific heat
     D) it is an oxide of hydrogen

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may think 'high specific heat' or 'good heat conductor' makes water a universal solvent, but the correct reason is its dipolar (polar) molecular nature enabling hydrogen bonding.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Water's dipolar nature allows it to surround and dissolve both ionic and polar compounds through hydrogen bonds and electrostatic interactions — answer is A.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Water is a universal solvent due to its dipolar (polar) nature
     - The O-H bond polarity creates partial charges enabling dissolution of most substances

**[Q578]** [2000]
> The stones formed in human kidney consist mostly of

  ✅ A) calcium oxalate
     B) sodium acetate
     C) magnesium sulphate
     D) calcium

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Calcium alone is not the constituent; the compound calcium oxalate is the most common kidney stone — not just calcium, and not magnesium sulphate.

**[Q579]** [2000]

**[Q755]** [1997]
> The most reactive among the halogens is

  ✅ A) fluorine
     B) chlorine
     C) bromine
     D) iodine

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Chlorine is a commonly known reactive halogen used in water treatment; candidates may choose it over fluorine.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Reactivity of halogens decreases down the group (F > Cl > Br > I) due to decreasing electron affinity and atomic radius; Fluorine is most electronegative element.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Fluorine is the most reactive halogen (and most electronegative element)
     - Halogen reactivity: F > Cl > Br > I (decreases down group 17)

**[Q260]** [1996]
> The offending substance in the liquor tragedies leading to blindness, etc.. is

     A) ethyl alcohol
     B) amyl alcohol
     C) benzyl alcohol
  ✅ D) methyl alcohol

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Ethyl alcohol (ethanol) is safe drinking alcohol — students may confuse it with methanol. Amyl and benzyl alcohols are industrial solvents.

**[2013]** Q17. (a) What is an FRP composite material ? How are they manufactured ? Discuss their applications in aviation and automobile industries. [100 words] 5
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 5m | ~100w*
  🏷️ Themes: Advanced materials, Industrial applications, Technology in aerospace

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~100 words: intro 30w / body 80w / conclusion 25w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Fibre Reinforced Polymer composites represent a class of advanced materials with superior strength-to-weight ratios critical to modern aviation and automobiles.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Composition: reinforcing fibers (glass/carbon/aramid) embedded in polymer (epoxy/polyester) matrix
      • Manufacturing: filament winding, hand lay-up, resin transfer molding, pultrusion
      • Aviation applications: fuselage panels, wing components, nacelles (Boeing 787 50% composites)
      • Automobile applications: body panels, crash structures, Formula 1 chassis
      • Advantages: high strength-to-weight, corrosion resistance, design flexibility
      • Challenges: high cost, difficult recyclability, repair complexity
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** FRP composites are transforming engineering sectors through weight reduction and performance improvement, though cost and recyclability challenges remain.

### Common Trap Patterns on This Topic
- Aqua regia famous for dissolving gold — students assume it dissolves everything. Conc. H₂SO₄ is powerful, suggesting it attacks glass. Neither attacks glass = SiO₂.
- Statement 1 is false: Baking soda is sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO3), NOT anhydrous sodium carbonate (Na2CO3, which is washing soda). This is the classic baking soda vs washing soda confusion.
- Candidates may think urea or ammonium sulphate are mixed fertilizers; a mixed fertilizer contains two or more of N, P, K—NPK is the classic example.
- Zinc-carbon batteries are used in ordinary torches; nickel-cadmium (NiCd) are used in rechargeable devices like shavers—candidates often confuse primary and secondary batteries.
- Students might omit oxygen or nitrogen; all four (C, H, O, N) are present in all proteins—sulfur is present in some but not all.
