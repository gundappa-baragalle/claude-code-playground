**[Q2068]** [2013]
> Which of the following statements is/are applicable to Jain doctrine?  1. The surest way of annihilating Karma is to practice penance. 2. Every object, even the smallest particle has a soul. 3. Karma is the bane of the soul and must be ended.  Select the correct answer using the codes given below.

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may exclude statement 2 (every object has a soul) thinking it is too extreme, not realising it is the Jain doctrine of Jiva in all matter (sarvajiva).
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three statements correctly describe Jain doctrine: penance annihilates karma (1), anekantavada includes soul in all matter (2), karma is bondage to be ended (3). Answer is D (1, 2, 3).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Jain doctrine: every object (animate and inanimate) has a jiva (soul) — this is the concept of sarvajiva
     - Karma in Jainism is material particles binding the soul; penance (tapas) is the key to annihilating accumulated karma

**[Q2359]** [2009]
> Anekantavada is a core theory and philosophy of which one of the following ?

     A) Buddhism
  ✅ B) Jainism
     C) Sikhism
     D) Vaishnavism

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Buddhism also has concepts of multiple perspectives (middle path, relativity of views); Anekantavada is specifically the Jain philosophical doctrine of many-sidedness of reality.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Anekantavada (non-absolutism/many-sidedness of truth) is a core Jain philosophical doctrine; associated with Syadvada (conditional predication) — answer is B (Jainism).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Anekantavada: Jain doctrine that truth and reality are complex and multifaceted
     - Associated Jain doctrines: Syadvada (conditional predication), Nayavada (partial views)

**[Q3404]** [2023] "Souls are not only the property of animal and plant life, but also of rocks, running water and many other natural objects not looked on as living by other religious sects." The above statement reflects one of the core beliefs of which one of the following religious sects of ancient India?

     A) Buddhism
  ✅ B) Jainism
     C) Shaivism
     D) Vaishnavism

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Buddhism also has respect for living beings; students may confuse Jain animism (souls in rocks, water) with Buddhist ahimsa — the specific attribution of souls to non-living matter is distinctly Jain.

**[Q3225]** [2022] With reference to Indian history, consider the following texts :  1. Nettipakarana 2. Parishishtaparvan 3. Avadanashataka 4. Trishashtilakshana Mahapurana  Which of the above are Jaina texts?

     A) 1, 2 and 3
  ✅ B) 2 and 4 only
     C) 1, 3 and 4
     D) 2, 3 and 4

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Nettipakarana and Avadanashataka are Pali/Buddhist texts; students may not know Parishishtaparvan and Trishashtilakshana Mahapurana are Jaina.

**[Q2783]** [2018] With reference to the religious practices in India, the "Sthanakvasi" sect belongs to

     A) Buddhism
  ✅ B) Jainism
     C) Vaishnavism
     D) Shaivism

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The unfamiliar term 'Sthanakvasi' may lead students toward Buddhism or Shaivism; it is one of the sub-sects of Jainism (Shvetambara branch).

**[Q1650]** [2011] The Jain philosophy holds that the world is created and maintained by

  ✅ A) Universal Law
     B) Universal Truth
     C) Universal Faith
     D) Universal Soul

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Options B and C sound like Jain philosophy's emphasis on truth and faith, but Jain philosophy holds the world is self-sustaining and governed by Universal Law — not created by any god or soul.

**[Q2273]** [2009] Mahamastakabhisheka, a great religious event, is associated with and done for who of the following?

  ✅ A) Bahubali
     B) Buddha
     C) Mahavir
     D) Nataraja

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse Mahamastakabhisheka with a Buddhist or Hindu ceremony — it is the grand anointing ceremony specific to the Jain deity Bahubali (Gomateshwara) at Shravanabelagola.

**[Q887]** [2004] With reference to ancient Jainism, which one of the following statements is correct?

     A) Jainism was spread in South India under the leadership of Sthalabahu.
     B) The Jainas who remained under the leadership of Bhadrabahu were called Shvetambaras after the Council held at Pataliputra.
  ✅ C) Jainism enjoyed the patronage of the Kalinga king Kharavela in the first century BC.
     D) In the initial stage of Jainism, the Jainas worshipped images unlike Buddhists.

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Options A and B confuse Digambara/Shvetambara split details; the Bhadrabahu group were Digambaras, not Shvetambaras — an easy reversal trap.

**[Q849]** [2003] Consider the following statements 1. Vardhamana Mahavira’s mother was the daughter of Lichchhavi chief Chetaka. 2. Goutama Buddha’s mother was a princess from the Kosha-lan dynasty. 3. Parshvanatha, the 23rd Tirthankara, belonged to Banaras. Which of these statements is/are correct?

     A) Only 1
     B) Only 2
  ✅ C) 2 and 3
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 1 about Mahavira's mother being daughter of Lichchhavi chief Chetaka is often confused — Mahavira's mother was Trishala, sister (not daughter) of Chetaka. Statement 2 about Buddha's mother being from Kosala dynasty is also tricky.

**[Q959]** [2000] Assertion (A) : The emphasis of Jainism on non-violence (ahimsa) prevented agriculturalists from embracing Jainism. Reason (R) : Cultivation involved killing of insects and pests.

  ✅ A) Both A and R are true, and R is the correct explanation of A
     B) Both A and R are true, but R is not a correct explanation of A
     C) A is true, but R is false
     D) A is false, but R is true

  ⚠️ **Trap:** The assertion-reason format requires checking both truth and causal linkage; both A and R are true and R logically explains why farming (involving pest killing) conflicted with ahimsa.

**[Q281]** [1996] Which one of the following is not a part of early Jaina literature ?

  ✅ A) Therigatha
     B) Acarangasutra
     C) Sutrakritanga
     D) Brihatkalpasutra

  ⚠️ **Trap:** All options sound like ancient religious texts. Therigatha belongs to the Pali Buddhist canon — it is Buddhist literature (Theravada), not Jain.

**[Q184]** [1995] The concept of Anuvrata was advocated by

     A) Mahayana Buddhism
     B) Hinayana Buddhism
  ✅ C) Jainism
     D) the Lokayata school

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Anuvrata means 'small vows' and candidates might confuse it with Buddhist vows; Anuvrata is specifically Jain—lesser vows for lay followers vs Mahavrata for monks.
