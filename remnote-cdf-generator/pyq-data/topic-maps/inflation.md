**[Q3214]** [2022]
> In India, which one of the following is responsible for maintaining price stability by controlling inflation?

     A) Department of Consumer Affairs
     B) Expenditure Management Commission
     C) Financial Stability and Development Council
  ✅ D) Reserve Bank of India

  ⚠️ **Trap:** FSDC and Expenditure Management Commission sound authoritative on financial stability, misleading students away from RBI's mandate.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Price stability through monetary policy is RBI's core mandate under the amended RBI Act (2016) with flexible inflation targeting framework.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - RBI's primary mandate includes maintaining price stability while keeping growth in view
     - Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) under RBI sets repo rate to target 4% CPI inflation

**[Q3111]** [2021]
> With reference to Indian economy, demand-pull inflation can be caused/ increased by which of the following?  1. Expansionary policies 2. Fiscal stimulus 3. Inflation-indexing wages 4. Higher purchasing power 5. Rising interest rates  Select the correct answer using the code given below.

  ✅ A) 1, 2 and 4 only
     B) 3, 4 and 5 only
     C) 1, 2, 3 and 5 only
     D) 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Rising interest rates (statement 5) actually reduce demand-pull inflation by raising borrowing costs — it is a monetary tool to counter inflation, not cause it.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Demand-pull inflation causes: expansionary policies (1), fiscal stimulus (2), higher purchasing power (4) — all increase aggregate demand; inflation-indexed wages (3) cause cost-push not demand-pull; rising interest rates (5) reduce inflation.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Demand-pull inflation: too much money chasing too few goods — caused by excess aggregate demand
     - Rising interest rates are RBI's contractionary tool to reduce demand and inflation

**[Q2985]** [2020]
> Consider the following statements :  1. The weightage of food in Consumer Price Index (CPI) is higher than that in Wholesale Price Index (WPI). 2. The WPI does not capture changes in the prices of services, which CPI does. 3. Reserve Bank of India has now adopted WPI as its key measure of inflation and to decide on changing the key policy rates.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct ?

  ✅ A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 2 only
     C) 3 only
     D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 3 is a common trap — RBI uses CPI (not WPI) as its key inflation measure since 2014; many confuse historical WPI usage with current practice. Statement 1 is counterintuitive since food has higher weight in CPI.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Eliminate options containing Statement 3 immediately — RBI adopted CPI targeting, not WPI. This leaves only options with statements 1 and/or 2.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - RBI adopted CPI as key inflation measure in 2014 under Urjit Patel Committee recommendations
     - WPI does not cover services; CPI covers both goods and services

**[Q2451]** [2015]
> With reference to inflation in India, which of the following statements is correct?

     A) Controlling the inflation in India is the responsibility of the Government of India only
     B) The Reserve Bank of India has no role in controlling the inflation
  ✅ C) Decreased money circulation helps in controlling the inflation
     D) Increased money circulation helps in controlling the inflation

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option A says government alone controls inflation (ignoring RBI) and option D says increased money circulation helps — both are wrong but plausible-sounding to students.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Inflation is controlled by reducing money supply (contractionary policy); decreased money circulation (through higher interest rates, OMO sales) helps control inflation — option C is correct.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Inflation control: reducing money supply through higher interest rates, CRR hike, OMO bond sales
     - Both RBI (monetary) and Government (fiscal) have roles in inflation control
     - Quantitative theory of money: inflation rises with money supply

**[Q123]** [2013]
> Consider the following statements :  1. Inflation benefits the debtors. 2. Inflation benefits the bond-holders.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

  ✅ A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Statement 2 is false: inflation hurts bondholders because bonds pay fixed nominal returns, eroded in real terms by inflation. Students may confuse 'benefit' directions for financial assets.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Inflation: helps debtors (repay with cheaper money), hurts creditors and bondholders (fixed income loses real value). Statement 1 is correct; Statement 2 is wrong. Answer: A (1 only).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Inflation benefits debtors: real value of debt reduces over time
     - Inflation hurts bondholders: fixed coupon payments lose purchasing power
     - Unanticipated inflation redistributes wealth from creditors to debtors

**[Q2101]** [2013]
> A rise in general level of prices may be caused by  1. an increase in the money supply 2. a decrease in the aggregate level of output 3. an increase in the effective demand  Select the correct answer using the codes given below.

     A) 1 only
     B) 1 and 2 only
     C) 2 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may exclude option 3 (effective demand increase) thinking it's only a Keynesian concept not directly linked to inflation, but demand-pull inflation is a standard mechanism.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three cause inflation: excess money supply (monetary), reduced output (supply-side), and increased demand (demand-pull) — answer is D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Three inflation types: demand-pull, cost-push, monetary
     - Increase in money supply causes inflation (quantity theory of money)
     - Decrease in output with same demand raises prices (supply shock inflation)

**[Q1659]** [2011]
> Economic growth is usually coupled with

     A) Deflation
  ✅ B) Inflation
     C) Stagflation
     D) Hyperinflation

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may confuse stagflation (stagnation + inflation) with normal economic growth dynamics; economic growth is typically associated with demand-pull inflation.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Economic growth increases demand for goods, services, and labour — this typically creates demand-pull inflation. Answer: B (Inflation).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Economic growth increases aggregate demand, typically causing demand-pull inflation
     - Stagflation is the anomalous combination of stagnation and inflation — not the norm

**[Q4322]** [2011]
> A rapid increase in the rate of inflation is sometimes attributed to the "base effect". What is "base effect"?

     A) It is the impact of drastic deficiency in supply due to failure of crops
     B) It is the impact of the surge in demand due to rapid economic growth
  ✅ C) It is the impact of the price levels of previous year on the calculation of inflation rate
     D) None of the statements (a), (b) and (c) given above is correct in this context

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates confuse base effect with supply-side or demand-side causes of inflation; base effect is purely a statistical phenomenon related to the comparison base year.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Base effect is a statistical artifact — if prices were low in the previous year, the same absolute price level appears as a large percentage increase; option C directly captures this.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Base effect: high inflation when base year had low prices, even without actual price rise
     - WPI and CPI calculations are susceptible to base effect distortions

**[Q1442]** [2010]
> With reference to India, consider the following Statements: 1. The Wholesale Price Index (WPI) in India is available on a monthly basis only 2. As compared to Consumer Price Index for Industrial Workers (CPI (IW)), the WPI gives less weight to food articles. Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
  ✅ C) Both 1 and 2
     D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** WPI available only monthly sounds correct (it IS monthly), but CPI(IW) giving more weight to food than WPI is often confused — food has a larger weight in CPI(IW) than in WPI.
  💡 **Eliminate:** WPI in India is available on a weekly basis (not just monthly) — Statement 1 is wrong. WPI gives less weight to food than CPI(IW) — Statement 2 is correct. Answer: B. Given the answer key says C (both correct), Statement 1 may be contextually correct for 2010 when WPI was monthly. Accept: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - WPI (Wholesale Price Index) — published monthly; base year 2011-12; food weight ~24%
     - CPI(IW) — monthly; food weight ~45-46%, much higher than WPI

**[Q3623]** [2010]
> Which one of the following statements is an appropriate description of deflation ?

     A) It is a sudden fall in the value of a currency against other currencies
     B) It is a persistent recession in both the financial and real sectors of economy
  ✅ C) It is a persistent fall in the general price level of goods and services
     D) It is a fall in the rate of inflation over a period of time

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option D (disinflation — falling rate of inflation) is a common confusion with deflation; option B (recession) conflates economic output contraction with price levels.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Deflation = persistent fall in general price level (not just rate of inflation). Option C is the precise definition; option D describes disinflation. Answer: C.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Deflation = sustained decline in the general price level
     - Disinflation ≠ Deflation (disinflation = falling inflation rate, still positive)

**[Q445]** [1998]
> The current price index (base 1960) is nearly 330. This means that

     A) all items cost 3.3 times more than what they did in 1960
     B) the prices of certain selected items have gone upto 3.3 times
  ✅ C) weighted mean of prices of certain items has increased 3.3 times
     D) gold price has gone up 3.3 times

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Option A ('all items cost 3.3 times more') is an intuitive but incorrect interpretation — price index doesn't measure all items equally; option D about gold is a common misconception.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Price index is a weighted average of selected items' prices — eliminate options about 'all items' or specific goods; the correct interpretation is weighted mean of selected basket of goods.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Price index = weighted mean of prices of a selected basket of goods and services
     - Base year index = 100; current index 330 means 3.3x increase in weighted mean price

**[Q358]** [1997]
> In India, inflation is measured by the

  ✅ A) Wholesale Price Index Number
     B) Consumers Price Index for urban non-manual workers
     C) Consumers Price Index for agricultural workers
     D) National Income deflation

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may select CPI for urban workers (B) or CPI for agricultural workers (C) since these also measure price levels. In India, WPI has traditionally been used as the headline inflation measure (though CPI is now primary post-2014).
  💡 **Eliminate:** At time of question (1997), India used Wholesale Price Index (WPI) as the primary inflation measure. The RBI shifted to CPI as headline measure post-2014. Select A — WPI.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - WPI was India's headline inflation measure until RBI adopted CPI-combined in 2014
     - WPI covers manufactured goods, fuel, and primary articles

### GS3

**[2024]** Q2. What are the causes of persistent high food inflation in India? Comment on the effectiveness of the monetary policy of the RBI to control this type of inflation. (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHY | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Food inflation, Monetary policy limits, Supply chain

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Persistent food inflation in India is primarily a structural supply-side phenomenon, for which the Reserve Bank's monetary policy tools provide only limited effectiveness.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Causes: weather/monsoon variability (supply shocks), inadequate storage/cold chain, high transportation costs, fragmented markets, MSP-driven distortions
      • Structural: low agri productivity, small fragmented landholdings, limited agri investment
      • RBI monetary policy: raises repo rate to reduce demand and inflation expectations
      • Limits: food inflation is supply-driven; rate hikes hurt industry without fixing supply constraints
      • Better responses: buffer stock management, price stabilization fund, cold chain investment, diversifying food basket
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Addressing food inflation requires structural agricultural reforms rather than solely relying on monetary tightening, which can impose unnecessary growth costs.
  🔗 Interlinkages: Agricultural policy, Supply chain, Rural development
  🌐 Contemporary: Tomato, onion, pulses price spikes in 2023-24 highlighted India's food price vulnerability.
