**[Q4923]** [2018]
> Consider the following phenomena :  1. Light is affected by gravity. 2. The Universe is constantly expanding. 3. Matter warps its surrounding space-time.  Which of the above is/are the prediction/predictions of Albert Einstein's General Theory of Relativity, often discussed in media ?

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may think the expanding universe is Hubble's observation (not Einstein's prediction), and that only 1 and 3 are Einstein's predictions, missing Statement 2.
  💡 **Eliminate:** All three — light affected by gravity (gravitational lensing), expanding universe (predicted by GR equations), and matter warping spacetime — are predictions of Einstein's General Theory of Relativity. Answer: D.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - General Relativity predicts: gravity bends light, spacetime curvature, expanding universe
     - Hubble observed expanding universe empirically, but Einstein's equations predicted it

**[Q127]** [2013]
> Consider the following phenomena :  1. Size of the sun at dusk 2. Colour of the sun at dawn 3. Moon being visible at dawn 4. Twinkle of stars in the sky 5. Polestar being visible in the sky  Which of the above are optical illusions?

     A) 1, 2 and 3
     B) 3, 4 and 5
  ✅ C) 1, 2 and 4
     D) 2, 3 and 5

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Moon visible at dawn (3) is NOT an optical illusion — the moon can genuinely be visible during daytime. Twinkling of stars (4) IS an optical illusion (atmospheric refraction). Polestar visibility (5) is a real phenomenon.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Optical illusions: sun appearing larger at dusk (1, atmospheric refraction), sun's colour at dawn (2, scattering), twinkling of stars (4, atmospheric refraction). Moon visibility at dawn (3) and Polestar visibility (5) are real. Answer: C (1, 2, 4).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Sun appears larger at horizon: optical illusion (Ponzo illusion + refraction)
     - Star twinkling: atmospheric refraction (turbulence), an optical illusion
     - Moon visible at dawn: real phenomenon, not an illusion

**[Q2110]** [2013]
> Rainbow is produced when sunlight falls on drops of rain. Which of the following physical phenomena are responsible for this?  1. Dispersion 2. Refraction 3. Internal reflection  Select the correct answer using the codes given below.

     A) 1 and 2 only
     B) 2 and 3 only
     C) 1 and 3 only
  ✅ D) 1, 2 and 3

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may pick only refraction or only dispersion — rainbow requires all three: dispersion (splitting white light), refraction (bending at water surface), and internal reflection (reflecting inside droplet).

  📌 **Key facts:**

     - Different wavelengths of light refract at different angles causing colour separation
     - Secondary rainbow forms due to two internal reflections

**[Q4330]** [2011]
> The surface of a lake is frozen in severe winter, but the water at its bottom is still liquid. What is the reason?

     A) Ice is a bad conductor of heat
     B) Since the surface of the lake is at the same temperature as the air, no heat is lost
  ✅ C) The density of water is maximum at 4°C
     D) None of the statements (a), (b) and (c) given above is correct

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Candidates may choose option A (ice as bad conductor) as the reason; while true, the primary reason water remains liquid at the bottom is the anomalous expansion of water — maximum density at 4°C.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Water's maximum density at 4°C means water at bottom stays at 4°C (densest), while surface freezes at 0°C; this density stratification prevents complete freezing from bottom up.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Water has maximum density at 4°C — anomalous expansion
     - This property enables aquatic life to survive under frozen lake surfaces

**[Q4337]** [2001]
> When water is heated from 0°C to 10°C, its volume

     A) increases
     B) decreases
     C) does not change
  ✅ D) first decreases and then increases

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Most students expect water to expand uniformly when heated; the anomalous contraction between 0–4°C is a counterintuitive exception often missed.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Water has maximum density at 4°C: it contracts from 0–4°C then expands from 4–10°C, so volume first decreases then increases.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Water has maximum density at 4°C
     - Anomalous expansion: water contracts 0–4°C, expands 4–100°C

**[Q474]** [1999]

  ✅ A) total internal reflection
     B) interference
     C) diffraction
     D) polarisation

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Diffraction and interference both involve wave behavior, which students associate with optical fiber; polarization is associated with light waves making it a plausible wrong choice.

**[Q756]** [1997]
> A girl is swinging on a swing in sitting position. When the same girl stands up, the period of swing will

  ✅ A) be shorter
     B) be longer
     C) depend on the height of the girl
     D) not change

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Standing vs sitting changes the weight distribution, making candidates think period increases; they may confuse with a physical pendulum where mass distribution matters.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Period of simple pendulum T = 2π√(L/g); when girl stands, the effective length L (distance to center of mass) decreases, so period becomes shorter.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Pendulum period T = 2π√(L/g); shorter L = shorter period
     - Standing raises center of mass → reduces effective pendulum length

**[Q670]** [1995]
> Assertion (A) : A diamond sparkles more than a glass imitation cut to the same shape. Reason (R) : The refractive index of diamond is less than that of glass. In the context of the above two statements, which one of the following is correct ?

     A) Both A and R are true, and R is the correct explanation of A
     B) Both A and R are true, but R is not a correct explanation of A
  ✅ C) A is true, but R is false
     D) A is false, but R is true

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Both A and R seem true at first glance — diamonds do sparkle more. The trap is in the Reason: diamond actually has a HIGHER refractive index than glass, not lower, making R false.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Assertion A is true (diamond sparkles more due to total internal reflection). Reason R is false — diamond's refractive index (~2.42) is much higher than glass (~1.5). Answer: C.

     - Diamond refractive index ~2.42 vs glass ~1.5 — diamond is higher
     - Higher refractive index causes greater total internal reflection, enabling more sparkle
