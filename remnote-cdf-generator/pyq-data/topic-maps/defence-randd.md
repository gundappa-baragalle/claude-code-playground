**[Q3413]** [2023]
> Consider the following statements :  1. Ballistic missiles are jet-propelled at subsonic speeds throughout their flights, while cruise missiles are rocket-powered only in the initial phase of flight. 2. Agni-V is a medium-range supersonic cruise missile, while BrahMos is a solid-fuelled intercontinental ballistic missile.  Which of the statements given above is/are correct?

     A) 1 only
     B) 2 only
     C) Both 1 and 2
  ✅ D) Neither 1 nor 2

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Both statements contain swapped descriptions — Agni-V is an ICBM (not cruise missile), and BrahMos is a supersonic cruise missile (not a ballistic missile). Both statements are completely wrong.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Statement 1 is wrong: ballistic missiles are rocket-powered initially then follow ballistic trajectory; cruise missiles are jet-propelled throughout. Statement 2 is wrong: Agni-V is an ICBM; BrahMos is a supersonic cruise missile — both false, answer is 'Neither 1 nor 2'.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Agni-V: solid-fuel ICBM with range ~5000 km; BrahMos: supersonic cruise missile, joint India-Russia, range ~450 km
     - Ballistic missiles: rocket-powered only initially, follow parabolic trajectory; cruise missiles: jet-powered throughout at low altitude

**[Q2760]** [2018]
> What is "Terminal High Altitude Area Defense (THAAD)", sometimes seen in the news?

     A) An Israeli radar system
     B) India's indigenous anti-missile programme
  ✅ C) An American anti-missile system
     D) A defence collaboration between Japan and South Korea

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Israel's Iron Dome is a famous missile defence system making option A tempting; India's Prithvi Air Defence (PAD) makes option B plausible too.
  💡 **Eliminate:** THAAD (Terminal High Altitude Area Defense) is a US Army anti-ballistic missile system — answer C. 'American' and 'terminal high altitude' uniquely identify it.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - THAAD is a US Army ground-based anti-missile defence system designed to intercept ballistic missiles at high altitude
     - THAAD was deployed in South Korea in 2017, causing diplomatic tensions with China

**[Q3490]** [2024] Consider the following aircraft:  1. Rafael 2. MiG-29 3. Tejas MK-1  How many of the above are considered fifth generation fighter aircraft?

     A) Only one
     B) Only two
     C) All three
  ✅ D) None

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may assume Rafale is 5th generation; Rafale, MiG-29, and Tejas MK-1 are all 4th/4.5th generation fighters — none qualifies as 5th generation (which requires stealth, supercruise, AESA radar, sensor fusion).

### GS3

**[2021]** Q5. How is S-400 air defence system technically superior to any other system presently available in the world? (150 words) 10
  *Paper: GS3 | Dim: WHAT | 10m | ~150w*
  🏷️ Themes: Defence technology, Strategic autonomy, Air defence

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~150 words: intro 30w / body 97w / conclusion 30w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** The S-400 Triumf represents one of the world's most capable air defence systems, offering India unprecedented multi-layered protection against aerial threats.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Range: up to 400 km for tracking, 40-400 km for different missile variants
      • Multi-layered: engages aircraft, ballistic missiles, cruise missiles, drones simultaneously
      • Radar: 360-degree detection, low-observable (stealth) target engagement capability
      • Superiority over alternatives: only system engaging targets at multiple altitudes and ranges simultaneously
      • India's rationale: Pakistan-China aerial threat; Patriot lacks equivalent versatility
      • Challenges: CAATSA waiver uncertainty, maintenance dependency on Russia, software vulnerability concerns
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** S-400's technical superiority lies in its simultaneous multi-target, multi-altitude engagement capability, making it uniquely suited for India's complex threat environment despite geopolitical complications.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS3: Defence technology, GS2: IR — India-Russia-US, Internal security
  🌐 Contemporary: Russia-Ukraine war's impact on S-400 supplies and CAATSA waiver debate remains active.
