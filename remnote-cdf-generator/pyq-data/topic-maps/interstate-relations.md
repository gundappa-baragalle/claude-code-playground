### GS2

**[2013]** Q6. Constitutional mechanisms to resolve the inter-state water disputes have failed to address and solve the problems. Is the failure due to structural or process inadequacy or both? Discuss.
  *Paper: GS2 | Dim: WHY | 10m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Federalism and water disputes, Constitutional mechanisms, Centre-state relations

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Inter-state river water disputes represent a critical test of India's federal architecture, where constitutional mechanisms have repeatedly fallen short of durable resolution.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Constitutional framework: Article 262 + ISWD Act 1956 — Parliament can adjudicate, SC jurisdiction excluded
      • Structural failures: no fixed time limit for tribunal awards (amended 2002 — 3 years + 2 years extension), no enforcement mechanism, states can approach SC despite bar
      • Process failures: political delays in tribunal formation, states' non-compliance with awards, data disputes
      • Cauvery: tribunal constituted 1990, award 2007 — still contested; Kerala-Karnataka-Tamil Nadu-Puducherry
      • Reforms needed: permanent inter-state water dispute authority, river basin organization, integrating National Water Framework
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Resolving inter-state water disputes requires institutionalizing cooperative federalism through a permanent water disputes authority with teeth for enforcement beyond current tribunal limitations.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS2: Federalism, GS3: Agriculture and irrigation, GS3: Disaster management — drought
  🌐 Contemporary: Mahadayi dispute (Goa-Karnataka-Maharashtra) and Cauvery water management authority debates remain active.
