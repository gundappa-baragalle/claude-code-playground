**[Q2280]** [2009]
> Where is the famous Virupaksha temple located?

     A) Bhadrachalam
     B) Chidambaram
  ✅ C) Hampi
     D) Srikalahasti

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Students may confuse Virupaksha temple with Chidambaram (Nataraja) or Srikalahasti (famous Shiva temple) — Virupaksha at Hampi is a Shiva temple that was the royal temple of the Vijayanagara Empire.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Virupaksha temple is at Hampi (Karnataka) — it was the royal temple of Vijayanagara Empire. Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Virupaksha Temple is located at Hampi, Karnataka
     - It was the principal temple of the Vijayanagara Empire (14th-16th century CE)
     - Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site

**[Q1180]** [2006]
> Who among the following laid the foundation of Rashtrakuta Empire?

     A) Amoghavarsha I
  ✅ B) Dantidurga
     C) Dhruva
     D) Krishna I

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Amoghavarsha I was the most famous Rashtrakuta king (great patron of literature), leading students to believe he founded the dynasty. Dantidurga actually founded it.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Dantidurga (r. 735–756 CE) founded the Rashtrakuta Empire by defeating the Chalukyas. Amoghavarsha I was the greatest but not the founder. Answer: B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Dantidurga founded Rashtrakuta Empire ~735 CE
     - Amoghavarsha I (814–878 CE): greatest Rashtrakuta king, patron of Kannada literature
     - Rashtrakutas ruled Deccan; displaced Chalukyas of Badami

**[Q998]** [2001]
> Hoysala monuments are found in

     A) Hampi and Hospet
  ✅ B) Halebid and Belur
     C) Mysore and Bangalore
     D) Sringeri and Dharwar

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Hampi is associated with Vijayanagara Empire, not Hoysala. Students may confuse Karnataka temple dynasties.
  💡 **Eliminate:** Hoysala monuments are at Halebid (Hoysaleswara temple) and Belur (Chennakesava temple) in Hassan district, Karnataka. Hampi = Vijayanagara. → Answer B.
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Hoysala monuments found at Halebid and Belur in Karnataka
     - Hoysala architecture is known for intricate soapstone carvings

**[Q1792]** [2001]
> Which one of the Chola kings conquered Ceylon ?

     A) Aditya I
  ✅ B) Rajaraja I
     C) Rajendra
     D) Vijayalaya

  ⚠️ **Trap:** Rajendra Chola is famous for conquering Southeast Asia and defeating the Pala empire, leading many to confuse him with Rajaraja I who actually conquered Ceylon (Sri Lanka).
  💡 **Eliminate:** Rajaraja I (985–1014 CE) conquered Ceylon and the Maldives — his naval campaigns are a key fact; Rajendra I extended Chola power to Southeast Asia (Srivijaya).
  📌 **Key facts:**
     - Rajaraja I conquered Ceylon (Sri Lanka) and Maldives
     - Rajendra I sent naval expedition to Southeast Asia — defeated Srivijaya
     - Aditya I defeated the Pallava king Aparajita

### GS1

**[2016]** Q2. Krishnadeva Raya, the King of Vijayanagar, was not only an accomplished scholar himself but was also a great patron of learning and literature. Discuss. (200 words) 12.5
  *Paper: GS1 | Dim: WHO | 12.5m | ~200w*
  🏷️ Themes: Medieval cultural patronage, Vijayanagara Empire, South Indian literature

  **Answer Framework:**
  *(~200 words: intro 30w / body 130w / conclusion 40w)*
  - 🔑 **Intro:** Krishnadevaraya's reign (1509-1529) represents the zenith of the Vijayanagara Empire not only in military power but in cultural and literary achievement.
  - 📋 **Body:**
      • Krishnadevaraya himself authored 'Amuktamalyada' — a devotional poem on Vishnu in Telugu
      • Patron of Ashtadiggajas — eight eminent Telugu poets including Allasani Peddana ('Andhra Kavita Pitamaha')
      • Also patronised Sanskrit scholars; supported Kannada and Tamil literature
      • His court at Hampi was described by Portuguese traveller Domingo Paes as magnificent
      • Promoted Vaishnavism while maintaining religious tolerance
      • Built the Hazara Rama temple complex — reflecting his literary and artistic sensibilities
  - 🎯 **Conclusion:** Krishnadevaraya exemplified the ideal of a warrior-scholar king whose cultural patronage created a golden age of Telugu literature that continues to influence South Indian civilisation.
  🔗 Interlinkages: GS1: Medieval art & architecture, South Indian history, Cultural heritage
  🌐 Contemporary: Vijayanagara legacy is central to debates about South Indian cultural identity and Hindu heritage.
