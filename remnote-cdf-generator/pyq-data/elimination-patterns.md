## General Factual Elimination

**Frequency:** 2246 questions | **Strategy:** If stuck, eliminate by extreme language ('all', 'only', 'always', 'never' = usually wrong) and by subject-specific logic (ask: is this consistent with India's constitutional framework / scientific consensus / historical record?).

**Most common in:** Science & Technology (430), History (401), Economy (346), Environment (251)

**PYQ Examples:**

**Example 1** [1999] *Economy*
> Persons below the poverty line in India are classified as such based on whether
- **Trap:** Option D ('wages below minimum wages') sounds logical since low wages = poverty. Option C ('agricultural labourers + SC/ST') conflates social identity with poverty criterion. India's official poverty line is purely consumption-based.
- **Elimination:** India's poverty line = minimum food basket/caloric intake (Tendulkar committee). Eliminate B (employment-based), C (caste/occupation-based), D (wage-based). Only A (food basket) is the official criterion.

**Example 2** [2004] *Science & Technology*
> Assertion (A)
: In our houses, the current in AC electricity line changes direction 60 times per second.
Reason (R)
: The frequency of alternating voltage supplied is 60 hertz.
- **Trap:** Students who know India uses 50 Hz may mark Assertion as false. Trap: the question tests logical consistency between A and R. The Reason incorrectly states India's frequency as 60 Hz — so R is factually wrong for India.
- **Elimination:** India's AC frequency = 50 Hz, NOT 60 Hz (60 Hz = USA/Canada). So the Reason is factually WRONG. At 50 Hz, current changes direction 100 times/sec (not 60). Answer C: the assertion may be stated as true (in 60 Hz context) but the Reason is false for India.

**Example 3** [2007] *International Relations*
> What was the purpose of the Operation Sukoon launched by the Government of India?
- **Trap:** Options A (Indonesia earthquake) and C (Darfur/UN) sound like plausible humanitarian missions. 'Sukoon' name exploited: sounds like it could relate to any peace operation.
- **Elimination:** 'Sukoon' = peace/relief in Urdu/Hindi → fits evacuation from conflict zone. 2006 = Israel-Hezbollah war in Lebanon → Indian nationals evacuated. INS Mumbai deployed. Eliminate A (earthquake relief, not evacuation) and C (UN Darfur).

## Common Confusion / Counter-Intuitive Traps

**Frequency:** 711 questions | **Strategy:** The trap IS the most plausible-sounding option. Identify it first ('this sounds right but...') then actively look for what makes it wrong — usually a subtle qualifier, a wrong region, or a category error.

**Most common in:** History (161), Science & Technology (129), Geography (107), Economy (85)

**PYQ Examples:**

**Example 1** [2004] *History*
> Consider the following statements about Sikh Gurus: 1. Banda Bahadur was appointed as the military leader of the Sikhs by Guru Tegh Bahadur. 2. Guru Arjan Dev became the Sikh Guru after Guru Ram Das....
- **Trap:** Statement 1 tricky — both Guru Tegh Bahadur (9th) and Guru Gobind Singh (10th) are prominent; students confuse who sent Banda Bahadur. Statement 3 about Gurumukhi plausible since Arjan Dev compiled the Adi Granth in Gurumukhi script.
- **Elimination:** Gurumukhi = Guru Angad Dev (2nd Guru), NOT Arjan Dev — eliminate S3. Banda Bahadur commissioned by Guru Gobind Singh (10th), not Guru Tegh Bahadur (9th). Guru Arjan Dev DID succeed Guru Ram Das — S2 correct.

**Example 2** [2015] *Economy*
> When the Reserve Bank of India reduces the Statutory Liquidity Ratio by 50 basis points, which of the following is likely to happen?
- **Trap:** Option B (FII capital inflow) tempting if SLR confused with interest rate cuts. Option D says 'drastically reduce liquidity' which is the exact OPPOSITE of SLR reduction's effect.
- **Elimination:** SLR reduction = banks hold LESS in liquid assets = MORE funds for lending = expanded credit supply = competitive pressure to cut rates = C. SLR reduction INCREASES liquidity (eliminate D). Doesn't directly affect FII flows (eliminate B).

**Example 3** [2001] *Science & Technology*
> The range of
Agni-II
missile is around
- **Trap:** Agni ranges commonly confused: Agni-I≈700km, Agni-II≈2000km, Agni-III≈3500km. Option C (3500km) = Agni-III's range — classic mix-up.
- **Elimination:** Memorize: Agni-I=700km, Agni-II=2000km, Agni-III=3500km, Agni-IV=4000km, Agni-V=5000+km. Answer = B (2000km).

## Match the Following

**Frequency:** 202 questions | **Strategy:** Never try to match all pairs at once. Fix the one you know with absolute certainty → use it to eliminate codes → reduce to 1–2 remaining codes → verify only what's necessary.

**Most common in:** History (43), Geography (42), Governance (41), Science & Technology (30)

**PYQ Examples:**

**Example 1** [1995] *International Relations*
> Match List I with List II and select the correct answer by using the codes given below the lists
List I | List II
I. Ernesto Zedillo | (A) Uruguay
II. Alberto Fujimori | (B) Brazil
III. Julio Maria...
- **Trap:** Names have misleading phonetics — 'Cardoso' sounds Central American, 'Zedillo' sounds generically Spanish. Without specific knowledge of 1990s Latin American politics, all options look plausible.
- **Elimination:** Anchor on Fujimori = Peru (famous Japanese-Peruvian, unique identifier). From the code that maps II to E (Peru), check which option has II→E and work outward.

**Example 2** [1999] *Geography*
> Match List I with List II and select the correct answer using the codes given below the Lists
List I | List II
I. Cotton | (A) Rainfall 1000-1500 mm; Temperature 40° - 60°C
II. Flax | (B) Rainfall...
- **Trap:** Jute needs HIGH rainfall (1500–2000mm) which students confuse with Cotton. Sugarbeet is a TEMPERATE crop (5–18°C) which is counter-intuitive since India is tropical.
- **Elimination:** Anchor on Jute = Bengal delta = high rainfall (1500–2000mm). Sugarbeet = European/temperate crop = low temperature (5–18°C). Cotton = semi-arid = moderate rainfall. Match remaining.

**Example 3** [1998] *Science & Technology*
> Match List I with List II and select the correct answer using the codes given below the lists
List I | List II
I. Theory of Mutation | (A) Beadk and Tatum
II. Theory of Evolution | (B) Jacob and...
- **Trap:** Beadle & Tatum (one gene-one enzyme) vs Jacob & Monod (operon) easily mixed up — both 20th-century molecular biology Nobel laureates.
- **Elimination:** Anchor: Darwin = Evolution. De Vries = Mutation (1901). Beadle & Tatum = One gene-one enzyme (1941, Nobel 1958). Jacob & Monod = Operon/lac operon (1961, Nobel 1965). Match: I-D, II-C, III-A, IV-B = B.

## Negation Questions ("Which is NOT / EXCEPT / INCORRECT")

**Frequency:** 200 questions | **Strategy:** Find ONE true statement — the answer is everything else. Don't read all options looking for the false one; read looking for the 3 true ones first, the odd one out becomes obvious.

**Most common in:** Geography (35), History (35), Science & Technology (34), Polity (28)

**PYQ Examples:**

**Example 1** [2007] *International Relations*
> Which one of the following is not a member of the Shanghai Cooperation Organization?
- **Trap:** All options are post-Soviet states — hard to distinguish without specific knowledge. Russia, Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan are original SCO members. Ukraine's European orientation is the key differentiator.
- **Elimination:** SCO = China + Russia + Central Asian states. Original members (2001): China, Russia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Uzbekistan. Ukraine oriented toward Europe/NATO, never joined SCO. Answer: C (Ukraine).

**Example 2** [2001] *Polity*
> Which one of the following duties is NOT performed by the Comptroller and Auditor General of India ?
- **Trap:** Students often think CAG 'controls' public money (like a treasurer), but it only audits — it does not control receipt/issue of public money. That is the function of the Controller General of Accounts, not CAG.
- **Elimination:** CAG (Article 148) audits expenditure but does NOT control public money receipts/issue — that role belongs to the Ministry of Finance/CGA. The question asks for what CAG does NOT do. Answer: D.

**Example 3** [2004] *Governance*
> Which of the following pairs is not correctly matched?
- **Trap:** Reinhold Messner is a famous mountaineer (first to climb all 14 eight-thousanders), NOT a computer technologist. The question asks for the 'not correctly matched' pair.
- **Elimination:** Reinhold Messner is a renowned mountaineer, not related to computer technology. All other pairs (Shapley-Astronomy, Mendel-Heredity, Hounsfield-CT Scan) are correct. Answer: A.

## Statement-Based ("Which statements are correct?")

**Frequency:** 124 questions | **Strategy:** For 'all/only/none' answer patterns: find one statement you're certain about. If Statement 1 is TRUE, eliminate options that say '1 is false'. One anchor eliminates 50–70% of options.

**Most common in:** Science & Technology (22), Economy (21), History (20), Polity (20)

**PYQ Examples:**

**Example 1** [2006] *Economy*
> Which one of the following is the correct statement? Service Tax is a/an
- **Trap:** Students confuse who levies it — service tax is Central Government (not State). Also confuse direct vs indirect: indirect tax = paid through intermediary (service provider) not directly by consumer to government.
- **Elimination:** Indirect tax = paid by intermediary and passed on to consumer (GST, service tax, excise). Direct tax = paid directly by taxpayer (income tax, corporate tax). Central Government levied service tax before GST. Answer: B.

**Example 2** [2020] *Science & Technology*
> With reference to Visible Light Communication (VLC) technology, which of the following statements are correct?

1. VLC uses electromagnetic spectrum wavelengths 375 to 780 nm.
2. VLC is known as...
- **Trap:** Statement 2 says VLC is 'long-range' which sounds plausible for modern tech, but VLC is explicitly SHORT-range (limited by line-of-sight and light intensity). This is the key trap.
- **Elimination:** VLC uses visible light (375-780nm) ✓ and has no EM interference ✓. But VLC is short-range (line of sight, blocked by walls), not long-range. Eliminate options containing statement 2 (A,B,D). Answer: C (1,3,4).

**Example 3** [2021] *History*
> With reference to the history of ancient India, which of the following statements is/are correct?

1. Mitakshara was the civil law for upper castes and Dayabhaga was the civil law for lower...
- **Trap:** Statement 1 is false — Mitakshara and Dayabhaga are NOT divided by caste; they are regional schools (Mitakshara: most of India; Dayabhaga: Bengal/Assam). Statement 3 is also false — Dayabhaga does not specifically cover female members.
- **Elimination:** Only Statement 2 is correct: under Mitakshara, sons get coparcenary rights by birth (during father's lifetime); under Dayabhaga, rights arise only after father's death. Answer: B (2 only).

## Location-Based Questions

**Frequency:** 79 questions | **Strategy:** State/region confusables are the most common trap. For every location fact, memorize the confusable wrong answer as a pair. Chilika = Odisha (not AP). Loktak = Manipur (not Assam).

**Most common in:** Geography (43), International Relations (7), Science & Technology (7), History (5)

**PYQ Examples:**

**Example 1** [1997] *Geography*
> The river shown on the map is
- **Trap:** Without the actual map, all four rivers (Irrawaddy, Mekong, Chao Phraya, Salween) flow through Southeast Asian mainland, making elimination by elimination necessary based on directional flow patterns.
- **Elimination:** Salween flows south through Myanmar close to Thailand border; Irrawaddy flows centrally through Myanmar; Mekong flows through Laos/Thailand/Vietnam. Map context needed but Salween D is the likely answer based on eastern Myanmar position.

**Example 2** [1998] *International Relations*
> Which one of the following was the venue for the preliminary talks between the Sri Lankan Government and representatives of Tamil United Liberation Front and other militant groups ?
- **Trap:** New Delhi and Colombo are obvious diplomatic capitals that appear logical for such talks; Chennai is plausible given Tamil Nadu's proximity to the Sri Lankan Tamil issue.
- **Elimination:** The correct answer 'Zirconiun' (likely Thimpu/another city — possibly a misprint in the original) is obscure; eliminate obvious capitals and recall that preliminary talks often happen in neutral/third locations.

**Example 3** [2002] *History*
> The motive behind Shahjahan’s Balkh campaign was to
- **Trap:** Option B (reconquer Mughal homelands Samarqand and Farghana) seems historically plausible given Babur's nostalgia for Central Asia; option C (scientific frontier on Amu Darya) sounds strategic — both are attractive distractors.
- **Elimination:** Shahjahan's Balkh campaign (1646-47) was primarily defensive — to install a friendly buffer ruler in Balkh (near Kabul's northern border) against Uzbek/Persian threats; option A is the strategic rationale.

## Chronological Order / Sequence

**Frequency:** 24 questions | **Strategy:** Anchor on the extreme — what came FIRST or LAST? If you know the earliest event, eliminate all codes where it is not first. Then check adjacent order only.

**Most common in:** History (22), Polity (1), Economy (1)

**PYQ Examples:**

**Example 1** [1996] *History*
> Consider the following landmarks in Indian education : (i) Hindu College, Calcutta (ii) University of Calcutta (iii) Adam’s Report (iv) Wood’s Despatch The correct chronological order of these...
- **Trap:** Adam's Report (1835) and Wood's Despatch (1854) are often swapped in chronology. Hindu College (1817) precedes the University of Calcutta (1857).
- **Elimination:** Chronological order: Hindu College, Calcutta (1817) → Adam's Report (1835) → Wood's Despatch (1854) → University of Calcutta (1857). This matches option A: I, III, IV, II.

**Example 2** [2003] *Polity*
> Which of the following Constitutional Amendments are related to raising the number of Members of Lok Sabha to be : elected from the States?
- **Trap:** Constitutional amendment numbers are frequently confused; candidates may guess based on chronology of amendments without knowing specific content.
- **Elimination:** The 7th Amendment (1956) reorganized states and affected Lok Sabha seats; the 31st Amendment (1973) increased Lok Sabha seats to 545. Answer: C (7th and 31st).

**Example 3** [2002] *Economy*
> Consider the following financial institution of India:
1. Industrial Finance Corporation of India (IFCI)
2. Industrial Credit and Investment Corporation of India (ICICI)
3. Industrial Development...
- **Trap:** Candidates may confuse the order; ICICI is often thought to have been established after IDBI due to its commercial bank prominence, but IFCI was first (1948).
- **Elimination:** IFCI (1948) → ICICI (1955) → IDBI (1964) → NABARD (1982): chronological sequence is A (1,2,3,4).

## Calculation / Formula-Based

**Frequency:** 15 questions | **Strategy:** Estimate before calculating. In most UPSC calculation questions, options are far enough apart that rough estimates suffice. Only calculate precisely when two options are close.

**Most common in:** Polity (5), Economy (5), History (2), Governance (2)

**PYQ Examples:**

**Example 1** [1996] *Polity*
> If the number of seats allocated to a state in the Lok Sabha is 42, then the number of seats reserved for the Scheduled Castes in that state will be
- **Trap:** Students may calculate 1/7 of 42 = 6, or remember the national SC population proportion (~15%) to arrive at different numbers. The actual reservation ratio needs to match population proportion.
- **Elimination:** SC reservation in Lok Sabha is approximately proportional to SC population (~15%). For a 42-seat state, 15% ≈ 6. Correct: D (6). Note: the exact formula is SC seats proportional to SC population of that state.

**Example 2** [2001] *Economy*
> The term National Income represents
- **Trap:** The distinction between NNP at market prices and NNP at factor cost (National Income) trips many students; option A (GNP minus depreciation = NNP at market prices, not National Income) is the classic trap.
- **Elimination:** National Income = NNP at Factor Cost = GNP at market prices − Depreciation − Net Indirect Taxes (indirect taxes minus subsidies); option C correctly captures this formula.

**Example 3** [2010] *History*
> After Quit India Movement, C. Raja Gopalachari issued a pamphlet entitled “The Way Out”.
Which one of the following was a proposal in this pamphlet?
- **Trap:** Multiple options sound like legitimate constitutional proposals; Rajagopalachari's 'Way Out' specifically proposed a solution to the constitutional deadlock rather than specific institutional arrangements.
- **Elimination:** Rajagopalachari's pamphlet was primarily aimed at breaking the political deadlock between Congress and the British by proposing a formula for Congress-League understanding — focus on its political purpose of resolving deadlock.

## Pair Elimination ("Common to both A and B")

**Frequency:** 14 questions | **Strategy:** List what's genuinely common to BOTH subjects. Reject options that are unique to only one. The trap is usually something strongly associated with only one subject.

**Most common in:** Geography (5), History (5), International Relations (2), Internal Security (1)

**PYQ Examples:**

**Example 1** [2010] *Geography*
> With reference to the mineral resources of India, consider the following pairs: Mineral 90% Natural sources in
1. Copper Jharkhand
2. Nickel Orissa
3. Tungsten Kerala
Which of the pairs give above...
- **Trap:** Copper is associated with Jharkhand (Singhbhum) but the '90%' claim for Jharkhand is debatable — Rajasthan also has significant copper. Tungsten is NOT from Kerala. Nickel from Odisha (Sukinda Valley) is correct.
- **Elimination:** Only Pair 2 (Nickel-Odisha/Orissa) is correctly matched — Sukinda Valley in Odisha has ~96% of India's nickel reserves. Copper's largest source is Rajasthan (Khetri), not Jharkhand. Tungsten is from Rajasthan/Karnataka. Answer: B.

**Example 2** [1996] *History*
> Which of the following were common to both Buddhism and Jainism ?
I. Avoidance of extremities of penance and enjoyment.
II. Indifference to the authority of the Vedas
III. Denial of efficacy of...
- **Trap:** Statement I (Middle Path — avoidance of extremities) is actually specific to Buddhism and NOT shared with Jainism, which advocates extreme penance (sallekhana). This is the key trap.
- **Elimination:** Jainism prescribes severe austerities unlike Buddhism's Middle Path, so Statement I is incorrect for Jainism. Statements II, III, IV are common to both — select option B.

**Example 3** [2009] *International Relations*
> Consider the following pairs : Automobile Manufacturer Headquarters
1. BMW AG USA
2. Daimler AG Sweden
3. Renault S A. France
4. Volkswagen AG Germany
Which of the pairs given above is/are correctly...
- **Trap:** BMW is German (Munich), not USA; Daimler is German (Stuttgart), not Swedish; Kookmin Bank is South Korean, not Japanese. Only Renault (France) and Volkswagen (Germany) are correctly matched.
- **Elimination:** Only pairs 3 (Renault-France) and 4 (Volkswagen-Germany) are correctly matched. BMW and Daimler are both German; Kookmin Bank is South Korean. Answer is B.

## Anchor Technique (Fix one known fact)

**Frequency:** 2 questions | **Strategy:** Identify the one pair/fact you know with certainty. Use the answer code that accommodates it to reduce options from 4 to 1–2.

**Most common in:** Science & Technology (1), Geography (1)

**PYQ Examples:**

**Example 1** [2006] *Science & Technology*
> In which one of the following areas did the Indira Gandhi Centre for Atomic Research make significant progress in the year 2005?
- **Trap:** Options B (radioisotopes/metallurgy) and C (heavy water) sound more broadly significant. Students unfamiliar with fast breeder milestones may choose them.
- **Elimination:** IGCAR = Kalpakkam = SPECIFICALLY for fast breeder reactor. FBTR = Fast Breeder Test Reactor = uses mixed carbide fuel. 2005 milestone = reprocessing this unique carbide fuel. Anchor on IGCAR=FBR=Kalpakkam.

**Example 2** [1999] *Geography*
> In the given map, which one of the following pairs of ocean currents is shown?
- **Trap:** This question references a map that is not available in text form, making it impossible to answer without the original visual. Among the options, Benguela and Guinea are both Atlantic currents near Africa, making option D plausible.
- **Elimination:** Without the map, anchor on geographic logic: Benguela (cold, South Atlantic off SW Africa) and Guinea (warm, tropical Atlantic off W Africa) are both near the African coast — likely shown together.
